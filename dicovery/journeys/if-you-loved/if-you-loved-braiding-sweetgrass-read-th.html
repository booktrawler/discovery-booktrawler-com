If You Loved Braiding Sweetgrass, Read These Nature-Inspired Books

Botanical poetry, lyrical nonfiction, and charming stories of the natural world await in these titles that echo the essence of Braiding Sweetgrass.

Who these books are for

  • Nature enthusiasts
  • Fans of lyrical nonfiction and poetry
  • Readers interested in environmental science and sustainability

Start here

If you only read one, choose Roots & Wrath, a poignant exploration of human-nature connections through personal narrative and scientific insight.

Braiding Sweetgrass, Robin Wall Kimmerer's eloquent blend of scientific expertise and poetic storytelling, has captivated readers with its nuanced exploration of the intricate relationships between humans and the natural world. For those enchanted by her unique voice and perspective, this reading journey offers a selection of books that share similar themes, styles, and a deep reverence for the beauty and complexity of nature.

The collection

  1. Braiding Sweetgrass for Young Adults Indigenous Wisdom, Scientific Knowledge, and the Teachings of Plants

    Robin Wall Kimmerer

    In "Braiding Sweetgrass for Young Adults," Robin Wall Kimmerer invites you to listen to the ancient wisdom of plants and the land. As a botanist and member of the Citizen Potawatomi Nation, Kimmerer shares how honoring the reciprocity between humans and the natural world enhances our understanding of ourselves and our place on the earth. Through stunning illustrations, reflection questions, and informative sidebars, this adapted edition inspires a deeper connection with the world around us, encouraging readers to recognize their role as stewards and give back in harmony with the earth's generosity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. This Hair Belongs

    JaNay Brown-Wood

    Celebrate the cultural and historical significance of Black hair with this enchanting picture book. Through lyrical verse and stunning illustrations, This Hair Belongs weaves together the intricate stories and stunning styles of African and American Black hair, empowering young readers to embrace their natural beauty and proud heritage from a very young age. A must-read nonfiction title honoring Black cultural heritage and self-love.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Roots & Wrath

    E J Wenstrom

    "Prepare for a spellbinding thrill ride as five former friends-turned-witches are forced back together by a deadly pact. Nineteen years after their dark ritual in the woods, deceit and murder stalk them as they race to uncover the traitor in their midst. With a ghostly past hauntingly lurking, only the most ruthless can survive the treacherous path to break their magical bond - and the chance to escape their dark pasts once and for all."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Legendary Frybread Drive-In Intertribal Stories

    Cynthia Leitich Smith

    Legendary Frybread Drive-In, edited by Cynthia Leitich Smith, is a vibrant, award-winning anthology that brings together interconnected stories and poems from acclaimed and up-and-coming Indigenous writers. Set against the backdrop of a beloved frybread joint that serves as a gathering place for Native teens, this collection serves up a rotating feast of laughter, love, and cultural pride. With its diverse voices and tales of resilience, Legendary Frybread Drive-In is a must-read for anyone seeking a rich, authentic portrayal of contemporary Indigenous youth.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. A Good Girl's Guide to Murder

    Holly Jackson

    In this gripping page-turner, Pip sets out to uncover the truth behind her friend's supposed murder, revealing a tangled web of secrets in her small town. With each new twist, Pip's own life is put in danger, propelling her toward an unexpected ending. Perfect for mystery lovers and fans of complex, suspenseful teen thrillers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Ballad of Falling Dragons

    Sarah A Parker

    Immerse yourself in the breathtaking world of The Ballad of Falling Dragons, where ancient magic, mysterious creatures, and an unforgettable love story collide. This New York Times #1 bestseller and eagerly anticipated sequel to When the Moon Hatched promises a thrilling adventure as Raeve navigates vengeance, forbidden romance, and the fate of realms. With daring quests, burning secrets, and the promise of a world-changing revelation, this epic fantasy romance will captivate and leave you yearning for the next moonfall. Pick up this instant classic to be swept into a mesmerizing tale of passion, power, and self-discovery.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Unsettling Salad!

    Aaron Reynolds

    Join Jasper Rabbit as he shares the chilling tale of Thadeus Badger and the broccoli that bites back! When this typical badger makes a salad-eating deal, he learns the hard way that even the healthiest foods can have a dark side. With humor and fright, this creepy chapter book will leave you on edge, warning you to beware of veggies that can turn the tables.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Lost and Found Jigsaw Book

    Oliver Jeffers

    Indulge in a heartwarming tale of unlikely friendship as a curious boy and an adventurous penguin navigate the ups and downs of companionship. With Oliver Jeffers' endearing storytelling and delightful illustrations, this charming picture book is a must-read for kids of all ages. Now, take the immersive experience to another level by completing the six included 12-piece jigsaw puzzles, bringing each whimsical scene to vivid life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. CABIN Off the Grid Adventures with a Clueless Craftsman

    Patrick Hutchison

    "In this charming, laugh-out-loud memoir, a clueless craftsman trades his office cubicle for a dilapidated Pacific Northwest cabin - and the adventure of a lifetime. With wit, humor, and heart, CABIN chronicles six years of thrilling renovations, revealing the transformative power of hands-on labor, perseverance, and embracing the unknown. A joyous ode to the great outdoors, DIY determination and the beauty in building something from nothing, this is a must-read for anyone craving a fresh start or simply a good story well-told."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Dark and Other Scary Stories

    Max Brallier

    Delve into ten chilling tales from New York Times bestseller Max Brallier, perfectly crafted for sleepovers or campfires. With haunting illustrations and scary stories that will send shivers down your spine, this collection is guaranteed to keep you up all night. As a must-have for fans of Brallier's Mister Shivers Acorn series, it features nine spine-tingling series stories plus an exclusive new tale - the ultimate read for those who crave monsters and darkness.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What are the key themes in the recommended books?
These titles share Braiding Sweetgrass' focus on the interconnectedness of humans and nature, the beauty and power of the natural world, and personal stories of environmentalism and growth.
Are these books suitable for children or young adults?
Some of the titles are geared towards younger readers, like This Hair Belongs and Lost and Found Jigsaw Book, while others, like Roots & Wrath and CABIN, may appeal more to adult readers.
Do the recommended books center around specific plant species like sweetgrass?
While sweetgrass is not a central focus, many titles explore relationships with particular flora, from frybread to wildflowers, as a way to delve into the natural world and human experiences within it
