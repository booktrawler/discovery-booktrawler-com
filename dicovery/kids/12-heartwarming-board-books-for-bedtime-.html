12 Heartwarming Board Books for Bedtime and Bonding

These timeless tales soothe little ones into dreamland while nurturing a love for reading.

Who these books are for

  • Parents seeking heartwarming books for their toddlers
  • Grandparents and caregivers looking for engaging bedtime stories
  • Teachers seeking classic children's literature

Start here

If you only read one bedtime board book, choose 'Goodnight Moon' for its iconic imagery and soothing rhymes.

Bedtime routines offer a special chance to connect with your little ones through stories. These 12 heartwarming board books are timeless treasures that will transport your child to fantastical worlds and teach them valuable lessons, all while soothing them into a peaceful slumber. From Margaret Wise Brown's iconic 'Goodnight Moon' to Sandra Boynton's playful rhymes, these books are sure to become your new favorites for bonding and bedtime reading.

The collection

  1. Besos for Baby A Little Book of Kisses

    Jen Arena

    In this delightful bilingual board book, baby showers love to everyone - from mami and papi to perro and gato - while gently introducing basic Spanish vocabulary. With rhythmic, accessible Spanish and charming graphics, Besos for Baby is a heartwarming read-aloud that proves love knows no language. Share this endearing tale with your little one and be sure to reciprocate with plenty of kisses of your own!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Iguana Miss You!

    Sandra Boynton

    A heartfelt, beautifully illustrated goodbye book that explores the emotions of separation and potential uncertainty in a relatable way for young readers and their caregivers.

    For: Children aged 4-8 and their parents, teachers, or loved ones preparing for a goodbye or transition.

    Strengths: Boynton's signature rhyming verse, endearing lizard characters, and warm color palette make the heavy topic of parting more palatable and accessible to young minds.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a tad slow due to the detailed emotional exploration.

  3. The Bats on the Bus

    Alison Jackson

    Join a bus full of playful monsters as they mischievously rework the classic "Wheels on the Bus" nursery rhyme to spook up Halloween fun! From witchy screams to ghostly boos and rattling bones, this silly, spirited adaptation will delight little ones with its cast of lovable (if mischievous) creatures. A perfect pick for the witching hour read-aloud session.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Very Hungry Caterpillar

    Eric Carle

    A timeless, visually stunning introduction to counting, days of the week, and metamorphosis, perfect for sharing with young children.

    For: Infants, toddlers, and early readers up to age 4.

    Strengths: Vibrant collage illustrations, interactive die-cut pages, and simple text make it engaging and easy to understand.

    Skip if: The repetitive, straightforward storyline may not hold older children's attention.

    Comparable: Other Eric Carle classics like The Grouchy Ladybug and The Very Busy Spider share his signature collage style and interactive elements.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Goodnight Moon

    Margaret Wise Brown

    This soothing bedtime classic has endured for generations thanks to its simple yet profound language and iconic illustrations, creating a perfect lullaby for young readers.

    For: Parents, caregivers, and children aged 0-4 will appreciate this tender story.

    Strengths: Goodnight Moon masterfully employs repetitive text and evocative imagery to ease little ones into dreamland, while its universal themes of comfort and goodnight wishes resonate with adults too.

    Skip if: Some may find the simple, sing-song rhythm a bit monotonous for repeated readings.

    Comparable: Other timeless bedtime books like The Runaway Bunny and Going to Bed share similar gentle, reassuring storylines.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Pajama Time!

    Sandra Boynton

    This charming board book combines playful rhymes with adorable animal illustrations, making bedtime a joyful experience for young readers.

    For: Toddlers and preschoolers who love Sandra Boynton's signature humor and animal characters.

    Strengths: Captures the energy and whimsy of bedtime routines through engaging text and expressive artwork. Perfect for reading aloud and encouraging participation.

    Skip if: Some parents may find the repetitive rhyming scheme a bit grating after multiple readings.

    Comparable: Fans of Sandra Boynton's other bedtime books like 'Going Night-Night' and 'Do You Want to Be Friends?' will enjoy the similar tone and style in 'Pajama Time!'.

    “s characters are as lovable as ever, all with expressions sure to win over toddlers.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Witchycakes #3: Puddles and Potions

    Kara LaReau

    Join little witch-to-be Blue on their magical adventures in the whimsical Witchycakes bakery! In the third installment, Blue tries to perfect deliveries with a borrowed "magic potion," learning that even the best intentions can have imperfect results. With the help of problem-solving and a sprinkle of magic, Blue's trials turn into tasty triumphs. Colorful illustrations and a delightful story make this chapter book a must-have for any young witch or wizard's library, along with the included magical recipe to try at home!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Mei Mei The Bunny

    Laufey

    A heartwarming tale about embracing mistakes and believing in oneself, with beautiful illustrations and a relatable protagonist, makes this an endearing addition to any bookshelf.

    For: Children aged 4-8, parents, and educators seeking inspiring stories about resilience and self-confidence.

    Strengths: Mei Mei's journey is both authentic and uplifting, showcasing the power of perseverance and positive self-talk. The illustrations are charming and expressive, bringing the story to life.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the narrative a bit straightforward and lacking in plot twists, but the message more than makes up for it.

    Comparable: Fans of uplifting picture books about overcoming fears and doubts, such as 'The Little Engine That Could' or 'Gossie and Gertie', will appreciate 'Mei Mei the Bunny' for its similar themes and inspiring tone.

    “A reassuring riff on embracing imperfections.”

Frequently asked questions

What age range are these board books suitable for?
These books are ideal for children aged 0-5.
Are all these board books classics?
Yes, except for 'Puddles and Potions', all books are considered modern or classic children's literature.
Can I use these books for early literacy activities?
Absolutely! These books are designed to introduce simple text and engaging storytelling, perfect for early literacy development.
