Best Nonfiction Books About Soccer for Kids and Adults

Delve into the world of soccer with these top nonfiction books covering its history, players, and thrilling matches.

Who these books are for

  • Soccer enthusiasts of all ages
  • Parents looking for engaging kids' books
  • Students researching sports
  • History buffs interested in the sport's development

Start here

If you only read one book about soccer, choose 'The World's Best Soccer Book' for an in-depth, visually stunning overview.

Soccer, or football as it's known worldwide, is a beloved sport with a rich history and global following. This curated collection of nonfiction books offers something for every soccer enthusiast, from young readers to lifelong fans. From biographies of legendary players to comprehensive histories and match recaps, these titles provide an engaging and informative look at the beautiful game.

The collection

  1. For the Love of Soccer! / ¡Por Amor Al Fútbol!

    Pelé

    Join soccer legend Pelé on the pitch as he shares the joy, speed, and teamwork that made the beautiful game his passion. Through vibrant bilingual illustrations by Frank Morrison, Pelé's inspiring story weaves with a young athlete's determination to emulate his hero's moves, all culminating in exhilarating action and a triumphant goal! For budding football fans, this heartwarming picture book delivers the fun and excitement of the world's most popular sport.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Pele

    Maria Isabel Sanchez Vegara

    Meet Pelé, the soccer phenom who lifted Brazil to World Cup glory three times! With nothing but a sock full of rags for a ball, this determined boy from a poor neighborhood achieved the impossible. Join Pelé on his journey from childhood dreams to becoming the greatest soccer player of all time, using his platform to inspire positive change. A must-read for kids who dare to dream big!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Kingfisher Soccer Encyclopedia Facts • Stats • Players • Teams • Skills and Tactics • Competitions

    Clive Gifford

    Immerse yourself in the thrilling world of soccer with The Kingfisher Soccer Encyclopedia! This comprehensive guide brings you up-to-date on top players like Messi and Haaland, teams from the MLS to Real Madrid, and major competitions like the World Cup. With expanded coverage of women's soccer, stunning photos, and in-depth analysis, it's a must-have for fans of all ages. Whether you're a casual supporter or a die-hard enthusiast, this book has everything you need to deepen your understanding and appreciation of the beautiful game.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Mystery of the Stolen World Cup Trophy

    Angela Cervantes

    Join 12-year-old soccer sleuth Diez Espada on a pulse-pounding adventure as he races to recover the stolen World Cup Trophy before the championship match. With his crush Rio and world-renowned detective Enzo by his side, Diez navigates a web of suspects, secret tunnels, and ticking clocks in this thrilling mystery full of humor, heart, and the electrifying energy of fútbol.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Lionel Messi's World Cup Triumph (History's Greatest Games #1)

    Chris Barish

    Experience the electrifying climax of the 2022 World Cup through the eyes of the game's greatest player, Lionel Messi. In this immersive, award-winning graphic novel, relive the intense final match against Kylian Mbappé and delve into the rich history of soccer, from its origins to the legendary players who shaped the sport. Get ready to be captivated by the drama, skill, and significance of this historic moment in Messi's quest for a coveted World Cup triumph.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Soccer (National Geographic Kids Readers, Level 2)

    Melissa Santoyo

    This book provides an engaging and informative introduction to the world of soccer, perfect for early readers eager to learn about the sport.

    For: Children aged 4-7 who are just starting to explore reading longer sentences and more complex vocabulary.

    Strengths: Well-structured, easy to follow, and packed with fun facts and dynamic visuals that bring the excitement of soccer to life.

    Skip if: Occasional sentences with multiple clauses may make it challenging for the youngest or most struggling readers to follow along smoothly.

    Comparable: Other Level 2 National Geographic Kids Readers, such as Basketball and Football, offer similar engaging introductions to popular sports for early readers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Pedro's Big Goal

    Fran Manushkin Aut

    Meet Pedro, a spirited young soccer player who sets his sights on the coveted goalie position for the big game! Overcoming self-doubt about his size and speed, Pedro learns perseverance and finds his place in the team. An inspiring tale for early readers that encourages pursuing one's dreams.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Beautiful Game

    Yamile Saied Méndez

    Dive into the inspiring world of The Beautiful Game as Valeria "Magic" Salomón must find her footing after an unexpected loss. With the help of her family and a newfound girls' team, Magic learns to trust in herself and play with a fierce, unapologetic spirit - a celebration of the power of resilience and female unity in the fast-paced arena of soccer.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Soccer GOAT: Messi v. Ronaldo

    Matt Oldfield

    Get ready for the ultimate showdown in this electrifying dual-biography that pits two soccer titans against each other. From childhood to Champions League glory, The Soccer GOAT: Messi v. Ronaldo delivers an action-packed, fact-filled ride through the careers and personalities of football's brightest stars. With humor and vivid illustrations, this must-have book for any soccer enthusiast is the perfect way to engage young readers in the age-old debate.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The World's Best Soccer Book Everything You Need to Know about Soccer!

    10ten Media

    A comprehensive, engaging visual guide to soccer that will delight kids and adults alike with its abundance of fun facts, photographs, and comics.

    For: Soccer enthusiasts, kids interested in the sport, and parents seeking an educational, entertaining resource.

    Strengths: Covers a wide range of soccer topics in an accessible, kid-friendly format; includes vibrant illustrations, photos, and comics.

    Skip if: May overwhelm younger readers with the sheer volume of information; some sections, like the comics, may appeal more to certain ages than others.

    Comparable: Compared to other soccer books, this one stands out for its unique blend of humor, history, and instruction, making it an excellent choice for those seeking a lighthearted yet informative read.

    “A thorough and accessible resource for beginner players learning the basics of soccer and for fans interested in teams, players, and engaging facts about the sport.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Greatest Stars of the Soccer World Cup The 50 Most Spectacular Match Players!

    Kevin Pettman

    A thorough, visually engaging reference book for soccer enthusiasts, collecting essential stats and profiles of the sport's all-time greats.

    For: Fans of the sport, especially those interested in World Cup history and the careers of legendary players.

    Strengths: Comprehensive coverage of men's and women's players, detailed profiles, and an abundance of facts and stats make this an authoritative resource.

    Skip if: The sheer volume of information may make it overwhelming for casual readers.

    Comparable: Fans of sports biographies and reference works like 'The FIFA World Cup: A Celebration of the Beautiful Game' will appreciate this book's depth and focus on World Cup legends.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Junior Soccer Encyclopedia

    Emily Stead

    This visually engaging and fact-packed encyclopedia delivers an in-depth look at the world of soccer, making it an excellent resource for young fans interested in the sport.

    For: Young readers aged 8-14 who are enthusiastic about soccer, from casual fans to aspiring players.

    Strengths: Provides an extensive overview of top competitions, teams, players, and key soccer skills, with bite-sized facts, stunning photography, and a user-friendly layout.

    Skip if: May be overwhelming for very young readers due to the sheer amount of information packed into its pages.

    Comparable: Fans of sports-themed encyclopedias or those who enjoy books like 'The Ultimate Book of Sports Science' will appreciate this in-depth look at soccer.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Cristiano Ronaldo The Sunday Times Bestseller

    Maria Isabel Sanchez Vegara

    Join the journey of the world's greatest footballer, Cristiano Ronaldo, from his humble beginnings on the island of Madeira to international stardom. With hard work, determination, and courage, he overcame setbacks and achieved the impossible, breaking records and inspiring millions. This inspiring biography, filled with dynamic illustrations and extra facts, shows how Ronaldo followed his passion and never gave up, becoming a role model both on and off the pitch. Perfect for young sports fans and dreamers alike, discover the incredible true story of a champion who proved that anything is possible with perseverance and self-belief.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. The World's Game

    Yamile Saied Méndez

    This vibrant picture book celebrates soccer's global appeal and its power to unite people, making it an excellent addition to any collection.

    For: Children ages 4-8 and their families, soccer enthusiasts, and anyone interested in diverse, uplifting stories.

    Strengths: Colorful, energetic illustrations and lively dialogue effectively convey the passion and excitement of the sport, while also highlighting themes of community and togetherness.

    Skip if: While the text and art work well together, some readers may find the storyline a bit simplistic and the message a tad heavy-handed.

    Comparable: Fans of sports-themed picture books like 'The Undefeated' or 'The Most Magnificent Thing' will appreciate 'The World's Game' for its energetic portrayal of a beloved global pastime.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. The World Game Soccer Circles the Globe

    John Coy

    Join a thrilling global soccer adventure as "The World Game" whisks you away to match-ups in five far-flung locations, from Chicago's park to a Nigerian seaside pitch, celebrating the unifying power of sports through vibrant illustrations and fast-paced action. With every turn of the page, feel the energy of the world's most popular game in motion, culminating in a triumphant "GOOOOOAL!" that transcends borders and time zones. A joyous, energetic read for young soccer fans everywhere.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
