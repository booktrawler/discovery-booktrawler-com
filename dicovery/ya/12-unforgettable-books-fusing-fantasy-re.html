12 Unforgettable Books Fusing Fantasy & Reality

Immerse yourself in these boundary-pushing stories blending the magical and the mundane.

Who these books are for

  • Fantasy readers eager for new twists
  • Literary fiction enthusiasts
  • Anyone seeking stories that challenge the ordinary

Start here

If you only read one book merging fantasy and reality, choose Akwaeke Emezi's haunting and beautiful 'Pet'.

Fantasy and literary fiction often occupy separate realms of storytelling. However, these twelve groundbreaking books boldly merge the two, crafting immersive tales that blur the lines between the magical and the mundane. From haunting monsters to gritty realism, each story presents a unique fusion that will challenge your perceptions and leave a lasting impact. Dive into these unforgettable narratives to discover the power of genre-bending storytelling.

The collection

  1. The Whyte Python World Tour A Novel

    Travis Kennedy

    "Join Rikki Thunder, a humble 22-year-old drummer, as he's catapulted from obscurity to international fame with his band Whyte Python in 1980s LA. But there's more to their meteoric rise than meets the eye - Rikki uncovers a shadowy world of espionage where the fate of the Cold War hangs in the balance of hair metal's greatest hits. This riotous, nostalgia-drenched debut delves into high-stakes intrigue with humor and heart, making for an unforgettable rock-n-roll ride."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Pet

    Akwaeke Emezi

    Pet is a genre-defying, timely, and haunting novel that explores complex themes of identity, justice, and societal denial through a unique blend of speculative elements and lyrical prose.

    For: Readers seeking a thought-provoking, atmospheric young adult novel with diverse characters and a gripping narrative that challenges assumptions.

    Strengths: Pet excels at crafting a sense of foreboding and unease, and its exploration of the consequences of denial is both timely and unsettling. The writing is vivid and evocative, with memorable characters at its center.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing deliberate and the world-building somewhat opaque, requiring close attention to unravel the mysteries.

    Comparable: Fans of speculative fiction and socially conscious young adult novels may also appreciate works like Octavia Butler's Fledgling or Nnedi Okorafor's Who Fears Death, which share similar themes and atmospheric tones.

    “With great tenderness, Pet questions a society silenced by denial.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Hunger's Bite

    Taylor Robin

    Hunger's Bite is a gripping supernatural adventure set on a luxurious 1920s ocean liner, combining elements of horror, mystery, and coming-of-age themes.

    For: Fans of historical horror, paranormal mystery, and graphic novels will enjoy this atmospheric tale.

    Strengths: Vivid colors, robust sound effects, and perfect pacing make for a thrilling ride, while the story explores class differences and personal growth.

    Skip if: The complex plot and character motivations may require close attention from readers.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy atmospheric, eerie tales like Coraline or The Graveyard Book will appreciate Hunger's Bite's blend of horror and coming-of-age elements.

    “This harrowing story of survival and justice is a feast for horror enthusiasts.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Elatsoe

    Darcie Little Badger

    Elatsoe is a groundbreaking, atmospheric debut that masterfully blends mystery, horror, and fantasy elements, set in an inventive alternate America shaped by Indigenous and immigrant magic.

    For: Readers of fantasy, mystery, and horror seeking a unique, atmospheric story with strong Indigenous themes and a compelling young protagonist.

    Strengths: Elatsoe excels at crafting a haunting, immersive world, infusing it with ancestral knowledge and magical realism, while delivering a engaging mystery and character-driven narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow due to the dense, lyrical prose and the complex, layered world-building.

    Comparable: Fans of urban fantasy and magical realism, particularly those drawn to stories with Indigenous and diverse protagonists, may also enjoy works like Hopeless, Monsters by Kate Karyus Quinn or The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August by Claire North.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. A Prince Among Pirates

    Katie Abdou

    A Prince Among Pirates is a captivating queer historical romance that seamlessly blends swashbuckling adventure, memorable characters, and a sweet love story.

    For: Teen readers seeking a charming, action-packed queer tale with a strong historical setting will love this debut novel.

    Strengths: Abdou's vivid world-building and authentic character development make the 18th century pirate life come alive. The slow-burn romance between Kit and Reggie is tender and satisfying.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow in the beginning as Kit adjusts to his new life aboard the Deliverance.

    Comparable: Fans of F.T. Lukens' Ash and Quill and Our Flag Means Death will appreciate the mix of historical fiction, adventure, and queer romance in A Prince Among Pirates.

    “Clever, action-packed, and gloriously queer, Katie Abdou crafts her debut novel with masterful humor.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Plum

    Andy Anderegg

    Andy Anderegg's debut novel, Plum, stands out for its innovative second-person narrative voice, vivid portrayal of toxic family dynamics, and powerful exploration of resilience and self-discovery in the face of trauma.

    For: Readers who appreciate raw, unflinching portrayals of dysfunctional family life and coming-of-age themes, particularly those drawn to experimental narrative styles.

    Strengths: Plum excels at immersing the reader in J's intense, often harrowing experiences, while also conveying her quiet strength and determination to forge a better life.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the unrelenting bleakness and graphic content challenging to engage with, particularly if they prefer more balanced or uplifting stories.

    Comparable: Fans of Sarah Rose Etter and Scott McClanahan's work will likely resonate with Plum's dark, lyrical prose and its unflinching examination of the complexities of modern girlhood.

    “s style and prose are a breath of fresh air.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What makes these books stand out?
The seamless blending of the fantastical and the realistic, often with a dark, thought-provoking tone.
Are these books part of a series?
Some are standalone, while others are the first in a series. Check the book details for more information.
Do I need prior knowledge of the genres?
No, these books can be enjoyed by readers new to both fantasy and literary fiction, as the unique storytelling styles make them accessible.
Can I find these books in eBook or audiobook formats?
Yes, the majority of these titles are available in digital and audio formats, making it easy to consume them however you prefer.
