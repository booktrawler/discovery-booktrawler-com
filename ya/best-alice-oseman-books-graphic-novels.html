Best Alice Oseman Books & Graphic Novels

Alice Oseman's works explore themes of identity, friendship, and romance in relatable, engaging stories.

Start here

If you only read one Alice Oseman book, start with 'Heartstopper #1: A Graphic Novel' for an unforgettable introduction to her poignant storytelling.

Alice Oseman is a beloved British author and illustrator known for crafting heartfelt, engaging stories that resonate with readers of all ages. Her works often revolve around themes of identity, friendship, and romance, frequently featuring LGBTQ+ characters and experiences. This curated collection showcases her most popular books and graphic novels, providing a comprehensive guide for both new and dedicated fans alike to explore and cherish her imaginative, authentic writing style.

  1. Radio Silence

    Alice Oseman

    From the bestselling author of Heartstopper, Radio Silence is a poignant, powerful coming-of-age tale of identity, belonging, and self-discovery. When introverted study machine Frances befriends shy podcasting genius Aled, she finds the courage to be her authentic self for the first time. But as their friendship deepens and their secrets unravel, Frances must navigate guilt, loss, and the suffocating pressure to conform. A riveting, relatable exploration of the complexities of youth and the journey to embrace one's true self.

Frequently asked questions

What genre are Alice Oseman's books?
Her works often blend elements of young adult fiction, romance, graphic novels, and coming-of-age stories.
Are Alice Oseman's books LGBTQ+-focused?
Yes, many of her books feature LGBTQ+ characters and themes, offering authentic representation and exploration of diverse identities.
Can I read Alice Oseman's books in any order?
While some books are part of series, they can generally be read independently. However, reading in publication order may enhance understanding of character development and relationships across her works.
