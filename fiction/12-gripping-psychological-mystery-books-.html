12 Gripping Psychological Mystery Books to Unravel

Master the twists and turns of the human psyche with these expertly crafted mysteries.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of true crime stories and mind-bending whodunits
  • Readers seeking complex, thought-provoking mysteries
  • Book clubs looking for psychological thrillers with depth

Start here

If you only read one psychological mystery, choose 'My Husband's Wife' by Alice Feeney for its expertly layered plot and chilling exploration of marriage and deceit.

Delve into the labyrinthine corridors of the human mind with this curated collection of 12 psychological mystery novels. From the intricate plotting of 'My Husband's Wife' to the mind-bending concept of 'The Ten Thousand Doors of January,' these books will keep you on the edge of your seat as you unravel the darkest secrets of the characters' psyches. Whether you're a seasoned mystery enthusiast or new to the genre, these gripping stories offer a thrilling exploration of the complexities of the human condition.

The collection

  1. Strange Houses A Novel

    Uketsu

    In Uketsu's mesmerizing debut, "Strange Houses," the eerie and alluring threads of a decaying mansion's labyrinthine past weave a haunting tapestry. As the protagonist delves into the secrets hidden within its crumbling walls, the blurred lines between reality and the supernatural cast a chilling spell. A hypnotic exploration of isolation, family trauma, and the power of inherited darkness, this atmospheric novel will immerse readers in a world where the house is both a character and a curse.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Ten Thousand Doors of January

    Alix E Harrow

    Step into a labyrinth of words and worlds with Alix E. Harrow's enchanting debut, The Ten Thousand Doors of January. As January Scaller uncovers a mysterious book that carries the scent of other realities, she embarks on a fantastical journey of self-discovery, blurring the lines between story and life. With its lush prose, intricate world-building, and celebration of the power of imagination, this captivating novel will transport you to realms of impossible beauty, unforgettable love, and the magic of the written word.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. How to Sell a Haunted House

    Grady Hendrix

    Embark on a spine-chilling journey with Grady Hendrix's How to Sell a Haunted House. When Louise returns to her childhood home after her parents' passing, she's forced to confront her troubled past, meddling brother Mark, and an eerie specter threatening to haunt her forever. With wit, heart, and menacing terror, Hendrix crafts a gripping tale that explores family legacies, success versus stagnation, and the fear of facing what lies within. Prepare to be both enthralled and unsettled in this wild ride that cements Hendrix as a horror master.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Delilah Green Doesn't Care

    Ashley Herring Blake

    Fantastical romances await in this sizzling queer comedy of love and second chances. When commitment-phobe Delilah Green reluctantly returns to her painful hometown for her stepsister's wedding, she sets her sights on Claire, the widow bookshop owner who's reeled in by Delilah's mischievous allure. As they navigate family drama and their own desire to resist each other, Delilah and Claire must confront their pasts and the risks of falling in love - again.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Someone Else's Husband: Katie Couric Book Club A Novel

    Kimberly McCreight

    Someone Else's Husband is a masterful, suspenseful exploration of relationships, secrets, and desires, with vivid settings and complex characters that will keep readers guessing until the very end.

    For: Fans of character-driven psychological thrillers and literary suspense will devour this.

    Strengths: McCreight skillfully weaves together multiple storylines and perspectives, creating a rich tapestry of relationships and secrets that culminate in a stunning, expertly plotted twist.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, layered narrative and complex character dynamics challenging to follow at times.

    Comparable: For those who enjoyed Gone Girl, The Silent Patient, or Tana French's psychological thrillers, Someone Else's Husband offers a similarly intricate, emotionally charged mystery with a strong focus on character development.

    “Kimberly McCreight has quickly become a favorite, with her twisty plots, wry sense of humor, and fully realized characterizations, and her new novel is simply a joy to read.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Iris Kelly Doesn't Date

    Ashley Herring Blake

    "Iris Kelly Doesn't Date is a charming, witty romantic comedy that turns the tables on a lovable commitment-phobe romance author. When a disastrous one-night stand leads to a chance encounter with the same woman auditioning for a play, Iris agrees to a fake relationship in exchange for a much-needed plot boost. But as they pretend to be a couple, the lines between fiction and reality blur, and Iris finds herself drawn to Stevie in a way she never anticipated. A delightful, heartfelt story about finding love in the most unexpected places."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Incidents Around the House A Novel

    Josh Malerman

    In Josh Malerman's spine-tingling horror novel, Incidents Around the House, eight-year-old Bela faces a malevolent presence haunting her family that demands an answer to a chilling question: "Can I go inside your heart?" As family bonds crumble and the entity's power grows, Bela must decide between safeguarding her loved ones or succumbing to the darkness threatening to consume them all. A truly unsettling tale of domestic horror that will leave you on edge until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Hollow Bones A Novel

    Jodi Picoult

    In Jodi Picoult's gripping new novel Hollow Bones, Molly Fitzgerald navigates a precarious world as she runs Rhode Island's emergency preparedness division, coping with her own trauma and the unexpected challenges of marriage. Moving seamlessly between past and present, this epic story delves into the lies we tell, the plans we make for disaster, and the unbreakable bonds between mothers and daughters. Picoult's masterful storytelling will keep you on the edge of your seat as Molly confronts the ultimate question: what will you fight for when everything is at stake?

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Strangers A Memoir of Marriage

    Belle Burden

    Strangers is a gripping, deeply personal memoir that offers a searing examination of marriage, intimacy, and the ways in which we can be fundamentally deceived by those closest to us.

    For: This book is a must-read for anyone who has experienced the shock of a relationship ending, or who is curious about the inner workings of marriage and the human psyche.

    Strengths: Belle Burden's vivid, lyrical prose makes the memoir a page-turner, while her unflinching honesty and willingness to confront painful truths elevates it to the level of a classic.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the book's introspective, meandering style off-putting or overly focused on the author's inner world.

    Comparable: For those drawn to deeply personal, psychologically astute memoirs, Strangers shares thematic and stylistic similarities with works like Cheryl Strayed's Wild and Dani Shapiro's Inheritance.

    “Reads like a love story and a horror story and, in one nail-biting section, like a financial thriller.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Little Fires Everywhere: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Celeste Ng

    Celeste Ng's Little Fires Everywhere is a captivating novel that delves into the lives of the picture-perfect Richardson family and the enigmatic Mia Warren, who upends their carefully ordered world with her mysterious past and disregard for the status quo. Set in the placid suburb of Shaker Heights, this riveting story explores the weight of secrets, the power of art and identity, and the intense bonds of motherhood, all while a custody battle over a Chinese-American baby dramatically divides the community, pitting Mia and Elena against each other. A must-read that will keep you spellbound until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. My Husband's Wife A Novel

    Alice Feeney

    My Husband's Wife is a masterful, twisty domestic thriller that expertly weaves together two complex storylines, keeping readers on the edge of their seats until the final page.

    For: Fans of psychological suspense, particularly those who enjoy layered, intricate plots and unexpected endings, will devour this book.

    Strengths: Alice Feeney's writing is propulsive, immersive, and skillfully manipulates tension and deception to create a truly unputdownable reading experience.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dual timelines and multiple narrators slightly confusing, requiring close attention to follow the story's intricate threads.

    Comparable: For readers who enjoyed the domestic suspense and layered storytelling of Gillian Flynn's Gone Girl or Paula Hawkins' The Girl on the Train, My Husband's Wife offers a similarly addictive, mind-bending thriller.

    “Beguiling. . . A funny, sexy, thoroughly satisfying read.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Nowhere Burning

    Catriona Ward

    In Nowhere Burning, a haunting tale of sibling survival and dark sanctuary, Catriona Ward weaves a chilling narrative that pits fleeing outsiders against the shadowy inhabitants of a once-abandoned movie star's ranch-turned-runaway haven. Secrets smolder in the scorched landscape as Riley and Oliver confront the terrible price of salvation amidst the ghosts of the past and the ruthless dangers of their new home. A gripping, atmospheric horror that melds the dark fairy tale of Peter Pan with the brutal chaos of Lord of the Flies, set against the unforgiving backdrop of the Colorado Rockies.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. We Burned So Bright

    TJ Klune

    Embrace the poignant journey of Don and Rodney, stalwart husbands of 40 years, as they embark on a final road trip across America to settle unfinished business before the world is consumed by a rogue black hole. Along the way, they meet fellow souls grappling with mortality's imminence, leading to unanticipated connections and a reexamination of a life well-lived. TJ Klune's heart-wrenching standalone novel We Burned So Bright is a bittersweet celebration of love, family, and the choices that define us in our twilight.

    Emily Jane Trent

    Bradan Reid is done with love after a devastating heartbreak. But everything changes when he encounters Cami Joubert - an alluring, aspiring chef who challenges him at every turn. Their electrifying chemistry is undeniable, yet secrets from Cami's past threaten to tear them apart. Will Bradan's determination to win her heart be enough to overcome the obstacles in their path? Dive into this steamy new adult romance for a captivating tale of passion, self-discovery, and the fight to make imperfect love perfectly right.

  15. Score

    Kennedy Ryan

    In "Score", Kennedy Ryan crafts an intimate, suspenseful tale of love and redemption that will keep you on the edge. Against the vivid backdrop of the basketball world, Ryan poignantly explores themes of trauma, healing, and the fiery passion that can heal even the deepest wounds. With lyrical prose and an unflinching gaze, "Score" is a special, unforgettable read that will linger in your heart long after the final buzzer sounds.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Skin Contact

    Elisa Faison

    Elisa Faison's debut novel offers a fresh perspective on modern marriage and polyamory, exploring complex relationships and identity through vivid, relatable characters and prose.

    For: Readers interested in contemporary fiction, relationships, and self-discovery will find this novel engaging.

    Strengths: The novel excels at portraying the emotional depth and intimacy of Frances and Ben's relationship, as well as the diverse experiences of their lovers and friends.

    Skip if: At times, the multiple narrative perspectives can feel disjointed, making it difficult to fully immerse in certain characters' stories.

    Comparable: Fans of novels exploring non-traditional relationships, such as The Fates and Furies or The End of Eddy, will appreciate Skin Contact's nuanced portrayal of love and identity.

  17. Her Body and Other Parties Stories

    Carmen Maria Machado

    In this inventive, genre-bending debut, Carmen Maria Machado delivers a stunning collection of stories that explore the darkest depths of women's experiences. From a green ribbon that refuses removal to a world consumed by sexual plague, these electrifying tales blur the lines between reality, science fiction, horror, and fantasy. Machado's unique voice and bold storytelling will leave you captivated, unsettled, and ultimately, enriched by her fearless exploration of the human condition.

When wallflower Harriet kidnaps notorious rake Lord Alexander to teach her filthy slang, a marriage of convenience blossoms into something far more complicated - and deliciously passionate. As they navigate the intricacies of their 'in-name-only' arrangement, they'll discover that the ultimate lesson in love could be the most thrilling education of all.

    Rebecca K Reilly

    In this charming, heartfelt novel, a brother and sister navigate love, identity, and family drama in contemporary Auckland. When Valdin is sent to Argentina, forced to confront his feelings for his ex, and Greta deals with her own crush and her family's chaotic secrets, their misadventures become a joyful celebration of love in its many mystifying forms – a fresh, exuberant tale perfect for fans of Schitt's Creek and Sally Rooney's Normal People.

  20. Heartwood (A Read with Jenna Pick) A Novel

    Amity Gaige

    In Amity Gaige's captivating novel Heartwood, the boundaries between fact and fiction blur as we follow the enigmatic Elena, a zookeeper-turned-hermit, in her quest to understand the natural world and her own turbulent past. Gaige's lyrical prose and richly detailed settings immerse readers in Elena's solitary existence, while her keen observations on the human condition make for a riveting exploration of what it means to be alive. This thoughtful, enchanting tale will resonate long after the final page is turned.

  21. Broken Country (Reese's Book Club)

    Clare Leslie Hall

    Broken Country masterfully weaves a suspenseful mystery with a poignant exploration of love, loss, and the weight of the past, making it a captivating read.

    For: Fans of character-driven fiction, family dramas, and mystery/suspense novels will appreciate this complex tale.

    Strengths: The author does an excellent job of creating a rich, atmospheric setting and complex, flawed characters whose pasts slowly unravel, leading to a surprising and satisfying conclusion.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow as the story builds gradually, requiring patience to fully immerse in the world and characters.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed the atmospheric, character-focused storytelling of Where the Crawdads Sing and The Paper Palace will likely find Broken Country equally engaging.

    “Layered and lyrical, this stunning novel expertly builds suspense while revealing its secrets, and it conveys deep truths about love and loss.”

Frequently asked questions

What makes these psychological mysteries stand out?
Unexpected plot twists, nuanced character development, and in-depth explorations of the human psyche.
Are these books suitable for all audiences?
While mature themes are present, the books can be enjoyed by readers 18 and older who appreciate complex storytelling.
Can I start with a particular author or jump between books?
Authors like Alice Feeney and Jodi Picoult offer standalone novels, while others like Kennedy Ryan and Elisa Faison are part of a series. Feel free to mix and match based on your reading preferences.
