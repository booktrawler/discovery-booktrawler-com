Books About Love and Relationships

Explore these 26 captivating novels that delve into the complexities of love and relationships.

Who these books are for

  • Romance readers looking for nuanced explorations of love
  • Literary fiction enthusiasts seeking complex characters and relationships
  • Anyone interested in stories about the human heart

Start here

If you only read one book about the peaks and valleys of love, choose Lily King's 'Heart the Lover'.

Love and relationships are timeless themes that captivate readers across genres. This diverse collection of 26 novels delves into the complexities of romantic love, familial bonds, and the intricacies of the human heart. From the tender to the tumultuous, these stories showcase the full spectrum of emotional experiences. Whether you're a romance enthusiast or a literary fiction aficionado, you'll find something to resonate with in this thought-provoking selection of books about love and relationships.

The collection

  1. A Tree Grows in Brooklyn [75th Anniversary Ed]

    Betty Smith

    "A Tree Grows in Brooklyn" by Betty Smith transports you back to early 20th century Brooklyn, immersing you in the gritty, tender beauty of Francie Nolan's coming-of-age. With poetic prose, Smith captures Francie's resilience, dreams, and heartbreak, drawing you into the affectionate but impoverished Irish-American family. You'll laugh and cry with Francie as she navigates the harsh realities of poverty, love, loss, and finding her voice in a world that often seems cruelly indifferent. A timeless classic, this anniversary edition invites you to experience again the emotional depth and profound humanity that have made "A Tree Grows in Brooklyn" a beloved staple of American literature.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Bell Jar A Novel

    Sylvia Plath

    In this haunting literary classic, Sylvia Plath masterfully captures the inner turmoil of Esther Greenwood, a brilliant and beautiful young woman grappling with the pressures of modern womanhood. Esther's poignant journey from promising internship to spiraling mental breakdown is a visceral and unflinching exploration of the darkest corners of the psyche. With its enduring relevance and Plath's perfectly wrought prose, The Bell Jar remains a seminal and deeply affecting work of fiction that will resonate with readers long after the final page.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Measure A Novel

    Nikki Erlick

    In a world where mysterious boxes reveal each person's exact lifespan, eight ordinary individuals face an extraordinary choice: to know their limited time or remain in blissful ignorance. Spanning continents, these interconnected characters grapple with the implications, from best friends intertwined by fate to a couple reevaluating their priorities. An inspiring, uplifting novel about hope, destiny, and the power of living life to the fullest.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Rejection Fiction

    Tony Tulathimutte

    In this audacious blend of satire and heartbreak, Tony Tulathimutte masterfully plumbs the depths of modern rejection. Through interwoven stories of lonely hearts, misguided crushes, and delusional yearnings, Rejection reveals the brutal and often hilarious truths of our quest for love, connection, and self in a hyper-connected world. Bracingly honest and uproariously funny, this National Book Award longlisted novel is a searing exploration of the rejection we inflict on ourselves and others, and a call to redefine what it means to be alive in our times.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Age of Innocence

    Edith Wharton

    In this timeless Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, Edith Wharton masterfully explores the tensions between societal expectations, duty, and the all-consuming power of love. Set in 1870s New York City's high society, The Age of Innocence follows the complex relationships between Newland Archer, May Welland, and the enchanting Countess Ellen Olenska, whose independent spirit and European charm stir a forbidden passion within Newland. A poignant and beautifully crafted tale of self-discovery and the struggle to follow one's heart, this Penguin Classics edition brings Wharton's quintessential work to life anew.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Color Purple A Novel

    Alice Walker

    Celebrate the 40th anniversary of Alice Walker's powerful novel that broke the silence on the domestic and sexual abuse endured by African American women in early 20th-century rural Georgia. Through a series of poignant letters spanning three decades, Celie and her sister Nettie's unbreakable bond and journey toward transformation is beautifully rendered. A winner of the Pulitzer Prize and National Book Award, The Color Purple remains an essential read, inspiring new generations to champion the multidimensionality of Black women's stories.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. If Beale Street Could Talk

    James Baldwin

    In the vibrant, melancholic prose of James Baldwin, "If Beale Street Could Talk" sings with the joy and sorrow of young love in Harlem. Against the backdrop of racial tumult, Baldwin masterfully weaves the poignant tale of Fonny and Tish's bittersweet pre-wedding odyssey, exploring themes of identity, community, and the indomitable human spirit. This timeless novel is a symphony of ThemeData - both a love letter to the African-American experience and a defiant cry for justice in a world that often seeks to silence its marginalized voices.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Dept. of Speculation

    Jenny Offill

    In 'Dept. of Speculation', Jenny Offill masterfully weaves together a mesmerizing tapestry of a modern marriage's rise and collapse. With prose as sleek and precise as it is emotionally charged, Offill crafts an intimate, unconventional novel that will resonate with anyone who's grappled with love, family, identity, and the search for meaning in a chaotic world. A triumph of concise, powerful storytelling, this book is a must-read for anyone seeking a profoundly affecting and deeply intelligent literary experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. My Year in Paris with Gertrude Stein A Fiction

    Deborah Levy

    Step into the vibrant world of 1920s Paris with Deborah Levy's inventive and enthralling novel, where a narrator embarks on an immersive journey to understand the trailblazing life and art of Gertrude Stein. Blending biographical fact with imaginative license, Levy crafts a complex, modern tale of identity, creativity, and love that mirrors Stein's own boundary-pushing spirit. This groundbreaking work defies genre conventions to create a unique literary experience that will captivate fans of literary fiction and the avant-garde alike.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Norwegian Wood

    Haruki Murakami

    Step into the enchanting world of 1960s Japan with Haruki Murakami's poignant debut, Norwegian Wood. This captivating coming-of-age tale follows college student Toru as he navigates the complexities of first love for Naoko, while grappling with the haunting loss of their shared friend. With a mesmerizing blend of nostalgia, introspection, and liberation, this masterpiece has cemented Murakami's status as a literary icon.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Conversations with Friends A Novel

    Sally Rooney

    Immerse yourself in the sharp, witty world of Conversations with Friends, where the complexities of youthful relationships unfold with piercing insight and humor. Follow Frances and Bobbi as their lives intertwine with a charismatic photographer and her husband, leading to a poignant exploration of intimacy, identity, and the messy edges of female friendship. Sally Rooney's lucid prose and acuity for capturing the human condition make this a breathtaking novel that will linger long after the final page.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. All Fours A Novel

    Miranda July

    Miranda July's bold, intimate, and often hilarious novel explores themes of desire, freedom, and identity through the eyes of a semi-famous artist in her 40s, making it a standout in contemporary fiction.

    For: Women in their 40s, fans of unconventional storytelling, and anyone seeking a refreshingly honest portrayal of midlife.

    Strengths: July's unique voice, fearless exploration of sexuality and relationships, and ability to balance humor and poignancy make this a riveting read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the unconventional narrative structure and explicit content off-putting.

    Comparable: Readers of July's earlier work or those who enjoyed novels like Cheryl Strayed's Tiny Beautiful Things will appreciate All Fours' unflinching look at womanhood and desire.

    “All Fours cast a spell on me and was hard to emerge from.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Trust (Pulitzer Prize Winner)

    Hernan Diaz

    "Trust is a masterful literary puzzle that will captivate anyone who loves history, family secrets, and psychological intrigue. Hernan Diaz weaves a complex narrative around the diaries of John William Barlow, a wealthy American family's patriarch, and his trembling efforts to document their legacy despite the scandals and betrayals that threaten to upend it all. Diaz's elegant prose and meticulous research shine a light on the enigmatic lives of the Barlows, making it easy to become entangled in their web of deception and obsession. Fans of historical fiction, literary thrillers, and the works of authors like Donna Tartt will find Trust a gripping, thought-provoking read that will leave you questioning the true nature of family, power, and the stories we tell ourselves."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. What Kind of Paradise A Novel

    Janelle Brown

    Immerse yourself in this gripping, atmospheric tale of a teenage girl's journey to uncover the truth about her enigmatic father and her own mysterious past. As Jane escapes her isolated cabin in 1990s Montana to the rapidly changing landscape of San Francisco, she must navigate the perils and possibilities of the internet age. With its masterful blend of coming-of-age drama, psychological suspense, and historical context, What Kind of Paradise is an unputdownable exploration of family secrets, personal identity, and the seismic shifts that redefine our worlds.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. The Correspondent A Novel

    Virginia Evans

    In "The Correspondent", Virginia Evans crafts a poignant, life-affirming tale about finding solace in literature and reconnecting with the past. Sybil Van Antwerp, a woman who has lived fully and written passionately, grapples with painful memories as old letters resurface. A novel about the transformative power of forgiveness and the beauty of slowing down, "The Correspondent" is a quietly dazzling, deeply moving story that will leave readers reflecting on the importance of human connection.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. The Typing Lady And Other Fictions

    Ruth Ozeki

    This collection stands out for its masterful blend of wit, warmth, and deep humanity, as Ozeki explores the complexities of human experience through diverse characters and settings.

    For: Readers seeking nuanced, thought-provoking stories about the human condition, identity, and the power of language will appreciate this work.

    Strengths: The Typing Lady excels in its vivid characterizations, clever plot twists, and Ozeki's distinctive writing style, which seamlessly weaves together humor, pathos, and philosophical introspection.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the shifts in narrative perspective and time periods initially disorienting, requiring a patient, attentive reading approach.

    Comparable: Fans of Ozeki's novels will recognize her signature themes and tone in this short story collection, which shares elements of social commentary, introspection, and a celebration of the written word with her previous works.

    “Delightful, moving, and profound, The Typing Lady is a book of love stories of every kind. It is a book of great treasures.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The Frenzy Stories

    Joyce Carol Oates

    From the masterful Joyce Carol Oates, The Frenzy: Stories plunges readers into the lives of characters teetering on the brink of crisis. With psychological suspense and high stakes, these propulsive tales unravel moments of crisis and confusion where characters' understanding of themselves and those they love unravels. Oates expertly evokes life's most vivid and perilous moments, where fate and free will collide, and a single choice can be the difference between an ordinary day and the point of no return.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Writers & Lovers

    Lily King

    In this gripping, lyrical novel, Casey Peabody, a talented but troubled writer, navigates the tumultuous summer of 1997 as she pursues her creative dreams and falls for two compelling men. With King's razor-sharp insight and vivid prose, Writers & Lovers is a compulsive read that explores the push-and-pull between ambition, love, and self-discovery in the hesitant days of late youth.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Heart the Lover

    Lily King

    This novel masterfully captures the intensity and complexity of young love, desire, and intellectual ambition, weaving a poignant coming-of-age tale that explores the lasting impact of first relationships.

    For: Readers who appreciate nuanced character studies, relatable coming-of-age narratives, and explorations of love, friendship, and identity during the college years.

    Strengths: Lily King's evocative prose, richly drawn characters, and thoughtful exploration of themes make for a captivating, emotionally resonant read.

    Skip if: Some may find the academic setting and references to 17th-century literature a barrier, requiring patience and interest in those subjects.

    Comparable: Fans of character-driven novels like Donna Tartt's The Secret History or Jeffrey Eugenides' The Marriage Plot will appreciate Heart the Lover's similar blend of intellectual curiosity, youthful passion, and the complexities of relationships.

    “King is a master of sexual tension, of the slow build, of gratification tortuously delayed. In this respect, Heart the Lover is her best work yet.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. It Lasts Forever and Then It's Over

    Anne de Marcken

    In Anne de Marcken's genre-bending novel It Lasts Forever and Then It's Over, a voraciously alive heroine navigates the bizarre landscapes of the afterlife, yearning for the love and humanity she left behind. With a dead but opinionated crow in her chest, she encounters a series of terrifying, hilarious, and heart-wrenching situations, shedding pieces of her body and self along the way. A bold and imaginative tale that probes mortality's transformative power, this acclaimed novel delivers a bracing and unforgettable reading experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. Intermezzo A Novel

    Sally Rooney

    Sally Rooney's lyrical prose, nuanced characterizations, and profound explorations of love and grief make Intermezzo a standout literary achievement

    For: Readers of contemporary, character-driven fiction, especially those who appreciate Rooney's signature style

    Strengths: Rooney's masterful storytelling, evocative descriptions, and ability to convey complex emotions through her characters' inner worlds

    Skip if: Some may find the introspective, meandering narrative pace slow or challenging to follow at times

    Comparable: Fans of Rooney's previous works, such as Conversations with Friends and Normal People, will appreciate Intermezzo's similar themes and literary depth, while newcomers can expect an equally immersive and thought-provoking reading experience

    “Kaleidoscopically beautiful and intimately human . . . To read a Sally Rooney novel is to grip humanity in the palm of your hand, and Intermezzo is no different.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  22. Reel

    Kennedy Ryan

    Lost in the mesmerizing world of Kennedy Ryan's 'Reel', I felt the passion, the strife, the raw vulnerability of the characters seep into my very being. With every turn of the page, the narrative drew me deeper, like a riptide, until I was hopelessly, deliciously caught in its embrace. Ryan's masterful storytelling ignites the heart, leaving readers breathless and forever changed by the unflinching honesty and unbridled emotion she weaves into every word.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  23. The Missed Connection

    Tia Williams

    In the captivating romance novel "The Missed Connection" by Tia Williams, casting agent Sasha Cruz embarks on an exhilarating quest to reunite with her enigmatic Parisian seatmate. When her email plea goes viral, an international hunt ensues, thrillingly complicated by a sensual new detective in her life. Williams' signature wit and passion set the stage for a deliciously sexy tale of second chances, perfect for readers craving a romantic adventure that spans the globe.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  24. Nebraska A Novel

    Monica Datta

    Dive headfirst into Monica Datta's mesmerizing doorstopper novel Nebraska, where a family's unraveling threads are woven into a sprawling, multigenerational tapestry. Reeling from Anna's enigmatic disappearance, her estranged children and husband must confront decades-old traumas as the narrative careens between past and present. With breathtaking emotional insight and an unparalleled scope, this modern masterpiece defies categorization, immersing you in a world where even the most resilient characters are forever changed by the century's greatest questions.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  25. Homebound A GMA Book Club Pick: A Novel

    Portia Elan

    "Embark on a mesmerizing journey across time and genres with Portia Elan's Homebound, a National Bestseller and Good Morning America Book Club Pick. This sweeping literary sci-fi odyssey weaves together four lives, each grappling with loneliness and the quest for meaning, as a forgotten 1980s video game holds the key to interconnected destinies spanning centuries. With its "joyful, hauntingly beautiful" exploration of the human condition, Homebound will keep you turning the pages until the very end."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  26. My Friends A Novel

    Fredrik Backman

    A sweeping, heartwarming tale of friendship, creativity, and the transformative power of art, exploring themes of resilience, self-discovery, and the enduring bonds between people.

    For: Adult readers seeking a moving, character-driven narrative with a touch of magical realism and a strong emotional resonance.

    Strengths: Backman's signature quirky characters, vivid storytelling, and profound insights into the human condition, weaving a narrative that is both humorous and poignant.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the non-linear, flashback-heavy structure and the sprawling ensemble cast a bit challenging to follow at times.

    Comparable: Fans of Backman's previous works, such as 'Anxious People', will appreciate the author's signature blend of humor, empathy, and philosophical depth, while readers of character-driven literary fiction may also enjoy this title.

    “A heart-warming, heart-breaking gem that shines with wisdom, compassion, and humor.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  27. Great Gatsby (Pocket Classics), The

    F. Scott Fitzgerald

    Step into the dazzling, yet disillusioned world of 1920s America through F. Scott Fitzgerald's timeless masterpiece, The Great Gatsby. This haunting tale of love, wealth, and the elusive dream propels you into the orbit of enigmatic millionaire Jay Gatsby and his quest to reclaim a lost love. Narrated with poignancy by Nick Carraway, the novel unfolds against a backdrop of lavish parties, shattered ideals, and the quiet ache of longing. Fitzgerald's poetic prose and unforgettable characters make this a must-read for anyone drawn to tragic romance, lyrical storytelling, and a poignant exploration of the American Dream.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  28. This Book Made Me Think of You

    Libby Page

    Tilly Nightingale's year of grief takes an unexpected turn when she receives a mysterious birthday gift from her late husband at her local bookstore. As she immerses herself in the 12 carefully curated books and handwritten notes, Tilly finds solace, new adventures, and a budding connection with the kind bookstore owner Alfie. This heartwarming tale celebrates the transformative power of reading and the love that can blossom in the most unexpected places.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

Are there any classic titles included?
F. Scott Fitzgerald's 'The Great Gatsby' and Edith Wharton's 'The Age of Innocence' offer timeless perspectives on love and society.
Can I find stories set outside of the US?
Yes, novels by Haruki Murakami, Miranda July, and Monica Datta offer international settings and cultural insights.
