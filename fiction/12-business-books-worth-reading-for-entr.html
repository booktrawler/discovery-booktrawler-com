12 Business Books Worth Reading for Entrepreneurs and Professionals

Bridge the gap between corporate culture and your personal style with these insightful business books.

Who these books are for

  • Aspiring entrepreneurs
  • Business professionals
  • Corporate employees seeking career growth
  • Small business owners
  • Startup founders

Start here

If you only read one book to better understand the nuances of business casual, choose 'Business Casual' by B.K. Borison.

In today's fast-paced, ever-changing business landscape, it's crucial to stay informed and adaptable. This curated list of 12 books offers valuable insights, practical advice, and thought-provoking perspectives to help entrepreneurs, professionals, and corporate employees navigate their careers and foster a deeper understanding of the business world. From the evolution of business casual to the intricacies of corporate culture, these titles provide a comprehensive overview of the modern professional environment, empowering readers to excel in their roles and achieve their goals.

The collection

  1. Blob A Love Story

    Maggie Su

    Meet Vi, a restless and lonely young woman who stumbles upon a peculiar blob that becomes her wild experiment in crafting the perfect, loving companion. As she molds this shape-shifting entity into her ideal partner, Vi must confront her complicated past, lingering heartaches, and the racial biases that have shaped her relationships. In this inventive, witty, and deeply moving debut, Maggie Su crafts a wondrous tale of identity, desire, and the impossible quest to control those we love. A story to captivate and move you, Blob is a must-read exploration of the human condition.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Good Spirits A Novel

    B.K. Borison

    Dive into this enchanting debut from B.K. Borison, where a charming ghost and a good-hearted woman collide in a magical romance. With the holiday deadline looming, Nancy and Nolan unravel her past to forge a future, their spirited connection growing stronger with every shared revelation. A whimsical, swoony tale filled with the cozy charm of Christmas, Good Spirits is Borison's best yet that will leave you feeling all the festive feels!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Games: A Love Story

    Anna Maria Volkova

    A thought-provoking exploration of power dynamics, generational differences, and the complexities of love, set against the backdrop of New York City's financial world.

    For: Readers of literary fiction who appreciate complex characters, philosophical discussions, and nuanced portrayals of relationships.

    Strengths: Volkova's masterful prose, the provocative dialogue, and the deeply immersive portrayal of the characters' inner lives make for a compelling read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, academic style and the slow-burning plot challenging to engage with, especially if they prefer lighter, more plot-driven fiction.

    Comparable: Fans of novels that blend romance with philosophical and social commentary, such as Donna Tartt's The Secret History or Zadie Smith's NW, may appreciate Volkova's style and thematic depth.

    “A soulful book about the politics of labor and submission. Tactile and frank, Games is full of feeling.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Hooked A Novel of Obsession

    Asako Yuzuki

    In this gripping exploration of obsession, loneliness, and the fragility of female friendship, Eriko, an ambitious but isolated young professional, becomes consumed by her fascination with popular lifestyle blogger Shoko, threatening to unravel the very lives they both covet perfection in. Asako Yuzuki's taut, psychologically astute novel plunges into the disquieting depths of modern womanhood, raising unsettling questions about the price of connection and the danger of desire.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Middlesex A Novel

    Jeffrey Eugenides

    In this Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, Jeffrey Eugenides weaves a dazzling epic that spans generations of a Greek-American family, from a tiny village to Motor City Detroit, uncovering a shocking secret that transforms a girl into a boy. With lyrical prose and an unforgettable narrator, Middlesex is a thrilling exploration of identity, family, and the astonishing power of genetics.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Call Me by Your Name: The Graphic Novel

    André Aciman

    Step into the breathtaking Italian summer with André Aciman's timeless tale of first love, tenderly adapted into a stunning graphic novel. Join Elio and Oliver as their initial disdain blossoms into a passionate connection, marked by stolen glances, sun-kissed memories, and the thrill of forbidden intimacy. A poignant portrayal of young desire, Call Me by Your Name: The Graphic Novel will captivate readers with its evocative artistry and exquisite storytelling.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Not in Love

    Ali Hazelwood

    Forbidden attraction ignites when biotech engineer Rue Siebert clashes with Eli Killgore, the handsome front man of the company trying to take over her job and stability. Their secret, no-strings-attached affair reaches boiling point as they navigate the treacherous world of corporate takeovers. But with their companies' fates intertwined, Rue and Eli must face the risks of falling for each other—or lose everything they hold dear.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Rogue x Gambit (An X-Men Love Story)

    Louangie Bou-Montes

    In this sizzling X-Men tale, Rogue and Gambit face down New Orleans' emerging threats - from corporate greed to lurking vampires - but their biggest battle is the unspoken desire that threatens to consume them. Forbidden attraction simmers between the touch-starved heroine and the charming thief-turned-avenger, testing their trust and resolve. Will their love prove their greatest strength or most lethal weakness? Dive into this dark, passionate romance to find out.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. In the Weeds

    B.K. Borison

    Join grumpy farmer Beckett Porter and social media influencer Evelyn St. James as they rediscover the magic of true love in this heartwarming romantic comedy. After an unforgettable weekend in Maine, they're given another chance at happiness when Evie unexpectedly arrives at Beckett's farm - but with no hint of their prior passion. As they navigate the complexities of online fame and rural life, will these two opposites find their way back to each other and the joy they once shared?

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Business Casual

    B.K. Borison

    A captivating, slow-burn romance with relatable characters, a charming setting, and a delightful blend of humor, vulnerability, and steamy chemistry.

    For: Fans of contemporary, emotionally driven romances with a focus on character development and relationships.

    Strengths: Expertly crafted tension, nuanced characterizations, and a tender, satisfying love story that explores trust, vulnerability, and the complexities of attraction.

    Skip if: While the pacing is deliberate and engaging, some readers might find the slow build of the central romance slightly frustrating at times.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate B.K. Borison's cozy, character-driven style and settings like Lovelight Farms may also enjoy other small-town romances like Amy Lea's Exes and O's or Jessica Joyce's You, with a View.

    “s writing feels like being wrapped in a blanket—a cozy, sexy, and one-of-a-kind blanket. Charlie and Nova are the flirty, funny, and, dare I say, my favorite Lovelight couple.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Road to Tender Hearts A Novel

    Annie Hartnett

    Embark on a heartwarming odyssey with PJ Halliday, a spirited septuagenarian determined to win back his high school sweetheart, as he navigates grief, love, and second chances in Annie Hartnett's delightful, darkly comic novel, The Road to Tender Hearts. Joining PJ on his cross-country quest are his estranged daughter, two orphaned grandkids, and a feline prophet of mortality—each a catalyst for a poignant and hilarious journey of self-discovery, family bonding, and the enduring power of hope and love.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Someday Garden

    Ashley Poston

    Escape to a magical summer at Lilymoor House, where a head gardener uncovers a secret, mysterious garden - and the man trapped inside. With its lush descriptions of flowers, hedges, and a charming coastal setting, The Someday Garden is a spellbinding romance filled with quirky characters, family secrets, and the power of nurturing love in all its forms.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. The Open Era

    Edward Schmit

    Get ready to serve up a heaping dose of love and drama with Edward Schmit's debut romance, The Open Era. When openly gay tennis star Austin Hardy finds himself in the media spotlight, his anxiety spirals out of control - until he stumbles, quite literally, into a flirtatious friendship with his talented and charming rival, Diego Cruz. As the tension builds on and off the court, Austin must decide if love is worth the risk amidst the high-stakes world of professional tennis. With Schmit's witty banter and heartfelt prose, this instant USA Today bestseller is a must-read for anyone who's ever played for keeps.

  14. The Go-Between

    L.P. Hartley

    Step into a captivating world of old English estates, forbidden love, and the timeless struggle between innocence and experience in L.P. Hartley's classic novel, The Go-Between. When 12-year-old Leo becomes a secret messenger in a sensual affair between his friend's sister and a groundskeeper, the consequences resonate through the years, exposing the complex layers of the human heart in a rich, spellbinding tale that transcends time and remains a true masterpiece.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Road Trip A Novel

    Mary Kay Andrews

    "Join sisters Maeve and Therese on a thrilling summer road trip from Savannah to Ireland, where past secrets, long-buried family history, and ruggedly charming men with Irish accents await.   16. Wasp's Nest A Novel

    Kat Stoddard

    Step into the world of Wasp's Nest, where love, secrets, and societal expectations collide at a decadent Cape Cod wedding. This sparkling modern retelling of The Philadelphia Story follows a tangled love triangle between Tess, her steady fiancé Warren, and her former flame Peter - who plans to attend with his new, much younger beau. Enter Mitch, an aspiring writer drawn into their orbit, and the sparks fly on all sides. With witty banter, relatable characters, and relationships that challenge the status quo, this captivating novel reveals the messy, beautiful complexity of desire and connection. A perfect read for anyone seeking a captivating, emotionally resonant summer romance.

  17. Scandal of the Summer

    Alexandra Vasti

    Escape to the wild Cornish coast and get swept away by the tantalizing tale of Lady Ruby, an unconventional debutante ripe for scandal, and Captain Malcolm Archer, a dashing smuggler whose charms can't mask his illicit activities. When Ruby's spirited rebellion collides with Archer's cunning schemes, their fiery attraction ignites a tangled web of deception that may just lead them to danger, desire, and ultimately, true love in this delightfully sexy and adventurous Regency romp.

  18. Red, White & Royal Blue: The Private Correspondence

    Casey McQuiston

    Step back into the unforgettable romance of Alex Claremont-Diaz and Henry Fox-Montaigne, reimagined in a captivating epistolary format. Red, White & Royal Blue: The Private Correspondence weaves a charming narrative through handwritten letters spanning over two decades, revealing the witty banter, tender vulnerabilities, and intimate passions of this beloved couple and their circle. Dive into the private archives to uncover the heartfelt milestones and everyday moments that make their enduring love story a treasured delight.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Late Bloomer A Novel

    Mazey Eddings

    Meet Opal, the quirky, newly-minted millionaire with a tender heart, and Pepper, her fiery, gorgeous opponent in a battle for a failing flower farm. Their opposing views and stubborn wills clash in a sensual, sapphic romance brimming with witty banter and blossoming attraction. Will these unlikely lovers find common ground and let their dreams grow together, or will their sparks of passion ignite a destructive force? Dive into the charming world of Thistle and Bloom Farms and discover the magic of Mazey Eddings' sparkling prose in this sexy, engaging tale of embracing change and finding love in the unlikeliest of places.

  20. Culpability

    Bruce Holsinger

    In this gripping family drama, a seemingly routine car crash reveals a tangled web of secrets that threaten to unravel the Cassidy-Shaw clan. As authorities probe the incident, each family member's hidden transgressions come to light, casting doubt on who is truly to blame. With its expertly crafted plot full of unexpected twists, Culpability is a thought-provoking exploration of morality, technology, and the complex bonds between parents and children.

  21. Helpless A Novel

    Jessica Knoll

    Jessica Knoll's Helpless is a sizzling, mind-bending thriller that reunites college sweethearts Faye and Henry, now leading dramatic lives, for a dark, intoxicating reckoning. When a cryptic event brings them together again, they descend into an psychological game of desire, secrets, and blackmail that challenges everything Faye thought she knew about their past. Knoll delivers a shocking twist that will leave you riveted until the final, unforgettable page.

  22. Sunburn Exclusive American Edition with Additional Material

    Chloe Michelle Howarth

    Sunburn is a poignant and intimate portrayal of first love, identity, and rebellion set against the backdrop of a conservative rural Irish community, making it a compelling read for fans of emotional, character-driven fiction.

    For: Readers who appreciate coming-of-age stories, queer themes, and the complexities of small-town life will find Sunburn a powerful and relatable debut.

    Strengths: The novel shines in its nuanced exploration of Lucy's inner turmoil, the authenticity of its Irish setting, and its evocative prose that captures the intensity of first love and the suffocating nature of secrets.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slow as the narrative unfolds at a deliberate, introspective pace, mirroring Lucy's emotional state.

    Comparable: Sunburn shares similarities with Normal People in its portrayal of forbidden love and the constraints of a tight-knit community, appealing to fans of Sally Rooney's work.

    “Tackling topics of queer identity, teenage angst, and religious guilt, this romantic novel burns with the intensity and yearning of first love.”

  23. Father Material

    Alexis Hall

    In 'Father Material', Alexis Hall crafts a darkly comedic, heartwarming portrait of parenthood that deftly balances biting satire and tender humanity. Through the story of a hapless, unqualified father-to-be, Hall probes the complexities of modern family, identity, and the perils of societal expectations. With witty prose and a wickedly sharp eye, this novel navigates the uncharted territories of masculinity, vulnerability, and the search for meaning in an often absurd world. A refreshingly honest exploration of fatherhood for the 21st century, 'Father Material' is both laugh-out-loud funny and unexpectedly poignant.

But as her jaunts through the past threaten to unravel her present, Ash confronts a chilling reality: escaping the present never truly meant escape at all.   26. Abby Offsides A Novel

    Anna McCallie

    Abby Offsides is a delightful, witty romance that explores the journey of self-discovery and second chances, set against the vibrant backdrop of Liverpool's football scene.

    For: Fans of character-driven, slow-burn romantic comedies will relish this charming debut.

    Strengths: The novel shines with sparkling dialogue, relatable protagonist Abby, and a nuanced portrayal of finding love and community in a new city. McCallie's humor and affection for the sport of football are infectious.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a touch leisurely, as the story unfolds at a comfortable, unhurried pace.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed Emily Giffin's Where We Belong or Jennifer Weiner's Who Do You Love will appreciate Abby Offsides' mix of humor, heart, and second-chance romance.

  28. Extracurricular

    Rachel Lynn Solomon

    A captivating romance between a former pop star and her psychology professor, exploring intimacy, trust, and self-discovery in a unique setting.

    For: Fans of adult romantic comedies, especially those seeking a more mature, nuanced portrayal of celebrity life and academics.

    Strengths: Vivid characters, witty dialogue, and a compelling slow-burn romance that balances humor and emotional depth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the academic setting and jargon-heavy psychology class scenes a bit dense.

    Comparable: Compared to the author's other works, Extracurricular delves deeper into the emotional journeys of the protagonists, with a similar blend of humor, heart, and steamy romance as seen in her other novels.
