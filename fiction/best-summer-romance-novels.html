Best Summer Romance Novels

Escape into a perfect summer with these captivating romance novels, perfect for a sun-soaked vacation or cozy staycation.

Who these books are for

  • Romance readers seeking light, feel-good summer stories
  • Fans of contemporary, new adult, and women's fiction
  • Book clubs looking for engaging, relatable reads

Start here

If you only read one summer romance this year, choose 'Our Perfect Storm' by Carley Fortune for its thought-provoking exploration of love and resilience.

As the days grow longer and sunnier, there's no better time to get lost in a captivating summer romance novel. This handpicked collection of the best summer reads offers a diverse range of stories, from light-hearted, humorous tales to more emotionally charged, thought-provoking explorations of love and relationships. Whether you're a long-time fan of the romance genre or simply seeking a engaging, uplifting read for your next vacation or staycation, these books are sure to delight and inspire.

The collection

  1. Cherry Baby A Novel

    Rainbow Rowell

    In Cherry Baby, Rainbow Rowell masterfully crafts a vivid portrait of a woman reeling from the demise of her marriage and the exposure of her likeness in a popular webcomic. With humor, tenderness, and unflinching honesty, this captivating novel follows Cherry's journey of self-discovery and growth as she navigates a new chapter in her life, from the intimate confessions of a shadowy past love to the tantalizing possibilities of a fresh start. Delving deep into the complexities of identity, relationships, and the power of art to both reflect and shape our perceptions, Cherry Baby is Rowell's most daring and sexy novel yet—a must-read for anyone seeking a rich, resonant, and unforgettable tale of the human experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Who Knows You by Heart A Novel

    C. J. Farley

    C.J. Farley's propulsive and enlightening novel Who Knows You by Heart follows Octavia, a brilliant, burnt-out coder, as she navigates the cutthroat world of Big Tech. When she's recruited by a secretive project, Zion, designed to revolutionize storytelling with AI, Octavia uncovers her employer's toxic underbelly. With wry humor and razor-sharp insight, Farley delivers a scintillating, timely exploration of race, relationships, and what matters most in a hyper-connected age.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Every Summer After

    Carley Fortune

    A captivating, nostalgic, and romantic tale of second chances and lost love, beautifully capturing the essence of summer in a small Ontario town.

    For: Romance readers seeking a poignant, character-driven story with a strong setting and themes of self-discovery and redemption.

    Strengths: Subtle, evocative writing, relatable characters, and a well-paced narrative that explores the complexities of relationships and personal growth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the introspective, emotional focus slower-paced compared to more plot-driven romances.

    Comparable: Fans of Emily Henry, Jennifer Weiner, and Sarah Dessen will appreciate the heartwarming, summery atmosphere and character-centered storytelling.

    “The magic and romance of summer is palpable.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. One Golden Summer

    Carley Fortune

    A captivating summer romance with a strong sense of place and character growth, transporting readers to the idyllic lake setting.

    For: Fans of feel-good, sun-kissed love stories will appreciate this sweet, slow-burn tale of second chances.

    Strengths: Carley Fortune masterfully evokes the nostalgia and magic of summer, crafting relatable characters and a sensual, atmospheric setting that pulls readers in.

    Skip if: Some may find the pace leisurely and the emotional depth a touch heavy-handed at times.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy heartwarming, small-town romances with a strong seasonal element, like Nancy Thayer's Beach House series, will find much to appreciate in One Golden Summer.

    “The way that Carley Fortune can bottle up the feeling of summer and put it into a book needs to be studied. Her books are so transportive!”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Romantic Hero

    Kirsty Greenwood

    This delightful romantic comedy brings a touch of interdimensional magic to a classic 'writer's block' story, with an endearing heroine, a charming hero, and a witty, playful narrative.

    For: Fans of romantic comedies, particularly those with a touch of the paranormal or magical realism, will adore this charming tale.

    Strengths: Greenwood's writing is engaging, warm, and full of humor, with a winning heroine and a swoon-worthy hero, making for a fun and heartfelt read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the premise of a character from a novel coming to life a bit far-fetched, but the execution is so well-done that it's easy to overlook.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed titles like The Love of My Afterlife or The Rosie Project will find similar humor, heart, and romantic escapades in Romantic Hero.

    “Kirsty has done it again, a total rom-com triumph! I laughed, I cried, I swooned! Five magical stars.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Dolly All the Time: A GMA Book Club Pick

    Annabel Monaghan

    "Step into the enchanting world of Dolly, a charming and neurotic social media influencer, in Annabel Monaghan's delightfully quirky debut novel Dolly All the Time. With its wicked wit, clever observations on modern life, and a dash of satire, this page-turner will have you hooked and rooting for Dolly as she navigates the perils of fame, friendship, and self-acceptance. Perfect for anyone fascinated by the contradictions of contemporary celebrity culture and the power of a good laugh."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Our Perfect Storm

    Carley Fortune

    Our Perfect Storm is a captivating friends-to-lovers romance that explores the complexities of childhood relationships, featuring richly detailed settings and a slow-burn narrative that builds to an emotional crescendo.

    For: Romance readers seeking a character-driven, atmospheric story with a strong focus on friendship and the evolution of relationships.

    Strengths: Carley Fortune's masterful storytelling, vivid descriptions of the Pacific Northwest setting, and the authentic portrayal of long-standing friendships make this novel stand out.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a touch slow, as the author takes time to develop the characters and their emotional journeys.

    Comparable: Fans of Emily Henry, Taylor Jenkins Reid, and Colleen Hoover will appreciate the introspective, relationship-focused storytelling and the romantic tension in Our Perfect Storm.

    “Carley Fortune, the reigning queen of summer romance, has returned with a slow-burn friends to lovers arc for the ages.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Summer's Never Over

    Darby Bozeman

    Unravel the tangled threads of the past in Darby Bozeman's captivating debut, Summer's Never Over. When Greer returns to the Georgia summer camp where her friend Steph tragically died five years prior, dark secrets and unresolved feelings threaten to consume her. As past and present collide, Greer must confront the possibility that Steph's demise was no accident. With its dual timelines, complex characters, and a haunting setting that will chill you to the core, Summer's Never Over is a mesmerizing blend of mystery, romance, and Southern Gothic that will keep you enthralled until the shocking finale.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Summer Girlfriend

    Kristina Forest

    In this delightfully charming romance, a pragmatic bookseller and a reformed playboy embark on a fake summer fling, only to find their hearts entwined. Noelle, a stand-in bridesmaid desperate to save for college, and Jeremiah, trying to prove his grown-up credentials, form an unexpected bond that deepens into a passionate summer affair. With witty dialogue and a picturesque beachside setting, The Summer Girlfriend is the perfect read for anyone looking for a heartwarming, feel-good romance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. June Baby A Novel

    Shannon Garvey

    Step into a captivating world of summer love, family secrets, and self-discovery with June Baby. Set against the idyllic backdrop of Block Island, this heartwarming tale follows Ruth as she navigates the beauty and pain of growing up, forging her identity, and confronting the mysteries of her past. A "book of the summer" with "juicy romance, explosive family secrets, and a delicious island setting" (Elin Hilderbrand), June Baby is a must-read for anyone seeking a transcendent, coming-of-age story that will leave you breathless and hopeful.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. One Last Stop

    Casey McQuiston

    From the New York Times bestselling author of Red, White & Royal Blue comes a whimsical romantic comedy that will sweep you off your feet. In One Last Stop, cynical 23-year-old August navigates the highs and lows of life in New York City, waiting tables and moving in with quirky roommates. But everything changes when she meets Jane, a stunning punk-rock inspired girl who's literally displaced from the 1970s. As August falls head over heels for Jane, she must use everything she's learned to defy time and bring this enchanting woman back to her own era. With its irresistible blend of humor, heart, and magic, One Last Stop is a dazzling romance that will leave you believing in the impossible.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Curvy Girl Summer

    Danielle Allen

    Curvy Girl Summer is a delightful, body-positive rom-com that shines with witty banter, steamy chemistry, and a heartwarming message about self-love and finding true connection.

    For: Fans of contemporary romance, especially those seeking a fun, relatable story with a plus-size heroine and a charming, supportive love interest.

    Strengths: The novel excels at crafting snappy dialogue, delivering laugh-out-loud humor, and balancing lighthearted moments with genuine emotional depth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the familial pressure and ticking clock plot device a bit predictable or heavy-handed at times.

    Comparable: Curvy Girl Summer shares similarities with other romantic comedies like Bridget Jones's Diary and The Hating Game, but with a fresh, modern spin and a strong focus on body positivity.

    “A fat-positive romance that blends the witty repartee of a buddy comedy with sexual tension.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Nevermore Bookstore A Hot, Kink-Positive, Morally Gray, Grumpy-Sunshine Romcom

    Kerrigan Byrne

    Get lost in the tantalizing world of Nevermore Bookstore, where forbidden desires simmer beneath the surface. Cadence, a fiercely independent bookstore owner, is unwittingly drawn into a tantalizing game of cat and mouse with the enigmatic "Fox." But when their late-night phone calls are interrupted, Fox must emerge from the shadows to protect her, igniting a passion that threatens to consume them both. As the truth slowly unfurls, can these star-crossed lovers find a way to bridge the gaps of their pasts and forge a future together, or will the weight of their secrets tear them asunder?

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. In Stormy Weather A Novel

    Chelsea Curto

    From the bestselling indie author of "The Ending Games" comes a captivating academic rivals-to-lovers romance set amidst the stormy backdrop of hurricane season in Florida. When meteorology prodigy Quincy Monroe lands a game-changing job, her path is derailed by her longtime rival and former best friend's brother, the charismatically infuriating Sebastian Dunn. As they're forced to put aside their animosity to chase storms and survive the record-breaking season, can they weather the growing tension between them before their hearts are swept away?

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. For the Love of Scott Ten Rigs Texas Tales, #1

    Jen FitzGerald

    "In this heartfelt debut, author Jen FitzGerald masterfully weaves together military vet Scott Hudson's gritty return to Texas life and the blossoming romance between him and Ben Thompson. As Scott rebuilds at the animal shelter, he must confront his fear of love, even as Ben's quiet devotion pulls him in. Will Scott's dark past and fear of vulnerability sabotage his chance at true connection with Ben? Dive into this moving tale of healing, second chances, and the unbreakable bond between humans and animals."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. The Fatal Unpleasantness at Netherfield A Novel

    Claudia Gray

    In this delightful, engaging mystery, Jonathan Darcy and his investigative partner Juliet Tilney must unravel a deadly puzzle at Netherfield Park, where Jonathan's uncle's brother-in-law is poisoned. As they dig deeper, they face danger, scandal, and the heartache of societal disapproval of their growing feelings for each other. With wit, charm and a pitch-perfect Regency voice, Claudia Gray masterfully updates Jane Austen's world while adding her own twists and suspenseful surprises. A must-read for fans of classic romance and mystery.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Problematic Summer Romance

    Ali Hazelwood

    In the scorching world of academic research, love and obsession blur lines in Hazelwood's debut "Problematic Summer Romance". As rival scientists clash over a groundbreaking discovery, their forbidden desires ignite a passionate battle of intellect and wills. Witness the steamy sparks fly in the midst of petri dishes and pipettes, as these two brilliant minds navigate the treacherous intersection of science and seduction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Down to Earth An 831 Stories Romance

    Julia Turshen

    "Diving into Julia Turshen's 'Down to Earth' is a cozy hug for your soul. This tender, 831-stories-strong romance weaves together moments of joy, heartache, and everyday triumphs, inviting readers to savor the beauty in the mundane. With each turn of the page, Turshen's delectable prose whisks you away to a world of intimate connection, prompting you to reflect on the simple pleasures and profound loves that shape our lives."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What type of books are included in this summer romance collection?
This curated list features contemporary women's fiction, new adult, and romantic comedies that focus on summer love stories.
Are these books suitable for all ages?
While the stories are generally wholesome, some novels may include mature themes, mild language, or sensual content, making them more suitable for readers 18 and older.
Can I find diverse perspectives and representation in these summer romance novels?
Yes, the collection includes works by authors of various backgrounds, ensuring a range of authentic voices and experiences.
Are there any historical or paranormal elements in these summer romances?
No, this selection exclusively features modern, realistic love stories set during the summer season, with no historical or supernatural elements.
