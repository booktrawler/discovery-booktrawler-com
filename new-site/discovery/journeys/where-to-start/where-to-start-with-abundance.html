Where to Start with Abundance

Begin with Ezra Klein's Abundance for a comprehensive introduction to the concept and its global implications.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone curious about the concept of abundance and its potential impact on society and personal lives
  • New readers looking for a thought-provoking and accessible introduction to the topic

Start here

If you only read one, choose Abundance by Ezra Klein for its exhaustive exploration of the abundance phenomenon.

The concept of abundance has far-reaching implications for how we live, work, and relate to one another. As technology and global connections continue to accelerate, understanding the complexities of abundance is essential for individuals and societies alike. This reading path provides a clear starting point for exploring this timely and thought-provoking topic.

The collection

  1. Abundance

    Ezra Klein

    In Abundance, Ezra Klein and Derek Thompson boldly challenge the prevailing ideology of scarcity that has hindered progress. Drawing on groundbreaking research, they argue that today's crises - from housing shortages to climate inaction - stem from our failure to adapt 20th-century solutions for the 21st century. The duo offers a visionary roadmap to revitalize the politics of plenty, rebuilding institutions to meet modern challenges and unlock a future of shared abundance for all.

  2. The Happiness Trap (Second Edition) How to Stop Struggling and Start Living

    Russ Harris

    Break free from the cycle of stress, anxiety, and depression with this groundbreaking guide. Author Russ Harris presents the powerful tools of Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) to help you clarify your values, build self-compassion, and find lasting fulfillment. Whether you're struggling with insecurity, workplace burnout, or the challenges of life, The Happiness Trap offers a proven path to authentic happiness and a richer, more meaningful existence. Over a million copies sold and now updated and expanded, this essential read belongs on every self-help shelf.

  3. Dopamine Nation Finding Balance in the Age of Indulgence

    Anna Lembke MD

    In Dopamine Nation, Dr. Anna Lembke expertly weaves together cutting-edge neuroscience, riveting patient stories, and practical wisdom to illuminate the complex interplay between pleasure, pain, and balance. A must-read for anyone struggling to resist the siren song of compulsive consumption and longing for a more fulfilling existence.

  4. Limitarianism The Case Against Extreme Wealth

    Ingrid Robeyns

    Join philosopher Ingrid Robeyns as she makes a compelling case for limitarianism, challenging the notion that an individual should be able to accumulate limitless wealth without restriction. This timely and provocative work sparks an urgent debate about the ethics of extreme wealth, capitalism, and neoliberalism, ultimately inviting a radical reimagination of our society. A must-read for anyone grappling with the dark side of unbridled capitalism and seeking a more democratic, sustainable future.

Autistic psychologist Sarah Bergenfield offers a revolutionary perspective on autism as a condition of sensory surplus, guided by powerful insights from neuroscience and internal family systems therapy. This groundbreaking guide helps readers understand their embodied experiences, regulate overwhelm, and cultivate resilience - ultimately reframing autism as perceptual diversity rather than deficit. A must-read for anyone seeking a deeper, kinder understanding of neurodiversity from the inside out."

With refreshing candor, the book reveals five essential truths and practical steps to excel in grantmaking, navigating artifacts like successes, failures, and industry insights. A must-read for foundation board members, staff, and anyone striving to do good, Please Be Good at Philanthropy transforms newcomers into effective philanthropists.

    Jennifer Breheny Wallace

    In her powerful new book Mattering, Jennifer Breheny Wallace reveals the transformative power of mattering - the fundamental human need to feel valued and have purpose. Through moving stories and insightful research, Wallace shows how strengthening our "mattering core" helps us reconnect with our sense of purpose, deepen relationships, and navigate life's challenges with greater resilience. A timely, urgent guide to building meaningful connections and cultures of value in our personal and professional lives.

Through 131 engaging, low-stakes exercises, discover how to tune out distractions and tune in to the world around you, sparking clearer thinking, deeper listening, and a renewed sense of what truly matters.   9. American Bulk Essays on Excess

    Emily Mester

    In this incisive and deeply personal collection, Emily Mester adeptly reframes the everyday rituals of consumption as a window into the human experience, peeling back the layers of our relationships with stuff and identity. With humor, vulnerability, and piercing insight, she navigates the contradictions and ironies of modern life, from the thrill of Costco hauls to the relics of family dysfunction in an Iowa hoard. A striking debut that offers a fresh lens on our obsession with abundance and the search for meaning in a consumer culture.

Frequently asked questions

What is abundance in this context?
Abundance refers to the exponential growth of resources, wealth, and opportunities that has reshaped the modern world.
Is abundance only about economic growth?
No, abundance encompasses not just financial wealth but also technological advancements, access to information, and the rising expectations of global citizens.
Is Abundance a pessimistic or optimistic book?
Ezra Klein offers a balanced view, acknowledging both the benefits and challenges that arise from living in an age of abundance.
