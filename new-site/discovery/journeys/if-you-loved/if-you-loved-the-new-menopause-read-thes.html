If You Loved The New Menopause, Read These

For a more in-depth exploration of menopause, try Dr. Jen Gunter's The Menopause Manifesto, which offers a comprehensive, evidence-based guide. For a distinct perspective, consider Lasara Firefox Allen's Genderqueer Menopause, which examines the experience of non-binary individuals.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone seeking more literature on menopause
  • Readers who enjoyed the personal, scientific approach of The New Menopause
  • Individuals interested in diverse perspectives on menopause

Start here

If you only read one, choose The Menopause Manifesto for its thorough, accessible take on the subject.

The New Menopause sparked a necessary conversation about midlife, health, and identity. If you found its blend of science, memoir, and cultural critique engaging, you're likely to appreciate these further readings that explore menopause from various angles. From comprehensive guides to personal reflections, these books offer a more in-depth look at this pivotal life stage.

The collection

  1. The Menopause Manifesto Own Your Health with Facts and Feminism

    Dr. Jen Gunter

    "Empower yourself with the ultimate menopause guide! Dr. Jen Gunter, the "internet's most fearless advocate for women's health," debunks myths, shares hard facts, and offers expert advice to help you navigate the complex and often misunderstood process of menopause. From hot flashes to sex, sleep, and more, this frank and humorous book is your reassuring companion for a smoother transition."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Genderqueer Menopause Navigating Menopause for Trans, Gender-Nonconforming, Genderfluid, and Other Queer-Bodied Folx

    Lasara Firefox Allen MSW

    "Genderqueer Menopause is a groundbreaking, affirming guide for nonbinary, trans, and queer-bodied individuals navigating menopause. With expert insights, practical tools, and empowering strategies, this vital resource reframes menopause care to center the experiences and needs of the genderqueer community. By demystifying symptoms, pushing back against heteronormative standards, and amplifying diverse voices, author Lasara Firefox Allen helps you redefine this life transition on your own terms. Whether you're seeking relief, redefining celebration, or simply finding solidarity, Genderqueer Menopause is a must-have companion for empowering yourself through this pivotal journey."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Second Sex

    Simone De Beauvoir

    A pioneering and essential work, Simone de Beauvoir's The Second Sex remains a powerful rallying cry for women's independence and self-advancement. In this unabridged edition, readers can experience the full breadth and depth of Beauvoir's groundbreaking argument, newly restored from the original French text. This provocative and timeless classic continues to inspire generations and remains a vital read for understanding gender politics.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Women A Novel

    Kristin Hannah

    In Kristin Hannah's riveting novel, The Women, 20-year-old Frankie McGrath trades her nursing studies for the Army Nurse Corps to serve in Vietnam amidst war's chaos and destruction. Through her eyes, we witness the bonds of sisterhood forged under fire, the disorienting journey back to a fractured America, and one woman's journey from idealism to the unyielding courage required to heal a nation's wounds, war's forgotten heroes.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The First Time I Saw Him

    Laura Dave

    In this captivating follow-up to The Last Thing He Told Me, Laura Dave masterfully weaves another spellbinding tale, this time delving into themes of forgiveness and the enduring power of love. Picking up where the previous story left off, The First Time I Saw Him promises an equally gripping, rich, and evocative reading experience, sure to leave readers eagerly turning the pages to unravel the next chapter in this unforgettable saga.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Before I Let Go

    Kennedy Ryan

    Rediscover the love that once seemed destined to last forever in Kennedy Ryan's impassioned second chance romance, Before I Let Go. When Yasmen and Josiah Wade are drawn back together after a decade apart, their rekindled attraction ignites a deep longing to reclaim their relationship. But the scars of their painful past threaten to tear them asunder once more. A heart-wrenching, intensely sensual journey that probes the depths of love, forgiveness, and second chances, Before I Let Go is a triumph from a New York Times bestseller sure to leave readers breathless.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Come As You Are: Revised and Updated The Surprising New Science That Will Transform Your Sex Life

    Emily Nagoski Ph.D.

    Breakthrough science reveals the ultimate secret to a joyful, confident sex life: it's not about your anatomy, but how you feel about it. In this revised guide, Dr. Emily Nagoski demystifies the intricacies of female sexuality, showing that stress, mood, trust, and body image are the keys to unlocking pleasure. By learning to embrace your wholeness and self, you'll discover a more fulfilling, satisfying erotic experience than ever before.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Meeting New People A Novel

    Daniel M. Lavery

    Discover an irresistible, wildly entertaining novel about friendship and self-discovery in midlife, from the acclaimed author of Women's Hotel. Fifty-something Barbara, twice-divorced, embarks on a witty, heartfelt journey to optimize finding her tenth, and hopefully last, best friend. Will she repeat past mistakes or try something new? With acerbic charm, Barbara navigates life's crucial relationships in a narrative that reads like an homage to Nora Ephron's Heartburn. An unforgettable tale by one of our most inventive and brilliant writers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. This Book Made Me Think of You

    Libby Page

    Tilly Nightingale's year of grief takes an unexpected turn when she receives a mysterious birthday gift from her late husband at her local bookstore. As she immerses herself in the 12 carefully curated books and handwritten notes, Tilly finds solace, new adventures, and a budding connection with the kind bookstore owner Alfie. This heartwarming tale celebrates the transformative power of reading and the love that can blossom in the most unexpected places.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Bell Jar A Novel

    Sylvia Plath

    In this haunting literary classic, Sylvia Plath masterfully captures the inner turmoil of Esther Greenwood, a brilliant and beautiful young woman grappling with the pressures of modern womanhood. Esther's poignant journey from promising internship to spiraling mental breakdown is a visceral and unflinching exploration of the darkest corners of the psyche. With its enduring relevance and Plath's perfectly wrought prose, The Bell Jar remains a seminal and deeply affecting work of fiction that will resonate with readers long after the final page.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What if I'm not a woman going through menopause?
These recommendations still apply, as menopause affects all genders and relationships.
Are these books only about menopause?
While menopause is the central topic, they also touch on broader themes like aging, identity, and human experience.
Do I need to have read The New Menopause to appreciate these suggestions?
No, this is geared towards anyone curious about menopause and its cultural, personal implications
