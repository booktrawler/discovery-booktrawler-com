Diverse Contemporary Fiction

A collection of powerful, thought-provoking novels exploring identity, culture, and the human condition.

Who these books are for

  • Book clubs seeking diverse reading lists
  • Readers interested in cultural studies
  • Fans of magical realism and speculative fiction
  • Anyone looking for inspiring, thought-provoking novels

Start here

If you only read one book exploring identity and culture, choose 'Americanah' by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie.

This curated collection of novels showcases the richness and diversity of contemporary fiction. From magical realism to historical fiction, these award-winning books explore complex themes like identity, culture, and the human condition. Readers will encounter immersive world-building, lyrical prose, and unforgettable characters that linger long after the final page is turned. Whether you're a book club looking for thought-provoking discussion material or an individual seeking inspiring stories that reflect our world, this collection has something for everyone.

The collection

  1. One Hundred Years of Solitude

    Gabriel Garcia Marquez

    A marvel of magical realism, Gabriel García Márquez's enchanting One Hundred Years of Solitude transports readers to the mystical town of Macondo and the fortunes of its beloved Buendía family. This sweeping, lyrical masterpiece—winner of the Nobel Prize in Literature—is a dazzling achievement that explores the profound, meaningful, and meaningless elements of life with compassion, humor, and unforgettable characters. A timeless, soul-stirring tale that belongs on every reader's list.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Death of the Author A Novel

    Nnedi Okorafor

    "Wow, you absolutely must read Nnedi Okorafor's 'Death of the Author'! As a scholar of Africanfuturism and postcolonial literature, I'm blown away by her innovative blend of myth, sci-fi, and social critique. Fans of authors like N.K. Jemisin or Chinua Achebe will revel in Okorafor's vibrant world-building and lyrical prose as she crafts an epic tale of power, perception, and the interplay of cultures. Get ready to be transported to a rich and thought-provoking fictional landscape that will linger long after you finish the book!"

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Americanah A novel

    Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

    Americanah is a powerful, nuanced exploration of the immigrant experience, racial identity, and belonging in a fractured world, written with virtuosity, empathy, and searing social acuity.

    For: Readers seeking a thought-provoking, multigenerational epic about identity, love, and the search for home will appreciate this novel.

    Strengths: Adichie's masterful storytelling weaves together complex themes with humor, warmth, and unforgettable characters, creating a rich, expansive narrative that spans continents and decades.

    Skip if: At nearly 500 pages, Americanah is a substantial commitment, requiring patience and dedication from the reader.

    Comparable: Fans of Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie's other works, including Half of a Yellow Sun and We Should All Be Feminists, will find similar depth, intelligence, and emotional resonance in Americanah.

    “Dazzling. . . . Funny and defiant, and simultaneously so wise. . . . Brilliant.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Things Fall Apart A Novel

    Chinua Achebe

    In Chinua Achebe's timeless masterpiece, Things Fall Apart, Okonkwo's fierce resistance to British colonialism offers a powerful lens into Africa's tumultuous encounter with European power. With lyrical prose and a deeply human protagonist, Achebe crafts a poignant, multilayered tale of cultural disintegration and personal despair that resonates across time and continents. A must-read for anyone seeking a profound understanding of the complex legacies of colonialism and their enduring impact on the world today.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Now I Surrender A Novel

    Álvaro Enrigue

    Now I Surrender is a sweeping, imaginative novel that recasts the story of the Mexico-US border wars through interwoven historical and fictional narratives, offering a searing elegy for a vanished way of life.

    For: Historical fiction enthusiasts, fans of innovative storytelling, and anyone interested in the complex relationship between Mexico and the US.

    Strengths: Its innovative, interdisciplinary approach seamlessly blends historical research, fictional characters, and metafictional elements to create a rich, immersive reading experience.

    Skip if: The dense, layered narrative may prove challenging for some readers to follow, particularly those unfamiliar with the historical context.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy revisionist Westerns or novels that blur the lines between history and fiction, such as Cormac McCarthy's Blood Meridian or Gabriel García Márquez's Autumn of the Patriarch, will appreciate Now I Surrender's bold storytelling.

    “So original and funny… with Enrigue living between worlds—the worlds of the past and the present, but also of the real and the imagined.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Here Where We Live Is Our Country The Story of the Jewish Bund

    Molly Crabapple

    In this vivid, award-winning narrative, artist and writer Molly Crabapple brings to life the Jewish Labor Bund, a revolutionary movement that fought for dignity and equality in eastern Europe. Through stirring portraits of Bundists, from poets and rebels to lovers on the barricades, Crabapple re-creates their extraordinary world and the vital lessons they offer for our own tumultuous times. As nationalism, repression, and the struggle for belonging resurface, the Bund's remarkable story serves as a powerful reminder that liberation begins where we stand, and that solidarity, even in darkness, can ultimately prevail.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. You Dreamed of Empires A Novel

    Álvaro Enrigue

    In this electrifying novel, Álvaro Enrigue reimagines the pivotal moment when Cortés and his entourage first entered the Aztec capital of Tenochtitlan in 1519. Told through the eyes of the conquistadors and indigenous princesses, You Dreamed of Empires delves into the labyrinthine palace, blurring the lines between reality and hallucination as both sides await the fateful meeting with Emperor Moctezuma. Spanning political intrigue, cultural clashes, and personal doubts, this groundbreaking work breathes new life into this epochal encounter, culminating in a fantastical, restitutive counterattack that feels both historically grounded and eerily prophetic. Dive into this visionary, revelatory tale of colonial revenge and discover Tenochtitlan

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Paradiso 17 A Novel

    Hannah Lillith Assadi

    Embark on a spellbinding journey through the diasporic odyssey of Sufien, a Palestinian man forever bound to the Idea of Home. From the tumultuous dawn of 1948's Nakba to his sojourns in Kuwait, Italy, New York, and finally Arizona, Sufien's restless search for belonging weaves a poignant tapestry of love, loss, and longing. With its lyrical prose and haunting lyricism, Paradiso 17 is a sweeping tale that probes the complexities of exile and the resilience of the human spirit. Dive into this unforgettable story to discover that even in the farthest reaches of displacement, the light of a life lived shines radiant and true.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The House on Mango Street

    Sandra Cisneros

    Step into the vivid world of Esperanza Cordero, a young Latina growing up in 1970s Chicago, in Sandra Cisneros's beloved classic, THE HOUSE ON MANGO STREET. With poetic, spare prose, Cisneros brings to life a treasured coming-of-age tale of self-discovery, family, and community. This acclaimed masterpiece has captivated readers for decades, earning its place as a timeless neighborhood novel, a celebration of voice and heritage. Dive in and discover why THE HOUSE ON MANGO STREET is a must-read for all ages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue

    V. E. Schwab

    In V.E. Schwab's enchanting novel, 'The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue', a centuries-long curse transforms a spirited young woman into an ethereal being invisible to most, yet not to one unexpected soul. With lyrical prose, Schwab weaves a poignant tale of longing, love, and the enduring human spirit, as Addie navigates the hidden contours of history and the bittersweet nature of immortality. A mesmerizing, emotional journey awaits those who delve into this captivating, genre-bending tale.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Covenant of Water (Oprah's Book Club)

    Abraham Verghese

    The Covenant of Water is a sweeping family epic that masterfully weaves together historical context, cultural insight, and intimate character studies, making it a standout work of literary fiction.

    For: Fans of historical fiction, literary epics, and readers seeking a rich, immersive reading experience.

    Strengths: Verghese's vivid storytelling, nuanced characterization, and meticulous research bring a complex and fascinating era to life.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, layered narrative and large cast of characters challenging to follow at times.

    Comparable: In scope and ambition, The Covenant of Water is reminiscent of other epic family sagas like Salman Rushdie's Midnight's Children or A.S. Byatt's Possession.

    “Sweeping, intimate yet vast . . . Languorous and often lyrical, morally ambitious.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Midnight's Children A Novel

    Salman Rushdie

    In Salman Rushdie's iconic novel, Midnight's Children, Saleem Sinai's birth at India's moment of independence sets him on a telepathic journey intertwined with the nation's tumultuous history. This "astonishing evocation" of India's people and politics is both a sweeping family saga and a magical exploration of the universal human experience, solidifying Rushdie as a literary master and Midnight's Children as a timeless classic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Bury Our Bones in the Midnight Soil

    V. E. Schwab

    Bury Our Bones in the Midnight Soil is a genre-bending masterpiece that weaves together dark fantasy, historical fiction, and queer romance across three timelines, offering a richly atmospheric and sensual exploration of female desire, power, and the eternal hunger of the undead.

    For: Fans of complex, character-driven fiction, LGBTQ+ readers, and those who appreciate atmospheric, gothic horror with a historical twist will find much to love in this novel.

    Strengths: Schwab's masterful storytelling, deft world-building, and vivid prose bring these disparate narratives together seamlessly, while her nuanced portrayals of female characters and their intense, often taboo desires are both captivating and thought-provoking.

    Skip if: The novel's non-linear structure can be confusing for some readers, and the sheer scope of the story may require close attention to fully appreciate the interconnectedness of the three main storylines.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy dark, sensual fiction with a historical bent, such as Anne Rice's Vampire Chronicles or Poppy Z. Brite's Lost Souls series, will likely appreciate the blend of horror, romance, and supernatural elements found in Bury Our Bones in the Midnight Soil.

    “Immersive… A time-sweeping, character-juggling, lesbian vampire mystery.”

  14. Bury Our Bones in the Midnight Soil

    V. E. Schwab

    Bury Our Bones in the Midnight Soil is a captivating, genre-bending novel that weaves together tales of immortality, queer desire, and female empowerment across three distinct eras, showcasing V. E. Schwab's masterful storytelling and lyrical prose.

    For: Fans of dark fantasy, historical fiction, and LGBTQ+ literature will devour this complex, sensual, and haunting narrative.

    Strengths: Schwab's writing is immersive, evocative, and skillfully paced, while the interconnected storylines and multifaceted characters offer depth and emotional resonance.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the non-linear structure and shifting perspectives initially disorienting.

    Comparable: For those who appreciate atmospheric, supernatural fiction with strong female leads, Bury Our Bones in the Midnight Soil is reminiscent of Anne Rice's Vampire Chronicles and Sarah Waters' Gothic novels.

    “Immersive… A time-sweeping, character-juggling, lesbian vampire mystery.”

  15. Last Night in Brooklyn A Novel

    Xochitl Gonzalez

    Step across the threshold into Xochitl Gonzalez's captivating debut, Last Night in Brooklyn. Amidst the vibrant creative scene of Fort Greene, Brooklyn in 2007, 26-year-old Alicia finds her life entwined with the enigmatic La Garza, a rising fashion star, and her wealthy cousin, whose seemingly perfect lives conceal sharp edges and impending crisis. As Alicia navigates the seductive allure of her neighbors' world, Gonzalez masterfully weaves a nuanced exploration of the American Dream's dark underbelly, revealing the destructive price of ambition and the lingering shadows of culture's fleeting moments. A riveting, timely tale that will keep you guessing until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Before All the World A Novel

    Moriel Rothman-Zecher

    In 1930s Philadelphia, a mesmerizing convergence of souls unfolds in Moriel Rothman-Zecher's Before All the World. Leyb, haunted by his village's tragic fate, finds solace with Charles, a charismatic Black poet. Then, miraculously, Leyb's childhood sweetheart Gittl arrives, her siblings' spirits manifest as malokhim. With poetic prose, Rothman-Zecher delves into the impossibility of escaping trauma, while exploring hope, responsibility to the future, and the power of believing in a better world ahead. Pick up this inventive, evocative novel to witness the struggle for renewal and the enduring human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Sula A Novel

    Toni Morrison

    In Toni Morrison's modern masterpiece Sula, two African American friends forge an unbreakable bond as they navigate the complexities of identity, love, and societal expectations in a small Ohio town. But when Sula's rebellious spirit leads to a heartbreaking betrayal, the depth of their friendship - and the darkness of their shared past - threaten to tear them apart. This powerful novel, hailed as one of the Great American Novels of the past century, is a must-read exploration of the resilience of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Half of a Yellow Sun

    Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

    Experience the tumultuous spark of Biafran independence through the lives of five unforgettable characters in Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie's acclaimed novel, Half of a Yellow Sun. With effortless prose, Adichie illuminates a pivotal moment of modern African history, capturing the promise, passion, and ultimate devastation of this seminal conflict. A National Book Critics Circle Award finalist and Women's Prize for Fiction winner, this sweeping historical fiction weaves love, loyalty, and survival into a haunting narrative that will leave you moved and transformed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Imago

    Octavia E Butler

    Nestled in the precarious aftermath of a global catastrophe, Octavia Butler's thought-provoking novel Imago delves into the extraordinary journey of Jodahs, a hybrid child who embodies the transformative power of an alien-human union. As the first ooloi to be born to a human mother, Jodahs must navigate a terrifying metamorphosis into a being wielding DNA manipulation, shapeshifting, and more. This gripping exploration of identity, power, and the delicate balance between coexistence and annihilation will captivate fans of science fiction and anyone seeking a rich, unsettling read.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. Autobiography of Cotton A Novel

    Cristina Rivera Garza

    In Cristina Rivera Garza's groundbreaking novel, Autobiography of Cotton, a family's journey from mining towns to cotton fields along the Mexico-US border intersects with the true story of José Revueltas's strike-supporting trek, offering a vivid and evocative history of the region's transformation through cotton cultivation. Through a masterful blend of archival research and personal narrative, Rivera Garza uncovers the persistence of cycles of deprivation and environmental devastation across generations, crafting a deeply personal and politically acute border novel that rewrites the social, labor, and ecological history of agricultural colonization and cross-border migration.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. The Oldest Bitch Alive

    Morgan Day

    In "The Oldest Bitch Alive", Morgan Day spins a singular tale of self-discovery through the unlikely protagonist of a French Bulldog named Gelsomina. When parasitic worms take up residence inside her, Gelsomina glimpses her mundane life in a new light - from her ties to her human family to her longings for a wild, unknown world. This inventive, polyphonic novel probes consciousness, domesticity, and the boundaries of the self, culminating in an exhilarating journey of awakening and transcendence as Gelsomina races towards the brink of life and death. A remarkable debut that challenges and delights.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  22. Perfection

    Vincenzo Latronico

    In this biting, thought-provoking novel, Vincenzo Latronico dissects the illusion of perfection in modern life. Follow Anna and Tom, a couple living their dream in Berlin, as they navigate the challenges of freelancing, relationships, and finding purpose in a world that always seems just out of reach. A hilarious and haunting exploration of the pitfalls of contemporary existence.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  23. She Who Remains

    Izidora Angel

    Immerse yourself in the haunting world of She Who Remains, a landmark Bulgarian novel shortlisted for the 2026 International Booker Prize. Through a poetic, stream-of-consciousness narrative, Izidora Angel's masterful translation reveals the devastating consequences when Bekija, desperate for freedom, chooses to become a "sworn virgin" in a rural Albanian village still shackled by centuries-old patriarchal laws. This powerful exploration of gender, trauma, and identity will resonate long after the final page.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  24. The Covenant of Water

    Abraham Verghese

    In this sweeping epic, Abraham Verghese masterfully weaves together three generations of an Indian family grappling with a mysterious curse: every generation, at least one member meets a tragic fate by drowning against the backdrop of Kerala's lush coastal landscape. Spanning 1900 to 1977, the novel follows the unforgettable matriarch Big Ammachi as she navigates unfathomable change, from patriarchal societies to medical breakthroughs. With humor, profound emotion, and a hymn to human resilience, The Covenant of Water is a stunning literary achievement that will captivate readers with its vivid depiction of a bygone era and the unbreakable bonds of love and faith.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  25. El pacto del agua / The Covenant of Water

    Abraham Verghese

    Immerse yourself in a sweeping family saga set in early 20th century India with Abraham Verghese's The Covenant of Water. This epic tale follows three generations of a Kerala family plagued by a mysterious curse: within each generation, at least one member dies by drowning in a land where water is omnipresent. With lyrical prose, Verghese masterfully weaves together themes of love, faith, and medical advancements, creating a transporting and unputdownable read that has captivated Oprah Winfrey and critics alike. A grand, unforgettable epic that explores the human condition and the struggles of past generations for the benefit of those living now.
