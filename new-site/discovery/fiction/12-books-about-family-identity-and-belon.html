12 Books About Family, Identity, and Belonging

These novels explore complex relationships, self-discovery, and the search for home.

Who these books are for

  • LGBTQ+ readers seeking relatable stories
  • Those grappling with family dynamics or absent parents
  • Anyone exploring identity, culture, or belonging
  • Fans of character-driven literary fiction

Start here

If you only read one book about family and identity, choose 'Good People' by Patmeena Sabit for its nuanced portrayal of complex relationships and the search for belonging.

These 12 novels delve into the multifaceted nature of family and identity, often blurring the lines between them. Through complex characters and poignant narratives, each book explores the search for belonging, the power of relationships, and the ever-evolving definition of 'home'. From absent parents to chosen families, these stories reflect the diversity of modern human experience, inviting readers to contemplate their own journeys of self-discovery.

The collection

  1. The Dutch House A Novel

    Ann Patchett

    In Ann Patchett's mesmerizing novel, the Dutch House serves as a labyrinthine backdrop for the complex, centuries-old bond between siblings Danny and Maeve Conroy, who must navigate the fallout of their family's shocking fortune and the secrets of their Dutch House childhood paradise lost. A dark and poignant tale spanning decades, "The Dutch House" explores themes of inheritance, love, forgiveness, and the unbreakable tie between two siblings forever changed by the house that both destroyed and defined them.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. A Founding Mother A Novel of Abigail Adams

    Stephanie Dray

    Step into the captivating world of Abigail Adams, a trailblazing Founding Mother who stood alongside her husband John and shaped the destiny of America. From the tumultuous streets of Revolutionary Boston to the nation's capital, Abigail's wit, resilience, and unwavering commitment to the cause illuminated her path to independence. A Founding Mother is a sweeping, intimate portrayal of a woman ahead of her time, whose fearless counsel, intellect, and determination continue to resonate powerfully in our own era. Revisit the birth of the United States through Abigail's remarkable story, a testament to the enduring strength of the American spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Twelve Tribes of Hattie Oprah's Book Club 2.0

    Ayana Mathis

    In this sweeping, unflinching family saga, Ayana Mathis masterfully weaves together the lives of Hattie Shepherd's twelve children, born between 1923 and 1969, each navigating the centuries-old legacy of hardship, sacrifice, and resilience passed down from their unyielding matriarch. A stunning tale of the African American experience, The Twelve Tribes of Hattie is a poignant, powerful exploration of motherhood, identity, and the unbreakable bonds of family.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Alan Opts Out

    Courtney Maum

    In this wildly funny and poignant novel, a high-powered ad exec storms out of the biggest pitch of his life, deciding to rebel against consumerism and the pressures of privilege. As he moves to live off the land, his family is forced to reexamine their values, leading to a most unlikely transformation. With its razor-sharp wit, achingly sexy moments, and heartwarming insights, Alan Opts Out is the perfect read for anyone seeking to laugh, cry, and reconsider the American Dream.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Dominion A Novel

    Addie E. Citchens

    Addie E. Citchens' Dominion is an explosive debut novel that masterfully explores themes of privilege, secrets, and the complexities of Southern family dynamics, earning its place in the collection.

    For: Readers drawn to Southern Gothic fiction, family dramas, and stories that grapple with faith and morality will appreciate this novel.

    Strengths: Citchens crafts vivid, multi-dimensional characters and a richly detailed setting that immerses readers in the world of Dominion, Mississippi.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the novel's dense, lyrical prose and complex plot lines challenging to follow.

    Comparable: Comparable to works like Zora Neale Hurston's Their Eyes Were Watching God or Jesmyn Ward's Salvage the Bones in its exploration of Southern identity and family secrets.

    “Fantastic . . . A perfect capsule of real life.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Lost Lambs A Novel

    Madeline Cash

    Lost Lambs is a darkly comedic, inventive novel that deftly balances satire, family drama, and mystery, making it a standout in contemporary literary fiction.

    For: Readers who appreciate offbeat humor, complex characters, and thought-provoking social commentary will enjoy this book.

    Strengths: Cash's unique voice, clever plotting, and vivid characterizations make this debut novel a triumph of style and substance.

    Skip if: The large cast of characters can be challenging to keep track of, and some subplots may feel underdeveloped.

    Comparable: In its blend of humor, family dysfunction, and incisive social commentary, Lost Lambs is reminiscent of works by authors like Lydia Millet, Thomas Pynchon, and Jonathan Franzen.

    “Delightfully cracked . . . A winning mixture of black comedy and innocent sweetness.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. White Teeth A Novel

    Zadie Smith

    In Zadie Smith's vibrant debut novel, White Teeth, London's bustling multicultural landscape serves as the backdrop for a rich tapestry of characters navigating love, family, identity, and the ever-changing fabric of society. Archie Jones and Samad Iqbal, two World War II veterans, find an unlikely bond, while their families - including Archie's second wife Clara and their precocious daughter Irie, and Samad's twins whose paths diverge in unexpected ways - become agents of transformation in this captivating, voice-driven literary masterpiece. With its "raucous energy and confidence" (NYTBR), White Teeth celebrates the beauty in life's messy intersections.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. A House for Mr. Biswas A Novel

    V. S. Naipaul

    V.S. Naipaul's classic novel, A House for Mr. Biswas, is a masterful exploration of the dignified, yet often tragic, quest for individuality in a society mired in tradition and dominance. Follow the unforgettable journey of Mohun Biswas, a man caught in a cycle of dependence and calamity, as he strives for the ultimate symbol of independence: a house of his own. With dark humor and biting social commentary, Naipaul crafts a richly textured, epic tale of one man's enduring battle against the forces that shape his destiny.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Complete Persepolis Volumes 1 and 2

    Marjane Satrapi

    Embark on a captivating journey through the eyes of a young girl growing up amidst Iran's tumultuous Islamic Revolution. Marjane Satrapi's autobiographical masterpiece, The Complete Persepolis, is a deeply personal and universally relatable tale of family, identity, and coming-of-age. With its unique blend of humor, heartache, and historical context, this iconic graphic memoir has become a classic for a new generation of readers, offering a stunning and unforgettable portrait of a nation and its people.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Should the Waters Take Us A Novel

    Stephanie Soileau

    In Stephanie Soileau's stunning debut, Should the Waters Take Us, one family's saga spans four centuries, from 17th-century France to the oil-soaked bayous of Louisiana. This epic tale of love, loss, and moral compromise delves into the intricate dance between individuals and the places they call home. With unforgettable characters and breathtaking scenes, Soileau masterfully weaves together the threads of Acadian history, environmental crises, and community drama, culminating in a powerful exploration of resilience and hope in the face of systemic injustice. A must-read for fans of Jesmyn Ward, Toni Morrison, and anyone captivated by the complex, ever-shifting landscapes of the American South.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Good Dirt A Novel

    Charmaine Wilkerson

    Unravel the mysteries of one family's deep roots and dark secrets in Charmaine Wilkerson's captivating novel, Good Dirt. When tragedy strikes the affluent Freeman clan, the jagged pieces of their past come to a head - propelling daughter Ebby on a journey to uncover the truth behind a treasured family heirloom and a lifetime of unspoken family burdens. With each turn of the page, Wilkerson masterfully weaves a multi-generational saga of love, loss, and legacy in this powerful exploration of identity and the indelible mark of history.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Pineapple Street: A GMA Book Club Pick A Novel

    Jenny Jackson

    Pineapple Street offers a witty, engaging portrayal of the lives and relationships within a wealthy Brooklyn family, providing a clever social commentary on class, love, and identity.

    For: Fans of satirical family dramas, those interested in exploring the complexities of the wealthy elite, and readers seeking a smart, escapist novel.

    Strengths: The book excels at crafting relatable, flawed characters and delivers sharp observations on the nuances of class and privilege, all while maintaining a light, entertaining tone.

    Skip if: While Pineapple Street is a delightful guilty pleasure, some readers may find the pacing a tad slow and the characters' struggles with their own privilege feel a bit too easy for the affluent.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed other satirical family sagas like The Nest or The Royal We may appreciate Pineapple Street's witty take on the lives of the ultra-wealthy.

    “This witty novel about the haves and have-mores is Succession with a soul.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Good People A Novel

    Patmeena Sabit

    A thought-provoking, ingeniously structured debut novel that uses multiple perspectives to explore the complexities of family, identity, and the immigrant experience in America.

    For: Readers interested in literary fiction, family sagas, and stories about the immigrant experience will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Propulsive storytelling, diverse perspectives, and a nuanced exploration of the American Dream make this a standout debut.

    Skip if: The non-linear structure and multiple narrators may make it challenging for some readers to follow the complex narrative.

    Comparable: Fans of novels like The Namesake and The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao will find this book's exploration of identity, culture, and the immigrant experience compelling.

    “Ingeniously structured, thought-provoking, and utterly addictive, Good People will have everyone talking.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. A Guardian and a Thief: Oprah's Book Club A Novel

    Megha Majumdar

    In this suspenseful portrayal of love and desperation, two families navigate the dire circumstances of a near-future Kolkata, where hunger and hopelessness loom large. When Ma's vital documents are stolen, she embarks on an urgent quest to recover them, while the thief Boomba's own struggles to feed his kin push them into a cycle of escalating crime. With unflinching prose and a deep understanding of the human condition, Megha Majumdar crafts a gripping tale of the lengths parents will go to protect their children in the face of calamity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Shuggie Bain A Novel (Booker Prize Winner)

    Douglas Stuart

    "Shuggie Bain is a soul-stirring, deeply moving tale of love, loss, and resilience. Set in 1980s Glasgow, it follows the struggles of Shuggie, a young boy grappling with his mother's addiction and the harsh realities of poverty. This powerful debut, winner of the 2020 Booker Prize, will captivate readers who relish authentic, lyrical prose and stories of the human spirit's enduring strength. Anyone drawn to character-driven fiction, especially those interested in the immigrant experience and the bonds of family, will cherish this unforgettable novel."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. The True True Story of Raja the Gullible (and His Mother) A Novel (National Book Award Winner)

    Rabih Alameddine

    "Laugh, cry, and celebrate with Raja, the endearingly gullible narrator of this National Book Award-winning novel. Rabih Alameddine's poignant and wickedly funny tale spans six decades of Lebanon's history, weaving a modern saga of family, memory, and the unbreakable mother-son bond. Join Raja on his unforgettable journey from Beirut to America as he grapples with mistakes, self-discovery, trauma, and the absurdities of love."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The Great Wherever A Novel

    Shannon Sanders

    In Shannon Sanders' sweeping family saga, The Great Wherever, heartbroken heroine Aubrey Lamb inherits a Tennessee farm rich in ancestral secrets and occupied by the insatiably gossipy ghosts of her past. As Aubrey navigates reconnecting with her living relatives while confronting the farm's burdensome history, she must weigh the hopes and burdens of her forebears with her own uncertain future. With a wry, distinctly modern voice, this expansive tale delves into the complex ways we define ourselves against our families, carry on after loss, and grapple with the unshakeable presence of the past in our lives.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. The Bright Years A Novel

    Sarah Damoff

    This sweeping family saga masterfully weaves together multiple perspectives to create a rich tapestry of love, loss, and self-discovery, making it a standout in contemporary literary fiction.

    For: Fans of character-driven novels with complex family dynamics will deeply connect with this story.

    Strengths: The Bright Years shines in its nuanced portrayal of addiction, the long-lasting effects of secrets and trauma, and the resilience of family bonds across generations.

    Skip if: Some readers may struggle with the slow, introspective pacing and the sheer volume of characters and timelines.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate multi-generational family stories with a strong emotional core, like Those Who Leave and Return (by Moshfegh) or The Burgess Boys (by Strout), will find The Bright Years a compelling, thought-provoking read.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What makes these books about family?
Each novel deeply examines relationships, whether biological, chosen, or spiritual, and how they shape a character's sense of self and belonging.
Can I find books with diverse family structures?
Yes, this collection includes works featuring LGBTQ+, interracial, and non-traditional families, offering a wide range of relatable scenarios.
Are these books heavy or uplifting?
While some tackle difficult subjects, the overall tone is hopeful, with many stories emphasizing resilience, love, and the capacity for growth and change.
