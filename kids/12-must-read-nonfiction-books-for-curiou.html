12 Must-Read Nonfiction Books for Curious Kids

Delve into fascinating topics with this collection of engaging nonfiction books perfect for inquisitive young readers.

Who these books are for

  • Parents seeking engaging educational books
  • Teachers looking for diverse classroom reads
  • Curious kids aged 4-12

Start here

If you only read one book, make it 'Mysterious Things' by Andy J Pizza for a thrilling adventure in exploration.

Fuel a love for learning in the young minds of your family with this carefully selected collection of twelve must-read nonfiction books. Perfect for curious kids aged 4-12, these captivating titles delve into a wide range of subjects, from the wonders of science and history to the power of self-improvement and cultural exploration. Whether you're a parent seeking engaging educational resources or a teacher looking for diverse classroom reads, this curated list has something for everyone, ensuring that young readers stay curious and inspired.

The collection

  1. Get It Done & Have Fun! ADHD Hacks for Awesome Kids

    Penn Holderness

    This engaging picture book empowers young readers with ADHD to tackle daily tasks creatively through 'hacks' and positive encouragement.

    For: Children aged 4-8 with ADHD, their parents, and caregivers seeking practical and fun strategies for household chores.

    Strengths: Playful rhyming text, relatable squirrel protagonist, and humor make household tasks more appealing and achievable.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the ADHD focus and humor appealing to a niche audience only.

    Comparable: Compared to other ADHD-themed books, Get It Done & Have Fun! stands out with its focus on practical 'hacks' and humor.

    “An empathetic...guide that may help kids (and caretakers) with ADHD try to find new ways to get through daily chores.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Murdle Jr.: Summer Fun! 40+ Mazes, Codes, and Other Puzzling Games

    G T Karber

    Engaging puzzles and mysteries make this an ideal, screen-free activity for kids on-the-go.

    For: Recommended for children aged 7-12 and families seeking entertaining, logic-building activities.

    Strengths: Offers a variety of kid-tested puzzles, including mazes, word searches, codes, and mysteries that increase in difficulty, providing hours of immersive fun.

    Skip if: Limited narrative depth in the mystery story, focusing primarily on the puzzles.

    Comparable: Fans of interactive puzzle books like 'Where's Waldo?' and 'The Magic Tree House' will appreciate the comparable blend of adventure and brain teasers in 'Murdle Jr.: Summer Fun!'

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Mummies and Pyramids A Nonfiction Companion to Magic Tree House #3: Mummies in the Morning

    Mary Pope Osborne

    Unravel the secrets of ancient Egypt with Jack and Annie! This nonfiction companion to their magical adventure "Mummies in the Morning" delves into the facts behind the fiction, covering crucial questions about mummy-making, hieroglyphics, daily life, and the incredible pyramids. With up-to-date photos, illustrations, and fun anecdotes, this engaging Fact Tracker is the perfect way for curious kids to expand their knowledge of this fascinating civilization.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Hair Love

    Matthew A. Cherry

    In this heartwarming, New York Times bestselling tale, a loving Black father steps up to style his daughter's joyful, kinkily curly hair for a special occasion, teaching a powerful lesson about self-confidence and the unbreakable bond between dads and daughters. With its uplifting message and vibrant illustrations, Hair Love is a must-read celebration of natural beauty and the love between parents and children.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Range (Adapted for Young Readers) How Exploring Your Interests Can Change the World

    David Epstein

    Discover the inspiring stories of athletes, artists, and innovators who defied traditional advice to "follow your one passion" and instead pursued a wide range of interests from a young age. In this adapted edition for young readers, Range reveals that cultivating many skills, rather than specializing early, often leads to exceptional achievements in life. With fun tales and real-world examples, this social sciences book shows you how to tap into your unique potential by exploring multiple paths, making it perfect for anyone seeking to expand their horizon and unlock their limitless possibilities.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Rock Paper Scissors Cheese

    W. Kamau Bell

    Get ready for a hilariously unpredictable twist on a classic game! In Rock Paper Scissors Cheese, stand-up comedian W. Kamau Bell and bestselling author Adam Mansbach inject a soggy rain day with infectious fun. From paper's surprising kryptonite to cheese, this zany rhyming romp with the Bell family will have you and your loved ones in stitches. With Breanna Chambers' delightful illustrations, this read-aloud treat celebrates the joy of embracing chaos and finding the best in any situation, especially when shared with family.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Danny Go's Volcano Adventure A Picture Book

    Danny Go

    Get ready for an explosive adventure! With interactive elements like tracing and tapping, this thrilling picture book from YouTube sensation Danny Go puts young explorers in the driver's seat as they navigate a volcano with the beloved creator. Packed with cool science facts, fun challenges, and heartwarming moments, this action-packed tale of treasure hunting is perfect for curious kids who love the natural world and spending time with friends. Join the fun and see where the journey takes you!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Mac & Cheese: The Origin Story (Delicious Duos Series)

    Michael L. Benner

    Embark on a humorous and heartwarming quest with Mac & Cheese in this debut tale of the Delicious Duos series! Follow these endearing friends as they journey across Pastaland, Candy Land, Dessert, and Cheddar Mountain to find true companionship. Packed with delightful illustrations and a story that balances laugh-out-loud moments with poignant charm, Mac & Cheese: The Origin Story is a delightful read-aloud perfect for little ones, guaranteed to bring smiles and giggles.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. All the Ice Cream in the Land

    Emmy Kastner

    In this delightful, laugh-out-loud tale, a curious princess finds herself tangled in a whimsical web of events when she desires just one lick of ice cream. Emmy Kastner's cumulative story builds hilariously, introducing a cast of characters more than eager to please, a kingdom overflowing with cold treats, and a princess struggling to keep up. Fans of If You Give a Mouse a Cookie will delight in this zany, unexpected adventure full of cows, surprises, and summer treats galore.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Tiny T. Rex Tries Soccer A Tiny T. Rex Book--Includes Stickers Inside!

    Jonathan Stutzman

    This charming, humor-filled story encourages kids to try new things, featuring Tiny T. Rex's soccer adventures with a helpful message about perseverance.

    For: Preschoolers, toddlers, and young soccer fans will enjoy this engaging picture book with its colorful illustrations and removable stickers.

    Strengths: The story's playful tone and relatable protagonist make it appealing and accessible, while the inclusion of stickers adds an interactive element.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the text's simplicity a bit too straightforward for more advanced early readers.

    Comparable: Fans of Tiny T. Rex will appreciate this lighthearted continuation of the series, similar in style and humor to other books in the collection.

    “Good for kids who need a nudge about perseverance. Extra good for budding soccer players.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Mysterious Things

    Andy J Pizza

    Mysterious Things offers an engaging, STEM-friendly exploration of unseen wonders, fostering curiosity and a growth mindset in young readers.

    For: Ideal for curious children aged 8-12 and educators seeking to spark their students' scientific inquiry.

    Strengths: Vibrant visuals, interactive elements, and accessible explanations make complex mysteries approachable and inspiring for young minds.

    Skip if: Some topics may delve too deeply for younger or less attentive readers, requiring parental guidance or repeated readings.

    Comparable: Fans of Invisible Things and other curiosity-driven science books like The Magic School Bus or National Geographic Kids will appreciate Mysterious Things' mix of fun and learning.

    “With short, intriguing pieces of text and fantastic artwork... this is great for sparking the imagination of readers to find out more about mysteries in this world.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Amazing Generation Your Guide to Fun and Freedom in a Screen-Filled World

    Jonathan Haidt

    Ditch the screen and discover your amazing self! In this empowering guide, learn the surprising secrets tech leaders keep from kids, and hear real-life stories from young adults who regret getting smartphones early. Packed with fun challenges, a graphic novel, and practical advice, The Amazing Generation shows you how to grow into your most confident, adventurous self - not just what NOT to do. Stand up for your happiness and choose a life of freedom, fun, and fulfillment, without letting screens hijack your journey.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Pizza and Taco: Scariest Sleepover Ever! (A Graphic Novel)

    Stephen Shaskan

    Join Pizza and Taco for their spookiest sleepover yet in this hilarious, chapter-book graphic novel! As they giggle over ghost stories with friends and wait to catch some Z's, these foodie besties will charm readers with their playful banter and adventures. Perfect for early readers transitioning to longer chapter books, this shivery tale will tickle funny bones and leave youngsters eager for more of Pizza and Taco's escapades.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. The Unordinary Book of Ideas Everywhen Projects for Rainy Days

    Half-Asleep Chris

    The Unordinary Book of Ideas stands out for its playful yet thought-provoking projects that encourage creative experimentation and imaginative thinking.

    For: Curious readers of all ages who enjoy hands-on creative pursuits and want to explore unconventional ideas.

    Strengths: Offers a diverse range of inventive and often humorous projects that can be adapted to various skill levels and interests, fostering a sense of discovery and wonder.

    Skip if: Some projects may require specialized materials or tools, potentially limiting accessibility for readers with limited resources.

    Comparable: Fans of books that inspire maker culture and creative play, such as 'The Art of Everyday Architecture' or 'The Book of Totally Useless Information', may appreciate this unique collection.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Detective Beans: And the Map of Mystery Volume 3

    Li Chen

    Join intrepid feline detective Beans on his most thrilling adventure yet! When a mysterious treasure map falls into his paws, Beans pieces together a puzzle that leads him and his trusty sidekick Biscuits through amusement parks, secret tunnels, and pirate ships. With his quick mind and brave spirit, Beans outsmarts danger at every turn to uncover a surprising twist that will leave readers purr-fectly delighted. A charming and clever addition to this acclaimed, award-winning series, perfect for mystery lovers and cat enthusiasts alike!

    Buy on Bookshop.org
