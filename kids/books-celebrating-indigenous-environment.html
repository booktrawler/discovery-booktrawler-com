Books Celebrating Indigenous Environmental Activism

These nonfiction books empower readers to take action, inspiring a new generation of environmental warriors.

Who these books are for

  • Teens and adults seeking environmental inspiration and activism
  • Readers interested in Native American and indigenous voices
  • Educators looking for diverse teaching resources on environmentalism

Start here

If you only read one book on indigenous environmental activism, choose 'Autumn Peltier, Water Warrior' for its accessible, inspiring story of a young Anishinaabe water advocate.

These powerful nonfiction books highlight the crucial role of indigenous voices in the global environmental movement. By sharing the stories of remarkable individuals, from young water warriors to pioneering musicians, these books inspire readers to take action and recognize the interconnectedness of human and natural worlds.

The collection

  1. The 1619 Project: Born on the Water

    Nikole Hannah-Jones

    This powerful picture book brings to life the 1619 Project's groundbreaking exploration of American history through poignant verse and stunning illustrations, offering a compelling narrative for young readers.

    For: Ideal for middle grade students and their families seeking a nuanced introduction to the country's complex racial past.

    Strengths: Vivid, evocative storytelling conveys the trauma of slavery and the resilience of Black people, while the lyrical poetry and expressive artwork create an emotionally impactful experience.

    Skip if: The dense, layered text may require close reading and discussion to fully absorb for younger or less literate readers.

    Comparable: Similar in its historical scope and poetic approach is 'The Underground Abductor' by Deborah Hopkinson, another powerful exploration of slavery and freedom in American history.

    “A gift to Black Americans and everyone else who reads it.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. We Are Water Protectors (Caldecott Medal Winner)

    Carole Lindstrom

    This powerful, award-winning picture book is a timely and vital addition to the collection, educating and inspiring young readers about Indigenous-led environmental movements and the importance of protecting our planet's water.

    For: Ideal for ages 4-8, this book is a must-read for parents, educators, and librarians seeking to foster empathy, activism, and ecological awareness in the next generation.

    Strengths: Lindstrom's poetic text and Goade's stunning watercolor illustrations seamlessly combine to create an immersive, emotive experience that both informs and motivates readers to take action.

    Skip if: Some scenes may be too intense for very young children due to the depiction of pollution and environmental conflict.

    “Observation is not enough, the book communicates: action is necessary... A passionate call for environmental stewardship.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Autumn Peltier, Water Warrior

    Carole Lindstrom

    This powerful picture book biography celebrates the crucial work of Indigenous water protectors like Autumn Peltier and her great-aunt Josephine Mandamin, offering a moving tribute to Native women's leadership in environmental activism.

    For: School-age children and adults seeking to amplify Indigenous voices and learn about water rights and environmental advocacy.

    Strengths: Lindstrom's vivid storytelling and Autumn's inspiring personal story make this a compelling call to action, while George's rich illustrations beautifully capture the Anishinaabe connection to nature.

    Skip if: The text can be dense at times, requiring attentive reading.

    Comparable: Similar in theme and style to We Are Water Protectors, this companion title extends the conversation about Indigenous water protectors and their importance in the fight for environmental justice.

    “A brilliant combination of story, culture, and activism in the cause of protecting the environment. Highly recommended for all collections.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Color Me Country: A Celebration of Black Women Who Shaped Country Music Kelly McCartney (Edited by), Rissi Palmer (Edited by), Rhiannon Giddens (Illustrated by)

    Kelly McCartney

    Color Me Country shines a long-overdue spotlight on the trailblazing Black women who shaped country and roots music, making a vital addition to any collection on music history or diversity.

    For: This book is perfect for young readers, especially Black and brown girls, as well as music enthusiasts and educators looking to broaden their understanding of country music's rich cultural heritage.

    Strengths: The book's vibrant, full-color portraits and engaging biographies bring these pioneering artists to life, while its comprehensive scope and accessible tone make it an excellent resource for both casual readers and academic researchers.

    Skip if: The text is dense with information, so readers may need to digest it in smaller chunks rather than devouring it in one sitting.

    Comparable: In terms of its focus on underrepresented artists and its blend of biography and illustration, Color Me Country is reminiscent of other books like Who Is Latasha Harlins? or She Persisted in Music.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What makes these books stand out?
They center indigenous perspectives, experiences, and activism, providing a much-needed counterbalance to the often Eurocentric environmental narrative.
Can these books work for classroom discussions?
Absolutely, offering rich opportunities for exploring diversity, social justice, and community-based approaches to environmental issues.
Will these titles be of interest to non-environmentalists?
Yes, the compelling personal stories and broader themes of resilience, activism, and social change will resonate with a wide audience.
