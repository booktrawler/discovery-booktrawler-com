Books About Spain, Spanish Cuisine and Culture

Discover authentic stories and flavors of Spain with these 9 captivating books.

Who these books are for

  • Foodies and culinary enthusiasts
  • Travelers planning a trip to Spain
  • Culture lovers and history buffs
  • Spanish language learners
  • Anyone curious about the country's traditions and heritage

Start here

If you only read one book about Spain, choose 'Spain My Way' by José Andrés, a personal and gastronomic journey through the country.

Spain, with its rich history, vibrant culture, and delectable cuisine, offers a wealth of fascinating stories to explore. These 9 books take you on a journey through the country's past, present, and flavors, providing unique perspectives and insider knowledge. Whether you're a food enthusiast, a travel buff, or simply curious about the Spanish way of life, this collection has something for everyone.

The collection

  1. Spain My Way Eat, Drink, and Cook Like a Spaniard

    José Andrés

    José Andrés's passion for Spanish cuisine shines through in this engaging, recipe-rich exploration of his homeland's culinary traditions.

    For: Food enthusiasts, Spanish cuisine aficionados, and home cooks interested in learning about and recreating authentic Spanish dishes.

    Strengths: The book showcases a diverse range of Spanish recipes, from classic tapas to seafood paella, while also delving into the cultural context and personal anecdotes that make the cuisine so vibrant.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the inclusion of detailed ingredient lists and cooking techniques makes the recipes more challenging for beginners.

    Comparable: Other books in this collection that focus on international cuisine, such as 'India: The Cookbook' or 'Jerusalem: A Cookbook,' offer similar cultural insights and authentic recipes for exploring global flavors.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The World Central Kitchen Cookbook Feeding Humanity, Feeding Hope

    José Andrés

    This cookbook stands out for its powerful stories of community resilience and delicious, globally-inspired recipes, all in support of a vital humanitarian cause.

    For: Foodies, philanthropists, and anyone seeking inspiration for meals with a purpose.

    Strengths: Captivating narratives from WCK's front lines, diverse and mouthwatering recipes, and stunning photography.

    Skip if: The collection of stories and recipes may feel overwhelming or disjointed at times, with a mix of urgency, hope, and celebrity contributions.

    Comparable: Fans of anthologies like 'Jars of Hope' or 'The Hundred-Year Farm' will appreciate this unique blend of stories and recipes with a humanitarian focus.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. I Sleep in My Kitchen Comfort Food Recipes from My Palestinian American Home: A Cookbook

    Mariam Daud

    This heartwarming cookbook stands out with its authentic blend of Palestinian heritage and modern twists, offering a unique culinary perspective.

    For: Home cooks and food enthusiasts interested in exploring international flavors and recipes.

    Strengths: Mariam Daud's collection showcases her signature ease in the kitchen, with clear instructions and beautiful photography. The variety of dishes, from comforting classics to innovative fusion recipes, caters to diverse tastes and dietary needs.

    Skip if: Some recipes may require specialized ingredients not readily available in all areas, so planning ahead and substitutions might be necessary.

    Comparable: For readers who enjoy celebrating cultural cuisine through food, this cookbook is reminiscent of other international cookbooks like 'The Food of Morocco' or 'Jerusalem: A Cookbook'.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. New School Barbecue Recipes for Next-Level Smoking and Grilling from Austin's Leroy and Lewis

    Evan Leroy

    Evan Leroy, pitmaster of Austin's acclaimed LeRoy and Lewis, shares his revolutionary approach to modern barbecue in New School Barbecue. With nearly 100 inventive recipes and techniques, Leroy demystifies the art of smoking and grilling, guiding home cooks to elevate their skills and explore new flavors. Whether using an offset smoker, pellet grill, or classic kettle, Leroy's methods empower readers to transform any cut of meat, produce, or cuisine into a mouthwatering masterpiece. This game-changing cookbook is a must-have for anyone seeking to take their BBQ skills to the next level.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. American Soul The Black History of Food in the United States - Including 40 Recipes

    Anela Malik

    "Embark on a mouthwatering journey through the untold history of Black contributions to American cuisine with Anela Malik's American Soul. Drawing from over 30 chef and culinary leader testimonials, historically-rooted recipes, and vibrant photographs, this book chronicles the profound impact of the Black diaspora on the evolution of iconic American food traditions. Dive into the stories behind soul food, barbecue, and more, and discover the hidden roots that shaped the country's culinary identity."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. New York's Family Grocer The Story of d'Agostino Supermarkets

    Paul Schmitz

    Step into the storied aisles of New York's Family Grocer, the captivating chronicle of d'Agostino Supermarkets from humble beginnings to beloved Big Apple staple. With vivid accounts, archival treasures, and insider perspectives, author Paul Schmitz reveals the heart and hustle behind the brand, from pushcarts to Manhattan-wide empires, and the timeless values that sustained this family business through generations and urban upheaval. A must-read for anyone who's ever savored the magic of a neighborhood grocery store and the people who make it home.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Now We're Cooking All-Purpose, Laid-Back Recipes (A Cookbook from @ACookNamedMatt)

    Matthew Broussard

    Now We're Cooking delivers a collection of bold, flavorful, and surprisingly easy recipes perfect for home cooks of all levels.

    For: Home cooks seeking weeknight-friendly recipes with a kick will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Matt Broussard's enthusiasm for cooking shines through in his approachable, technique-focused recipes and engaging writing style.

    Skip if: Some recipes may require a bit of planning ahead, as they involve prep work and multiple components.

    Comparable: Fans of The Food Lab and other modern cookbooks emphasizing flavor and technique will find Now We're Cooking similarly appealing.

  8. Bake Sum Asian American Pastries, Breads, and Other Treats

    Joyce Tang

    Bake Sum's stunning photography and creative Asian-inspired recipes make it a standout addition to any cookbook collection, offering both beginners and experienced bakers a wealth of new flavors and techniques to explore.

    For: Bake Sum is perfect for adventurous home cooks and bakers interested in Asian flavors and desserts, as well as anyone looking to learn about the cultural migration of foods through recipes and essays.

    Strengths: This book excels at presenting a diverse range of authentic Asian desserts and baked goods in approachable recipes, with beautiful photography that makes each dish look irresistible.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the organization by difficulty level a bit confusing, as the complexity of recipes can vary within sections.

    Comparable: For those who enjoy Bake Sum's blend of traditional and modern Asian desserts, other notable titles in the collection might include 'The Food of China' and 'Japanese Soul Cooking'.
