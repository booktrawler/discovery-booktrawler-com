Books to Understand and Connect with Others

These 20 thought-provoking nonfiction books explore how to know, understand, and relate to people, animals, and ourselves.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone seeking to improve interpersonal communication and empathy
  • Students of psychology, sociology, and animal behavior
  • Professionals in healthcare, education, and social work

Start here

If you only read one book about human connection, choose 'How to Know a Person' by David Brooks.

Building strong relationships with others, whether human or animal, is a universal quest. These 20 books offer a multidisciplinary exploration of the complex dynamics at play, from the depths of the human psyche to the cognitive abilities of our furry friends. From renowned psychologists to acclaimed authors, each title provides a unique perspective on understanding, connecting, and growing alongside others.

The collection

  1. Ways of Seeing Based on the BBC Television Series

    John Berger

    Experience a radical perspective on art with Ways of Seeing by renowned author John Berger. This seminal work, based on his groundbreaking BBC series, invites readers to rethink their very perception of paintings, challenging the viewer-reader's relationship and illuminating the intricate connections between art and society. Berger's thought-provoking insights will forever alter your gaze upon the visual world.

  2. It's Good to Be Lost Once in a While

    Professor Emeritus Robert J Wicks

    This book offers profound insights and practical guidance for embracing life's transitions, uncertainty, and growth, making it a valuable resource for anyone seeking a more meaningful existence.

    For: Individuals navigating life changes, seeking personal growth, or simply yearning for a deeper sense of purpose and fulfillment.

    Strengths: Wicks' thought-provoking reflections and real-world examples help readers unlearn harmful patterns, cultivate mindfulness, and discover their unique gifts. The book's themes of resilience, self-care, and post-traumatic growth are particularly noteworthy.

    Skip if: Some may find the philosophical and introspective nature of the writing dense and slow-paced, requiring patience and a willingness to engage deeply with the material.

    Comparable: For readers drawn to self-help and personal development, this book shares similarities with works by Brené Brown, Dan McGinn, and Viktor Frankl in its emphasis on resilience, mindfulness, and finding meaning in adversity.

  3. The Life You Want

    Adam Phillips

    In "The Life You Want," renowned psychoanalyst Adam Phillips probes the elusive quest for genuine life satisfaction, where our ideals often disappoint. With his signature insight and literary flair, he illuminates the complex interplay between personal desires and societal pressures, revealing the ambivalence we face in striving for joy in a culture that both extols and obstructs it. A profound and thought-provoking exploration of what it truly means to "want" a fulfilling life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Dare to Lead Brave Work. Tough Conversations. Whole Hearts.

    Brené Brown

    Brené Brown's groundbreaking research on vulnerability, empathy, and courage provides actionable strategies for leaders to develop these essential human traits.

    For: Leaders, managers, and anyone seeking to cultivate braver, more compassionate leadership.

    Strengths: Practical, research-driven advice; accessible to a wide audience; Brown's engaging storytelling and relatable examples make complex concepts understandable.

    Skip if: Theoretical chapters can be dense; some readers may find the writing style or anecdotes less compelling than others in Brown's oeuvre.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate Brown's previous works, such as 'Daring Greatly' or 'Rising Strong', will find 'Dare to Lead' a natural progression of her ideas on courage and leadership.

    “Applying the principles from Dare to Lead to my work as a principal has transformed the way I show up with parents, students, and colleagues, and how I lead. Brené”

  5. Atlas of the Heart Mapping Meaningful Connection and the Language of Human Experience

    Brené Brown

    Brené Brown's groundbreaking research on human emotions and experiences makes this a must-read for anyone seeking to better understand themselves and others.

    For: Anyone interested in personal growth, emotional intelligence, and building meaningful connections with others.

    Strengths: Provides a comprehensive, research-based framework for understanding and navigating the full spectrum of human emotions, with actionable tools for fostering empathy and connection.

    Comparable: Fans of Brown's previous work, such as 'Daring Greatly' and 'Rising Strong,' will find this book a natural next step in their personal and professional growth journeys.

  6. How to Know a Person The Art of Seeing Others Deeply and Being Deeply Seen

    David Brooks

    A comprehensive guide to fostering deeper, more meaningful human connections, offering practical advice and insights drawn from various fields.

    For: Anyone seeking to improve their interpersonal skills, deepen relationships, and cultivate a more empathetic society.

    Strengths: Provides an integrated approach to understanding others, with engaging examples from psychology, neuroscience, and other disciplines.

    Skip if: May require patience for readers looking for quick, easy-to-digest advice, as Brooks' style is thoughtful and often dense.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate David Brooks' previous works or those interested in human connection and interpersonal dynamics may also enjoy other self-help and social psychology titles.

Richly illustrated and filled with exuberant observations, The Dog's Gaze is a captivating journey that will resonate with both art lovers and dog enthusiasts alike, offering a fresh perspective on our shared humanity through the eyes of our loyal friends.
