Best Books for Understanding and Managing ADHD

These 16 books offer expert insights and practical strategies for living with ADHD, parenting children with ADHD, and achieving personal growth.

Who these books are for

  • Adults with ADHD
  • Parents of children with ADHD
  • Mental health professionals
  • Educators and caregivers
  • Individuals seeking personal growth and self-improvement

Start here

If you only read one book about ADHD, choose 'ADHD Is Awesome' by Penn Holderness for its engaging, relatable perspective.

Understanding ADHD and finding effective management strategies can greatly improve daily life. This collection of 16 essential nonfiction books offers a wealth of expertise, personal insights, and practical guidance for adults with ADHD, parents of children with ADHD, mental health professionals, educators, and anyone seeking to better understand and cope with ADHD.

By embracing this paradigm shift, families can break free from fruitless punishment and reward systems to foster more resilient, capable children.

Frequently asked questions

What are the best books on ADHD for adults?
Try 'Taking Charge of Adult ADHD' by Russell Barkley or 'ADHD 2.0' by Edward Hallowell.
How can I help my child with ADHD?
Start with 'The Explosive Child' by Ross Greene for evidence-based strategies and 'You Are the Parent' by Dr. Siggie Cohen for a mindful approach.
Can books really help with managing ADHD symptoms?
Absolutely! These books offer expert guidance, practical tools, and personal stories to empower individuals and families navigating ADHD.
