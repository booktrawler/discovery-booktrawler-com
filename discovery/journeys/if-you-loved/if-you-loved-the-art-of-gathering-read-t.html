If You Loved The Art of Gathering, Read These 7 Books

If you appreciated Priya Parker's thought-provoking exploration of gathering and connection, delve into these seven books for more insights on community-building, noticing the world around you, and sparking meaningful conversations.

Who these books are for

  • Readers who enjoyed The Art of Gathering
  • Anyone looking for books on community-building and connection
  • Fans of thought-provoking nonfiction

Start here

If you only read one, choose The Art of Noticing by Rob Walker - it shares similarities with The Art of Gathering in its focus on the power of observation and the art of living deliberately.

The Art of Gathering by Priya Parker is a compelling exploration of the art of connection and community-building in our modern world. If you found yourself nodding along to her insights and wanting more, you're in luck! This curated list of seven books offers thought-provoking companions on your journey to cultivate deeper relationships and a more deliberate, fulfilling life.

The collection

  1. The Art of Noticing 131 Ways to Spark Creativity, Find Inspiration, and Discover Joy in the Everyday

    Rob Walker

    Reclaim your curiosity and creativity with Rob Walker's inspiring guide, The Art of Noticing. Through 131 engaging, low-stakes exercises, discover how to tune out distractions and tune in to the world around you, sparking clearer thinking, deeper listening, and a renewed sense of what truly matters. A thought-provoking, beautifully illustrated gift for anyone seeking to experience life with fresh eyes.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Art of Fighting The Transformative Power of Conflict

    Priya Parker

    In "The Art of Fighting", Priya Parker powerfully argues that constructive conflict is essential for building strong communities, whether in personal or professional spheres. Drawing on her expertise as a facilitator, research, and real-world examples, Parker guides readers on identifying, addressing, and harnessing the transformative power of disagreement to foster growth and success. A must-read for anyone seeking to navigate and leverage healthy conflict in all aspects of life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Gift of Not Belonging How Outsiders Thrive in a World of Joiners

    Rami Kaminski

    In "The Gift of Not Belonging", Rami Kaminski reveals that embracing your status as an outsider can be a powerful catalyst for self-discovery and success. By shedding the pressure to conform, you unlock fresh perspectives and opportunities that often elude those consumed by a need to belong. This thought-provoking guide shows you how to harness the unique strengths of your nonconformity to thrive in a world that often favors "joiners".

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Haunting of Hill House

    Shirley Jackson

    Step into Hill House, a notorious mansion where the line between reality and the supernatural blurs. This chilling classic, the inspiration for Netflix's hit series, follows four seekers as they uncover the dark secrets and malevolent forces lurking within the eerie walls. Shirley Jackson's masterful tale of horror and psychological unease has been thrilling readers for decades. Are you brave enough to enter the haunting world of Hill House?

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Art of Saving Democracy An Action Kit for Making Change

    People for the American Way

    Engage in the art of democracy with this empowering kit from renowned advocacy group People for the American Way. Featuring works from 20 visionary artists and practical guides on activism, advocacy, and argumentation, it equips you to effect change, promote justice, and defend freedom through creative expression. By pen, postcard, and protest sign, you'll learn to amplify your voice and inspire others to join the mission. All proceeds support People for the American Way's enduring fight against bigotry and for creative liberties.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. You Can Change the World

    Brad Meltzer

    Inspirational quotes and vibrant artwork from the acclaimed Ordinary People Change the World series come together in this uplifting graduation gift, featuring the courageous wisdom of trailblazers like Amelia Earhart, Albert Einstein, and Martin Luther King Jr. Perfect for heroes-in-training, it's a fun, friendly book to cherish and share.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Hunt, Gather, Parent What Ancient Cultures Can Teach Us About the Lost Art of Raising Happy, Helpful Little Humans

    Michaeleen Doucleff

    Discover the revolutionary parenting approach from the world's oldest cultures and raise happy, helpful children without the yelling, nagging or punishments. Michaeleen Doucleff's Hunt, Gather, Parent reveals the cooperative, trust-based relationship-building that yields well-adjusted kids in Maya, Inuit and Hadzabe communities. With practical strategies for discipline, emotional intelligence and stress reduction, you can apply these timeless techniques to modern American families and transform your parenting for the better. More than 1 million copies sold worldwide!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Having People Over A Modern Guide to Planning, Throwing, and Attending Every Type of Party

    Chelsea Fagan

    Host like a pro with this modern guide, packed with practical tips and stylish inspiration for planning and enjoying every type of gathering. From cocktail hours to dinner parties, adult sleepovers to being a great guest, author Chelsea Fagan shares her expertise on curating spaces, menus, and moods for unforgettable connections. With a dash of history, humor, and gorgeous visuals, Having People Over will make you the perfect host and foster deeper communities in your life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. J.R.R. Tolkien 4-Book Boxed Set: The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings The Hobbit, The Fellowship of the Ring, The Two Towers, The Return of the King

    J.R.R. Tolkien

    Step into Middle-earth with this magnificent 4-volume set, featuring J.R.R. Tolkien's beloved classic, The Hobbit, and the epic high fantasy trilogy, The Lord of the Rings. Join Bilbo Baggins on a thrilling adventure as he discovers hidden depths of courage and wit, and follow the perilous quest of Frodo and his Fellowship as they battle to save their world from the dark lord Sauron's grasp. Immerse yourself in Tolkien's richly detailed world of myth and legend, where orcs, elves, dwarves, and hobbits collide in a timeless battle between good and darkness. This complete boxed set is a must-have for any fantasy fan.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. We Are Gathered Here Today

    Bobby Finger

    Join Fin, a gay man reevaluating marriage, at his riotous cousin's Wild West-themed Texas wedding where decades-old wedding pact traditions are put to the test. As Fin grapples with his own engagement doubts alongside a rowdy group of old friends and unexpected guests, hilarious confessions and poignant revelations unfold, ultimately guiding him to a deeply personal truth. A witty, heartfelt exploration of love, commitment, and the meaning of marriage.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What kind of books should I read after The Art of Gathering?
Look for titles that explore community, connection, and the art of living well, often through a lens of observation, conversation, and personal growth.
Are these books similar in style or tone to The Art of Gathering?
While each book has its own unique voice, they all share a thoughtful, engaging, and often unconventional approach to exploring the human experience.
Can I find fiction recommendations like The Art of Gathering?
While this list focuses on nonfiction, if you're seeking fiction with similar themes, consider classic works like The Lord of the Rings or contemporary novels that explore relationships and community, such as We Are Gathered Here Today.
