If You Loved The Autistic Burnout Workbook, Read These 5 Books

For a deeper dive into autistic burnout and self-care, consider these empowering resources

Who these books are for

  • People who enjoyed The Autistic Burnout Workbook and want to explore similar topics
  • Individuals seeking practical self-care advice for autistic burnout
  • Parents, caregivers, and educators looking for resources on neurodivergence

Start here

If you only read one, choose End Emotional Outsourcing for a comprehensive guide to burnout prevention and recovery.

The Autistic Burnout Workbook offers a valuable toolkit for navigating the complex emotional landscape of autistic burnout. If you found its practical advice and empathetic support invaluable, you may be eager to explore more books that illuminate the experiences of neurodivergent individuals and provide guidance for cultivating resilience and wellbeing. The following five titles offer a diverse yet cohesive set of perspectives and strategies to help you further understand and support yourself or loved ones on this journey.

The collection

  1. End Emotional Outsourcing How to Overcome Your Codependent, Perfectionist, and People-Pleasing Habits

    Beatriz Victoria Albina NP MPH Sep

    In End Emotional Outsourcing, certified somatic life coach Beatriz Victoria Albina provides a transformative roadmap for overcoming codependent, perfectionist, and people-pleasing habits that drain your self-worth and relationships. With science-backed guidance, Albina helps you regulate your nervous system, reframe limiting thoughts, and reclaim your time, energy, and power. Perfect for anyone struggling to set healthy boundaries and develop self-love, this groundbreaking guide is an essential tool for stepping into your truth and living authentically.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Neurodivergence Skills Workbook for Autism and ADHD Cultivate Self-Compassion, Live Authentically, and Be Your Own Advocate

    Jennifer Kemp Mpsych

    Embark on a journey of self-acceptance and empowerment with this compassionate, neuro-affirming workbook. Tailored to the needs of Autistic, ADHD, and AuDHD individuals, it offers practical skills in self-compassion, environmental adjustments, social connection, and more to help you thrive as your authentic self. By acknowledging and honoring your unique neurodivergent identity, you'll learn to overcome self-doubt, become your own effective advocate, and build meaningful relationships. This workbook is your key to unlocking pride, reducing stress and burnout, and living your best life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. ADHD Is Awesome A Guide to (Mostly) Thriving with ADHD

    Penn Holderness

    "Ditch the negative narratives - ADHD is not a curse but a unique brain with incredible upsides! In this witty, empowering guide, Penn Holderness shares his own journey living with ADHD and provides tools, humor, and positivity to help you thrive, not just survive. Let this instant New York Times bestseller be your reboot on how to embrace and celebrate your awesomely different mind."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Self-Care for Autistic People 100+ Ways to Recharge, De-Stress, and Unmask!

    Dr. Megan Anna Neff

    "Discover a collection of over 100 self-care exercises specifically designed for autistic individuals, celebrating neurodiversity and helping you prioritize wellness without pretending to be neurotypical. This expert guide from therapist Megan A. Neff provides a wealth of activities to accept yourself, find your community, manage challenging aspects of autism, and bring out the positive aspects of your identity. A must-read for autistic individuals seeking to make the most of their life and diagnosis."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Burnout The Secret to Unlocking the Stress Cycle

    Emily Nagoski PhD

    Unlock the secret to breaking free from the cycle of burnout and reclaiming joy with this groundbreaking self-help guide. "Burnout" by Emily Nagoski PhD shines a light on the unique stressors faced by women in modern society, offering a roadmap to managing emotions, minimizing stress, and embracing self-love. Packed with accessible science, practical advice, and empowering exercises, this NYT Bestseller is a must-read for any woman seeking to transform her relationship with herself and the world around her.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Wired to Feel Autism as a Condition of Sensory Surplus

    Sarah Bergenfield Ma

    "Embark on a profound journey of self-acceptance and empowerment in Wired to Feel. Autistic psychologist Sarah Bergenfield offers a revolutionary perspective on autism as a condition of sensory surplus, guided by powerful insights from neuroscience and internal family systems therapy. This groundbreaking guide helps readers understand their embodied experiences, regulate overwhelm, and cultivate resilience - ultimately reframing autism as perceptual diversity rather than deficit. A must-read for anyone seeking a deeper, kinder understanding of neurodiversity from the inside out."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Do the Work! An Antiracist Activity Book

    W Kamau Bell

    "Ready to make a meaningful impact? DO THE WORK! reveals the insidious nature of systemic racism through engaging activities, games, and thought-provoking exercises. With everything from interactive puzzles to challenging conversation scripts, this innovative workbook equips you with the tools to critically examine and dismantle racism. Dive in and discover hundreds of ideas to amplify your antiracist actions."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Gift of Not Belonging How Outsiders Thrive in a World of Joiners

    Rami Kaminski

    In "The Gift of Not Belonging", Rami Kaminski reveals that embracing your status as an outsider can be a powerful catalyst for self-discovery and success. By shedding the pressure to conform, you unlock fresh perspectives and opportunities that often elude those consumed by a need to belong. This thought-provoking guide shows you how to harness the unique strengths of your nonconformity to thrive in a world that often favors "joiners".

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. This Book Is Anti-Racist 20 Lessons on How to Wake Up, Take Action, and Do the Work

    Tiffany Jewell

    In this timely and powerful anti-racism guide, Tiffany Jewell offers 20 lessons to help readers awaken to racial injustices, understand their own biases, and take action for change. Through vivid storytelling, thought-provoking exercises, and inspiring examples of resistance, Jewell empowers individuals to unlearn racism and work towards a more equitable society. A must-read for anyone seeking to develop a deeper understanding of racial oppression and themselves, This Book Is Anti-Racist provides a comprehensive, accessible roadmap for personal growth and social change.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. You Can Change the World

    Brad Meltzer

    Inspirational quotes and vibrant artwork from the acclaimed Ordinary People Change the World series come together in this uplifting graduation gift, featuring the courageous wisdom of trailblazers like Amelia Earhart, Albert Einstein, and Martin Luther King Jr. Perfect for heroes-in-training, it's a fun, friendly book to cherish and share.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What topics do these recommended books cover?
These books delve into autistic burnout, self-care strategies, neurodivergence skills, ADHD, and related mental health topics.
Are these books suitable for all ages?
While aimed at adults, some titles may be applicable to older teenagers and young adults with guidance from parents or caregivers.
Do the recommended books focus solely on autistic burnout?
While The Autistic Burnout Workbook is a primary focus, these recommendations also cover broader topics like ADHD, neurodivergence, and general self-care for mental wellness.
