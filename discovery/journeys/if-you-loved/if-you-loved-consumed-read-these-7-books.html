If You Loved Consumed, Read These 7 Books

Consumed by Hannu Rajaniemi delves into themes of technology, artificial life, and existentialism, so if you appreciated its unique blend, try these thought-provoking, speculative novels.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of speculative fiction and science fiction
  • Readers who appreciate philosophical themes
  • Viewers of thought-provoking films and TV shows

Start here

If you only read one, choose The Delectable Negro by Vincent Woodard for its haunting exploration of the intersection of technology, identity, and humanity.

Consumed, a novel by Hannu Rajaniemi, offers a rich tapestry of speculative fiction, intertwining philosophical questions about the nature of life, consciousness, and humanity in a world where technology and artificial intelligence blur the lines. If you resonated with its thought-provoking themes and imaginative world-building, you may enjoy these 7 novels that similarly explore the intersection of emerging technologies and existential crises, inviting you to ponder the future of our species and the meaning of existence in an increasingly complex world.

The collection

  1. The Delectable Negro Human Consumption and Homoeroticism Within Us Slave Culture

    Vincent Woodard

    Uncover the shocking, long-suppressed connections between race, sexuality, and consumption in 19th century America through Vincent Woodard's groundbreaking study, The Delectable Negro. By scrutinizing slave narratives, runaway ads, and other rarely-studied materials, Woodard exposes how enslaved men's bodies were fetishized and consumed, both literally and figuratively, by their white owners – and the profound homoeroticism that underpinned this toxic dynamic. Through this meticulously researched work, Woodard sheds new light on the brutal, dehumanizing realities of slavery, and the ongoing struggle for language to articulate the violence and hunger that Black men endured.

  2. American Bulk Essays on Excess

    Emily Mester

    In this incisive and deeply personal collection, Emily Mester adeptly reframes the everyday rituals of consumption as a window into the human experience, peeling back the layers of our relationships with stuff and identity. With humor, vulnerability, and piercing insight, she navigates the contradictions and ironies of modern life, from the thrill of Costco hauls to the relics of family dysfunction in an Iowa hoard. A striking debut that offers a fresh lens on our obsession with abundance and the search for meaning in a consumer culture.

  3. Lost Lambs A Novel

    Madeline Cash

    In Madeline Cash's razor-sharp debut, Lost Lambs, the dysfunctional Flynn family is pulled apart - and unexpectedly together - when their eccentric world collides with a criminal conspiracy threatening their small coastal town. With a pitch-perfect blend of humor, warmth, and style, Cash's wildly inventive novel is a fresh take on family dysfunction for the 21st century.

In this potent and provocative #1 New York Times bestseller, discover how racist ideas have shaped America and how to identify and combat them in the here and now - a crucial step towards building a more equitable future.

    Christian Robinson

Through luminous illustrations and a thoughtful, empathetic perspective, this Caldecott Honoree invites young readers to see the world from diverse viewpoints and understand that every individual is vital and valuable, no matter their size or role. A powerful and eye-opening read perfect for nurturing compassion and global understanding.

  6. Stolen Focus Why You Can't Pay Attention--and How to Think Deeply Again

    Johann Hari

    In today's digitally saturated world, our ability to focus is under siege. Johann Hari's thought-provoking Stolen Focus exposes the relentless external forces stealing our attention, from pollution to corporate profit schemes. Rather than blaming personal failures of willpower, Hari offers a groundbreaking exploration of the 12 root causes of this crisis, highlighting potential solutions from Silicon Valley innovators and an office in New Zealand that dramatically boosted productivity. A Times bestseller, this essential read empowers individuals and society to reclaim their focus and live more deeply engaged lives.

    Chip Heath

By unpacking six key "stickiness" traits and showcasing compelling case studies, Heath provides a masterclass in crafting messages that resonate with audiences. Part gripping storytelling, part practical playbook, this bestselling book will transform the way you communicate and influence others. Pick it up to arm yourself with the knowledge to make your ideas unforgettable.
