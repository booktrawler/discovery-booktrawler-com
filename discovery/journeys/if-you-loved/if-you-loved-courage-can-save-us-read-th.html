If You Loved Courage Can Save Us, Read These 10 Books

For inspiring stories of courageous action, try these books that share the same spirit of hope and determination.

Who these books are for

  • Readers of 'Courage Can Save Us' seeking similar inspiring stories
  • Fans of heroic narratives with a focus on social change
  • Individuals looking for uplifting reads promoting courage and hope

Start here

If you only read one, choose 'Women Heroes of the American Revolution' for its powerful portrayal of unsung heroines.

Rye Barcott's 'Courage Can Save Us' is a testament to the power of individual courage in the face of adversity. If you found the true stories of brave activists and community leaders inspiring, here are ten more books that share a similar spirit of hope and determination. From the American Revolution to contemporary movements, these reads showcase courageous individuals making a difference through activism, leadership, and inspiring actions.

The collection

  1. Courage Can Save Us Ten Extraordinary Americans and the Fight for Our Future

    Rye Barcott

    Meet 9 principled veterans and a former FBI agent who are charting a courageous path in public life, defying political polarization and setting an inspiring example. In "Courage Can Save Us", Rye Barcott shares the stories of these trailblazers as they navigate the moral and personal costs of leadership, exposing the darkness of negativity and outrage in favor of service and the common good. A timely, rousing call to action for a more united America.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Women Heroes of the American Revolution 20 Stories of Espionage, Sabotage, Defiance, and Rescue

    Susan Casey

    "Discover the unsung heroines who shaped the American Revolution, from Sybil Ludington's 40-mile midnight ride to Martha Bratton's daring sabotage. Through vivid profiles drawn from historical archives and interviews, Susan Casey shines a light on 20 exemplary women who risked everything for independence, highlighting their courage, ingenuity, and patriotism in a singular collection that every history enthusiast should read."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. You Can Change the World

    Brad Meltzer

    Inspirational quotes and vibrant artwork from the acclaimed Ordinary People Change the World series come together in this uplifting graduation gift, featuring the courageous wisdom of trailblazers like Amelia Earhart, Albert Einstein, and Martin Luther King Jr. Perfect for heroes-in-training, it's a fun, friendly book to cherish and share.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Democracy or Else How to Save America in 10 Easy Steps

    Jon Favreau

    From the creators of Pod Save America comes this timely, illustrated guide to taking action and making a difference in American politics. With a mix of humor and practical advice, "Democracy or Else" breaks down the complex process into 10 manageable steps for getting informed, donating, volunteering, organizing, and more. Featuring insights from savvy experts and refreshingly down-to-earth politicians, this book is the perfect resource for political newcomers and seasoned activists alike, empowering readers to join the fight to save democracy in the face of unprecedented challenges and contradictions.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. We Are Mighty 12 Ordinary Americans Who Did the Next Needed Thing

    Sharon McMahon

    Meet 12 unsung heroes whose ordinary acts sparked extraordinary change, from María de López's wartime ambulance service to the unlikely friendship that propelled Norman and Alan into leadership roles. In this uplifting picture book, award-winning author Sharon McMahon and illustrator Susanna Chapman bring to life the pivotal choices that made a mighty impact on American history. This inspiring collection reminds us that courage, kindness, and determination can come from anywhere – and that even the smallest step can create lasting ripples.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. What We Can Know A Novel

    Ian McEwan

    From Booker Prize-winner Ian McEwan comes an astonishing novel that spans centuries, delving into the mysteries of human connection and the power of words. Through the lens of a lost poem, What We Can Know weaves together stories of love, betrayal, and the blurred lines between past and future. With McEwan's trademark mastery, this timeless tale explores what we gain by chasing secrets and the enduring legacy of our relationships. A captivating literary journey that will leave you pondering the depths of human knowledge.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Fire Next Time

    James Baldwin

    In this electrifying, personal, and profound work, James Baldwin confronts the enduring, corrosive effects of racism in America. Through two impassioned letters, Baldwin calls upon both black and white Americans to confront the nation's shameful legacy and work towards a more just society. Written in searing, brilliant prose, this National Bestseller is a classic of literature that continues to resonate and inspire today.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. All We Can Save Truth, Courage, and Solutions for the Climate Crisis

    Katharine K. Wilkinson

    In this timely, powerful collection, more than forty visionary women at the forefront of the climate movement share their expertise, insights, and innovative solutions to the crisis. With diverse perspectives from science, art, activism, and more, All We Can Save inspires with the courage and resilience required to turn the tide and create a better world. A national bestseller, this urgently needed book illuminates the voices and visions of women leading the charge for climate justice and a more sustainable future.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Giving Up Is Unforgivable A Manual for Keeping a Democracy

    Joyce Vance

    From acclaimed constitutional law professor Joyce Vance comes a galvanizing guide for preserving democracy. In "Giving Up Is Unforgivable," Vance offers a hopeful yet candid roadmap through today's crisis, contextualizing the challenges in historical perspective while empowering readers to take action as individuals and citizens collectively. A call to unite against the erosion of democratic norms, this manifesto is both a spirited defense of the rule of law and an impassioned plea to strengthen our shared democratic future.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Read This When Things Fall Apart Letters to Activists in Crisis

    Kelly Hayes

    When the struggles of activism lead to heartbreak, burnout, and despair, seek solace in Kelly Hayes' compassionate letters. Drawing from personal experience and inspiring stories from fellow change-makers, Hayes offers a lifeline for those grappling with the dark side of justice work. Part survival guide, part healing balm, these powerful letters remind us that even in the bleakest moments, we can find strength, hope, and the wisdom to keep fighting.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What types of stories are similar to 'Courage Can Save Us'?
These books feature courageous individuals making a difference through activism, leadership, and inspiring actions.
Are all the recommended books non-fiction?
No, while most are non-fiction, 'The Fire Next Time' and 'Read This When Things Fall Apart' offer historical and contemporary perspectives through a mix of memoir and essay.
Can I find these books at my local library or bookstore?
Yes, all the titles listed are widely available in print and digital formats from major bookstores and libraries worldwide
