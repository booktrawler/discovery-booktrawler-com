If You Loved The Apothecary's Wife, Read These 10 Books

Immerse yourself in more historical fiction with a touch of mysticism and strong female characters in these captivating reads.

Who these books are for

  • Readers who enjoyed The Apothecary's Wife's blend of historical fiction and mysticism
  • Fans of strong female protagonists in literary fiction
  • Those seeking immersive stories set in past eras

Start here

If you only read one, choose The Lost Bookshop for its poignant historical setting and mysterious bookstore.

The Apothecary's Wife transported readers to a captivating world of historical fiction, mysticism, and strong female characters. If you're seeking more immersive stories with these elements, look no further. Here are 10 books that share The Apothecary's Wife's striking blend of past eras, mysterious undertones, and empowered women at their center.

The collection

  1. The Lost Bookshop

    Evie Woods

    Step into the enchanting world of The Lost Bookshop, where the magic of storytelling brings together three unlikely souls. Opaline, Martha, and Henry's ordinary lives are forever changed when they stumble upon a mysterious Dublin bookshop that seems to vanish and reappear at will. As they unravel the secrets hidden within its shelves, they become embroiled in a captivating tale that blurs the lines between reality and fantasy. With its beautifully woven prose and nods to classic literature, this charming novel is a must-read for book lovers everywhere, promising to sweep you away on a journey of self-discovery and the transformative power of reading.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Fox Wife A Novel

    Yangsze Choo

    In Yangsze Choo's captivating new novel, THE FOX WIFE, a gifted detective and a vengeful fox spirit weave an intricate tale of myth and mystery in early 20th century Manchuria. As Bao unravels the enigma of a courtesan's death, he must navigate the blurred lines between gods and mortals. Meanwhile, Snow's quest for justice leads her from China to Japan. This epic tale of love, loss, and ancient curses pulses with unforgettable characters and a world of wonder that will keep you mesmerized until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Women A Novel

    Kristin Hannah

    In Kristin Hannah's riveting novel, The Women, 20-year-old Frankie McGrath trades her nursing studies for the Army Nurse Corps to serve in Vietnam amidst war's chaos and destruction. Through her eyes, we witness the bonds of sisterhood forged under fire, the disorienting journey back to a fractured America, and one woman's journey from idealism to the unyielding courage required to heal a nation's wounds, war's forgotten heroes.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Second Sex

    Simone De Beauvoir

    A pioneering and essential work, Simone de Beauvoir's The Second Sex remains a powerful rallying cry for women's independence and self-advancement. In this unabridged edition, readers can experience the full breadth and depth of Beauvoir's groundbreaking argument, newly restored from the original French text. This provocative and timeless classic continues to inspire generations and remains a vital read for understanding gender politics.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Kin: Oprah's Book Club A Novel

    Tayari Jones

    "Tayari Jones's luminous novel Kin is a testament to the unbreakable bonds between women and the power of resilience in the face of adversity. Spanning decades and worlds, this masterpiece follows the divergent paths of Vernice and Annie, two childhood friends forged by the unforgiving landscape of the American South. Jones weaves a mesmerizing tale of sisterhood, motherhood, and the complexities of identity that will stay with you long after the final page is turned. Don't miss this unforgettable exploration of what it means to be a woman, a friend, and a force to be reckoned with."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. My Husband's Wife A Novel

    Alice Feeney

    In this twisted, addictive thriller, two women's lives intertwine when they each claim ownership of the mysterious Spyglass estate. Artist Eden Fox returns home only to find a doppelganger-like stranger inside, married to her husband. Meanwhile, Birdy, grappling with a life-altering diagnosis, seeks solace in the inherited Spyglass, but uncovers a dark London clinic that predicts death dates. As secrets unleash a tangled web of deception, you'll be compelled to unravel the ultimate mind-bending marriage mystery within this enchanting yet sinister Fairytale of a house. Prepare to be both unputdownable and unsettled by Alice Feeney's latest instant New York Times bestselling hit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The First Time I Saw Him

    Laura Dave

    In this captivating follow-up to The Last Thing He Told Me, Laura Dave masterfully weaves another spellbinding tale, this time delving into themes of forgiveness and the enduring power of love. Picking up where the previous story left off, The First Time I Saw Him promises an equally gripping, rich, and evocative reading experience, sure to leave readers eagerly turning the pages to unravel the next chapter in this unforgettable saga.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Calamity Club

    Kathryn Stockett

    From the bestselling author of The Help, Kathryn Stockett, comes a riveting new novel set in Depression-era Oxford, Mississippi. Meet Meg, abandoned as a child, and Birdie, a fiery outsider, who join forces with Charlie in a daring scheme for independence during a time of societal hypocrisy. Immersive, exciting, and "pure, hell-raising entertainment" (NYTBR), The Calamity Club is a triumphant testament to the power of female friendship and the unbreakable human spirit. Prepare to cheer, laugh, and cry with these unforgettable characters in this epic tale of new beginnings.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Heart the Lover

    Lily King

    From the New York Times bestselling author of Writers & Lovers, Lily King's Heart the Lover is a poignant, witty, and thought-provoking exploration of first love, lifelong friendships, and the transformative power of literature. Spanning decades, it follows Jordan as she navigates a charged love triangle with her charismatic classmates Sam and Yash during a whirlwind college semester. Years later, a surprising reunion forces her to confront the choices and deceptions of her youth, leading to a profound reckoning with the past and the enduring bonds that shape our lives. With King's signature emotional depth and literary flair, this masterful novel celebrates the complexities of love, friendship, and forgiveness, affirming her as a modern chronicler of the human experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. This Book Made Me Think of You

    Libby Page

    Tilly Nightingale's year of grief takes an unexpected turn when she receives a mysterious birthday gift from her late husband at her local bookstore. As she immerses herself in the 12 carefully curated books and handwritten notes, Tilly finds solace, new adventures, and a budding connection with the kind bookstore owner Alfie. This heartwarming tale celebrates the transformative power of reading and the love that can blossom in the most unexpected places.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

Are these books direct copies of The Apothecary's Wife's style?
No, each offers a unique voice and narrative, but share similar themes and elements.
Can I read these if I'm not a historical fiction fan?
While historical settings are prominent, the books' engaging characters and universal themes make them accessible to a wider audience.
Are all the books here equally mysterious?
While some delve deeper into the supernatural, others focus more on character-driven storytelling with historical intrigue
