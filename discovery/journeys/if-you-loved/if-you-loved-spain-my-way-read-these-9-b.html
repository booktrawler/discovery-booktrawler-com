If You Loved Spain My Way, Read These 9 Books

For a similar blend of memoir, culture, and coming-of-age themes in a Spanish context, try these captivating reads.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of travel memoirs with a personal touch
  • Readers intrigued by Spanish culture and history
  • Those seeking relatable coming-of-age stories

Start here

If you only read one, choose Swann's Way by Marcel Proust for an unparalleled literary journey.

The enchanting world portrayed in Spain My Way, with its vivid descriptions of places and people, leaves readers eager for more immersive experiences. These nine book recommendations capture similar essences of travel, culture, and self-discovery, transporting you to diverse settings within and beyond Spain. From Proust's timeless classic to contemporary voices, these stories offer engaging companions for your next literary adventure.

The collection

  1. Swann's Way In Search of Lost Time, Volume 1 (Penguin Classics Deluxe Edition)

    Marcel Proust

    Step into the enchanting world of Marcel Proust's Swann's Way, the first volume of his transcendent In Search of Lost Time. With Lydia Davis's award-winning translation, rediscover childhood memories and impressions that vividly come to life, as seen through the eyes of a sensitive young narrator. Also featured is the iconic novella "Swann in Love", a profound exploration of jealousy that intertwines to form the sprawling masterpiece of Proust's life in art. Dive into this timeless work of literary fiction, a perfect blend of satire, skepticism, and introspection that renders the passage of time in exquisite detail.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Las maneras en que papi dice te quiero (Spanish Edition)

    Delia Ruiz

    Experience the unconditional love between a father and daughter on a heartwarming road trip adventure. Through big gestures and small, Papi's love shines through as he and his daughter Maricruz embark on a fun-filled journey. A beautifully illustrated bilingual picture book, suitable for ages 3-7, celebrating the special bond between parent and child.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Mexican Gothic

    Silvia Moreno-Garcia

    "Step into a hauntingly atmospheric world where Gothic horrors lurk beneath the surface of a decaying Mexican mansion in Silvia Moreno-Garcia's chilling debut, 'Mexican Gothic.' Inspired by the dark, eerie beauty of 1930s Mexican architecture, Moreno-Garcia crafts a riveting tale of family secrets, forbidden love, and supernatural terror. As young socialite Noemí del Valle ventures into the foreboding High Place, she uncovers sinister mysteries hidden behind crumbling walls and cobwebbed corridors, weaving a spellbinding narrative that will leave you utterly captivated by the shadows within its pages."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Offside: A Graphic Novel

    Christina Diaz Gonzalez

    Join this diverse group of middle school soccer teammates as they navigate rivalries, misunderstandings, and more in Christina Diaz Gonzalez's engrossing graphic novel Offside. With its Breakfast Club vibe, dual language storytelling, and emphasis on unity over division, this compelling tale of friendship and teamwork is a must-read for kids (and their grown-ups!) looking for relatable characters and an uplifting story.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Alchemist A Fable About Following Your Dream

    Paulo Coelho

    In 'The Alchemist', Paulo Coelho weaves a mesmerizing fable about the transformative power of following one's dreams. This deceptively simple tale, set against the backdrop of a young shepherd's mystical journey across the Sahara Desert, reveals profound insights into spirituality, self-discovery, and the unseen forces that guide us towards our destinies. Through lyrical prose and vivid imagery, Coelho creates an immersive reading experience that will resonate deeply with anyone seeking to unlock the secrets of their soul. Prepare to be enchanted, inspired, and forever changed by this timeless classic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Soccer in Sun and Shadow

    Eduardo Galeano

    Embark on a captivating journey through the rich history and global culture of the beautiful game with Eduardo Galeano's acclaimed book, Soccer in Sun and Shadow. This lyrical and witty literary tour explores the evolution of soccer from ancient China to modern-day pitches, highlighting the unique styles, legendary players, and revelatory moments that capture the essence of different eras and places. With Galeano's masterful storytelling and Mark Fried's pitch-perfect translation, this is an unforgettable account that will delight both soccer enthusiasts and literary lovers alike.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Road to Unfreedom Russia, Europe, America

    Timothy Snyder

    From the bestselling author of On Tyranny comes a chilling and timely exposé of the authoritarian menace threatening democracy globally. Timothy Snyder reveals how Russia's Vladimir Putin has exported his fascist ideology, finding willing allies in nationalists, oligarchs, and radicals across Europe and America. Snyder's urgent, meticulously researched chronicle warns of the vulnerability of Western societies, whose erosion of values and institutions has left them prey to Russian subversion. This indispensable work illuminates the stark choices before us, between equality, individuality, and truth or oligarchy, totality, and falsehood, offering a beacon of hope for those seeking to defend and renew democratic principles in the face of unprecedented danger.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Choose Your Own Adventure Cryptid Chronicles: Chupacabra

    Cristin Bishara

    Immerse yourself in this thrilling supernatural adventure as you navigate the storm-battered streets of Old San Juan to find your cousin, only to discover the terrifying chupacabra is real and on the hunt. With 20 possible endings, every choice puts you in the heart of danger - will you outrun the creature, solve its mysterious curse, or succumb to its bloodthirsty wrath? Packed with suspense and Cryptid lore, this interactive tale will keep 9-12 year old readers on the edge of their seats!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. I Am Not Your Perfect Mexican Daughter

    Erika L. Sánchez

    In "I Am Not Your Perfect Mexican Daughter", Erika L. Sánchez delivers a gut-wrenching and unflinching portrayal of grief, identity and cultural expectation as 15-year-old Julia navigates life after her sister Olga's tragic death, forced to confront the secrets and lies that shaped her family. With a voice that crackles with raw emotion and intelligence, Julia embarks on a journey of self-discovery as she unravels the myth of the "perfect" Mexican daughter, forging a path towards authenticity and healing in the process. This stunning debut is a must-read for anyone looking to be moved, provoked and ultimately uplifted by a story that will linger long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. A Year of Marvelous Ways

    Sarah Winman

    Sarah Winman's poignant and uplifting novel, A Year of Marvelous Ways, brings together the unlikely friendship of an elderly woman named Marvelous and a young soldier, Francis, in 1940s Cornwall. When Francis arrives at Marvelous's creek, wounded and adrift after the war, she offers the solace and purpose he desperately needs. Their bond blossoms as Marvelous shares the stories of her life, filled with love and loss. A testament to the transformative power of connection, this "bighearted" tale celebrates the magic of finding friendship and meaning in the most unexpected of places.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What type of books should I expect in these recommendations?
A mix of memoirs, travel writing, historical fiction, and young adult novels that share elements of cultural discovery and self-discovery.
Are these books primarily in English or Spanish?
Some are in Spanish, while others are translations or originally in English. You'll find a balance to suit various reading preferences.
Are these recommendations suitable for all ages?
The books vary in their intended audience, from young adult to adult readers. Parents and educators may find some appealing for teens and pre-teens as well.
