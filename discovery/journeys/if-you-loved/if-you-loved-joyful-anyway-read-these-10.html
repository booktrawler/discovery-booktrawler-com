If You Loved Joyful, Anyway, Read These 10 Books

For a deeper exploration of resilience, humor, and personal growth, dive into these uplifting, memoir-inspired novels and non-fiction essays.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of humorous, uplifting memoirs and essays
  • Readers seeking stories of resilience and personal growth
  • Anyone looking for inspiring, feel-good books with a touch of humor

Start here

If you only read one, choose 'Loveless' for its relatable portrayal of mental health struggles and the power of finding joy in the little things.

Joyful, Anyway's wit, candor, and infectious positivity have captivated readers. If you're looking to deepen your exploration of resilience, humor, and personal growth, this curated list of readalikes offers more uplifting stories to inspire and delight you. From mental health memoirs to essays that blend humor and insight, these 10 books share Joyful, Anyway's spirit of finding joy in the face of life's challenges.

The collection

  1. Loveless

    Alice Oseman

    Discover a refreshing, authentic voice in Alice Oseman's Loveless, a heartwarming and humorous YA novel. Meet Georgia, a relatable heroine who dares to disrupt the pervasive narrative around love and sexuality, instead embracing her identity as asexual and aromantic. With wit, vulnerability, and the support of her imperfect friends, Georgia navigates the highs and lows of self-discovery, proving that love truly comes in many forms. Delight in this timely, honest tale of finding oneself and forging meaningful connections in a world that often misunderstands them.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Tomorrow I'll Be Happy

    Jessica Hische

    In Jessica Hische's uplifting new picture book, Tomorrow I'll Be Happy, young readers learn that every day brings new opportunities and fresh perspectives. This relatable tale explores a range of emotions, from struggles to triumphs, reassuring kids that all feelings, big and small, make them who they are. A perfect bedtime read or gift, Hische's beautifully hand-lettered messages offer wisdom, motivation, and understanding, reminding readers that tomorrow is always full of new possibilities.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Set My Feelings Free

    Jon Fogel

    In this delightful picture book, Jon Fogel offers a compassionate approach to help children navigate their emotions. Through engaging rhymes and whimsical illustrations, "Set My Feelings Free" normalizes emotional expression and provides young readers with simple, evidence-backed strategies to manage anger, sadness, and more. Perfect for bedtime, the classroom, or therapy sessions, this book fosters emotional intelligence and resilience in children as they learn to "feel feelings, feelings, and set them free."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. All the Ice Cream in the Land

    Emmy Kastner

    In this delightful, laugh-out-loud tale, a curious princess finds herself tangled in a whimsical web of events when she desires just one lick of ice cream. Emmy Kastner's cumulative story builds hilariously, introducing a cast of characters more than eager to please, a kingdom overflowing with cold treats, and a princess struggling to keep up. Fans of If You Give a Mouse a Cookie will delight in this zany, unexpected adventure full of cows, surprises, and summer treats galore.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Unsettling Salad!

    Aaron Reynolds

    Join Jasper Rabbit as he shares the chilling tale of Thadeus Badger and the broccoli that bites back! When this typical badger makes a salad-eating deal, he learns the hard way that even the healthiest foods can have a dark side. With humor and fright, this creepy chapter book will leave you on edge, warning you to beware of veggies that can turn the tables.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Lost and Found Jigsaw Book

    Oliver Jeffers

    Indulge in a heartwarming tale of unlikely friendship as a curious boy and an adventurous penguin navigate the ups and downs of companionship. With Oliver Jeffers' endearing storytelling and delightful illustrations, this charming picture book is a must-read for kids of all ages. Now, take the immersive experience to another level by completing the six included 12-piece jigsaw puzzles, bringing each whimsical scene to vivid life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Look Both Ways A Tale Told in Ten Blocks

    Jason Reynolds

    Step into a vibrant neighborhood where the journey home is filled with humor, curiosity, and poignant reflection. In this inventive novel, National Book Award finalist Jason Reynolds weaves together ten interconnected stories, each set on a different city block, to reveal the diverse detours and discoveries that shape young lives. A masterful blend of wit and wisdom, "Look Both Ways" is a nuanced celebration of the everyday moments that make us who we are.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Read This When Things Fall Apart Letters to Activists in Crisis

    Kelly Hayes

    When the struggles of activism lead to heartbreak, burnout, and despair, seek solace in Kelly Hayes' compassionate letters. Drawing from personal experience and inspiring stories from fellow change-makers, Hayes offers a lifeline for those grappling with the dark side of justice work. Part survival guide, part healing balm, these powerful letters remind us that even in the bleakest moments, we can find strength, hope, and the wisdom to keep fighting.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Joy Luck Club A Novel

    Amy Tan

    In this captivating novel, four Chinese immigrant mothers form the Joy Luck Club, gathering to share memories, gossip, and the secrets of their pasts. As each woman reveals her story, the intricate, often fraught ties between mothers and daughters are laid bare. With Tan's masterful prose and a deep understanding of the complex emotions at play, The Joy Luck Club offers a nuanced, moving exploration of the power of love and the legacy of silence. A timeless, bestselling classic that will resonate with readers long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Mack & Fred, Side by Side

    Erin Larivee

    Join Mack and Fred on their laugh-out-loud friendship adventure! When their shared dream home turns into a side-by-side showdown, fueled by jealousy and outrageous upgrades, the best friends must rediscover what really matters. This humorous and heartwarming picture book, perfect for ages 4-8, playfully explores themes of friendship, comparison culture, and emotional growth, reminding us that sometimes the most special things are right in front of us.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What themes do these books share with Joyful, Anyway?
Humor, resilience, personal growth, and finding joy in life's challenges are common threads.
Are all the recommendations memoirs or essays?
No, while some are memoir-inspired, others blend fictional storytelling with non-fiction elements.
Will I enjoy these if I prefer lighter, humorous reads?
Absolutely! Each book offers a unique blend of humor, heart, and inspiring messages.
