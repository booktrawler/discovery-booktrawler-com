Where to Start with Capitalism

For a foundational understanding, begin with Karl Marx's 'Capital', then explore contemporary perspectives like Mariana Mazzucato's 'The Common Good Economy' and David McNally's 'Slavery and Capitalism'.

Who these books are for

  • Students of economics and sociology
  • Business professionals seeking a broader context
  • Activists and thinkers interested in social and political theory

Start here

If you only read one book, choose Karl Marx's 'Capital' for a comprehensive, influential introduction to capitalism's core principles and critiques.

Capitalism, an economic system based on private ownership of the means of production, has shaped modern society. To understand its impact, it's essential to explore both classic works that defined capitalist thought and contemporary analyses that challenge and evolve its principles. This reading journey provides a solid foundation for grasping the concept of capitalism and its complex relationships with power, inequality, and human progress.

The collection

  1. Capital A Critique of Political Economy, Volume 1

    Karl Marx

    This seminal work by Karl Marx fundamentally reshaped our understanding of economics, politics, and society. In vivid detail, Marx critiques the exploitative nature of capitalism and prophesies its downfall, making a compelling case for a more equitable, socialist future. As the "Bible of the working class," Capital has been a guiding force for centuries, remaining remarkably relevant in today's divided world economy. A must-read for anyone seeking to grasp the origins and implications of modern capitalism.

Witness the transformative power of human connections as lives and destinies entwine across ages, in a tale of love, betrayal, and resilience that defies conventional narrative structures.   3. The Common Good Economy How to Make Capitalism Work for Us All

    Mariana Mazzucato

    In this visionary book, economist Mariana Mazzucato boldly reimagines the economy to serve the common good, rather than just individual interests. Mazzucato lays out a compelling new theory that embodies principles of reciprocity, collectivism, and collective action to address global challenges like inequality, climate change, and more. With inspiring global examples and a stirring call to action, The Common Good Economy is a must-read for anyone seeking a meaningful transformation of our economic systems to benefit all humanity.

    Clara E. Mattei

    "Economist Clara Mattei shatters the myths of a supposedly "natural" capitalist system, revealing instead a man-made construct perpetuating poverty, inequality, and the prioritization of profits over people. This eye-opening manifesto offers a compelling vision for a more just economy and a roadmap for escaping the destructive grip of capitalism to achieve genuine economic freedom."

With a nuanced blend of self-help, politics, and cultural critique, "How to Do Nothing" empowers readers to break free from capitalist narratives and redefine what matters most.     Robert B. Reich

    In this deeply personal memoir, renowned economist and former cabinet member Robert B. From bullies to civil rights, Kennedy to Obama, Reich's narrative is a galvanizing chronicle of the cultural and political forces shaping our nation. With characteristic humor and principle, he ultimately asks: Did his generation come up short on realizing America's ideals, and what can we do to reclaim our democracy's future? An essential read for understanding today's America and its potential for a better tomorrow.

  9. Slavery and Capitalism A New Marxist History

    David McNally

    "David McNally's groundbreaking Slavery and Capitalism offers a much-needed Marxist reinterpretation of Atlantic slavery, revealing it as a crucial foundation of capitalist commodity production. Drawing from diverse sources, McNally convincingly argues that enslaved laborers constituted a modern working class engaged in fierce struggles for freedom and control over their lives and labor, laying bare the roots of racial capitalism and reshaping our understanding of the plantation system and its lasting impacts."

  10. Caliban and the Witch Women, the Body and Primitive Accumulation

    Silvia Federici

    In this groundbreaking work, Silvia Federici delves into the oft-ignored history of how the transition to capitalism fundamentally reshaped the human experience, with the body as its battleground. From medieval peasant uprisings to the witch hunts and rise of mechanical philosophy, Federici reveals the pivotal role of power over the body in creating the principles of labor and self-ownership that define modern society. A provocative inquiry into the costs of capitalist progress, Caliban and the Witch is a must-read for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of our world's evolution and the fight for human freedom.

Frequently asked questions

What is the primary focus of Marx's 'Capital'?
Marx examines the economic and social structures of capitalism, highlighting its inherent contradictions and the exploitation of labor.
How does 'The Common Good Economy' differ from traditional capitalist perspectives?
Mazzucato argues that capitalism can be harnessed for societal benefit when guided by public purpose and investment in innovations that drive progress.
What themes do 'Slavery and Capitalism' and 'Caliban and the Witch' explore in relation to capitalism?
These works examine the historical and ongoing entanglements between capitalist systems and systems of oppression, slavery, and colonialism.
