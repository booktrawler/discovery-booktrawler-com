Where to Start with Comforting Reads

Begin with Matt Haig's The Comfort Book, a powerful memoir that explores the healing power of literature and personal anecdotes.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone seeking uplifting, reassuring reads during difficult times
  • Book clubs looking for soothing group reads
  • Mental health advocates and professionals recommending comforting literature

Start here

If you only read one, choose The Comfort Book - its blend of memoir, personal essays, and literary musings makes it an unparalleled comfort companion.

In times of stress, uncertainty, or emotional turmoil, few things provide solace like a great book. Comforting reads offer a respite from the chaos, helping us feel seen, heard, and less alone. Whether you're seeking escapism, inspiration, or simply a deeper connection to yourself and others, these uplifting texts are here to comfort your mind and nourish your spirit.

The collection

  1. The Comfort Book

    Matt Haig

    In this heartening companion, Matt Haig shares tender reminders and inspiring insights gleaned from across history and the globe, offering solace during life's tumult and encouraging readers to savor the present moment. Through stories, lists, and personal musings, Haig's The Comfort Book provides a soothing balm for the soul, reminding us to find hope and beauty amidst life's complexities.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Comfort of Crows (Reese's Book Club Pick) A Backyard Year

    Margaret Renkl

    Join beloved New York Times columnist Margaret Renkl on an intimate journey through a backyard's seasons, from the resourceful crows of winter to the songbirds of spring. With 52 short, lyrical chapters woven together by her moving reflections on life, hope, and the human condition, "The Comfort of Crows" invites readers to slow down, appreciate nature's bounty, and find solace in the smallest, yet most radiant wonders of the world around us.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. How to Read a Book A Novel

    Monica Wood

    In this heartwarming, character-driven novel, three strangers - Violet, a young woman released from prison, Harriet, a retired English teacher, and Frank, a grieving widower - converge in a Portland bookstore. As their lives entwine, Monica Wood explores themes of forgiveness, second chances, and the life-altering power of shared stories. With wit, grace, and profound understanding, Wood crafts an unsparingly honest and deeply humane tale that will resonate with book clubs and fans of emotional fiction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Book Club for Troublesome Women

    Marie Bostwick

    "The Book Club for Troublesome Women" by Marie Bostwick is a heartwarming, feel-good novel that transported me to a tight-knit community of spirited women bound together by the power of literature. With poignant character development and witty banter, this story had me laughing, crying, and cheering for these unforgettable heroines as they navigate the challenges of love, loss, and self-discovery. Bostwick's evocative writing and masterful storytelling created an immersive experience that left me marveling at the transformative impact of friendship and the written word. A pure delight for anyone seeking a uplifting, character-driven tale.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Anxious Person's Guide to Non-Monogamy Your Guide to Open Relationships, Polyamory and Letting Go

    Lola Phoenix

    Embark on a journey of self-discovery and open relationships with Lola Phoenix's The Anxious Person's Guide to Non-Monogamy. Written from a place of personal experience, this refreshingly honest guide equips you with the practical and emotional tools to navigate the challenges of polyamory, open relationships, and prioritizing your mental health. From finding your "anchor" to managing jealousy and negotiating boundaries, this comprehensive resource is an invaluable companion for anyone seeking a happy, fulfilling non-monogamous life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Convenience Store Woman

    Sayaka Murata

    Meet Keiko Furukura, the 36-year-old Tokyo woman who has found her niche and happiness working at the Hiiromachi branch of "Smile Mart" for 18 years. In this captivating novel, Sayaka Murata portrays an unconventional life and psyche with nuance and humor, delivering a unique look at postmodern Japanese society and the relentless pressure to conform. A bestselling and award-winning debut, Convenience Store Woman is an irresistible tale of self-discovery, individuality, and the quirky beauty of finding one's place in the world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Happiness Trap (Second Edition) How to Stop Struggling and Start Living

    Russ Harris

    Break free from the cycle of stress, anxiety, and depression with this groundbreaking guide. Author Russ Harris presents the powerful tools of Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) to help you clarify your values, build self-compassion, and find lasting fulfillment. Whether you're struggling with insecurity, workplace burnout, or the challenges of life, The Happiness Trap offers a proven path to authentic happiness and a richer, more meaningful existence. Over a million copies sold and now updated and expanded, this essential read belongs on every self-help shelf.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Good Inside A Guide to Becoming the Parent You Want to Be

    Becky Kennedy

    "Good Inside" by Dr. Becky Kennedy offers a groundbreaking parenting approach that prioritizes connecting with children over correcting them. This insightful guide provides practical strategies for parents seeking to raise resilient, emotionally healthy kids without relying on outdated techniques like reward charts and time-outs. Dr. Kennedy's compassionate philosophy empowers parents to move from uncertainty and self-doubt to confident, effective leadership. Perfect for a generation of caregivers seeking a more supportive, effective way to nurture their children's well-being and set them up for lifelong success.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Wandering Inn Book One, Part One of The Wandering Inn Series

    pirateaba

    Step into the Innverse, an unparalleled epic fantasy world, with The Wandering Inn. When Erin Solstice, an ordinary woman from our world, is transported to a realm of magic and monsters, she must adapt to survive. With wit, humor, and resilience, she faces the challenges of this new reality, battling Goblins, Rock Crabs, and the evil [Necromancers] who threaten her and her unlikely friends. Dive into this captivating series, praised by New York Times bestseller Matt Dinniman, for a unique blend of fantasy, humor, and heart-pounding adventure that will leave you enchanted.

  10. It Didn't Start with You How Inherited Family Trauma Shapes Who We Are and How to End the Cycle

    Mark Wolynn

    This groundbreaking book uncovers the hidden role of inherited family trauma in shaping our emotional and physical health. Gaining insight from cutting-edge research, acclaimed expert Mark Wolynn provides a powerful, prescriptive guide to transform inherited trauma through the Core Language Approach. By diagnosing and reconfiguring our inherited fears and anxieties, we can break cycles of trauma and reclaim our well-being.

Frequently asked questions

What kind of books are considered 'comforting'?
Comforting reads often feature uplifting narratives, soothing prose, relatable characters, and a focus on hope, resilience, and well-being.
How does The Comfort Book differ from other memoirs?
While many memoirs focus on hardship, The Comfort Book stands out for its emphasis on the healing power of literature, personal anecdotes, and the author's reflections on finding comfort in difficult times.
Are comfort books only for those experiencing mental health issues?
No, comforting reads can benefit anyone looking for a soothing, uplifting, or thought-provoking literary experience, regardless of their current emotional state.
