Where to Start with Think Again

Begin with Adam Grant's 'Think Again' for a comprehensive guide to cultivating a growth mindset and embracing lifelong learning.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone interested in personal growth and self-improvement
  • Professionals seeking to enhance their learning agility
  • Students looking to optimize their academic performance

Start here

If you only read one book on the topic, choose Adam Grant's 'Think Again'. Its thought-provoking insights and practical advice make it an essential starting point for anyone looking to refine their thinking and learning skills.

Adam Grant's 'Think Again' offers a compelling case for rethinking your approach to learning and personal growth. By challenging assumptions and embracing curiosity, you can develop a growth mindset that propels you forward in both your personal and professional life. This book serves as a valuable guide for anyone seeking to enhance their cognitive agility, overcome biases, and cultivate a lifelong love of learning.

The collection

  1. Think Again The Power of Knowing What You Don't Know

    Adam Grant

    In THINK AGAIN, organizational psychologist Adam Grant argues that embracing intellectual humility is more vital than ever in our rapidly changing world. He reveals how we can cultivate the art of rethinking, unlearning, and opening minds - both our own and others'. By examining real-life examples, Grant shows us how to overcome the comfort of conviction, welcome disagreement, and even enjoy being wrong. This groundbreaking guide invites us to prize mental flexibility over foolish consistency, making it a powerful tool for personal growth, professional success, and building a wiser, more curious society.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Begin Again James Baldwin's America and Its Urgent Lessons for Our Own

    Eddie S. Glaude Jr.

    "In this powerful exploration, Eddie Glaude delves into James Baldwin's life and work to reveal critical lessons for our tumultuous era. Begin Again is a searing analysis of America's enduring struggle with racism, weaving together biography, history, and personal reflection. Glaude's insightful interpretation of Baldwin's experiences during the civil rights movement offers a vital roadmap for navigating our current moment of racial reckoning and political upheaval."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Restart

    Gordon Korman

    "Restart is an unforgettable New York Times bestseller that shines a light on what it means to start anew. When Chase wakes up with no memory of who he is, he must recreate his life from scratch, navigating a complex web of friendships and loyalties. With humor, heart, and insight, Gordon Korman masterfully explores themes of identity, forgiveness, and the power of second chances in this must-read for kids and anyone looking to rediscover the beauty of a fresh beginning."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Reversing Alzheimer's The New Toolkit to Improve Cognition and Protect Brain Health

    Heather Sandison

    In "Reversing Alzheimer's", Dr. Heather Sandison, a leading expert in functional medicine and dementia care, presents a groundbreaking, evidence-based approach to fortify brain health, improve cognition, and potentially even reverse the effects of Alzheimer's disease. This accessible guide distills complex research into actionable steps, empowering patients, caregivers, and anyone concerned about age-related cognitive decline to transform their environment, adopt rehabilitative lifestyle changes, and make informed choices for long-term brain wellness. With the goal of making Alzheimer's a reversible condition, not a terminal diagnosis, "Reversing Alzheimer's" is a vital resource for anyone seeking to reclaim control and rewrite their future in the face of this insidious disease.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Stolen Focus Why You Can't Pay Attention--and How to Think Deeply Again

    Johann Hari

    In today's digitally saturated world, our ability to focus is under siege. Johann Hari's thought-provoking Stolen Focus exposes the relentless external forces stealing our attention, from pollution to corporate profit schemes. Rather than blaming personal failures of willpower, Hari offers a groundbreaking exploration of the 12 root causes of this crisis, highlighting potential solutions from Silicon Valley innovators and an office in New Zealand that dramatically boosted productivity. A Times bestseller, this essential read empowers individuals and society to reclaim their focus and live more deeply engaged lives.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Hidden Potential The Science of Achieving Greater Things

    Adam Grant

    In "Hidden Potential", #1 New York Times bestselling author Adam Grant shatters common assumptions about talent and success. He reveals that progress depends less on innate genius than on character development and effective learning. Grant provides a new framework for realizing one's full potential, exploring how anyone can rise to achieve more through nurturing key skills and motivational structures. A must-read for anyone seeking to grow and excel, "Hidden Potential" will empower you to recognize and develop your own capabilities, regardless of where you start.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Art of Noticing 131 Ways to Spark Creativity, Find Inspiration, and Discover Joy in the Everyday

    Rob Walker

    Reclaim your curiosity and creativity with Rob Walker's inspiring guide, The Art of Noticing. Through 131 engaging, low-stakes exercises, discover how to tune out distractions and tune in to the world around you, sparking clearer thinking, deeper listening, and a renewed sense of what truly matters. A thought-provoking, beautifully illustrated gift for anyone seeking to experience life with fresh eyes.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Codependent No More How to Stop Controlling Others and Start Caring for Yourself (Revised and Updated)

    Melody Beattie

    Discover the liberating truth about codependency and start caring for yourself in this revised and updated modern classic. By understanding the destructive pattern of sacrificing your own life to help someone else, you'll break free and find the healing, hope, and happiness you deserve. With personal anecdotes, practical exercises, and new guidance on trauma and anxiety, Codependent No More is your clear path to breaking free of old patterns and embracing your true self.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. America, U.S.A. How Race Shadows the Nation's Anniversaries

    Eddie S. Glaude Jr.

    In this searing, timely assessment, Eddie S. Glaude Jr. dismantles the myths concealed beneath America's major anniversaries, exposing the nation's unfinished story of race, freedom, and national identity. With a masterful blend of history, literature, and urgent reflection, Glaude argues we must confront our grim past to forge a more just future, delivering a profound meditation for these perilous times. A clarion call to question our assumptions and scrutinize the narratives that shape America's self-image, this book is essential reading for anyone seeking to understand the complex, contested history that defines the United States.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Year We Learned to Fly

    Jacqueline Woodson

    Immerse yourself in this captivating tale where a brother and sister learn to overcome boredom and anger through the power of imagination, guided by their wise grandmother's inspiring advice. Jacqueline Woodson's poetic prose and Rafael Lopez's vibrant artwork transport you on a journey of discovery, celebrating the extraordinary resilience and creativity that lies within us all, connecting us to our ancestors' strength and ingenuity.

Frequently asked questions

What is the key takeaway from 'Think Again'?
The book emphasizes the importance of questioning assumptions, being open to new ideas, and embracing a growth mindset to continually improve and adapt.
Who is Adam Grant's target audience?
Grant's book is for anyone interested in self-improvement, learning, and professional development, including individuals in various stages of their careers.
What sets 'Think Again' apart from other self-help books?
Grant's unique approach combines scientific research, real-life examples, and actionable strategies to make the concept of 'thinking again' accessible and applicable to readers
