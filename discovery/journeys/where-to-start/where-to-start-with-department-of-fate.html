Where to Start with Department of Fate

Start with Rachel Maddow's Department of Fate, then explore similar themes in Jenny Offill's Dept. of Speculation and Philip Pullman's The Book of Dust.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of thought-provoking fiction
  • Readers interested in politics and mystery
  • Book clubs seeking complex discussions

Start here

If you only read one, choose Department of Fate by Rachel Maddow for its unique blend of politics, history, and personal narrative.

Department of Fate by Rachel Maddow is a captivating read that weaves together memoir, historical mystery, and politics. For readers new to Maddow's work or these themes, this page offers a curated path to engage with thought-provoking fiction. The recommended books share elements of complex storytelling, moral inquiry, and the human experience, making them excellent companions for a journey through the intersection of fate, choice, and societal forces.

The collection

  1. Department of Fate The Promise, the Power, and the Collapse of America's Most Consequential Institution

    Rachel Maddow

    In this explosive history, Rachel Maddow uncovers the Department of Justice's profound impact on America, tracing its power from the KKK to Trump's thug rule. With searing insight, Maddow reveals both heroic and treacherous moments, showing how the DoJ's decisions have shaped our freedoms, economy, and democracy - and now points a way forward for rebuilding a stronger, more resilient republic.

  2. Dept. of Speculation

    Jenny Offill

    In 'Dept. of Speculation', Jenny Offill masterfully weaves together a mesmerizing tapestry of a modern marriage's rise and collapse. With prose as sleek and precise as it is emotionally charged, Offill crafts an intimate, unconventional novel that will resonate with anyone who's grappled with love, family, identity, and the search for meaning in a chaotic world. A triumph of concise, powerful storytelling, this book is a must-read for anyone seeking a profoundly affecting and deeply intelligent literary experience.

  3. The Book of Dust: La Belle Sauvage (Book of Dust, Volume 1)

    Philip Pullman

    Step into the enchanting world of Philip Pullman's latest masterpiece, The Book of Dust: La Belle Sauvage, a stunning prequel to the beloved His Dark Materials trilogy. Follow Malcolm Polstead, an astute young boy, as he unravels the mysteries surrounding the enigmatic Lyra Belacqua, a girl destined for greatness. With its richly detailed setting, unforgettable characters, and Pullman's signature blend of fantasy and philosophical depth, this first volume of the Book of Dust trilogy is an unmissable adventure that will captivate readers of all ages.

  4. The Ministry of Time A Novel

    Kaliane Bradley

    A dazzling, genre-bending debut, The Ministry of Time weaves a thrilling tale of time travel, espionage, and romance. When a modern civil servant is sent to monitor an 1845 Arctic expedition commander who's inexplicably appeared in the present, an unlikely bond forms and evolves into a profound love story. As secrets unfold, the protagonist must grapple with the consequences of her choices and whether she can alter the course of history. With wit, sensuality, and a captivating blend of science fiction and literary fiction, this original masterpiece by Kaliane Bradley will captivate readers and leave them pondering the power of love and change.

With new allies and fresh challenges around every corner, this hilarious and action-packed novel will keep you on the edge of your seat until the very end. Bonus material in this print edition adds even more excitement to this New York Times Bestseller.

Rebecca Yarros masterfully weaves a tale of forbidden love, heart-wrenching loss, and unrelenting resilience, evoking a visceral emotional response with every turn of the page. Frequently asked questions

What genres does Department of Fate belong to?
It combines elements of memoir, historical mystery, and politics, making it a unique blend of genres.
Are the other recommended books also about politics?
While they touch on political themes, Dept. of Speculation focuses more on relationships and family, and The Book of Dust is a fantasy adventure.
Can I start with any of the other books if I'm interested in Department of Fate's themes?
Absolutely, each book explores complex ideas in engaging ways, and can serve as a great introduction to the themes found in Department of Fate.
