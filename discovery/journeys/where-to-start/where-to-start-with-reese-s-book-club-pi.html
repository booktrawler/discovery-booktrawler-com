Where to Start with Reese's Book Club Picks

Begin with Daisy Jones & The Six, Taylor Jenkins Reid's captivating tale of a fictional 1970s rock band. This critically-acclaimed novel will draw you in for its engaging characters, poignant love story, and nostalgic musical backdrop.

Who these books are for

  • Book clubs and reading groups
  • Fans of character-driven fiction
  • Music enthusiasts
  • Those seeking a nostalgic 1970s setting

Start here

If you only read one Reese's Book Club pick, choose Daisy Jones & The Six.

Reese's Book Club has introduced readers to a diverse range of captivating stories across genres. For those new to the series, navigating the various titles can be daunting. By starting with Daisy Jones & The Six, you'll gain a solid understanding of what to expect from Reese's future recommendations and be well on your way to exploring the many wonderful books the club has to offer.

The collection

  1. Daisy Jones & The Six: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Taylor Jenkins Reid

    Dive into the electrifying origin story of DAISY JONES & THE SIX, a musical phenomenon that shook the 1970s. Told through vibrant oral histories, this captivating novel explores the whirlwind rise of a legendary rock band and their enigmatic lead singer, Daisy Jones. From the Sunset Strip to sold-out tours, the sexual awakening of young L.A., and the intoxicating allure of stardom, experience the highs and lows of a supercharged era through the eyes of those who lived it. With its irresistible surface energy and raw, authentic voice, this #1 bestselling novel is a must-read for anyone who's ever dreamed of living life on their own terms.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. First Lie Wins: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Ashley Elston

    "In this gripping, twisty mystery, Reese Witherspoon's book club pick introduces Evie Porter, an enigmatic operative with a troubled past and a new mission: to win over Ryan Sumner. With her razor-sharp skills and an ever-shifting identity, Evie navigates a tangled web of secrets and deception. But as the stakes rise and her emotions deepen, Evie must outmaneuver her elusive boss and confront her own desires to secure her future and vanquish her haunting past. This electrifying thriller keeps you guessing until the very end."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. True Biz: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Sara Novic

    "Immerse yourself in the vibrant world of a boarding school for the deaf, where a year of seismic shifts in romance, politics, and family await. Meet Charlie, Austin, and February as they navigate first love, loss, and the fight for deaf rights. This stunning novel, a Reese's Book Club pick, weaves together sign language, civil rights, and the unbreakable bonds of human connection in a celebration of resilience and joy."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. A Pair of Aces: Reese's Book Club

    Marie Benedict

    In 1930s New York, two trailblazing women form an unlikely alliance to take down notorious mob boss Lucky Luciano. Eunice Carter, Manhattan's first Black female prosecutor, targets Luciano through his role in prostitution, while high-class madam Polly Adler supplies crucial evidence. This gripping historical thriller, a Reese's Book Club pick, follows their daring collaboration as they bridge divides and risk everything in the most sensational trial of the era.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. I'm Still Here: Reese's Book Club Black Dignity in a World Made for Whiteness

    Austin Channing Brown

    In this powerful New York Times bestseller, Austin Channing Brown shares her unflinching memoir about growing up Black, Christian, and female in a world where diversity often falls short of its ideals. With vulnerability and insight, Brown confronts the apathy and racism she faced in her predominantly white schools and churches, and reveals how blackness, if embraced, can save us all from our current era of rising racial hostility. A must-read for those seeking to understand and dismantle systemic racism, and to learn from a masterful storyteller.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Great Big Beautiful Life: Reese's Book Club

    Emily Henry

    In this dazzling novel, two writers, Alice and Hayden, find their professional rivalry blossoming into something more amidst the lush backdrop of Little Crescent Island and the enigmatic story of their subject, octogenarian Margaret Ives. As they compete for the privilege of chronicling her life, Alice and Hayden must navigate the blurred lines between their careers and hearts, all while unraveling the tantalizing mysteries of Margaret's past amidst a web of family scandals, tragic twists, and forbidden love. A Reese's Book Club pick and instant #1 New York Times bestseller, Great Big Beautiful Life is a sweeping romance that will keep you eagerly turning the pages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Read Your Color Discover Your Reading Type and Build a Life-Changing Love of Books

    Steven J. Reese

    Discover your reading essence and transform your relationship with books with Read Your Color. This groundbreaking guide identifies your core reading type - Red, Blue, Green, or one of the three other unique profiles - and provides a personalized path to a more fulfilling, purposeful reading life. Break free from mindless scrolling and genre hopping, and learn to savor books that truly resonate with you. With its engaging, psychology-backed approach and 120-day plan, Read Your Color is the ultimate reading companion for anyone seeking a deeper, more joyful connection with the written word.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Book Club for Troublesome Women

    Marie Bostwick

    "The Book Club for Troublesome Women" by Marie Bostwick is a heartwarming, feel-good novel that transported me to a tight-knit community of spirited women bound together by the power of literature. With poignant character development and witty banter, this story had me laughing, crying, and cheering for these unforgettable heroines as they navigate the challenges of love, loss, and self-discovery. Bostwick's evocative writing and masterful storytelling created an immersive experience that left me marveling at the transformative impact of friendship and the written word. A pure delight for anyone seeking a uplifting, character-driven tale.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. How to Read a Book

    Monica Wood

    In this profound and uplifting novel, three individuals - Violet a young woman released from prison, Harriet a retired English teacher, and Frank a widower - find unexpected common ground at a Portland bookstore. As their lives intertwine, Monica Wood explores themes of guilt, second chances, and the transformative power of stories. Through honest, deeply humane portrayals, Wood reminds us that kindness, forgiveness, and the love of books can illuminate even the darkest paths to redemption and a life worth living.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Into the Blue: Reese's Book Club A Love Story

    Emma Brodie

    "Embark on a decades-spanning love story as two souls from different worlds forge an unbreakable bond, bridging the gap between reality and the silver screen. With irresistible charm, sizzling chemistry, and profound insight, Into the Blue is a literary romance that explores the power of true connection, the fragility of relationships, and the unyielding pursuit of a life lived on one's own terms."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What genre is Daisy Jones & The Six?
Fiction, specifically a novel about a rock band and its rise to fame.
Will I need prior knowledge of the '70s music scene to enjoy the book?
No, the story is accessible to all readers, with historical context woven in for added depth.
Is Daisy Jones & The Six a heavy, dense read?
No, the writing is engaging and easy to follow, with a focus on character development and relationships over complex themes
