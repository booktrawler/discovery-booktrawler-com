Where to Start with The Autistic Burnout Workbook

Begin with The Autistic Burnout Workbook by Dr. Megan Anna Neff, then expand to related titles on autistic self-care, emotional regulation, and neurodiversity.

Who these books are for

  • Individuals on the autism spectrum
  • Supporters and loved ones of autistic people
  • Professionals working with autistic clients

Start here

If you only read one, choose The Autistic Burnout Workbook as a foundational resource for understanding and navigating autistic burnout.

The Autistic Burnout Workbook is a groundbreaking resource that sheds light on the complex experience of autistic burnout. As you begin exploring this topic, it's essential to establish a solid foundation with this core title. From there, you can expand your knowledge by incorporating related books that delve into autistic self-care, emotional regulation, and the broader realm of neurodiversity. By embarking on this curated reading journey, you'll gain a deeper understanding of the challenges faced by autistic individuals and develop strategies for promoting their overall well-being.

Frequently asked questions

What other books are recommended for autistic burnout?
Self-Care for Autistic People by Dr. Megan Anna Neff and Unmasking for Life by Devon Price PhD offer valuable insights into autistic self-care and identity.
Can I start with books on more general burnout and self-care?
Yes, Burnout by Emily Nagoski PhD provides a relatable and accessible introduction, while End Emotional Outsourcing by Beatriz Victoria Albina NP MPH Sep explores emotional regulation strategies for all individuals.
Will these books be helpful if I have ADHD alongside autism?
The Neurodivergence Skills Workbook for Autism and ADHD by Jennifer Kemp Mpsych is specifically designed for individuals with co-occurring diagnoses, offering a tailored approach to skills development and management.
