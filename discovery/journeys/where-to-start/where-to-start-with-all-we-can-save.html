Where to Start with All We Can Save

Begin with the foreword by Ayana Elizabeth Johnson, then dive into Katharine K. Wilkinson's compelling essays and contributions from other climate leaders.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone new to All We Can Save looking for a reading path
  • Climate change activists seeking deeper insights
  • Book clubs exploring environmental topics
  • Researchers interested in climate leadership perspectives

Start here

If you only read one, choose Katharine K. Wilkinson's essay collection All We Can Save for its authoritative, accessible climate insights and diverse voices.

Katharine K. Wilkinson's All We Can Save is a groundbreaking collection of essays from women at the forefront of the climate movement. This influential book provides an authoritative, accessible introduction to climate change challenges and solutions, as told through the diverse voices of women leaders. Whether you're a climate activist, researcher, or simply someone seeking to better understand this critical issue, All We Can Save offers a compelling reading path.

The collection

  1. All We Can Save Truth, Courage, and Solutions for the Climate Crisis

    Katharine K. Wilkinson

    In this timely, powerful collection, more than forty visionary women at the forefront of the climate movement share their expertise, insights, and innovative solutions to the crisis. With diverse perspectives from science, art, activism, and more, All We Can Save inspires with the courage and resilience required to turn the tide and create a better world. A national bestseller, this urgently needed book illuminates the voices and visions of women leading the charge for climate justice and a more sustainable future.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Begin Again How We Got Here and Where We Might Go - Our Human Story. So Far.

    Oliver Jeffers

    "In this profound and visually stunning book, acclaimed author Oliver Jeffers takes us on a journey through the very history of humanity and our collective future. With insightful commentary and iconic illustrations, Jeffers encourages us to reexamine our stories and systems, challenging us to create a brighter, more inclusive tomorrow. A must-read for anyone seeking to shape a better world for generations to come."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. We Love You An Optimistic Guide to Life on a Rock Floating Through Space

    Thomas Sullivan

    Embark on a thought-provoking journey with lifelong friends Andy Min and Thomas Sullivan as they explore humanity's relationship with nature, self, and the vast universe we inhabit. Through a mix of poetic prose, narrative, and stunning visuals, We Love You inspires readers to reexamine life's big questions and find meaning, joy, and hope in the everyday world we often take for granted. With its optimistic spirit and heartfelt wisdom, this National Bestseller is a must-read for anyone seeking a deeper connection to the world and themselves.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Democracy or Else How to Save America in 10 Easy Steps

    Jon Favreau

    From the creators of Pod Save America comes this timely, illustrated guide to taking action and making a difference in American politics. With a mix of humor and practical advice, "Democracy or Else" breaks down the complex process into 10 manageable steps for getting informed, donating, volunteering, organizing, and more. Featuring insights from savvy experts and refreshingly down-to-earth politicians, this book is the perfect resource for political newcomers and seasoned activists alike, empowering readers to join the fight to save democracy in the face of unprecedented challenges and contradictions.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Climate Wayfinding Healing Ourselves and the Planet We Call Home

    Katharine K Wilkinson

    In Climate Wayfinding, visionary leader Katharine K. Wilkinson offers a transformative guide for navigating the uncertainty of our climate crisis. Drawing on her years of climate work, Wilkinson shares a compassionate process of introspection, curiosity, and courage to help readers find their unique role in collective healing. With stirring essays, poetry, and practical exercises, this empowering book invites you to sit with the terrain ahead, embracing a path of grounding, growth, and possibility.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Courage Can Save Us Ten Extraordinary Americans and the Fight for Our Future

    Rye Barcott

    "Meet an extraordinary group of post-9/11 veterans who have carried the spirit of service from the battlefield to the halls of power. Courage Can Save Us is a poignant and timely portrait of nine principled leaders, five Democrats and five Republicans, who are striving to rise above the partisan divide and govern with courage, integrity, and compassion. This inspiring book challenges us all to consider how we can embody courage in our own lives and strengthen our democracy."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. We Should All Be Birds A Memoir

    Brian Buckbee

    Brian Buckbee's poignant memoir We Should All Be Birds is a stirring exploration of resilience, connection, and the transformative power of unexpected relationships. When a mysterious illness forces him into solitude and he rescues a wounded pigeon named Two-Step, their bond ignites a new purpose. As Buckbee's condition worsens, he partners with editor Carol Ann Fitzgerald to chronicle his illuminated life through the eyes of his feathered friend. A captivating, heartrending journey of love, loss, and finding solace in the wild beauty of the natural world, We Should All Be Birds is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Nature's Best Hope A New Approach to Conservation That Starts in Your Yard

    Douglas W Tallamy

    Join renowned ecologist Douglas W. Tallamy on a groundbreaking journey to transform your yard into a thriving wildlife haven and conservation corridor. Nature's Best Hope provides a practical, effective, and empowering approach to preserving biodiversity, starting in your very own backyard. With Tallamy's expert guidance, discover how to create a haven for native plants and animals, leaving a lasting impact on the health of our planet. By turning our private spaces into vital habitats, we can stand in solidarity with nature and ensure a sustainable future for generations to come.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. How Can I Help? Saving Nature with Your Yard

    Douglas W Tallamy

    With his trademark clarity and passion, acclaimed wildlife ecologist Douglas Tallamy offers a vital, actionable guide to harnessing your yard as a force for nature. In How Can I Help?, he shows how even modest changes – from reducing lawn to planting natives – can collectively transform backyards into havens for wildlife. Join the ecological revolution starting with your very own patch of earth.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Maintenance of Everything Part One

    Stewart Brand

    Embrace the radical act of responsibility with Stewart Brand's groundbreaking exploration, Maintenance of Everything. From the high-stakes solo sailboat race to the battle against corrosion in manufacturing, Brand uncovers the profound importance of maintenance in our daily lives and on a global scale. This thought-provoking book invites you to expand your understanding of maintenance as not just preventative tasks, but as a grand process for keeping things - and life itself - going. With insights into vehicle maintenance, military sustainment, and the art and science of keeping things running smoothly, Maintenance of Everything is a paradigm-shifting read for anyone seeking to make a meaningful impact, from the tiny to the cosmic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
