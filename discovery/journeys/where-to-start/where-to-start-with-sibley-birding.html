Where to Start with Sibley Birding

Begin with the Sibley Backyard Birding Flashcards, Revised and Updated, then progress to the comprehensive Sibley Field Guide to Birds of Eastern North America for in-depth identification knowledge.

Who these books are for

  • New bird enthusiasts seeking an accessible entry point into the world of birding
  • Nature lovers interested in learning the basics of bird identification
  • Casual backyard birdwatchers aiming for more in-depth knowledge

Start here

If you only read one Sibley book, choose the Sibley Field Guide to Birds of Eastern North America for its unmatched accuracy and comprehensive coverage.

David Allen Sibley is a renowned ornithologist and artist whose birding resources have become classics in the field. For those new to birding, his comprehensive guides and engaging flashcards provide an accessible entry point into the fascinating world of avifauna. By following this logical progression from flashcards to a field guide, beginners can build a strong foundation in bird identification and appreciation.

Frequently asked questions

What's the best way to start birding?
Begin with flashcards like Sibley's Backyard Birding Flashcards to learn key identification features.
How do I transition from flashcards to more advanced birding?
Graduate to the Sibley Field Guide for in-depth species accounts and range maps.
Which Sibley book is most suitable for beginners in urban areas?
The Sibley Field Guide covers urban and backyard birding in Eastern North America, making it an excellent choice for city dwellers.
