The collection

  1. Hole in the Sky A Novel

    Daniel H. Wilson

    This book stands out by offering a fresh perspective on first contact, drawing from the author's unique blend of Cherokee heritage and scientific background.

    For: Fans of science fiction, particularly those interested in indigenous themes and perspectives, will appreciate this novel.

    Strengths: Hole in the Sky excels at weaving together complex science, Cherokee cosmology, and suspenseful thriller elements into a cohesive narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow as Wilson builds tension and explores the characters' emotional journeys.

    Comparable: If you enjoyed works that blend science fiction with cultural or historical elements, such as Octavia Butler's Xenogenesis trilogy or Nnedi Okorafor's Binti series, you may appreciate Hole in the Sky's similar approach.

    “Wilson, the author of Robopocalypse, is back with another epic.... A well-executed thriller.... Wilson slowly ratchets up the weirdness.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Hobbit

    J.R.R. Tolkien

    The Hobbit is a timeless classic of high fantasy, with a richly detailed world, memorable characters, and an epic adventure that has captivated readers for generations.

    For: Fantasy fans, especially those who enjoy quest narratives and immersive world-building, will greatly appreciate this book.

    Strengths: The Hobbit expertly blends humor, peril, and wonder, crafting an unforgettable journey through Middle-earth filled with memorable characters, clever dialogue, and vivid descriptions.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slow in parts, as Tolkien takes time to develop the world and characters.

    Comparable: For those who enjoy The Hobbit, other classic fantasy novels like The Chronicles of Narnia or The Princess Bride offer similar enchanting worlds and engaging stories.

    “All those, young or old, who love a finely imagined story, beautifully told, will take The Hobbit to their hearts.”
