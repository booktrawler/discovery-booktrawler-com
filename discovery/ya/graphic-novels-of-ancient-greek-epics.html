Graphic Novels of Ancient Greek Epics

Experience Homer's timeless tales in a modern visual format with these acclaimed graphic adaptations of The Iliad and The Odyssey.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of Homer's classic poetry seeking a fresh, visual interpretation
  • Readers interested in ancient Greek history and mythology
  • Graphic novel enthusiasts discovering epic storytelling in a new medium
  • Educators looking for engaging teaching tools for literature and history classes

Start here

If you only read one graphic novel about ancient Greek literature, make it Gareth Hinds' The Odyssey.

Gareth Hinds' acclaimed graphic novel adaptations of Homer's iconic epics, The Iliad and The Odyssey, offer a fresh perspective on timeless stories of heroism, adventure, and the human condition. These meticulously researched and beautifully illustrated books breathe new life into ancient Greek literature, presenting the classic tales in a modern, accessible format. Whether you're a seasoned classicist or simply a lover of great storytelling, these graphic novels provide a unique and captivating way to experience the enduring power of Homer's works.

The collection

  1. The Odyssey: A Graphic Novel (A Modern Visual Adaptation of Homer's Epic Tale of Greek Mythology for Young Adults)

    Gareth Hinds

    Gareth Hinds' masterful adaptation of Homer's classic epic poem brings the timeless story to life with stunning visuals and a faithful retelling, making it a must-have for any collection.

    For: Fans of classical literature, graphic novels, and adventure stories will appreciate this captivating interpretation of The Odyssey.

    Strengths: Hinds' beautiful watercolor illustrations, lovingly detailed artwork, and ability to balance action and character development make this a standout graphic novel adaptation.

    Skip if: The dense, detailed storytelling may overwhelm some readers, requiring a certain level of familiarity with the original poem or a strong attention span.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy graphic novel retellings of classic literature, such as Rick Riordan's Percy Jackson series, will find Hinds' Odyssey a similarly engaging and imaginative adaptation.

    “Hinds’s beautiful watercolors skillfully capture the rosy-fingered dawn, the wine-dark sea, the land of the dead, and many other settings and characters that will inspire readers.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Iliad: A Graphic Novel (A Modern Visual Adaptation of Homer's Epic War Story of Greek Mythology for Young Adults)

    Gareth Hinds

    This graphic novel adaptation of Homer's Iliad masterfully condenses the epic poem into an accessible, visually stunning work that brings the Trojan War to life.

    For: Classics enthusiasts, students of ancient literature, and anyone interested in the origins of Western storytelling will appreciate this adaptation.

    Strengths: Gareth Hinds' dynamic artwork and judicious abridgment make the complex tale of gods, mortals, and warfare engaging for a modern audience.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the plain language and abridgments occasionally sacrifice the poetic richness of the original text.

    Comparable: Fans of Hinds' other graphic adaptations of classic works, such as The Odyssey, Beowulf, and Macbeth, will enjoy his signature style in this Iliad adaptation.

    “the war is the work of angry gods and angry men.”

Frequently asked questions

What makes these graphic novels special?
Gareth Hinds' meticulous research and artistic skill bring Homer's poetry to vivid life, staying faithful to the originals while innovating in the comic medium.
Are these books suitable for all ages?
While the stories deal with mature themes, the graphic novel format makes them accessible to readers of all ages who can appreciate the epic tales.
Can I use these in the classroom?
Absolutely! The Iliad and The Odyssey graphic novels serve as excellent teaching tools for exploring classic literature, mythology, and ancient civilizations in an engaging way.
Are there more books in this series?
Yes, Gareth Hinds has adapted both The Iliad and The Odyssey into standalone graphic novels. For now, there are no further announced projects in the series, but stay tuned for future updates!
