Essential Fantasy Books for Young Adults

Dive into epic adventures and rich world-building with these classic and modern fantasy novels perfect for young adult readers.

Who these books are for

  • Teenagers and young adults seeking immersive fantasy stories
  • Fans of Ursula K. Le Guin, Patrick Rothfuss, and Neil Gaiman
  • Readers looking for character-driven, imaginative tales

Start here

If you only read one series about a young wizard, choose the Earthsea Cycle by Ursula K. Le Guin for its thought-provoking magic and coming-of-age themes.

Fantasy offers a portal to other worlds, where imagination knows no bounds and heroes emerge. For young adult readers, a great fantasy novel can ignite a lifelong love of storytelling. This curated selection features timeless classics and modern masterpieces that blend rich world-building, memorable characters, and epic adventures. Whether you're a seasoned fan or just discovering the genre, these essential fantasy books will transport you to realms of wonder and magic.

The collection

  1. A Wizard of Earthsea

    Ursula K. Le Guin

    A Wizard of Earthsea masterfully weaves a rich, layered tale of magic, morality, and self-discovery, establishing the foundation for Le Guin's beloved Earthsea series.

    For: Fantasy readers and scholars of the genre, particularly those interested in morally complex protagonists and nuanced world-building.

    Strengths: Deeply immersive world-building, a compelling and flawed protagonist in Ged, and a thought-provoking exploration of the consequences of power and the balance between light and darkness.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing deliberate and the magical system intricate, requiring close attention.

    Comparable: Compared to other fantasy novels, A Wizard of Earthsea shares elements of J.R.R. Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings in its epic scope and detailed world-building, but with a more introspective, philosophical tone.

    “New and longtime Earthsea fans will be drawn to these impressive new editions.”

  2. The Adventure Zone: Story and Song

    Griffin McElroy

    This final installment of The Adventure Zone series masterfully weaves together the characters' backstories, relationships, and the overarching threat they've faced, delivering a satisfying and emotional conclusion.

    For: Fans of the podcast and previous graphic novels, as well as readers who enjoy meta-fictional stories, humor, and fantasy adventure.

    Strengths: Exquisitely balances humor, heart, and high-stakes action, showcasing the McElroy brothers' signature wit and storytelling prowess.

    Skip if: May require deep familiarity with the series' complex characters and lore to fully appreciate; newcomers may find it challenging to jump in.

    Comparable: Fans of The Adventure Zone will also enjoy other meta-fictional comics like Penny Arcade's The Crux and The Guild Codex by Rachel Aaron, while those seeking humor-infused fantasy should check out The Order of the Stick by Rich Burlew.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Tombs of Atuan

    Ursula K. Le Guin

    This sequel to A Wizard of Earthsea deepens the world of Earthsea, exploring themes of identity, power, and the struggle between light and darkness, cementing its place in fantasy literature.

    For: Fans of epic fantasy, particularly those who appreciate complex world-building and character development.

    Strengths: Le Guin's masterful storytelling, vivid characterization, and philosophical undertones make The Tombs of Atuan a compelling and immersive read.

    Skip if: The pacing can be slower compared to action-driven fantasy, as Le Guin focuses on Tenar's inner journey and the mystical atmosphere of the Tombs.

    Comparable: In terms of Earthsea series books, it shares the same atmospheric, introspective tone with Tehanu, while The Farthest Shore features more high-stakes action and adventure.

    “New and longtime Earthsea fans will be drawn to these impressive new editions.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Farthest Shore

    Ursula K. Le Guin

    The Farthest Shore masterfully concludes the Earthsea Cycle, exploring complex themes of balance, morality, and the limits of power in a richly imagined fantasy world.

    For: Fantasy readers, especially those who appreciate thoughtful world-building and character-driven stories.

    Strengths: Le Guin's evocative prose, nuanced characterization, and thought-provoking ideas make this a standout in the series.

    Skip if: The pacing can be slow for some readers, as Le Guin delves deeply into philosophical concepts and emotional journeys.

    Comparable: Shares Earthsea's signature blend of magic, morality, and atmospheric storytelling with the other novels in the series, while introducing new themes and challenges in its conclusion.

    “New and longtime Earthsea fans will be drawn to these impressive new editions.”

Frequently asked questions

What's the best fantasy book for a reluctant reader?
Start with something relatable like The Adventure Zone, a humorous story based on a popular podcast.
Are these books heavy on magic and little on character?
No, these novels focus on the emotional journeys and growth of complex characters alongside their fantastical adventures.
Are there any graphic novels in this collection?
No, the books listed are traditional prose novels, but there are many engaging fantasy comics and graphic novels available.
