16 YA LGBTQ+ Novels for Authentic Representation

Discover a diverse selection of 16 YA novels that deliver authentic LGBTQ+ experiences and stories.

Who these books are for

  • Teenagers and young adults seeking relatable LGBTQ+ stories
  • Parents, educators, and librarians looking for inclusive YA recommendations
  • Anyone interested in exploring diverse queer voices and experiences

Start here

If you only read one book about nuanced LGBTQ+ portrayals, choose Aiden Thomas' critically acclaimed 'Cemetery Boys'.

YA literature has made significant strides in recent years in terms of representation, particularly in showcasing authentic LGBTQ+ experiences. This curated selection of 16 novels celebrates diverse queer voices and stories, from heartfelt coming-out tales to fantastical adventures featuring vibrant LGBTQ+ characters. Whether you're a teenager seeking relatable stories or an adult looking for thought-provoking explorations of identity, this collection offers a wealth of engaging and important reading material.

The collection

  1. Cemetery Boys

    Aiden Thomas

    Cemetery Boys is a groundbreaking, inclusive paranormal romance that brings together Latinx culture and LGBTQ+ themes in a vivid, spellbinding tale.

    For: Teen readers and adults interested in diverse, supernatural stories with a strong focus on identity and acceptance.

    Strengths: The novel excels at weaving together elements of Latinx culture, Mayan mythology, and queer romance, creating a rich, immersive world. Its characters are deeply human and relatable, with Yadriel's journey to self-discovery particularly resonant.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow as the story takes time to develop characters and cultural context.

    Comparable: Fans of magical realism and paranormal romance with a strong emphasis on cultural identity may also enjoy The House of Shattered Wings by Alix E. Harrow or The Poppy War by R.F. Kuang.

    “s Literature!A New York Times Bestseller!A YALSA Best Fiction for Young Adults Top Ten pick!”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Flamer

    Mike Curato

    This groundbreaking graphic novel offers a poignant, relatable coming-of-age story for LGBTQ+ teens and allies, tackling themes of self-discovery, acceptance, and resilience in a uniquely accessible format.

    For: Teen readers, especially those exploring their identities or facing bullying, will find solace and inspiration in Aiden's journey.

    Strengths: Curato's masterful storytelling and expressive artwork bring authenticity and emotional depth to Aiden's struggles, making a difficult topic both relatable and empowering.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the summer-camp setting and limited character pool somewhat restrictive compared to more expansive narratives.

    Comparable: For those who appreciate nuanced portrayals of adolescence, Flamer shares similarities with other award-winning coming-of-age stories like Persepolis and Fun Home, albeit with a unique focus on LGBTQ+ identity.

    “This is a story that will be read and reread, and for some, it will be the defining book of their adolescence.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Cemetery Boys

    Aiden Thomas

    Cemetery Boys is a groundbreaking, heart-rending debut that combines a queer paranormal romance with vivid Latinx culture and identity, making it a must-have for collections serving diverse teens.

    For: Teen readers, especially those in the LGBTQ+ and Latinx communities, will deeply resonate with and appreciate this authentic, romantic, and mystical tale.

    Strengths: Thomas masterfully weaves together elements of Latin American spirituality, family drama, and blossoming queer love, crafting a spellbinding narrative rich in cultural detail and emotional depth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the supernatural elements and slow-burn romance pace too atmospheric, requiring patience to fully immerse in the story.

    Comparable: Fans of supernatural YA romances like 'The Mortal Instruments' and 'Anna and the French Kiss' will enjoy Cemetery Boys' blend of fantasy, coming-of-age themes, and romance, set within a unique Latinx context.

    “s Literature!A New York Times Bestseller!A YALSA Best Fiction for Young Adults Top Ten pick!”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Cemetery Boys: Espíritu

    Aiden Thomas

    Cemetery Boys: Espíritu is a captivating sequel that deepens the magical world and emotional resonance of the original, with a new protagonist's struggles and relationships forming a compelling narrative.

    For: Fans of supernatural coming-of-age stories, particularly those drawn to Latinx culture and LGBTQ+ themes, will relish this sequel.

    Strengths: The novel expertly balances action, mystery, and character development, exploring complex themes of identity, grief, trauma, and the power of found family.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing uneven at times, as the story shifts between high-stakes plot points and quieter character moments.

    Comparable: Those who enjoyed the first Cemetery Boys will appreciate this continuation's similar blend of paranormal elements, emotional depth, and cultural richness.

    “This paranormal mystery centering on Latine characters delves into themes of family, love, grief, and trauma.... A richly thematic and suspenseful mystery.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Hell Followed with Us

    Andrew Joseph White

    Hell Followed with Us is a fierce, timely, and imaginative novel that confronts anti-transgender legislation and explores themes of identity, power, and belonging in a post-apocalyptic world.

    For: Fans of speculative fiction, LGBTQ+ literature, and dark, fast-paced stories will find much to appreciate in this vividly written debut.

    Strengths: The book excels at creating a compelling, diverse cast of characters and delivering high-stakes action, horror, and emotional drama in equal measure.

    Skip if: The complex plot and multiple themes can make the narrative feel dense at times, requiring close attention from the reader.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed Gideon the Ninth's blend of dark fantasy and diverse characters, or Annihilation's atmospheric, body-horror elements, will likely find Hell Followed with Us equally compelling.

    “This authentic story of consuming fury and the power of found family to heal is an excellent choice. . . .”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

Are these novels exclusively gay or focus on other orientations too?
Yes, the collection encompasses a range of sexualities and gender identities, including gay, lesbian, bisexual, pansexual, and non-binary characters.
What's the age range of these YA novels?
The books in this selection are geared towards young adults aged 13 to 19, with some appealing to a slightly older audience due to their thematic depth.
Are these novels purely fiction or do some blend genres?
The collection features a mix of contemporary, historical, fantasy, and magical realism, ensuring there's something for every reading preference within the LGBTQ+ YA genre.
