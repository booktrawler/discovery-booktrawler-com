Fantasy and Romance Novels for Young Adults

From mythical realms to forbidden love, these enchanting YA stories will transport you to new worlds.

Who these books are for

  • Teenagers and young adults seeking magical adventures
  • Fantasy and romance readers looking for their new favorite series
  • Parents and educators searching for engaging, appropriate reads
  • Anyone craving a escape into imaginative, heartfelt stories

Start here

If you only read one fantasy romance series, choose 'Her Hidden Fire' for its lush world-building and tender, star-crossed love story.

Immerse yourself in the magic of young adult fantasy and romance novels, where mythical creatures, enchanting realms, and passionate love stories await. From bestselling authors to exciting new voices, this curated collection offers something for every taste in these captivating genres. Whether you're a long-time fan of YA or simply seeking a transporting read, these imaginative tales are sure to leave you spellbound and eager for more.

The collection

  1. Strange Bedfellows A Graphic Novel

    Ariel Slamet Ries

    This award-winning graphic novel masterfully blends speculative fiction with deeply human themes, crafting a captivating story of self-discovery, love, and the blurred lines between reality and fantasy.

    For: Fans of LGBTQ+ literature, science fiction, and coming-of-age tales will appreciate Strange Bedfellows' unique blend of genres and emotional depth.

    Strengths: Ariel Slamet Ries' imaginative world-building, vibrant illustrations, and nuanced characterization shine throughout the narrative, making each page a visual treat.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing deliberate and the themes heavy, requiring a thoughtful, meditative approach to fully absorb the story's complexities.

    Comparable: Those who enjoyed other Ignatz Award-winning works, such as Witchy or Cry Wolf Girl, will likely appreciate Strange Bedfellows' similarly inventive storytelling and queer representation.

    “An ambitious, lushly detailed blending of genres and brilliant cartooning— and the chemistry between Oberon and Kon is electric.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Cruel Prince Volume 1

    Holly Black

    In this darkly enchanting debut from Holly Black, 19-year-old Jude is a mortal fighting to survive among the treacherous fey of the High Court. With a heart full of longing and a spirit forged in defiance, Jude must navigate deadly palace politics and a complicated, hate-filled attraction to Prince Cardan. As faerie civil war looms, Jude's desperate quest for belonging and power ensnares her in a web of bloodshed and intrigue, threatening the very existence of faerie realms. Prepare to be spellbound by Black's masterful storytelling and vivid world-building as Jude's dark path unfolds.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Infernal Vow (Deluxe Limited Edition) Volume 3

    Tigest Girma

    "In this stunning finale to the NYT best-selling Immortal Dark series, worlds collide and nothing is sacred. Kidan Adane faces a choice: how much more will she break to save a humanity she's already doomed? With dark academia, villainous romantic leads, and morally gray characters, this delicious romantasy will devour you whole."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Favorites A Novel

    Layne Fargo

    Step into the breathtaking world of elite figure skating with Layne Fargo's captivating novel, The Favorites. This epic love story follows Katarina Shaw, a determined Olympic hopeful, and Heath Rocha, a talented skater from a disastrous past, as their intense partnership on and off the ice catapults them to fame. With Fargo's masterful storytelling, you'll be swept up in the whirlwind romance, heart-pounding performances, and the shocking secrets revealed in the wake of their devastating split. A thrilling, emotional ride, The Favorites is a must-read for anyone who's ever dreamed of flying across the ice and finding true love in the spotlight.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. On a Sunbeam

    Tillie Walden

    On a Sunbeam masterfully weaves together romance, adventure, and coming-of-age themes in a unique, imaginative sci-fi setting, showcasing Walden's lyrical art and poignant storytelling.

    For: Teen readers and adults who appreciate thoughtful, character-driven graphic novels with a touch of science fiction will love this.

    Strengths: Interconnected timelines, vivid world-building, and an authentic portrayal of queer relationships make this a standout in the genre.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slow as the narrative unfolds gradually, with a focus on character development over action.

    Comparable: Fans of introspective, atmospheric sci-fi like Liu Cixin's Three-Body Problem or Ira Glass's graphic memoir, Three-Ring Circus, will appreciate On a Sunbeam's blend of cosmic adventure and emotional depth.

    “Walden creates the intoxicating effect of a universe as mysterious as our real one.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Once Upon a Broken Heart Collector's Edition [Limited Stenciled Edge edition]

    Stephanie Garber

    Step into a bewitching tale of true love, deception, and the price of a kiss in this enchanting collector's edition. When heartbroken Evangeline Fox bargains with the cunning Prince of Hearts, she unwittingly entangles herself in a perilous dance. As the Prince claims his promised kisses, Evangeline's fate is inextricably bound to his designs, threatening to either culminate in the ultimate happily ever after or a devastating tragedy. Dive into this sumptuously illustrated, limited edition copy of Stephanie Garber's Once Upon a Broken Heart to be spellbound by a dark fairytale of love, power, and the lengths to which dreams and desires will lead us.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Until the Clock Strikes Midnight

    Alechia Dow

    In Alechia Dow's enchanting Until the Clock Strikes Midnight, Darling and Calam, two misfit Guardians and Misfortunes, must navigate a whirlwind of mistaken identities, meddling makeovers, and uncertain hearts as they vie for a coveted mentorship by reforming Lucy Addlesberg, a hapless villager. But as they insert themselves into Lucy's life as a fictional betrothed couple, Darling and Calam find their own goals and loyalties crumbling, replaced by an unexpected attraction that might just rewrite their destinies. Witty, tender, and delightfully chaotic, this magical romance proves that happily-ever-afters can be messy, magical, and worth every risk.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The River She Became

    Emily Varga

    This epic, action-packed fantastical romance from acclaimed author Emily Varga delivers a richly detailed, mythology-inspired world, complex characters, and a thrilling quest that will captivate fans of the genre.

    For: Readers who enjoy high-stakes, romantically charged fantasies with diverse protagonists and intricate plots will love this.

    Strengths: Varga masterfully weaves together elements of Pakistani culture, fae lore, and ancient magic, crafting a unique, immersive setting. The characters' evolving relationships and the relentless pacing keep the story engaging.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, world-building-heavy first half challenging to get through before the plot really takes off.

    Comparable: Fans of books like The Mummy series, The Wrath and the Dawn, and The Darkest Part of the Forest will appreciate this enchanting, suspenseful adventure.

    “s writing will absolutely steal your heart.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Girl from the Sea: A Graphic Novel

    Lee Knox Ostertag

    Escape to the captivating coastal world of "The Girl from the Sea," a lyrical graphic novel from acclaimed author Lee Ostertag-Burkins. As 15-year-old Morgan grapples with her stifling island life and unspoken desires, an enchanting new friend, Keltie, blows open the seams of her reality. A tender tale of first love, secrets, and self-discovery unfolds, carrying readers on a poignant journey of growth and acceptance. With each turn of the page, Ostertag-Burkins weaves an irresistible narrative that will resonate with anyone yearning to break free and find their true voice.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Aurora Volume 2

    Red

    Embark on a thrilling new adventure with Kendal and his companions as they pursue their epic quest to rescue the god Vash, confront the enigmatic Void Dragon, and unravel the mysteries of the Collector's sinister plans. With vivid world-building, captivating characters, and heart-pounding action, Aurora: Volume 2 is a must-read for fans of fantasy and heroic journeys.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Magic Fish (A Graphic Novel)

    Trung Le Nguyen

    In this stunning debut graphic novel, fourteen-year-old Tien turns to fairy tales to bridge the cultural gap between himself and his Vietnamese immigrant parents. But when he realizes his true identity as a gay boy, he faces a daunting challenge: finding the words in Vietnamese to reveal his hidden self. This poignant, beautifully illustrated story explores the complexities of family, identity, and the transformative power of stories to connect us across cultures and secrets. A must-read about self-discovery and the enduring magic of tales.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Her Hidden Fire (A Good Morning America YA Book Club Pick)

    Cliodhna O'Sullivan

    Her Hidden Fire is a captivating debut that masterfully blends Irish mythology and dystopian fantasy, weaving a richly detailed world of magic and power struggles.

    For: Fans of romantasy, dark fantasy, and those drawn to unique world-building will appreciate this gripping tale.

    Strengths: The book excels at crafting a believable, immersive setting and delivering a fast-paced narrative with unexpected twists and a compelling central conflict.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the complex world-building and character dynamics require close attention to fully follow.

    Comparable: Comparable in tone and style to Sarah J. Maas's Throne of Glass series, with its blend of magic, politics, and forbidden romance.

    “This story will captivate any romantasy reader and have them eagerly awaiting the sequel.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. The Secret World of Briar Rose

    Cindy Pham

    This lush queer retelling of Sleeping Beauty weaves together escapism, grief, and dreams, offering a complex exploration of identity, hope, and redemption.

    For: Readers seeking a fantasy with intersectionally diverse characters, emotional depth, and a thought-provoking narrative.

    Strengths: Pham masterfully crafts an immersive world, balancing action and introspection, while tackling mental health and grief with nuance.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing deliberate, as it builds atmosphere and character development.

    Comparable: Fans of unique magical realism and character-driven fantasy, such as 'The Night Circus' or 'The Hazel Wood', will appreciate Pham's debut.

    “Overall, this ambitious, intelligent novel hits all its marks and provides a beautiful, challenging read.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. All-American RPG Level 1 Civilian

    Tyler Sundin

    "In this wildly inventive LitRPG, a down-on-his-luck warehouse workerNamed Sam awakens in a magical medieval world as the 'American' class, uniquely outfitted with firearms and explosives. With his American Arsenal skill and reality-warping patriotic buffs, Sam single-handedly upends the fantasy realm's power structure with a modern twist on old-school combat. Loaded with action, humor, and a tongue-in-cheek take on guns and freedom, All-American RPG is a must-read for fans of high-octane fantasy and cheeky genre-bending."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Ladies of the Knight

    Fiona Marchbank

    Embark on a thrilling quest through a colorful medieval world in Fiona Marchbank's debut graphic novel, Ladies of the Knight. Meet George, an aspiring knight determined to shatter the odds against her size and lack of training, and her prickly mentor Serafina, the league's top rider growing restless and reckless in her stunning prowess. As their unlikely partnership forms, you'll cheer them on through laughter and peril as they learn to trust each other and take the knightly world by storm. Perfect for fans of Squire, Ella Enchanted, and medieval adventure!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. The Shameful Secret of Pride Month

    Shane And Jood Lannette

    In "The Shameful Secret of Pride Month", 14-year-old Shevy navigates the complex entanglement of family, faith, and identity when her queer aunts invite the family to a Pride Parade, threatening to upend her Orthodox Jewish community's beliefs. As Shevy uncovers more hidden family secrets, she must confront her own fears and decide whether love and loyalty can bridge the chasm between tradition and acceptance. A timely and poignant coming-of-age tale that will resonate deeply with readers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What's the most popular YA fantasy romance series?
While opinions vary, 'A Court of Thorns and Roses' by Sarah J. Maas is a fan favorite.
Are these books suitable for my tween child?
Most are recommended for readers 13+, though some may be more mature in themes or content.
Can I find graphic novels or illustrated books in this collection?
Yes, the list includes titles like 'The Girl from the Sea' and 'The Magic Fish', perfect for visual storytelling enthusiasts.
Are there any standalone novels or can I expect epic series?
Both! Some books, like 'Her Hidden Fire', are complete stories, while others like 'The Cruel Prince' launch ongoing series.
