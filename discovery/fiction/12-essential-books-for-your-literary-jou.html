12 Essential Books for Your Literary Journey

Embark on a timeless adventure with these 12 must-read novels that have shaped the literary canon.

Who these books are for

  • Book clubs
  • Literature students
  • Classics enthusiasts
  • Aspiring writers
  • Anyone seeking depth in storytelling

Start here

If you only read one book about the power of storytelling, choose 'To Kill a Mockingbird' by Harper Lee.

Dive into the rich world of literature with this carefully curated list of 12 essential novels. Spanning multiple genres and eras, these iconic books have left an indelible mark on the literary canon. From the high seas of 'Moby-Dick' to the racial tensions of 'To Kill a Mockingbird', each tale offers a profound exploration of the human condition, prompting reflection and empathy in readers. Whether you're a literature student, book club member, or simply a lover of great storytelling, this collection provides the perfect starting point for your literary journey.

The collection

  1. Hound of the Baskervilles

    Arthur Conan Doyle

    Embark on a haunting adventure with the illustrious Sherlock Holmes as he uncovers the mystery of the monstrous Hound of the Baskervilles. In this gripping tale, Holmes dispatches his trusted friend Dr. Watson to the eerie Dartmoor to safeguard the new heir to the Baskerville estate from a supposed supernatural threat. A classic of detective fiction, The Hound of the Baskervilles masterfully weaves together legend, suspense, and clever detection that continues to captivate readers to this day.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. To Kill a Mockingbird

    Harper Lee

    To Kill a Mockingbird is a timeless classic that masterfully explores themes of racial injustice, childhood innocence, and the struggle for moral integrity in a deeply flawed society.

    For: This novel is a must-read for anyone seeking to understand the complexities of the American South during the mid-20th century.

    Strengths: Lee's vivid characters, particularly the precocious Scout Finch and her father Atticus, bring the story to life with their nuanced portrayals. The author's evocative prose and historical context make the novel a powerful commentary on racial tensions and the loss of innocence.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pace slow, as Lee takes time to develop her characters and build the narrative's emotional resonance.

    Comparable: For those who appreciate coming-of-age stories with strong social commentary, other notable works include The Catcher in the Rye and The Color Purple.

    “A powerful and enduring piece of literature.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Hobbit: Illustrated by Tove Jansson

    J.R.R. Tolkien

    Embark on a captivating quest with Bilbo Baggins and a mischievous band of dwarves, brought to life by the beloved illustrator Tove Jansson in this enchanting, never-before-published-in-English edition of J.R.R. Tolkien's classic fantasy tale. Jansson's whimsical, tender drawings perfectly capture the humor and magic of Bilbo's journey from the cozy confines of Bag End to the riddles and perils beneath the mountain, infusing every page with fresh charm and wonder. Join this unforgettable tale of discovery, friendship, and the underdog's triumph over adversity in a lushly illustrated edition that will delight readers of all ages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Moby-Dick or, The Whale

    Herman Melville

    Moby-Dick is a timeless masterpiece of literary fiction that explores themes of obsession, the human condition, and the power of nature, with unforgettable characters and haunting prose.

    For: Classic literature enthusiasts, scholars, and readers seeking a profound work of imagination.

    Strengths: Richly detailed whaling lore and vivid descriptions of the sea, complex exploration of human nature and morality, and its status as an enduring cultural touchstone.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, poetic language and long, philosophical digressions slow or challenging to follow.

    Comparable: It shares its epic scope and thematic depth with other classics like The Odyssey and The Scarlet Letter, and its poetic, symbolic style with works like The Great Gatsby and One Hundred Years of Solitude.

    “Vibrant, minimalist new typographic covers…. Bonus points for the heartening gender balance of the initial selections.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Go Tell It on the Mountain

    James Baldwin

    Go Tell It on the Mountain is a timeless coming-of-age tale of self-discovery, spirituality, and identity, offering lyrical prose and a powerful portrayal of African-American life in 1950s Harlem.

    For: Readers interested in American literary classics, coming-of-age stories, and exploration of identity, faith, and social justice.

    Strengths: Baldwin's evocative writing style, nuanced character development, and unflinching depiction of adolescence and the Black experience make this a landmark novel.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, poetic language and non-linear structure challenging to follow.

    Comparable: Compared to other works in this collection, Go Tell It on the Mountain shares themes of personal and societal struggle with Richard Wright's Native Son, while its poetic prose and focus on faith resonate with Ralph Ellison's Invisible Man.

    “With vivid imagery, with lavish attention to details, Mr. Baldwin has told his feverish story.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Moby-Dick

    Herman Melville

    Melville's masterpiece is a sweeping, poetic epic that explores themes of obsession, man vs. nature, and existential philosophy, making it a timeless classic.

    For: Literature students, classic fiction enthusiasts, and anyone interested in philosophical or adventure stories.

    Strengths: Meticulously researched naturalism, complex characters, and a rich, symbolic narrative that rewards close reading.

    Skip if: The Victorian-era prose can be dense and slow-paced for modern readers.

    Comparable: Fans of classic sea stories and philosophical novels like 'The Count of Monte Cristo' or 'The Picture of Dorian Gray' will appreciate Melville's work.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Go Tell It on the Mountain (Deluxe Edition) A Novel

    James Baldwin

    This classic novel masterfully explores themes of identity, religion, and social justice, offering a poignant coming-of-age story set in the vibrant context of 1950s Harlem.

    For: Readers seeking a profound, lyrical portrayal of the African American experience and the struggles of self-discovery during a pivotal moment in American history.

    Strengths: Baldwin's evocative prose paints a vivid picture of the characters and their environment, while his nuanced exploration of faith, family, and sexuality remains deeply resonant.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the writing dense and the narrative slow-burning, requiring patience and attention to fully appreciate Baldwin's literary craftsmanship.

    Comparable: Fans of rich, character-driven fiction that grapples with the complexities of race, religion, and personal growth will also enjoy works by authors like Richard Wright, Zora Neale Hurston, and Toni Morrison.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Lolita

    Vladimir Nabokov

    In Vladimir Nabokov's iconic masterpiece, Lolita, a complex web of obsession, desire, and transformation unfolds as aging Humbert Humbert's fixation on the enigmatic Dolores Haze propels him into a maelstrom of moral extremes. This unforgettable tale of love, literature, and the blurred lines between art and depravity is a towering achievement of 20th century literature that will leave readers enthralled and grappling with its profound implications.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man

    James Joyce

    Immerse yourself in the coming-of-age story of Stephen Dedalus, a young man grappling with faith, identity, and artistic ambition in early 20th century Ireland. James Joyce's seminal work, A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man, masterfully explores the formative experiences that shape a creative spirit. This classic novel, first published in 1914, remains a must-read for anyone fascinated by the journey to self-discovery and the power of art to transform our understanding of the world and ourselves.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Old Man and the Sea The Hemingway Library Edition

    Ernest Hemingway

    In this timeless, elegantly crafted tale, Hemingway masterfully weaves themes of resilience, courage, and tragic beauty around the epic struggle of an aging fisherman against a monstrous marlin. A supreme showcase of the author's distinctive prose style, The Old Man and the Sea is a 20th-century classic sure to captivate readers with its poignant, potent storytelling.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
