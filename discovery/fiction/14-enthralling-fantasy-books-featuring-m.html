14 Enthralling Fantasy Books Featuring Mischievous Creatures

Immerse yourself in the captivating world of mythical beings with these spellbinding tales.

Who these books are for

  • Fantasy readers seeking immersive tales
  • Fans of mythical creatures and folklore
  • Authors and writers looking for inspiration

Start here

If you only read one book about mischievous creatures, choose 'No Killing Goblins' for its delightful blend of humor and heart.

Delve into the captivating realm of fantasy where mythical creatures come alive in these 14 must-read books. From the mischievous antics of goblins to the ethereal beauty of fae, these enchanting tales showcase a wide array of magical beings. Whether you're a longtime fan of folklore or simply seeking an immersive escape, these spellbinding stories are sure to delight and inspire.

The collection

  1. The Wandering Inn Book One, Part One of The Wandering Inn Series

    pirateaba

    The Wandering Inn stands out with its unique blend of humor, heart, and epic fantasy elements, all set in a richly detailed world.

    For: Fans of webserials, LitRPG, and epic fantasy will relish this immersive and humorous tale.

    Strengths: Pirateaba's world-building is exceptional, with a complex, evolving world filled with memorable characters. The story balances lighthearted moments with genuine danger and emotional depth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing uneven, with sections focusing on world-building and character development alternating with rapid plot progression.

    Comparable: Fans of epic fantasy series like Mistborn or The First Law Trilogy may appreciate The Wandering Inn's similar blend of world-building, magic, and complex characters in an alternate realm.

    “Pirateaba is an amazing world builder, and as a writer, I am in awe of their story constructing skills….The Wandering Inn is a favorite... You won”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. No Killing Goblins Book One, Part Two of The Wandering Inn Series

    pirateaba

    Dive into a rich, immersive LitRPG world filled with humor, adventure, and complex characters, all expertly crafted by a talented author.

    For: Fans of fantasy, LitRPG, and character-driven stories will love this epic series.

    Strengths: The Wandering Inn excels at world-building, character development, and blending humor with high stakes, creating a unique reading experience.

    Skip if: With a dense, evolving world and multiple storylines, this book may be slow to engage readers unfamiliar with the series.

    Comparable: For readers who enjoy complex, humorous fantasy like Ready Player One or The Kingkiller Chronicle, The Wandering Inn offers a captivating LitRPG twist.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Fae and Fare Book Two, Part One of The Wandering Inn Series

    pirateaba

    Fae & Fare is a wildly imaginative and entertaining LitRPG epic that masterfully blends humor, drama, and high fantasy adventure, making it a standout in the genre.

    For: Fans of LitRPG, fantasy, and humorous storytelling will love this book.

    Strengths: Pirateaba's world-building and storytelling skills shine as the complex Innverse comes alive with memorable characters, intriguing plotlines, and endless imaginative possibilities.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slow or the focus on character development and world-building over plot progression initially underwhelming.

    Comparable: For readers of The Wandering Inn series, Fae & Fare is the next thrilling installment; for those new to the Innverse, it offers an engaging entry point into this rich and humorous fantasy world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Immortal Games Book Two, Part Two of The Wandering Inn Series

    pirateaba

    Immersive world-building and intricate plotlines make this a standout in the LitRPG genre, offering a unique blend of humor and high-stakes fantasy.

    For: Fans of epic fantasy, LitRPG, and character-driven stories will find much to appreciate in this series.

    Strengths: Pirateaba's detailed world-building, complex characters, and well-paced storytelling set this apart from other LitRPG novels.

    Skip if: The sheer scope and depth of the world and characters may overwhelm some readers, particularly those seeking a fast-paced, action-oriented tale.

    Comparable: Readers of The Wandering Inn may also enjoy other epic fantasy series like Mistborn or The Kingkiller Chronicle, as well as LitRPG works like Ready Player One or Sword Art Online.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. How to Lose a Goblin in Ten Days

    Jessie Sylva

    Step into a whimsical world of magic, mayhem, and blossoming love in Jessie Sylva's enchanting debut, How to Lose a Goblin in Ten Days. When a halfling and a goblin unwittingly claim the same cozy cottage, a bargain is struck: whoever's driven out first loses the property. As they navigate forced proximity and cultural clashes, their initial animosity gives way to a sweet enemies-to-lovers romance. But when a common threat imperils their homes and communities, this unlikely duo must put aside differences and rally goblins and halflings together. Dive into this charming tale of opposites attracting, grumpy sunshine, and there being only one bed, perfect for fans of cozy fantasy romance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Waterspell Book 1: The Warlock Waterspell, #1

    Deborah J. Lightfoot

    Immersive world-building, intricate plotting, and complex characters make this a standout in fantasy and cross-genre storytelling.

    For: Fans of eco-fantasy, mystery, and dark dystopian elements will appreciate this layered tale.

    Strengths: Deftly weaves together multiple genres, features a unique magical system, and explores themes of identity, belonging, and redemption through a richly detailed setting.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, layered storytelling and poetic language challenging to follow at first.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy complex, character-driven fantasy series like The Kingkiller Chronicle or The First Law Trilogy will find similar depth and intrigue in Waterspell.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Kill the Beast

    Serra Swift

    "Embark on a spellbinding quest of revenge, friendship, and faerie lore with Kill the Beast. Serra Swift's captivating debut weaves together elements of The Witcher and Howl's Moving Castle, delivering a gritty yet cozy fantasy tale. Join Lyssa Cadogan, an indomitable huntress, and her unlikely ally Alderic as they navigate treacherous landscapes and hidden agendas in pursuit of the immortal monster that shattered Lyssa's life. With lyrical prose and a dash of humor, Swift crafts a story of dark secrets and unlikely alliances that will keep you enthralled until the very end."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Works of Vermin

    Hiron Ennes

    Step into the eerie city of Tiliard, where beauty and decay entwine, in Hiron Ennes' thrilling debut, "The Works of Vermin." When exterminator Guy Moulène takes on a job hunting a monstrous centipede devouring the city's art, he uncovers a sinister metamorphosis reshaping Tiliard's very future. This dark fantasy/horror masterpiece will draw you in with its lush, unsettling world and the gritty determination of its unforgettable protagonist. With praise from Gillian Flynn, you won't want to miss this gripping, imaginative tale.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

While some titles may be geared towards adult readers, there are also enchanting tales suitable for young adults and even older children.

Absolutely! These fantasy books offer richly detailed worlds and engaging storylines that will keep you turning the pages.
