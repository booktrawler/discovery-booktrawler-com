12 Best Sci-Fi Space Opera Novels for Aspiring Astronauts

Explore the cosmos with these gripping space opera novels that blend adventure, politics, and alien encounters.

Who these books are for

  • Science fiction fans
  • Aspiring astronauts and space enthusiasts
  • Readers looking for immersive, imaginative stories set in space

Start here

If you only read one space opera, choose Becky Chambers' 'Record of a Spaceborn Few' for its richly detailed worldbuilding and poignant exploration of humanity's place in the galaxy.

From grand spacefaring empires to intimate stories of alien encounters, space opera novels offer a vast exploration of the human condition and our place in the universe. These 12 highly acclaimed titles showcase the best of the genre, delving into politics, adventure, and the unknown reaches of the cosmos. Whether you're a seasoned space enthusiast or simply looking to escape into an immersive, imaginative world, there's something for everyone in this curated collection.

The collection

  1. The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet

    Becky Chambers

    This novel stands out for its refreshing focus on character development, diversity, and optimistic themes in a richly imaginative sci-fi setting.

    For: Fans of character-driven space opera, diverse protagonists, and hopeful stories will love this book.

    Strengths: The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet excels at crafting a relatable protagonist, building a vibrant and inclusive cast of characters, and exploring complex social issues within a compelling narrative.

    Skip if: The pacing can be slow, with some plot threads unfolding more subtly than others, so readers seeking fast-paced action should temper their expectations.

    Comparable: If you enjoy character-centric space opera with a focus on diversity and optimistic themes, you may also appreciate works like Ann Leckie's Imperial Radch trilogy or Becky Chambers' own subsequent novels in the Wayfarers series.

    “A quietly profound, humane tour de force that tackles politics and gender issues with refreshing optimism.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. A Closed and Common Orbit

    Becky Chambers

    A Closed and Common Orbit is a character-driven, optimistic space opera with a unique AI protagonist and heartfelt exploration of identity, family, and the human condition, set against a richly imagined galaxy-spanning backdrop.

    For: Fans of character-centric, emotionally resonant science fiction seeking a hopeful, inclusive vision of the future, particularly those who enjoyed The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet.

    Strengths: Deeply nuanced, relatable characters; thoughtful examination of artificial intelligence and identity; engaging dual timeline narrative structure; vivid world-building.

    Skip if: Pacing can be slow for some readers, as the novel focuses on character development over plot action.

    Comparable: Compared to other works in the collection, A Closed and Common Orbit shares similarities with Firefly and Star Wars in its focus on a diverse, eclectic crew navigating the galaxy, while offering a more introspective, character-focused experience.

    “Warm, engaging, properly science-fictional, A Closed and Common Orbit is a very likable novel indeed.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Record of a Spaceborn Few

    Becky Chambers

    Record of a Spaceborn Few is a standout in the Wayfarers series, offering a richly detailed exploration of a unique community within the Galactic Commons universe.

    For: Readers who enjoy character-driven science fiction, space opera, and stories of community and identity.

    Strengths: Chambers masterfully weaves together a complex cast of characters, each with their own compelling story arcs and personal growth, set against a richly imagined interstellar backdrop.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slower than other space operas, as the novel focuses on character development over action-driven plot.

    Comparable: Fans of the Wayfarers series and those who enjoy character-focused science fiction like N.K. Jemisin's Broken Earth trilogy will appreciate Record of a Spaceborn Few's nuanced exploration of community and identity.

    “A quietly profound, humane tour de force that tackles politics and gender issues with refreshing optimism.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. As You Wake, Break the Shell A Novel

    Becky Chambers

    Embark on a tender and thrilling journey to the resource-poor planet of Fortune, where two unlikely outsiders, Signy the brilliant botanist and Cora the rorqual pilot, forge a deep bond that alters the course of their lives. As their relationship blossoms from cautious trust to profound love, Chambers masterfully explores the resilience of the human spirit, the power of found family, and the profound connections that make life worth fighting for even in the harshest of worlds. With its richly imagined universe and achingly beautiful prose, As You Wake, Break the Shell is a must-read for fans of heartfelt, thought-provoking science fiction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Star Shipped A Novel

    Cat Sebastian

    In Cat Sebastian's inventive space opera, "Star Shipped," readers embark on a thrilling journey through the cosmos alongside unlikely companions. When trainee pilot Amar and his AI crewmate, Zara, are unexpectedly star-shipped together, they must navigate treacherous alien encounters, ship malfunctions, and the blossoming of an unexpected bond that defies artificial intelligence. Sebastian's vivid world-building and poignant character development create a captivating, intimate tale of adventure, self-discovery, and the power of connection in a vast, mysterious universe.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. I Got Abducted by Aliens and Now I'm Trapped in a Rom-Com

    Kimberly Lemming

    Dorothy Valentine's quest for her PhD takes an unexpected detour when a lion attack lands her on a strange alien planet alongside a new furry friend. There, she falls hard for not one, but two, handsome extraterrestrials - the sexy savior Sol and the dangerous, intriguing Lok. As Dory navigates a world of dinosaurs, danger, and intergalactic attractions, she must decide whether to return to Earth or stay and explore the uncharted territories of love and adventure on this eccentric alien romance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Ode to the Half-Broken

    Suzanne Palmer

    Step into a world still grappling with the aftermath of near-apocalyptic conflict in Suzanne Palmer's moving sci-fi tale, Ode to the Half-Broken. When a worn-out robot named Be is attacked in the abandoned New York Botanical Gardens, they're forced out of decades of isolation and must confront the dark past they've tried to forget. Joined by a sassy cyborg dog and a human mechanic haunted by her own demons, Be embarks on a quest to reclaim their lost leg - and discovers a sinister force seeking to reignite old wartime hostilities. A powerful exploration of loneliness, connection, and resilience, Ode to the Half-Broken is a hopeful and unforgettable journey into a world struggling to heal and become whole again.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Orbital A Novel (Booker Prize Winner)

    Samantha Harvey

    Orbital is a masterful novel that explores the human experience through the unique lens of astronauts in space, delving into themes of existence, isolation, and our relationship with the Earth.

    For: Readers seeking a thought-provoking, lyrical work of literary fiction that blends elements of science, philosophy, and character study.

    Strengths: Harvey's evocative prose paints vivid portraits of the astronauts' daily lives, crafting a sense of intimacy and immediacy despite the extraordinary setting. The novel's themes resonate deeply, inviting reflection on our place in the universe.

    Skip if: The fragmented, episodic structure may challenge some readers, as the narrative jumps between the astronauts' present and past lives.

    Comparable: Fans of introspective, philosophically-driven fiction like Don DeLillo's 'Zero K' or Annie Proulx's 'Barkskins' may appreciate Orbital's lyrical, contemplative style and its exploration of humanity's relationship with the environment.

    “Do we look at space with an optimistic gaze? With dollar signs in our eyes? With imperial designs? With despair? I won”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Employees A Workplace Novel of the 22nd Century

    Olga Ravn

    In this chilling and exhilarating workplace novel set in the 22nd century, an interstellar crew chronicles their mundane tasks on the Six-Thousand Ship via staff reports and memos. When the vessel brings aboard enigmatic objects from a new planet, crew members develop profound and unsettling attachments, leading to tensions that threaten to boil over. With Olga Ravn's prose crackling with dark humor and foreboding, The Employees is a scathing critique of life driven by productivity, while probing the very nature of humanity in the process.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. A Memory Called Empire

    Arkady Martine

    A Memory Called Empire is a gripping space opera that skillfully weaves together intrigue, interstellar politics, and identity. When Ambassador Mahit Dzmare arrives in the sprawling Teixcalaanli Empire to replace her murdered predecessor, she must navigate treacherous court politics while uncovering the truth behind the death and protecting her independent mining station from the empire's relentless expansion. A thrilling ride full of action, deception, and cultural intrigue, this award-winning debut by Arkady Martine is not to be missed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. A Psalm for the Wild-Built A Monk and Robot Book

    Becky Chambers

    A Psalm for the Wild-Built offers a hopeful and optimistic vision of the future, exploring themes of abundance, kindness, and the value of human connection.

    For: Readers seeking a thought-provoking, character-driven science fiction story with a gentle, optimistic tone.

    Strengths: Chambers' masterful storytelling, nuanced character development, and thought-provoking philosophical ideas make this a standout work.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow, as the story focuses on introspection and relationships over high-stakes action.

    Comparable: Fans of Becky Chambers' Wayfarers series and character-centric, emotionally resonant science fiction will appreciate A Psalm for the Wild-Built's similar blend of thoughtful storytelling and compassionate world-building.

    “This was an optimistic vision of a lush, beautiful world that came back from the brink of disaster. Exploring it with the two main characters was a fun and fascinating experience.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. A Prayer for the Crown-Shy A Monk and Robot Book

    Becky Chambers

    In this uplifting novel by celebrated author Becky Chambers, join Tea Monk Dex and robot Mosscap as they embark on a poignant journey through Panga's vibrant villages and cities, seeking wisdom on what truly matters in a world where most desires are already fulfilled. With Chambers's signature warmth and insight, this story of friendship and self-discovery will resonate deeply with anyone searching for kindness, purpose, and the beauty in the everyday.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Monk and Robot A Psalm for the Wild-Built and A Prayer for the Crown-Shy

    Becky Chambers

    In this enchanting companion duology, celebrated author Becky Chambers weaves lush, imaginative worlds around her unlikely protagonists - a former monk and a robot - without ever losing sight of their deeply relatable human (and artificial) experiences. With prose as lyrical as a hymn, Chambers crafts a tender, poignant, and often humorous exploration of purpose, faith, and connection in a complex, sprawling universe. Readers will be captivated by the richly detailed settings and the indelible characters at their heart, savoring every moment of this profound, uplifting journey into the unknown.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Rapport: Friendship, Solidarity, Communion, Empathy A Tor Original

    Martha Wells

    In Martha Wells' gripping novelette, Rapport, corporate intrigue meets high-stakes adventure at Perihelion, a station on the brink of a hostile takeover. As tensions escalate, Wells masterfully weaves a tale of unlikely friendships and solidarity that will keep you on the edge of your seat. With its Hugo Award-finalist status and pulse-pounding action, Rapport is an unmissable sci-fi thriller that explores the power of empathy in the face of danger.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Space Opera

    Catherynne M. Valente

    "Space Opera is a wildly inventive, laugh-out-loud fusion of science fiction and music as humanity's future hangs in the balance of a galaxy-spanning song competition. When Earth's first contact results not in diplomacy or warfare, but a chance to shine in the Metagalactic Grand Prix, Decibel Jones and her rock band must prepare to face the strangest, most fabulous creatures in the cosmos - and save their planet in the process. Prepare to be transported to a glittering, irreverent spectacle that will rock your world."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Ancestral Night

    Elizabeth Bear

    From Hugo Award winner Elizabeth Bear comes an electrifying space opera of groundbreaking discovery and perilous peace. When salvage operators Haimey Dz and Connla Kurucz uncover a shocking truth about an ancient alien species, the universe may never be the same. Prepare to boldly venture into a dazzling, high-stakes adventure that defies genre conventions and will leave you breathless. If you're a fan of epic space opera, Ancestral Night is not to be missed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Machine A White Space Novel

    Elizabeth Bear

    Doctor Jens, a brilliant and intrepid xenobiologist, finds herself drawn into an enigmatic crisis when a routine rescue mission uncovers an ancient and modern spacecraft locked in a deadly embrace. As Jens delves into the mystery, she unwittingly stumbles upon life-altering truths. With Bear's signature blend of rollicking action, suspense, and poignant sentiment, Machine delivers a fresh, electrifying space opera that will keep you captivated from start to finish.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Persephone Station

    Stina Leicht

    Join Angel, a no-nonsense ex-marine, and her ragtag crew of benevolent outlaws on a high-stakes adventure through the criminal underworlds of the galaxy. When an exclusive job request from Rosie, owner of Monk's Bar, leads them into a tense conflict with the ruthless Serrao-Orlov Corporation, Angel and her team must choose between their own code of honor and the corrupt forces threatening the mysterious Persephone Station. With its intoxicating blend of military science fiction, queer space opera, and gritty noir, Persephone Station is a thrilling ride sure to delight fans of The Mandalorian and Cowboy Bebop.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. These Savage Stars A Companion to The Stars Are Legion

    Kameron Hurley

    In this companion to The Stars Are Legion, author Kameron Hurley propels readers into a gritty, suspenseful tale of revenge set aboard a doomed worldship hurtling towards destruction. Former spy Reyka must embark on a perilous rescue mission to save her lord's daughter from a terrorist, confronting a deadly foe she's already faced - her own war-hardened wife. With its pulse-pounding action and complex characters, These Savage Stars delivers a gripping, grimdark space opera exploring themes of love, war, and ultimate sacrifice.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. Dawn

    Octavia E Butler

    In Octavia E Butler's gripping novel Dawn, Lilith lyapo awakens centuries later to find humanity has been preserved and healed by the enigmatic Oankali, an alien race that intervened after a devastating nuclear war. Now, with Earth revitalized, Lilith must confront the Oankali's plan for her people's return - a transformation that threatens the very essence of humanity. Butler's masterful storytelling delves into complex themes of identity, power, and adaptation, making Dawn a must-read exploration of what it means to rebuild in the face of catastrophic change.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. Sunward A Novel

    William Alexander

    In a distant future grappling with galactic strife, planetary courier Captain Tova Lir mentors teenage androids, forging unlikely bonds that become her most vital resource when secrets and danger unite against her and her latest charge, the inquisitive Agatha. William Alexander's debut Sunward navigates the intersections of AI, family, and a fractured solar system, perfect for readers who cherish poignant explorations of humanity's technological and emotional destiny, à la Becky Chambers and Ursula K. Le Guin.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  22. The Infinite Sadness of Small Appliances A Novel

    Glenn Dixon

    In this inventive, near-future novel, a young sentient Roomba named Scout embarks on a poignant quest to save her beloved human family from the sinister forces of the omnipresent Grid. Through her journey of self-discovery and unlikely alliances with humans, Scout illuminates the infinite sadness and resilience of both our technological creations and our most precious human connections.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  23. Of Monsters and Mainframes

    Barbara Truelove

    Embark on a thrilling, genre-bending ride with Of Monsters and Mainframes. This stand-out sci-fi horror mashup, praised by USA Today as perfect for Murderbot fans, teams a dedicated spaceship with an unlikely squad of supernatural misfits - a werewolf, undead engineer, pharaoh, vampire, and robotic spider drones - to take down the evil Dracula. A heartwarming exploration of monster identity and friendship, this dazzling adventure blends pulp horror and classic science fiction in a must-read journey that will leave you eager for more.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  24. Buzzard

    Inez Ray

    "Buzzard soars with dark whimsy, its eerie landscape mirroring the fractured psyche of protagonist Delphine. As Ray's haunting prose weaves together childhood trauma, Creole mysticism, and the brutal realities of 19th century Louisiana, a moody, atmospheric tale emerges - a hypnotic blend of magical realism and gothic suspense that lingers in the mind like a vengeful spirit."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between science fiction and space opera?
Science fiction often focuses on hard science and technological advancements, while space opera emphasizes sweeping narratives, politics, and adventure in a sci-fi setting.
Are these books part of a series?
While some novels are standalone, others are part of ongoing series or share common universes. The page highlights standalone works for easier reading.
Are there any books for younger readers?
Yes, 'Sunward' by William Alexander and 'Dawn' by Octavia Butler are suitable for a younger adult audience due to their engaging storytelling and relatively tame content
