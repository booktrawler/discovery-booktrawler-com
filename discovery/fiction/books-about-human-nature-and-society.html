Books About Human Nature and Society

This curated collection explores the complexities of human behavior and society through dystopian, allegorical, and thought-provoking novels.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of dystopian and speculative fiction
  • Readers interested in philosophical and psychological explorations of human nature
  • Anyone seeking thought-provoking, well-written novels that challenge perspectives on society

Start here

If you only read one book about the darker aspects of human nature, choose William Golding's haunting and influential 'Lord of the Flies'.

This carefully curated collection delves into the intricacies of human nature and society through a diverse range of dystopian, allegorical, and thought-provoking novels. From classic works like '1984' and 'Gulliver's Travels' to more contemporary titles, these books offer insightful explorations of civilization, morality, and the human condition. Whether you're a fan of speculative fiction, philosophical musings, or simply well-crafted storytelling, this selection has something to offer for readers seeking to engage with deeper questions about what it means to be human in the face of societal challenges and personal struggles.

The collection

  1. Brave New World

    Aldous Huxley

    Brave New World is a seminal dystopian novel that masterfully critiques the dangers of unchecked technological progress, conformity, and the erosion of individual freedom and humanity.

    For: Literate readers interested in thought-provoking science fiction and philosophical explorations of the human condition.

    Strengths: Huxley's prescient vision and biting satire, coupled with his profound insights into human nature, make Brave New World a powerful and enduring work of literature.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the novel's dense, lyrical prose challenging and the complex themes and characters require close attention.

    Comparable: Fans of classic dystopian fiction, such as George Orwell's 1984, will appreciate Brave New World's similarly prophetic and cautionary tone.

    “Chilling. . . . That he gave us the dark side of genetic engineering in 1932 is amazing.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Voyagers A Novel

    Meg Charlton

    Voyagers is a confidently written and propulsive debut that skillfully blends elements of science fiction and literary fiction, exploring complex themes of memory, friendship, and the human condition.

    For: Fans of character-driven, thought-provoking fiction will appreciate this novel's unique blend of genres and its well-developed protagonists.

    Strengths: Charlton's prose is elegant and engaging, and she does an excellent job of balancing the fantastical elements of the story with relatable, human emotions.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow in the first half as the author sets up the characters and their backstory.

    Comparable: For readers who enjoy novels that combine science fiction and literary elements, such as Gabrielle Zevin's Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, Voyagers is a compelling and satisfying choice.

    “Charlton writes with such pristine propulsion and packs a punch on every page. This is going to be the book everyone loves and will be the talk of the town come summer.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Children of the Wild A Novel

    Kevin Powers

    In this poignant and powerful novel, Kevin Powers masterfully weaves together the lives of three young people - Roy, Samantha, and wild boy Ennis - set against the backdrop of rural Virginia and the battlefields of World War I France. Their stories intertwine in a beautiful exploration of loyalty, honor, heroism, and the enduring power of love, even in the darkest of times. With spare, exquisite prose reminiscent of Powers' award-winning debut, The Yellow Birds, Children of the Wild is a stunning, haunting love story that will resonate deeply with readers long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Gulliver's Travels

    Jonathan Swift

    "Gulliver's Travels" by Jonathan Swift is a wickedly clever satire that will delight readers who relish biting social commentary with a dose of absurd humor. First published in 1726, this classic tale follows Lemuel Gulliver as he embarks on fantastical journeys to bizarre lands where the inhabitants often serve as biting caricatures of Swift's contemporaries. With its clever wordplay, memorable characters, and scathing critique of politics, religion, and more, "Gulliver's Travels" remains an enduring masterpiece of English literature that satire enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone looking for a witty and thought-provoking read will adore.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Children of Time

    Adrian Tchaikovsky

    Adrian Tchaikovsky's Children of Time is a masterful blend of imaginative worldbuilding, complex characters, and thought-provoking themes, earning its place as a modern science fiction epic.

    For: Fans of classic space opera, post-apocalyptic fiction, and readers seeking a rich, cerebral novel with a grand scope will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Tchaikovsky's prose is vivid and immersive, his alien species are intriguingly evolved, and the novel's exploration of survival, legacy, and interspecies understanding is both engaging and intellectually stimulating.

    Skip if: The multiple narrative threads and large cast of characters can make it challenging to keep everyone straight, requiring some patience and close attention.

    Comparable: For readers who enjoy wide-ranging, character-driven science fiction with philosophical underpinnings, Children of Time shares similarities with works by Peter F. Hamilton, Stephen Baxter, and Greg Bear.

    “Children of Time is a joy from start to finish. Entertaining, smart, surprising and unexpectedly human.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Moonflow

    Bitter Karella

    Dive into the heart-pounding, queer, and eldritch horror of MOONFLOW, an unsettling journey through the depths of the Pamogo forest. When desperate mushroom grower Sarah stumbles upon the legendary King's Breakfast, an otherworldly, reality-bending feast, she's drawn into a terrifying descent into the woods' darkest corners. But she's not alone - something ancient and ravenous is awakening, hungry for her. With its stunning blend of body horror, cosmic dread, and biting social commentary, MOONFLOW is a singular, unsettling debut that will leave you breathless and disturbed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Slow Gods

    Claire North

    In Claire North's mind-bending, award-winning space opera Slow Gods, an immortal being, once a man shattered by loss and betrayal, rises from the ashes as something both divine and monstrous. Charting a catastrophic supernova's destruction and rebirth across the galaxy, this haunting tale of vengeance and purpose will captivate fans of A Memory Called Empire and redefine the boundaries of human existence in the stars.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Children of Strife

    Adrian Tchaikovsky

    In the heart-pounding finale of Adrian Tchaikovsky's acclaimed Children of Time series, a motley crew awakens to a haunting mystery aboard their long-lost ark ship. As they venture to terraformed worlds, a tangled web of secrets and disappearances threatens to unravel the fragile harmony among humans, intelligent arachnids, and a charismatic mantis shrimp captain. With its heady blend of breathtaking space exploration, pulse-pounding suspense, and refreshing twists on classic alien encounters, Children of Strife is not to be missed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Cloud Atlas A Novel

    David Mitchell

    Embark on a mesmerizing journey through time and space with David Mitchell's breathtaking Cloud Atlas, a genre-bending masterpiece that weaves six interconnected stories spanning from the 19th century to a postapocalyptic future. Witness the transformative power of human connections as lives and destinies entwine across ages, in a tale of love, betrayal, and resilience that defies conventional narrative structures. This critically acclaimed, award-winning novel will transport and inspire you, cementing its place as a timeless classic of 21st-century literature.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Cat's Cradle A Novel

    Kurt Vonnegut

    Join the unforgettable journey into a dystopian future with Kurt Vonnegut's "Cat's Cradle." This genre-bending satire masterfully weaves together dark prophecy, original theology, and sidesplitting humor, all through the eyes of a midget protagonist. A staple of 20th-century literature, "Cat's Cradle" is a must-read for those who relish Vonnegut's singular blend of irreverence, social commentary, and apocalyptic wit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. A Children's Bible

    Lydia Millet

    "In Lydia Millet's haunting, lyrical novel, 'A Children's Bible', a group of children uncovers the dark undertones of their idyllic summer camp amidst a precarious ecological apocalypse. Blending biblical allegory with a dystopian edge, Millet crafts an otherworldly tale that pierces the innocence of childhood, revealing the fragility of our relationships with nature and each other. With prose that is at once poetic and unsettling, this unsettling masterpiece reimagines the end of the world as a strange, beautiful liminal space where the boundaries between human and animal, wild and domestic, dissolve into chaos."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Lord of the Flies

    William Golding

    Lord of the Flies is a timeless and thought-provoking exploration of human nature, offering a haunting portrayal of how quickly civilization can break down.

    For: This classic novel is a must-read for anyone interested in psychology, sociology, or philosophical inquiry into the nature of humanity.

    Strengths: Golding's masterful writing crafts a gripping, suspenseful narrative that delves deeply into the inner lives and conflicts of the stranded boys, showcasing his keen insight into the darker aspects of human nature.

    Skip if: The novel's pace can be slow, especially in the early sections as the characters establish their dynamics, and some readers may struggle with the lack of clear moral guidance.

    Comparable: Fans of dystopian and survival stories like The Hunger Games and 1984 will appreciate Lord of the Flies' thought-provoking themes and tense, isolated setting.

    “One of the most complex studies of human nature and the tendencies of societal forces ever written.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. 1984 75th Anniversary

    George Orwell

    In this seminal 75th anniversary edition, George Orwell's dystopian masterpiece '1984' remains an uncanny warning about the insidious creep of totalitarianism. With its top-secret government, thought police, and surveillance state, this gripping tale plunges you into a chilling world eerily reminiscent of modern data collection and manipulation. Orwell's prophetic vision and masterful storytelling craft immerse you in the mind-numbing horror and desperate humanity of Winston and Julia's rebellion against Big Brother's suffocating control. This enduring classic is an unforgettable, unsettling journey into the darkest depths of human nature, warning and educating with equal ferocity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Animal Farm 75th Anniversary Edition

    George Orwell

    "First published in 1945, George Orwell's timeless allegory of power abuse, 'Animal Farm', is a searing critique of totalitarian regimes. Through the eyes of a wise and witty pig named Napoleon, Orwell illuminates the dangers of unchecked ambition, propaganda, and the erosion of individual freedom. With its piercing prose and dark humor, 'Animal Farm' remains a cautionary tale for our times, urging readers to question authority and resist the forces that seek to dominate and oppress."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Flights Nobel Prize and Booker Prize Winner

    Olga Tokarczuk

    Embark on a mesmerizing journey through time and space with Olga Tokarczuk's Nobel Prize-winning masterpiece, Flights. This visionary novel weaves lyrical meditations on travel and the human condition with haunting, intricately woven tales of identity, longing, and transformation. From the secret return of Chopin's heart to the surreal vanishings and reappearances of loved ones, each story probes the mysteries we carry within, both as physical beings and wanderers through life. Prepare to be enchanted and unsettled by Tokarczuk's enchanting, complex storytelling as she deftly explores what it means to be in motion, both outwardly and inwardly.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Pedro Páramo

    Juan Rulfo

    In Pedro Páramo, Juan Rulfo masterfully weaves a haunting, surreal tale of one man's quest to uncover the truth about his elusive father, only to find himself entangled in the dark, haunted history of the town of Comala. This groundbreaking novel, widely hailed as a 20th-century masterpiece, explores themes of family, power, and the blurred lines between reality and illusion, influencing generations of Latin American writers along the way. With a new, authoritative translation and a foreword by Gabriel García Márquez, Pedro Páramo remains an essential and deeply captivating read for anyone drawn to literary fiction's most innovative and thought-provoking works.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The Last Samurai

    Helen DeWitt

    Helen DeWitt's inventive and unsettling debut, The Last Samurai, follows Sibylla, a single mother, as she raises her precocious son Ludo without a father figure. Obsessed with the film Seven Samurai, Ludo embarks on a perilous search for his unknown dad. A daring blend of philosophy, childcare, and kidnap caper, this cult classic explores the limits of parental ambition and the search for identity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. The Rings of Saturn

    W G Sebald

    Embark on a captivating, unconventional journey through the English coastline with W.G. Sebald's The Rings of Saturn. This virtuosic work of literary nonfiction weaves together profound musings on history, culture, and the human condition, as a peripatetic narrator encounters an astonishing array of characters, artifacts, and landscapes. With its innovative blend of fact, fiction, and photography, this "astonishing masterpiece" will enchant and unsettle readers, inviting them to reflect on the past, the present, and the mysteries of memory and identity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Austerlitz

    W.G. Sebald

    Austerlitz is a masterpiece of historical fiction that explores identity, memory, and the Holocaust through a unique narrative structure and lyrical prose.

    For: Literate readers seeking a profound, philosophical, and emotionally resonant work of Holocaust literature.

    Strengths: Sebald's evocative, fragmented storytelling, the depth of his characters' psychological journeys, and his exploration of the interplay between history, memory, and personal identity.

    Skip if: The non-linear, meandering style can be challenging for some readers, requiring patience and close attention.

    Comparable: Fans of literary fiction and Holocaust narratives may also appreciate works by Primo Levi, Imre Kertész, and Martin Amis, for their shared themes and lyrical prose.

    “Sebald stands with Primo Levi as the prime speaker of the Holocaust and, with him, the prime contradiction of Adorno”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. Green City Wars

    Adrian Tchaikovsky

    Immerse yourself in a gritty future where animals toil unseen beneath humanity's solar cities. Skotch, a resourceful raccoon PI, takes on a lucrative case when a valuable mouse goes missing, plunging him into the heart of the Green City wars. This engrossing noir tale, blending the wit of Philip Marlowe with the charm of Redwall, will keep you on the edge of your seat as Skotch uncovers the secrets and lies that threaten the very fabric of the animal underworld.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. Somewhere Beyond the Sea

    TJ Klune

    Dive into a mesmerizing tale of forbidden love and second chances with TJ Klune's heart-stirring novel, Somewhere Beyond the Sea. When navigator Dani Ramos returns to his hometown years after a shattering summer, he's forced to confront the ocean of emotions he never thought he'd feel again - until he reconnects with the one person able to chart a course for his heart: Ryan, his first love and the reason he left in the first place. Klune masterfully weaves together themes of identity, family, and the power of embracing one's true self in this poignant, deeply romantic exploration of what it means to find home in both the places we're from and the people we love.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  22. Wild Seed

    Octavia E Butler

    In this masterful tale of immortals, Octavia E Butler weaves an unforgettable epic of power, seduction, and transformation across centuries. Doro, an ancient spirit who sustains his life by possessing humans, seeks to breed gifted empires to obey him. But his world is upended by Anyanwu, a mysterious entity with healing and regenerative powers. Their collision course becomes a mesmerizing, generations-spanning dance of desire, dominance, and metamorphosis that forever alters the destiny of humanity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  23. Sky Full of Elephants A Novel

    Cebo Campbell

    "Sky Full of Elephants" by Cebo Campbell is a mesmerizing debut novel that weaves together elements of magical realism, historical research, and powerful storytelling set in Nigeria during the 1967-70 Biafran War. Through his vivid prose and complex characters, Campbell delves into the brutal realities of conflict while also celebrating resilience, community, and the supernatural threads that bind us. This gripping tale will captivate readers drawn to unique cultural narratives, historical fiction, and magical realism, offering a rich and haunting reading experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  24. You Weren't Meant to Be Human A Novel

    Andrew Joseph White

    Immerse yourself in Andrew Joseph White's unsettling debut, where an alien infestation riddles rural West Virginia with hives in exchange for human loyalty and sacrifice. Crane, a transformative figure, seeks salvation among these twisted colonies, falling for a brutal ex-Marine branded Levi. But when Levi impregnates Crane and the hive demands they birth their aberrant child, Crane's desperation unleashes a devastating spiral of violence and bloodshed. A visceral, deeply personal horror story that echoes the works of Stephen Graham Jones and Eric LaRocca, White's debut will draw you in with its haunting exploration of marginalized existence.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  25. Heart of Darkness

    Joseph Conrad

    Immerse yourself in the haunting, psychologically complex world of Joseph Conrad's Heart of Darkness. This enduring masterpiece exposes the harsh realities of colonialism through the experiences of Captain Marlow as he ventures into the depths of the Congo. Navigate Marlow's three encounters with darkness - the unforgiving wilderness, the cruelty of European oppressors, and the sinister darkness within humanity itself. A gripping, symbolic tale woven within a frame narrative, Heart of Darkness remains a powerful exploration of the human condition and the blurred lines between civilization and savagery.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  26. Cloud Cuckoo Land A Novel

    Anthony Doerr

    "From Pulitzer Prize winner Anthony Doerr, comes a sweeping, imaginative novel that traverses centuries and continents, weaving together the lives of intriguing characters - from a 15th century orphan to octogenarian actors to an interstellar stowaway. Transport yourself into worlds in peril as you experience the power of storytelling and the resilience of the human spirit. This breathtaking tale of hope, courage, and the enduring magic of literature is sure to enchant readers and leave a lasting impression."

Frequently asked questions

Are there any books in this collection that are considered classic dystopian works?
Yes, George Orwell's '1984' and 'Animal Farm' are renowned classics that have shaped the dystopian genre.
Can I find any books with allegorical themes in this collection?
Yes, Jonathan Swift's 'Gulliver's Travels' is a classic example of an allegorical novel, while 'Lord of the Flies' also contains strong allegorical elements.
Are there any books on this list that deal with post-apocalyptic scenarios?
While not the primary focus, several novels touch on post-apocalyptic elements, such as 'The Last Samurai' and 'Cloud Cuckoo Land', which explore the aftermath of catastrophic events
