12 Gripping Books About Assassins That Will Keep You on the Edge

From ancient Rome to fantasy realms, these electrifying stories of assassins will captivate you with their intrigue and suspense.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of historical fiction and ancient cultures
  • Readers of fantasy, particularly epic fantasy
  • Those fascinated by the art and psychology of assassination
  • Book clubs seeking complex, thought-provoking discussions
  • Anyone looking for a thrilling page-turner with intricate plots
  • Literary enthusiasts of character-driven narratives

Start here

If you only read one book about assassins, choose 'Royal Assassin' by Robin Hobb – a masterpiece of character development and world-building that will leave you breathless.

Step into the shadowy world of assassins with this captivating collection of 12 books. From the calculating killers of ancient Rome to the mystical artisans of fantasy realms, these gripping stories explore the art, psychology, and morality of taking a life. Dive into historical fiction that reimagines the Roman Empire's most feared agents, or immerse yourself in epic fantasies where assassins wield magic and intrigue. Whether you're drawn to literary character studies or fast-paced action, this diverse selection of titles offers something for every reader who craves a thrilling tale of deadly skill and cunning.

The collection

  1. The Princess Knight A Novel

    Cait Jacobs

    In this captivating debut fantasy, a determined princess sets out to win back her prince despite his refusal of her proposal. Clía follows Domhnall to the prestigious military academy, where she must prove herself in brutal dueling arenas to gain his respect. But sparks fly when she captures the attention of Warrior Ronan, who finds himself intrigued by her spirit and charm. As a real war looms, the trio must navigate love, loyalty, and self-discovery amidst the swashbuckling adventures and heart-pounding battles. A delightful, enchanting tale that will leave you charmed and eager for more!

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Warrior Princess Assassin A Novel

    Brigid Kemmerer

    In Brigid Kemmerer's addictive adult fantasy debut, interwoven fates and forbidden passions ignite amidst political intrigue and ancient magic. Meet Princess Jory, duty-bound to secure her kingdom's survival through a marriage of convenience with feared warrior King Maddox Kyronan. But their budding desires are threatened by Jory's secret: her family's magic is fading. Enter Asher, a former nobleman turned deadly assassin, whose job to kill Jory and Ky sets him on a collision course with his own heart. Will this trio navigate treacherous alliances, hidden deceptions, and their own increasingly irresistible attractions to save their realms and discover the true cost of love? With pulse-pounding action, lush world-building, and smoldering romance, Warrior Princess Assassin is your next obsession

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Mort A Discworld Novel

    Terry Pratchett

    Join Death's cheerful if hapless apprentice Mort on a fantastical journey through the Discworld as he discovers the grim reality of his "dream job" working for the Grim Reaper. But when Mort falls for his boss's daughter Ysabell, hilarious hijinks ensue in this witty, satirical tale of love, life, and the afterlife from New York Times bestselling author Terry Pratchett.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Assassin's Apprentice

    Robin Hobb

    Assassin's Apprentice is a masterful, character-driven fantasy that draws readers into a richly detailed world of politics, magic, and coming-of-age, with a unique protagonist and compelling story.

    For: Fantasy readers seeking immersive, thoughtful storytelling with complex characters and world-building.

    Strengths: Deeply realized characters, especially the complex, conflicted Fitz; evocative world-building; nuanced exploration of power, privilege, and morality.

    Skip if: Pacing can be slow for readers seeking fast-paced action; some may find the magic system and politics complex.

    Comparable: Fans of character-centric fantasy like George R.R. Martin, Glen Cook, or Patrick Rothfuss will appreciate Assassin's Apprentice's similar blend of politics, magic, and coming-of-age themes.

    “A gleaming debut in the crowded field of epic fantasies . . . a delightful take on the powers and politics behind the throne.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Royal Assassin

    Robin Hobb

    This critically acclaimed fantasy novel delivers richly detailed world-building, complex characters, and a gripping plot that explores the psychological toll of violence and the bonds of loyalty.

    For: Fantasy readers seeking a deep, character-driven story with political intrigue and epic scope.

    Strengths: Robin Hobb's masterful storytelling, vivid prose, and nuanced exploration of morality and identity make this a standout in the genre.

    Skip if: At over 900 pages, this is a long, dense novel that may not appeal to readers seeking a quicker, more action-oriented fantasy.

    Comparable: For fans of George R.R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire series, Hobb's Farseer Trilogy offers a similarly immersive, character-focused fantasy experience.

    “Fantasy as it ought to be written . . . Robin Hobb”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Assassin's Quest

    Robin Hobb

    Assassin's Quest is a masterful, emotionally resonant conclusion to the Farseer Trilogy, delivering a rich tapestry of complex characters, politics, magic, and epic adventure.

    For: Fans of deep, character-driven fantasy and those who appreciate a satisfying trilogy arc will find much to love.

    Strengths: Robin Hobb's nuanced characterizations, world-building, and plotting make this a standout in the genre. The story's emotional depth and thematic resonance linger long after the final page.

    Skip if: The dense, detailed world and character development may overwhelm some readers, requiring a dedicated investment of time and focus.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate the character-centric, slowly unfolding storytelling of George R. R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire series may enjoy Assassin's Quest for its similarly complex world and character dynamics.

    “An enthralling conclusion to this superb trilogy, displaying an exceptional combination of originality, magic, adventure, character, and drama.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Crown Me Dead a Dark Fantasy Romance

    LIV Zander

    A dark, morally complex fantasy romance that subverts traditional hero/villain tropes, Crown Me Dead offers a gripping tale of power, seduction, and betrayal.

    For: Fans of dark fantasy and romance will devour this twisted, gothic tale.

    Strengths: Masterful world-building, complex characters, and a gripping plot that keeps readers on edge.

    Skip if: Some may find the graphic violence and disturbing themes off-putting.

    Comparable: Readers of Sarah J. Maas's Throne of Glass series or Holly Black's The Cruel Prince will appreciate this morally gray, dark fantasy romance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Crown Me Yours a Dark Fantasy Romance

    LIV Zander

    This dark fantasy romance stands out for its bold, villainous protagonist and the unconventional, violent premise.

    For: Fans of morally complex anti-heroes, dark fantasy, and romance with a twist will devour this unique tale.

    Strengths: Crown Me Yours excels at crafting a ruthless, seductive female lead and a captivating, doomed love story amidst a richly detailed, dark world.

    Skip if: Some readers may struggle with the graphic violence and disturbing themes.

    Comparable: Readers of The Poppy War and The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August will appreciate the dark fantasy elements and morally ambiguous characters in Crown Me Yours.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

Are these books suitable for young adult readers?
Some titles may appeal more to adult readers due to mature themes, violence, or complex storytelling. However, many can be enjoyed by mature teens.
What genres do these assassin books cover?
The selection spans historical fiction, epic fantasy, dark fantasy, and literary fiction, offering a diverse range of styles and tones.
Can I find audiobook versions for these titles?
Yes, most of the featured books are available in audio formats, making them perfect for a commute or workout listen.
Are there any standalone assassin novels in this list?
While some books are part of a series, there are standalone titles like 'Mort' by Terry Pratchett and 'Crown Me Dead' by LIV Zander, offering a self-contained assassin story.
