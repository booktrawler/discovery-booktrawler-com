Best Multicultural & Diverse Books to Read

Discover a rich tapestry of stories from diverse cultures and experiences in these acclaimed novels.

Who these books are for

  • Book clubs seeking diverse reading material
  • Students of multicultural literature
  • Readers interested in global perspectives
  • Educators looking for inclusive teaching resources

Start here

If you only read one book about cultural diversity, choose 'The Joy Luck Club' by Amy Tan, a poignant portrayal of the immigrant experience.

Dive into the vibrant world of multicultural literature with this curated collection of acclaimed novels. These stories celebrate the richness and diversity of human experience, weaving together tales from around the globe. From the immigrant journey to the struggles of global minorities, each book offers a unique window into the lives and perspectives of diverse characters.

The collection

  1. Daughter of the Moon Goddess A Novel

    Sue Lynn Tan

    This captivating epic fantasy, inspired by the legend of Chang'e, the Chinese moon goddess, transports readers to an immersive world of magic, romance, and intrigue.

    For: Fantasy readers seeking a rich, immersive tale with strong female leads, forbidden romance, and mythological inspiration will adore this book.

    Strengths: Tan's vivid world-building, lyrical prose, and complex characters make this a standout debut in the fantasy genre, with a story that balances action, romance, and emotional depth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slow in the early chapters as Tan establishes the world and characters, but the payoff is well worth the investment of time.

    Comparable: Fans of Stephanie Garber's Caraval series, Sarah J. Maas's Throne of Glass, and Leigh Bardugo's Grisha Trilogy will appreciate the epic scope, magical systems, and romantic elements of Daughter of the Moon Goddess.

    “The prose is lovely and fluid, lush descriptions of magic and immortal life buoying the narrative.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Joy Luck Club A Novel

    Amy Tan

    In this captivating novel, four Chinese immigrant mothers form the Joy Luck Club, gathering to share memories, gossip, and the secrets of their pasts. As each woman reveals her story, the intricate, often fraught ties between mothers and daughters are laid bare. With Tan's masterful prose and a deep understanding of the complex emotions at play, The Joy Luck Club offers a nuanced, moving exploration of the power of love and the legacy of silence. A timeless, bestselling classic that will resonate with readers long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Teller of Small Fortunes

    Julie Leong

    This enchanting debut novel offers a heartwarming tale of found family, self-discovery, and the power of small acts in a richly imagined fantasy world.

    For: Fantasy readers seeking a cozy, uplifting story with diverse characters and a touch of magic will love this.

    Strengths: Leong masterfully crafts a captivating narrative with vivid world-building, relatable characters, and a perfect balance of whimsy and substance.

    Skip if: Some may find the pacing a tad slow as the story meanders through the characters' daily lives, but this only adds to the book's cozy charm.

    Comparable: Fans of Sarah Addison Allen, Alice Hoffman, and Katherine Arden's cozy fantasies will find a delightful new voice in Julie Leong's The Teller of Small Fortunes.

    “Warm, welcoming, and endlessly inventive... feels like being invited in for tea with a dear friend (and expert storyteller!).”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Tusk Love

    Thea Guanzon

    Join Guinevere, a merchant's daughter suffocating under societal expectations, and Oskar, a brooding half-orc on a journey to find his true family, as sparks of passion ignite amidst the danger of the Amber Road. This lush fantasy romance, penned by New York Times bestseller Thea Guanzon in collaboration with Critical Role's Jester Lavorre, delivers a heartwarming, saucy tale of unlikely love and self-discovery that will keep you turning the pages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Fox Wife A Novel

    Yangsze Choo

    In Yangsze Choo's captivating new novel, THE FOX WIFE, a gifted detective and a vengeful fox spirit weave an intricate tale of myth and mystery in early 20th century Manchuria. As Bao unravels the enigma of a courtesan's death, he must navigate the blurred lines between gods and mortals. Meanwhile, Snow's quest for justice leads her from China to Japan. This epic tale of love, loss, and ancient curses pulses with unforgettable characters and a world of wonder that will keep you mesmerized until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Poet Empress

    Shen Tao

    This sweeping debut fantasy offers a lush, deadly world where poetry magic is power, making it perfect for fans of epic and intimate storytelling.

    For: Readers of adult epic fantasy, particularly those who enjoy darker themes, complex worldbuilding, and strong female protagonists.

    Strengths: Intricate court politics, poetic prose, and a compelling heroine navigating a treacherous palace environment.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, layered storytelling and mature content challenging to digest.

    Comparable: Compared to other epic fantasies, The Poet Empress stands out for its unique blend of Chinese-inspired culture, poetic magic, and a focus on a young woman's survival and empowerment in a cruel world.

    “Shen Tao creates a world both terrible and wonderful in The Poet Empress that will enrapture readers even as it horrifies them.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Poet Empress

    Shen Tao

    In the dying Azalea Dynasty, desperation fuels the story of Wei Yin, a young woman forced to become a concubine to a violent prince. As famine ravages the land and poetry magic all but vanished, Wei must harden her heart, rely on her wits, and learn to become dangerous herself. Trapped between a brutal palace and the edge of civil war, Wei's journey spirals into a dark reckoning as she discovers the forbidden art of poetry and the most potent magic of all - the spell of her own survival, bound to a haunting ballad of death and love.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Pool House A Novel

    Mary H.K. Choi

    In this bold, genre-defying debut, Mary H.K. Choi masterfully navigates the complexities of mother-daughter relationships, Hollywood, and the struggles of growing up and growing older. When Stevie flees her suffocating life with her eccentric, grieving actress mother Moon to the subterranean world of a dead-end job, they're forced into a cramped, glass-walled pool house in their own backyard. With the added presence of Adam, Moon's late TV son and Stevie's longtime crush, this razor-sharp exploration of class, fame, identity, and family deception simmers with wit, humor, and raw emotion, offering a refreshing, deeply relatable portrayal of women's lives in Los Angeles.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Pachinko (National Book Award Finalist)

    Min Jin Lee

    Reading Min Jin Lee's sweeping epic Pachinko is an immersive odyssey that wrenches your heart and lifts your spirit. This masterful tale of a Korean family's resilience and longing, spanning decades and continents, is a poignant reminder of the human spirit's capacity to endure, adapt, and ultimately, to find beauty in the darkest of times. Each pageturn is a revelation, weaving a rich tapestry of love, sacrifice, and the unbreakable bonds of family that will resonate long after the final, tear-streaked page.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Island of Sea Women A Novel

    Lisa See

    In this stunning historical novel, Lisa See masterfully portrays the unbreakable bond between two young Korean women, Mi-ja and Young-sook, as they navigate their destinies on the island of Jeju. From Japan's colonial rule to World War II and beyond, their friendship is tested by the forces of war, family secrets, and differences that seem insurmountable. With vivid storytelling and a richly detailed setting, The Island of Sea Women is a powerful ode to the haenyeo, the extraordinary women who dive for sea treasures, that will captivate readers and leave them in awe of the strength and resilience of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Free Food for Millionaires

    Min Jin Lee

    Immerse yourself in the mesmerizing world of Manhattan's elite through Min Jin Lee's debut novel, Free Food for Millionaires. Casey Han, a Korean-American aspiring to break free from her immigrant parents' expectations, navigates a treacherous landscape of debt, relationships, and social privilege. This addictive and insightful portrayal of intergenerational struggle, class disparity, and the American Dream will captivate readers with its sharp-eyed exploration of a community clinging to tradition in the face of rapid change.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. American Hagwon

    Min Jin Lee

    This sweeping, masterful novel explores the complexities of family, identity, and the immigrant experience through the Koh family's journey across continents and generations, weaving a profound meditation on what constitutes a meaningful life.

    For: Readers who appreciate literary fiction, nuanced character studies, and stories that delve into the intricacies of family dynamics and cultural identity will find much to cherish in this epic tale.

    Strengths: Min Jin Lee's extraordinary writing, richly detailed settings, and her ability to balance intimate character moments with broad social commentary make American Hagwon a standout work of contemporary literature.

    Skip if: The novel's dense, layered narrative and large cast of characters may initially overwhelm some readers, requiring patience and attention to fully immerse oneself in the story.

    Comparable: Fans of Pachinko and other family sagas that explore themes of identity, belonging, and the immigrant experience, such as The Namesake or The Joy Luck Club, will find much to appreciate in American Hagwon's similar scope and depth.

    “An absolutely propulsive read. If your faith in either humanity or storytelling has dimmed, American Hagwon will make it blaze again.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. The Lion Women of Tehran

    Marjan Kamali

    Immerse yourself in the vibrant world of Tehran's female dynasts through Marjan Kamali's captivating novel, 'The Lion Women of Tehran'. This epic tale weaves the intricate lives of three generations of the Azadi family - from the tumultuous years of the Shah's reign to the Islamic Revolution's aftermath - through the eyes of its indomitable women. With lush prose and a keen eye for period detail, Kamali brings to life the fierce devotion, quiet defiance, and secret dreams of these remarkable women who shaped their family's fate amidst the tumult of modern Iran.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. The Goose Girl

    Shannon Hale

    In Shannon Hale's captivating The Goose Girl, Crown Princess Ani of Kildenree flees for her life after her lady-in-waiting orchestrates a treacherous plot. Hiding in plain sight as a goose girl, Ani must master the forbidden language of animals and forge her own path to reclaim her rightful throne and find her voice in a world where she once felt silenced. This enchanting and empowering tale of self-discovery, courage, and the power of one's true voice is a must-read for fans of classic fairy tales and YA fantasy.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Notes of a Crocodile

    Qiu Miaojin

    Immerse yourself in the poignant, coming-of-age tale of "Notes of a Crocodile" by Qiu Miaojin, a winner of the 2018 Lucien Stryk Asian Translation Prize. Set in 1980s Taipei, this postmodern novel follows Lazi, a lesbian narrator and misfit, as she navigates love, friendship, and artistic identity at Taiwan's most prestigious university. Through a kaleidoscope of diaries, vignettes, and aphorisms, experience the process of liberation and radical self-discovery in a story of queer teenagers finding their place in a society still reeling from martial law's end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Daughters of the Sun and Moon A Novel

    Lisa See

    This powerful historical novel weaves a rich tapestry of the lives of three Chinese women navigating post-Civil War Los Angeles, exploring themes of resilience, friendship, and the unbreakable human spirit.

    For: History buffs, fans of strong female protagonists, and anyone interested in the Chinese American experience will find this book compelling.

    Strengths: Lisa See masterfully crafts complex, multidimensional characters and immerses readers in the gritty, often brutal realities of 19th century LA, while also celebrating the enduring bonds of female friendship.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the historical detail and cultural context dense, requiring patience and attention to fully appreciate the story.

    Comparable: Those who enjoyed Lisa See's other works, such as Snow Flower and the Secret Fan or The Tea Girl of Hummingbird Lane, will appreciate the similar blend of historical fiction, cultural insight, and poignant character studies.

    “See offers a stunning piece of historical fiction based in truth. It will touch readers with the characters”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Cloud Cuckoo Land

    Anthony Doerr

    Cloud Cuckoo Land, Anthony Doerr's sweeping epic, weaves together the intertwined stories of nine characters from different eras and places, from an ancient library to a distant spaceship, creating an unforgettable journey that explores the power of storytelling, resilience, and hope in the face of chaos and destruction. With his signature lyrical prose and profound insight, Doerr masterfully crafts a testament to the indomitable human spirit, making this a must-read for fans of storytelling at its finest.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Parable of the Sower

    Octavia E Butler

    In a dystopian near-future, Octavia Butler's prophetic novel 'Parable of the Sower' plumbs the depths of human resilience, illuminating the unbreakable spirit that arises from the ashes of societal collapse. Through the harrowing journey of 15-year-old Lauren Olamina, a young founder of the Earthseed religion, Butler crafts a searing exploration of climate change, class warfare, and the unyielding power of hope in the face of unrelenting turmoil. A prescient and haunting masterwork, 'Parable of the Sower' heralds a new era of speculative fiction, where darkness and light ignite in a fierce, unquenchable flame.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Razorblade Tears

    S a Cosby

    In this explosive debut, S.A. Cosby weaves a tense thriller as two former convicts, a black ex-cop and a white ex-military man, join forces to avenge their murdered sons, diving into a world of racial hatred and violent retribution. A gripping, hard-hitting page-turner that probes the darkest aspects of American society.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. Parable of the Talents

    Octavia E Butler

    In this haunting, prescient novel, Octavia E. Butler crafts a chilling vision of a dystopian America where fundamentalism, xenophobia, and authoritarianism have consumed the nation. Against this bleak backdrop, Marguerite "Margo" Younger emerges as a fierce and resilient young heroine fighting to preserve humanity's last vestiges of empathy and compassion. A searing exploration of oppression, resistance, and the indomitable human spirit, 'Parable of the Talents' is a timeless classic that will leave readers pondering the fragility of our shared values and the unbreakable will to survive.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. A Fortune of Sand A Novel

    Ruta Sepetys

    Step into the glamour and shadow of 1920s Detroit in Ruta Sepetys' captivating new novel, A Fortune of Sand. Marjorie Lennox, a spirited young woman suffocated by her controlling family, seizes a chance at artistic freedom at an exclusive retreat backed by a mysterious patron. But as she delves deeper into his world, sinister secrets and missing women threaten to trap her in a labyrinth of power and control. Be swept away by this gothic, historical page-turner that illuminates the often-overlooked stories of women fighting for their truths.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What makes these books diverse?
Each novel showcases unique perspectives, cultures, and experiences not typically represented in mainstream literature.
Are these books suitable for all ages?
Content ranges from young adult to adult themes; some titles may not be appropriate for very young readers.
Can I find these books in my local library?
Most titles are widely available in public libraries and can also be purchased online or at independent bookstores.
