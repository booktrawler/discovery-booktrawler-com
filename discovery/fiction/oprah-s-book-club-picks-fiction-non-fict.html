Oprah's Book Club Picks: Fiction & Non-Fiction Recommendations

Discover Oprah's handpicked book club selections, including critically acclaimed fiction and non-fiction titles for meaningful reading experiences.

Who these books are for

  • Book clubs seeking diverse, thought-provoking reads
  • Individuals looking for inspiring, emotionally resonant fiction
  • Non-fiction enthusiasts interested in memoir, history, and self-improvement

Start here

If you only read one book from Oprah's Book Club, choose 'The Calamity Club' by Kathryn Stockett, a gripping exploration of friendship and resilience.

Since its inception, Oprah's Book Club has introduced readers to exceptional, impactful books. These carefully curated selections from Oprah's Book Club offer a wide range of styles, genres, and themes. Whether you're part of a book club or an avid solo reader, these thought-provoking choices provide ample opportunities for discussion, self-reflection, and emotional connection.

The collection

  1. The Librarianist A Novel

    Patrick deWitt

    In this charming and poignant novel, Patrick deWitt masterfully weaves together the whimsical tale of Bob Comet, a retired librarian, as he navigates the quiet rhythms of his Portland life. From his chance encounters with eccentric strangers to the painful secrets of his childhood, Bob's story is a delightful and unexpected exploration of the beauty found in the everyday. DeWitt's signature humor and affection for his quirky characters make this a must-read for anyone who's ever found solace in the pages of a book. The Librarianist is a heartwarming celebration of the introvert's condition and the magic in the mundane.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Borrowed Life of Frederick Fife A Novel

    Anna Johnston

    In Anna Johnston's charming debut, The Borrowed Life of Frederick Fife, an unlikely mistake transforms an elderly loner's bleak future into a heartwarming adventure of found family and second chances. Meet Fred, the kindly octogenarian mistaken for a missing nursing home resident, and Denise, the recently betrayed caregiver who finds unexpected joy as Fred walks in another man's shoes. With humor, warmth, and wit, Johnston's zany tale of mistaken identity becomes a poignant exploration of forgiveness, redemption, and the power of kindness to mend lives. Fans of A Man Called Ove and Remarkably Bright Creatures will adore this witty, uplifting novel.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Whistler A Novel

    Ann Patchett

    Whistler is a masterful, emotionally resonant novel that explores the complexities of family, memory, and second chances with Patchett's signature lyrical prose and profound insights.

    For: Lovers of character-driven fiction, family sagas, and literary storytelling will appreciate this moving exploration of human connection and its power to transform lives.

    Strengths: Patchett's vivid characterization, nuanced exploration of relationships, and ability to balance the intimate and the universal make Whistler a standout.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the non-linear structure and introspective focus a slow burn, requiring patience to fully engage with the characters' journeys.

    Comparable: Fans of Patchett's other works, such as Bel Canto and Commonwealth, will find similar themes of love, loss, and the enduring bonds of family and friendship woven throughout Whistler's pages.

    “s a simple yet soaring tale of love, memory and one extraordinary horse.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Disgrace A Novel

    J. M. Coetzee

    A scorching, critically acclaimed novel that probes the depths of human frailty and social upheaval, J.M. Coetzee's Disgrace follows the downfall of David Lurie, a professor whose obsessive desires lead to professional ruin and a desperate retreat to his estranged daughter's farm. There, he and Lucy must confront the brutal new reality of post-apartheid South Africa, and the tangled, painful bond between them. A searing, compulsively readable exploration of identity, morality, and redemption.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Still Life: A GMA Book Club Pick A Novel

    Sarah Winman

    In this sweeping and tender tale, acclaimed author Sarah Winman weaves a captivating story of love, art, and resilience set against the backdrop of war-torn Italy and post-war London. A chance encounter between an English soldier and a middle-aged art historian leads to a lifelong bond and a journey of self-discovery. With beautiful prose and extraordinary tenderness, Still Life is a deeply moving celebration of the beauty and power of human connection in all its forms.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Tell Me Everything: Oprah's Book Club A Novel

    Elizabeth Strout

    A masterful exploration of human connection, regret, and the search for meaning, weaving together Strout's beloved characters in a rich, suspenseful, and moving tale.

    For: Fans of character-driven, introspective fiction will appreciate this novel's thoughtful portrayal of complex relationships and themes.

    Strengths: Strout's insightful prose, nuanced characterizations, and masterful interweaving of storylines make for a compelling, emotionally resonant read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow, as the novel focuses on character development and subtle plot progression.

    Comparable: Like Olive Kitteridge and Lucy by the Sea, Tell Me Everything showcases Strout's signature style of exploring the inner lives of ordinary people in extraordinary circumstances.

    “A generous, compassionate novel about the human need for connection, understanding and love, and the damage that occurs when those things are denied.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Buckeye: A Read with Jenna Pick A Novel

    Patrick Ryan

    From the postwar boom to the late 20th century, Patrick Ryan's meticulously crafted narrative weaves the intimate lives of two Ohio families - the Salt and Jenkins clans - into an epic tale of love, loyalty, sacrifice, and forgiveness. With vivid characters and profound emotional depth, Ryan explores the consequences of choices made across generations, revealing the ways that secrets and mistakes can forever reshape our lives. A once-in-a-decade novel, Buckeye will transport you to the heart of midwestern America, immersing you in stories that linger long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Small Things Like These (Oprah's Book Club)

    Claire Keegan

    In this critically-acclaimed tale, coal merchant Bill Furlong's quiet life in 1980s Ireland is upended by a revelatory discovery at a local convent. As he grapples with the truth, Bill must confront his own past and the town's complicit silence, demonstrating the power of empathy and quiet heroism. Winner of the Orwell Prize and shortlisted for the Booker, this deeply moving story of hope and redemption is a standout work by an iconic author.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Foster

    Claire Keegan

    In this heart-rending literary classic, Claire Keegan masterfully weaves a poignant tale of childhood, loss, and love as a young girl is fostered by her relatives on a rural Irish farm during a sweltering summer. With its universally relatable themes and quietly powerful storytelling, Foster is an emotional depth charge that showcases Keegan's remarkable talent, cementing her status as one of our most important contemporary authors.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. John of John (Oprah's Book Club)

    Douglas Stuart

    In this breathtaking novel, Booker Prize-winning author Douglas Stuart returns to the windswept Isle of Harris, where a young man named Calum grapples with his identity and desires amidst his father's expectations and a community's secrets. With its vivid portrayal of loyalty, duty, and the power of truth, John of John is a moving masterpiece that cements Stuart's status as one of our greatest living novelists.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Olive Kitteridge Fiction

    Elizabeth Strout

    Elizabeth Strout's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel masterfully explores the complexities of human relationships and the passage of time through Olive Kitteridge's sharp, insightful, and often merciless observations.

    For: Adult readers seeking nuanced, character-driven fiction with depth and emotional resonance.

    Strengths: Vivid, memorable characters; razor-sharp dialogue; and a masterful blend of humor, pathos, and profound insight into the human condition.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the episodic structure and focus on character studies rather than plot-driven narratives a challenging, slower pace.

    Comparable: Fans of character-centric, literary fiction like Louise Erdrich's 'The Master Butchers Singing Club' or Alice Munro's short stories will appreciate Strout's immersive, character-driven storytelling style.

    “Olive Kitteridge still lingers in memory like a treasured photograph.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Origins of Heartbreak Lakeside Hospital, #1

    Cara Malone

    "The Origins of Heartbreak" by Cara Malone is a poignant romance that explores the weight of grief and the fragility of healing hearts. Alex and Megan's paths intertwine as they confront their darkest moments, offering a hopeful portrayal of two women piecing their lives back together amidst the challenges of a bacterial outbreak. A captivating blend of literary fiction and romance, this debut novel delves into the complexities of the human experience, urging readers to face their fears and embrace the possibility of new love in the aftermath of heartbreak.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Lady Tremaine Reese’s Book Club Pick (A Novel)

    Rachel Hochhauser

    In this captivating reimagining of Cinderella, Rachel Hochhauser masterfully flips the script, making the wicked stepmother the protagonist. Meet Lady Etheldreda Verity Isolde Tremaine Bramley, a fierce and determined widow fighting to secure a future for her daughters through marriage to royalty. When her plans unexpectedly collide with her stepdaughter's engagement to the future king, Ethel uncovers a shocking secret that forces her to confront the true meaning of motherhood and the power of women's strength. This enthralling tale will keep you flying through its pages, from the opulence of the royal court to the gritty struggles of a single mother in 19th-century England.   14. Gilead (Oprah's Book Club) A Novel

    Marilynne Robinson

    In this award-winning novel, Marilynne Robinson crafts a tender yet profound meditation on the complex bonds between fathers and sons, faith, and the passage of time. With a sweeping narrative that spans generations, Gilead explores how one reverend's life is shaped by his ancestors' struggles for racial equality and how he grapples with his own legacy as he prepares to leave behind a young son. A lyrical, introspective masterpiece, Gilead is a powerful exploration of the human condition and the enduring power of love and faith.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. The Mad Wife

    Meagan Church

    Step into the suffocating world of 1950s suburbia as Lulu Mayfield, the picture-perfect housewife, grapples with the dark secrets threatening to unravel her carefully constructed life. From bestselling author Meagan Church comes THE MAD WIFE, a chilling exploration of identity, motherhood, and the suffocating grip of societal expectations that will leave you questioning the very fabric of reality.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Christine A Novel

    Stephen King

    Experience the terror that grips Arnie Cunningham when he falls for Christine, the 1958 Plymouth Fury he lovingly restores, only to be consumed by the car's sinister influence. As Christine transforms into a malevolent force, Arnie's relationships crumble and his life spirals into hellish chaos. With its atmospheric setting, chilling suspense, and masterful storytelling, Stephen King's classic horror novel will leave you on the edge of your seat, warning you that some loves can be deadly.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The Calamity Club

    Kathryn Stockett

    This novel shines with vivid characters, engaging historical context, and an empowering story of female resilience and friendship, making it a compelling addition to any bookshelf.

    For: Fans of Southern literature, historical fiction, and strong female protagonists will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: The Calamity Club masterfully weaves together three distinct narratives, each with its own humor, heartache, and triumphs, set against the backdrop of the Great Depression in Mississippi.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the large cast of characters initially challenging to keep track of, requiring close attention to begin with.

    Comparable: In its portrayal of the struggles and triumphs of women in the early 20th century South, this novel shares similarities with works like Kathryn Stockett's own 'The Help' and works by Fannie Flagg and Sue Monk Kidd.

    “So immersive, exciting, and downright fabulous, you never want it to end.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Culpability (Oprah's Book Club)

    Bruce Holsinger

    Immersive, thought-provoking, and suspenseful, Bruce Holsinger's Culpability masterfully weaves a family drama exploring the profound implications of artificial intelligence on humanity. When a devastating car crash reveals each Cassidy-Shaw member's secrets, they must confront the harsh moral dilemmas posed by the technology that defines their world. As the family grapples with the consequences, Holsinger propels readers into a timely, thrilling tale that serves as both a gripping page-turner and a stimulating examination of the future we're rapidly creating.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Hamnet

    Maggie O'Farrell

    In this exquisite historical novel, Maggie O'Farrell plumbs the mysterious life of Hamnet, the forgotten son of legendary playwright William Shakespeare. Drawing on tantalizing scraps of historical record, she crafts a poignant, intimate portrait of a family in crisis, as Hamnet's untimely death at 11 years old sends ripples through their lives. A stunning exploration of grief, love, and the enduring power of art to transcend time and sorrow.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. Olive Kitteridge

    Elizabeth Strout

    Olive Kitteridge is a masterful exploration of the complexities of human relationships and the passage of time, told through a unique, interconnected narrative structure.

    For: Readers who appreciate character-driven fiction, nuanced portraits of aging, and philosophical musings on life's struggles and triumphs.

    Strengths: Strout's prose is lyrical and evocative, her characters are richly drawn and relatable, and the novel's episodic structure effectively captures the fluidity of memory and experience.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing deliberate and the character perspectives somewhat fragmented, requiring close attention to fully absorb the story.

    Comparable: Fans of Alice Munro's short stories, The Elegance of the Hedgehog by Muriel Barbery, and novels of psychological insight like Those Who Save Us by Jenna Blum will appreciate Olive Kitteridge's literary depth and character-driven narrative.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. When the Cranes Fly South A Novel

    Lisa Ridzén

    In this heartwarming and laugh-out-loud novel, an aging man, Bo, fights to mend his complicated relationship with his son before it's too late – all while trying to keep his beloved dog at his side. Winner of the Swedish Book of the Year Award, When the Cranes Fly South is a poignant tale of love, friendship, family, and second chances that will leave you grinning and tears-eyed. A captivating "one-of-a-kind" story of fatherhood, told with both humor and poignancy.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  22. The Shampoo Effect: A Read with Jenna Pick A Novel

    Jenny Jackson

    Jump into the world of Greenhead, Massachusetts, where friendships, marriages, and secrets simmer beneath the picture-perfect coastal town's surface. Meet Caroline, an ambitious young woman who shakes things up upon arrival, falling for the charming Van and becoming entangled with his tight-knit circle. Full of wit, sex, and stunning drama, The Shampoo Effect is a page-turning novel that explores love, money, and the complex bonds of female friendship, ultimately revealing the dark undercurrents lurking beneath the town's sun-kissed facade.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  23. Go Gentle: Oprah's Book Club

    Maria Semple

    Get ready for a wild, witty, and wise ride with Maria Semple's latest, Go Gentle. Meet Adora, a sharp-witted Stoic philosopher navigating life's unexpected turns. From contented solo status to international intrigue, Adora's charming journey of self-discovery will grip you from start to finish. With humor, heart, and a dash of romance, this New York Times bestseller explores what it means to find peace and purpose at midlife. Semple's newest gem is a must-read for anyone seeking a story that's both laugh-out-loud funny and deeply satisfying.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  24. Stitched

    Eli McCann

    "Diving into 'Stitched' by Eli McCann was like unraveling a dark, twisted spool of thread - each word carefully, jarringly woven together to ensnare the reader in a tangled web of dread and fascination. As the novel's deftly crafted tension builds, McCann masterfully manipulates your emotions, leaving you both enthralled and horrified by the sinister world unfolding on the pages. This gripping, unsettling debut will leave you breathless and changed, haunted by the echoes of its haunting narrative long after the final stitch is sewn."

    Buy on Bookshop.org
