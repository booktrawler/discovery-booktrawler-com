Books About Whales

A collection of novels exploring the fascinating world of whales, from fiction to nonfiction.

Who these books are for

  • Marine biology enthusiasts
  • Animal lovers
  • Fans of nature and wildlife stories
  • Book clubs seeking diverse topics
  • Anyone curious about whales

Start here

If you only read one book about whales, choose Daniel Kraus's 'Whalefall', a gripping blend of science and fiction.

Whales have long captivated humans with their majestic size, complex social behaviors, and mysterious depths. From literary fiction to scientific nonfiction, this curated collection of books about whales delves into the fascinating world of cetaceans. Whether you're a marine biology enthusiast or simply someone drawn to the allure of these ocean giants, these whale-themed books offer engaging stories and insightful information to enrich your understanding and appreciation of these remarkable creatures.

The collection

  1. The School for Good and Evil Now a Netflix Originals Movie

    Soman Chainani

    Immerse yourself in a whimsical world where fairy tales come to life in Soman Chainani's captivating series, The School for Good and Evil. Follow the journey of unlikely best friends Sophie and Agatha as they discover the truth about themselves amidst the enchanted halls of the fabled School for Good and Evil, where they'll learn the art of becoming heroes or villains. With its lush setting, relatable characters, and thrilling twists, this series will have you spellbound from start to finish.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy

    Douglas Adams

    Comedy and cosmic adventure collide in this laugh-out-loud, bestselling novel from Douglas Adams. Join hapless human Arthur Dent as he's propelled through space after Earth's destruction, accompanied by a motley crew of alien misfits and his indomitable fish friend. Armed with only a towel and The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy, Arthur's absurd journey delves into the deep mysteries of the universe, revealing the ultimate answer while leaving the most profound questions tantalizingly out of reach. Prepare for liftoff with this expert blend of irreverent humor, clever wordplay, and mind-bending science fiction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Hollywood, Ending (Standard Edition)

    John Green

    Experience the glamour and trials of Hollywood through the eyes of two up-and-coming stars in John Green's captivating novel, Hollywood, Ending. With a unique dual-point-of-view narrative, Green masterfully weaves a behind-the-scenes love story against the backdrop of a buzzy biopic about Andy Warhol's life, expertly juxtaposing the spotlight and private struggles of young love. Tender, hilarious, and thought-provoking, this is a must-read about the pursuit of fame, identity, and the love we all crave.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Project Hail Mary A Novel

    Andy Weir

    In "Project Hail Mary", Andy Weir crafts a thrilling sci-fi tale that combines meticulous scientific research with pulse-pounding action. Drawing on cutting-edge astrophysics and astrobiology, Weir transporting readers aboard a lone spaceship hurtling through the cosmos, where a small crew faces an existential threat and a desperate plan to save humanity. With infectious enthusiasm for the hard sciences and breakneck pacing, Weir masterfully weaves together riveting storylines, creating an utterly immersive reading experience that will leave you marveling at the wonders of the universe.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Catch-22 50th Anniversary Edition

    Joseph Heller

    "Meet Yossarian, the indomitable bombardier at the center of Joseph Heller's 50-year-old masterpiece, Catch-22. This wickedly funny novel follows Yossarian's desperate attempts to survive World War II while navigating the Catch-22 logic that declares him insane for wanting to avoid lethal missions, yet sane enough to be grounded if he chooses to quit. A mind-bending satire of bureaucratic absurdity, Catch-22 remains a timeless classic that continues to amuse and provoke readers with its unforgettable characters and reckless humor."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Whalefall A Novel

    Daniel Kraus

    Whalefall combines the riveting, suspenseful elements of a thriller with the emotional depth and scientific accuracy of literary fiction, making it a standout in both genres.

    For: Readers who enjoy literary thrillers, scientific adventure, and character-driven stories will appreciate Whalefall's unique blend.

    Strengths: Whalefall excels at crafting a tense, cinematic narrative that immerses the reader in the undersea world while also exploring complex themes of grief, guilt, and redemption.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the scientific details and poetic descriptions dense, slowing the pace.

    Comparable: Fans of novels that merge scientific accuracy with gripping storytelling, such as Joe Hanneman's Coldwater Canyon or Andy Weir's The Martian, will likely enjoy Whalefall's blend of action and introspection.

    “This hard sci-fi thriller is full of cinematic and wild suspense and would be great for fans of Andy Weir.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Whalefall A Novel

    Daniel Kraus

    Whalefall is a gripping, cinematic thriller that seamlessly blends suspense, science, and poignant character study, making it an unforgettable reading experience.

    For: Fans of sci-fi, survival stories, and character-driven fiction will love this book.

    Strengths: Kraus masterfully crafts a tense, heart-pounding narrative that's grounded in scientific accuracy, while also exploring complex themes of grief, guilt, and redemption.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the technical details of marine biology overwhelming, and the slow pacing in the beginning may not engage everyone immediately.

    Comparable: If you enjoyed The Deep by Rivers Solomon or The Abyss by Orson Scott Card, you'll likely appreciate Whalefall's unique blend of undersea adventure and emotional depth.

    “This hard sci-fi thriller is full of cinematic and wild suspense and would be great for fans of Andy Weir.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Angel Down (Pulitzer Prize Winner) A Novel

    Daniel Kraus

    Angel Down is a masterful blend of historical fiction, horror, and philosophical inquiry, exploring the darkest depths of human nature in the midst of World War I.

    For: Fans of complex, thought-provoking literary fiction with elements of the supernatural will appreciate this novel.

    Strengths: Kraus's evocative prose vividly captures the brutal realities of war, while his inventive premise and polyphonic narrative structure make for a gripping, unsettling read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the short, fragmented paragraphs and dense, poetic language challenging to absorb at times.

    Comparable: For those who enjoy literary speculative fiction, works like John Milas's The Militia House and Agustina Bazterrica's The Unworthy also grapple with the darker aspects of human nature and the supernatural in times of war and social upheaval.

    “A bold and exceptional novel from the author of Whalefall. Kraus has taken the classic war story and crafted something completely unique.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Angel Down (Pulitzer Prize Winner) A Novel

    Daniel Kraus

    Daniel Kraus's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel Angel Down is a gripping, immersive exploration of World War I's hellish trenches and a group of soldiers' encounter with a mysterious fallen angel, delving into themes of survival, morality, and the human condition.

    For: Readers of historical fiction, particularly those interested in World War I, supernatural elements, and character-driven narratives.

    Strengths: Kraus masterfully weaves together historical accuracy, vivid prose, and a compelling plot, bringing the horrors of war to life while introducing an intriguing supernatural element.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, complex narrative and multiple viewpoints challenging to follow.

    Comparable: Fans of historical fiction with supernatural elements, such as Birdsong by Sebastian Faulks or All the Light We Cannot See by Anthony Doerr, will appreciate Angel Down's unique blend of gritty realism and otherworldly intrigue.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Project Hail Mary (Deluxe Edition) A Novel

    Andy Weir

    Project Hail Mary is a gripping, scientifically-grounded space thriller that combines nail-biting suspense with humor and fascinating astronomy details.

    For: Science fiction fans, especially those interested in realistic space travel and astronomy, will devour this book.

    Strengths: Weir's meticulous research and ability to make complex concepts accessible shine through in his propulsive storytelling, richly detailed world-building, and relatable protagonist.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, academic explanations of scientific principles a slow-going pace at times.

    Comparable: For fans of The Martian, this novel offers a similar blend of hard science, survival against daunting odds, and a likable astronaut hero, but with a distinct focus on astrobiology and the search for extraterrestrial life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
