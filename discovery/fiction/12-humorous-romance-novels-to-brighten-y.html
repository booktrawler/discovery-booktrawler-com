12 Humorous Romance Novels to Brighten Your Day

Laugh and love with these witty, feel-good romances that will lift your spirits.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of romantic comedies looking for a pick-me-up
  • Readers seeking uplifting, witty love stories
  • Anyone needing a break from heavy, serious fiction

Start here

If you only read one funny romance, choose 'People We Meet on Vacation' by Emily Henry - a delightful, laugh-out-loud tale of friends-to-lovers.

Laughter is the best medicine, and these 12 humorous romance novels are the perfect prescription for when you need a break from life's stresses. From clever banter to hilarious misunderstandings, these witty love stories will have you grinning from ear to ear. Whether you're a longtime fan of romantic comedies or just looking for a lighthearted escape, this collection of feel-good romances is sure to brighten your day and leave you feeling hopeful and uplifted.

The collection

  1. Less

    Andrew Sean Greer

    In "Less", Andrew Sean Greer embarks on a luminous, globe-spanning journey as a retired actor, Walter Moretti, grapples with love, loss, and reinvention. With crystalline prose and an impeccable sense of timing, Greer weaves an enchanting tale that spans decades and continents, all while probing the deepest human yearnings. A singular novel that will captivate readers and linger in their hearts long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. What Alice Forgot

    Liane Moriarty

    "Join Alice on a journey of self-discovery as she awakens from a decade-long memory loss and navigates the twists and turns of her altered life. With humor, relatability, and a gripping narrative, Liane Moriarty's What Alice Forgot is a captivating exploration of identity, relationships, and the power of love and family to shape our lives."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Marriage Portrait: Reese's Book Club A novel

    Maggie O'Farrell

    Immerse yourself in the vibrant world of 16th century Florence with Maggie O'Farrell's The Marriage Portrait, Reese's Book Club Pick. Meet Lucrezia de' Medici, a talented young duchess navigating the treacherous court of Ferrara as she's thrust into an arranged marriage, torn between artistic pursuits and the pressure to produce an heir. With O'Farrell's masterful storytelling, Lucrezia's resilient spirit shines through the intricate web of Renaissance politics, making for an unforgettable tale of a woman's quest for survival and self-discovery.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Happy Place

    Emily Henry

    Emily Henry's skillful storytelling and relatable characters make this a captivating read about second chances, friendship, and the complexities of adult relationships.

    For: Romance readers and fans of women's fiction will appreciate this heartwarming, witty novel.

    Strengths: Henry excels at crafting believable, nuanced characters and weaving together their interconnected stories with humor and poignancy.

    Skip if: Some readers may initially struggle with the characters' deception and find it takes time to fully invest in their journey.

    Comparable: For those who enjoy character-driven stories with a strong focus on relationships, Henry's other works like 'Beach Read' and 'People We Meet on Vacation' offer similar engaging narratives.

    “With tender insight and quick wit, Henry delivers prosecco and sea breezes alongside startling mediations on friendship, loss, and adulthood.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Funny Story

    Emily Henry

    This captivating romance novel brings to life a heartwarming, humorous tale of two unlikely roommates navigating love, loss, and new beginnings in a charming lakeside town.

    For: Romance readers seeking a lighthearted, character-driven story with witty banter and a strong emotional payoff.

    Strengths: Henry's signature sparkling dialogue, relatable characters, and vivid small-town setting make this a delightful, easy-to-devour read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the supporting cast a bit underdeveloped compared to the leads.

    Comparable: Fans of Emily Henry's other works, such as Beach Read and People We Meet on Vacation, will appreciate the similar blend of humor, heart, and romantic tension found in Funny Story.

    “Emily Henry has made a name for herself as among the hottest romance writers in the game right now.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Paris Match

    Kate Clayborn

    "A poignant and romantic tale of second chances, THE PARIS MATCH whisks readers away to the City of Light where Layla, a recently divorced doctor, reconnects with her ex and sparks a new attraction with the wedding's enigmatic best man, Griffin. As they work together to salvage the destination nuptials, Layla and Griffin's feelings deepen, forcing her to confront the past and reconsider the future. With vivid descriptions of Paris and a nuanced exploration of love, loss, and self-discovery, Kate Clayborn crafts a heartwarming, must-read romance for 2026."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Leave and Come Back A Novel

    Lavanya Lakshmi

    Join Simran as she returns to her family's home for a whirlwind Indian wedding, where old secrets and new love await. With the help of her charming (but unknowing) boyfriend Leo, and a dash of Bollywood inspiration, Simran must navigate the drama of her close-knit family while confronting her past pain and embracing her happily ever after. A heartwarming, hilarious tale of love, identity, and the power of family.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Case of the Missing Finger Ellie Tappet Cruise Ship Mysteries, #1

    Cheri Baker

    Step aboard for a sun-soaked whodunit as widowed Ellie Tappet trades retirement for a first-ever cruise. But when a gruesome fingertip with diamond ring turns up in her cocktail, Ellie's matchmaking efforts are swept aside by the shocking crime. With ship security seemingly in denial, Ellie's 30 years of experience as a police chief's wife have primed her for detection. Can she untangle the mystery before it capsizes her new friends and the ship itself? A lighthearted, twisty debut for Ellie Tappet, the cruise ship's unlikely amateur sleuth.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Red, White & Royal Blue A Novel

    Casey McQuiston

    Red, White & Royal Blue is a laugh-out-loud, tender, and steamy romance between America's First Son Alex and the Prince of Wales Henry, amidst the intrigue of international politics. With its witty banter, charming characters, and delicious forbidden fling, this debut novel will captivate readers with its bold exploration of identity, ambition, and the power of love to change the world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Beautiful World, Where Are You A Novel

    Sally Rooney

    Experience the intimacy and vulnerability of four complex characters as they navigate love, friendship, and the challenges of growing up in modern society. With Sally Rooney's masterful prose, Alice, Felix, Eileen, and Simon grapple with desire, heartache, and the search for meaning in this poignant and deeply relatable novel.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Wedding People A Novel

    Alison Espach

    A heartwarming, humorous novel about unexpected connections and self-discovery, with a propulsive and nuanced exploration of life's challenges and the human spirit's resilience.

    For: Readers seeking an uplifting, character-driven story with wit and emotional depth, particularly those interested in family dynamics, relationships, and personal growth.

    Strengths: Engaging, relatable characters; expertly woven plot with surprising twists; balanced humor and poignancy; insightful commentary on life's complexities and the power of chance encounters.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow in the first third as the story establishes its multiple narrative threads.

    Comparable: Fans of character-driven novels like Elin Hilderbrand's beach town stories or Jennifer Weiner's contemporary women's fiction will appreciate The Wedding People's mix of humor, heart, and thought-provoking themes.

    “A feel-good testament to the life-altering magic of chance meetings.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Unwedding Reese's Book Club Pick (a Novel)

    Ally Condie

    A chilling whodunit with a captivating setting, this novel weaves together mystery, family drama, and introspective themes, making it a standout in the genre.

    For: Fans of psychological suspense, mystery enthusiasts, and readers drawn to stories of family, love, and self-discovery will appreciate this riveting tale.

    Strengths: Condie masterfully crafts a complex, suspenseful plot filled with intriguing characters, while also exploring the pain of loss and the complexities of relationships.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow as the story builds tension and delves into character backstories.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy atmospheric, character-driven mysteries with elements of family drama, such as The Last Mrs. Parrish by Liv Constantine or The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides, may find The Unwedding equally compelling.

    “A gorgeous murder mystery that explores what it means to be human--the pain and the love.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Red, White and Royal Blue

    Casey McQuiston

    Get ready for a swoon-worthy, feel-good romance! "Red, White & Royal Blue" brings together America's First Son, Alex, and the dashing Prince of Wales, Henry, in a forbidden love story that will capture your heart. As their fake friendship blossoms into something real, they must navigate the treacherous waters of royal protocol, political pressures, and their own desires. With witty banter, steamy scenes, and a healthy dose of humor, this charming debut novel is the perfect escape - and a shining example of how true love can conquer all, even when it's not always diplomatic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. The Romance Revival

    Christina Lauren

    In Christina Lauren's mesmerizing novel, The Romance Revival, a groundbreaking scientific breakthrough gives troubled couple Emery and Luca a rare second chance at love. When a fateful accident wipes their marriage from Emery's memories, she leverages her cutting-edge research to resurrect Luca – minus his recollections of her and their life together. As they navigate this unconventional "do-over," Emery must convince her amnesiac husband of their deep connection, all while confronting the reasons their relationship nearly crumbled the first time around. A poignant exploration of love, loss, and the power of second chances, The Romance Revival will captivate readers with its unique premise, witty humor, and tender portrayal of two souls desperate to rekindle their spark.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Samantha Spük: Paranormal Wedding Planner

    Aleese Lin

    Immerse yourself in this delightfully spooky contemporary romance debut! When Samantha Spük inherits her grandmother's Salem estate, she's forced to return to her hometown's supernatural shenanigans. As executor of the will, Sabby must take on quirky jobs, including wedding planning for not-so-human locals. With the help of a charming graveyard stalker, a sassy talking-head sidekick, and a manic pixie assistant, Sabby navigates paranormal nuptials while clinging to her "normal" 9-to-5 accounting career. Perfect for fans of Legends & Lattes and Netflix's Wednesday, this delightful read will leave you enchanted.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Untitled Empyrean (Not Book Four)

    Rebecca Yarros

    Blurb: Join Rebecca Yarros, the #1 NYT bestselling author, for an untitled new installment in the Empyrean series, promising more thrilling romance and heartfelt drama. With her proven skill in crafting compelling characters and captivating storylines, this untitled book is sure to delight fans of the series and new readers alike. Dive into this latest chapter of the Empyrean saga and discover why Yarros has earned widespread acclaim for her captivating novels.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Mysterious Affair at Styles

    Agatha Christie

    Step into the world of Hercule Poirot with Agatha Christie's pioneering mystery, The Mysterious Affair at Styles. When Emily Inglethorp's death at the English manor Styles arouses suspicion, Belgian detective Poirot is called in to unravel the complex web of motives and alibis. A classic tale of justice and intrigue from the legendary golden age mystery writer, perfect for fans of whodunits and introducing them to the iconic Poirot.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. People We Meet on Vacation

    Emily Henry

    A witty, warm, and engaging romance that explores the complexities of friendship and love through its relatable characters and clever dialogue.

    For: Fans of contemporary romance, particularly those who appreciate strong female leads, witty banter, and slow-burning relationships.

    Strengths: Henry's writing shines in capturing the natural rapport between Poppy and Alex, making their journey both humorous and emotionally resonant.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit uneven, with the story's second half delving deeper into character introspection.

    Comparable: Compared to other offerings in the romance genre, People We Meet on Vacation stands out for its nuanced portrayal of friendship and the gradual development of romantic feelings.

    “Henry has become synonymous with modern and heartfelt takes on the romance genre.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. We Are Gathered Here Today

    Bobby Finger

    Join Fin, a gay man reevaluating marriage, at his riotous cousin's Wild West-themed Texas wedding where decades-old wedding pact traditions are put to the test. As Fin grapples with his own engagement doubts alongside a rowdy group of old friends and unexpected guests, hilarious confessions and poignant revelations unfold, ultimately guiding him to a deeply personal truth. A witty, heartfelt exploration of love, commitment, and the meaning of marriage.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
