Best Books Recommended by Reese's Book Club and Oprah's Book Club

Explore this curated list of the most popular, highly-recommended books selected by Reese Witherspoon's Book Club x Hello Sunshine and Oprah's Book Club.

Who these books are for

  • Book clubs
  • Readers seeking new releases and bestsellers
  • Anyone looking for inspiring stories and thought-provoking themes

Start here

If you only read one book from these recommendations, choose Taylor Jenkins Reid's 'Daisy Jones & The Six' – a captivating tale of 1970s rock 'n' roll and the rise of a legendary band.

Reese Witherspoon and Oprah Winfrey have built reputation as influential voices in the literary world through their book clubs. With a keen eye for compelling stories and relatable characters, these clubs have introduced readers to a diverse array of talented authors and unforgettable tales. This collection brings together some of the most popular and highly-recommended books from both Reese's Book Club x Hello Sunshine and Oprah's Book Club, providing a unique opportunity to explore a wide range of engaging stories and thought-provoking themes.

The collection

  1. Wreck the Halls A Novel

    Tessa Bailey

    Step into a holiday rom-com full of humor, heart, and rockstar antics with Wreck the Halls by Tessa Bailey. When bookworm Melody Gallard teams up with charismatic Beat Dawkins to reunite their estranged rock icon mothers for a Christmas Eve concert, sparks fly and secrets surface. As they navigate a chaotic 24-hour film shoot, adoring fans, and brawling Santas, Mel discovers a newfound courage with Beat by her side. But their happiness is threatened by a decades-old scandal just as it seems their love might last a lifetime - or at least make it to the end of the holiday season. A touching, festive read perfect for fans of uplifting winter romances.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Such a Fun Age: Reese's Book Club

    Kiley Reid

    "In this incisive novel, Kiley Reid delves into the complex dynamics of race and privilege through the eyes of Emira, a young black woman navigating her career as a nanny. When a viral video sparks an explosive confrontation with her employers, Emira's world is turned upside down, forcing her to confront the power structures she's always skirted. A searing exploration of identity, power and the hypocrisy that binds us all, 'Such a Fun Age' is a page-turning must-read."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Kin: Oprah's Book Club A Novel

    Tayari Jones

    Tayari Jones' masterful storytelling and nuanced exploration of complex relationships, identity, and the Black experience in the Jim Crow South make Kin a standout addition to any book collection.

    For: Readers who appreciate character-driven literary fiction, historical contexts, and thought-provoking themes about family, loyalty, and resilience will find Kin a compelling and emotionally resonant novel.

    Strengths: Jones expertly weaves together multiple narrative threads, creating a rich tapestry of the characters' lives and relationships, and her vivid prose paints a vivid picture of the historical era.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit uneven, with Annie's story line occasionally overshadowing Vernice's, but this doesn't detract from the overall impact of the novel.

    Comparable: Fans of Jones' previous work, such as An American Marriage, will recognize her signature style and themes, while readers of historical fiction like Colson Whitehead's The Underground Railroad or Zora Neale Hurston's Their Eyes Were Watching God may also appreciate Kin's unique blend of character study and historical context.

    “A triumphant return of one of the most important literary voices today. Vibrant, funny, moving and powerful, Kin is an unforgettable read.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Atmosphere: A GMA Book Club Pick A Love Story

    Taylor Jenkins Reid

    Atmosphere is a captivating tale of ambition, love, and breaking barriers, transporting readers to the high-stakes world of 1980s NASA while delivering an emotionally resonant character study.

    For: Fans of historical fiction, women's stories, and science will appreciate this thrilling, heartfelt novel.

    Strengths: Atmosphere masterfully weaves together action, romance, and historical detail, crafting a compelling narrative with richly drawn characters and a satisfying, uplifting conclusion.

    Skip if: The dense, meticulous research and vivid setting may slow the pace for some readers.

    Comparable: For those who loved The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo and Daisy Jones & The Six, Atmosphere offers another immersive, character-driven epic from Taylor Jenkins Reid.

    “Atmosphere is a character study brimming with heart, both quietly lyrical and an action-packed nail-biter.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. True Biz: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Sara Novic

    "Immerse yourself in the vibrant world of a boarding school for the deaf, where a year of seismic shifts in romance, politics, and family await. Meet Charlie, Austin, and February as they navigate first love, loss, and the fight for deaf rights. This stunning novel, a Reese's Book Club pick, weaves together sign language, civil rights, and the unbreakable bonds of human connection in a celebration of resilience and joy."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Black Cake: A Read with Jenna Pick A Novel

    Charmaine Wilkerson

    In this sweeping family saga, estranged siblings Byron and Benny unearth their mother Eleanor's hidden past through her traditional Caribbean black cake recipe. Spanning generations and continents, the novel delves into secrets, betrayals, and the complex bonds between family members. With its immersive storytelling and richly drawn characters, Black Cake is a poignant exploration of identity, inheritance, and how the past shapes our present.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Great Big Beautiful Life: Reese's Book Club

    Emily Henry

    A captivating, sweeping novel about storytelling, love, and the choices we make, with a unique blend of romance, humor, and heart.

    For: Fans of contemporary romance, literary fiction, and anyone drawn to stories about writers, family secrets, and second chances.

    Strengths: Vivid, complex characters; clever, layered plot with surprising twists; and a thoughtful exploration of the writing process.

    Skip if: At over 400 pages, it's a substantial, immersive read that may not appeal to those seeking a quicker, lighter romance.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed novels like 'The Storied Life of A.J. Fikry' or 'The Unfinished Symphony of Living' will appreciate its blend of character-driven drama, family secrets, and a romantic subplot.

    “Her best-selling romances have made her a new standard-bearer of the genre.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. First Lie Wins: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Ashley Elston

    First Lie Wins offers a gripping, twisty thriller with a unique premise and strong protagonist, making it a compelling read.

    For: Fans of suspenseful, cat-and-mouse stories with unexpected turns will enjoy this book.

    Strengths: The clever plot, with its secret identities and mysterious boss, keeps readers guessing, while the protagonist Evie is a smart and relatable character.

    Skip if: Some may find the Southern setting and characters a bit clichéd, but this doesn't detract from the core thrills.

    Comparable: Readers who liked Gone Girl or The Girl on the Train will appreciate the similar blend of psychological suspense and intrigue in First Lie Wins.

    “This fast-paced read has everything you could want in a thriller: secret identities, a mysterious boss and a cat & mouse game that kept me guessing the whole way through.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. A Pair of Aces: Reese's Book Club

    Marie Benedict

    In 1930s New York, two trailblazing women form an unlikely alliance to take down notorious mob boss Lucky Luciano. Eunice Carter, Manhattan's first Black female prosecutor, targets Luciano through his role in prostitution, while high-class madam Polly Adler supplies crucial evidence. This gripping historical thriller, a Reese's Book Club pick, follows their daring collaboration as they bridge divides and risk everything in the most sensational trial of the era.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Where the Crawdads Sing: Reese's Book Club

    Delia Owens

    Immerse yourself in the haunting tale of Kya, the "Marsh Girl", who's long been shrouded in mystery in a quiet North Carolina town. Delia Owens masterfully weaves a murder mystery, coming-of-age narrative, and ode to nature in this New York Times bestselling novel with over 18 million copies sold. With its vivid settings and unforgettable characters, Where the Crawdads Sing is a breathtakingly beautiful read you won't soon forget.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Hot Girl Murder Club A Novel

    Ashley Winstead

    Meet Scout Sage, a rising star in the cutthroat Hollywood scene, whose life is turned upside down when a series of murders rocks the city—and she's fingered as the prime suspect. To clear her name and uncover the truth, Scout must confront a web of secrets and sisterhood, alongside an unconventional young detective determined to bring a long-buried reign of terror to light. Prepare for a dark, addictive thriller that delves into the shadows of fame, ambition, and the unbreakable bonds of sisterhood.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Long Island Girls A Novel

    Gabrielle Korn

    "Dive into the raw, nostalgic coming-of-age story Long Island Girls, where indie rock meets queer identity in early 2000s Long Island and Brooklyn. Gabrielle Korn masterfully captures the intense longing and self-discovery of youth, as Susan navigates the music scene, small-town secrets, and the complicated connection that defines her story."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo A Novel

    Taylor Jenkins Reid

    Grace, glamour, heartbreak, and self-discovery intertwine in this captivating tale. Evelyn Hugo, a reclusive Hollywood star, invites a young journalist to chronicle her seven marriages, revealing a life of passion, deceit, and resilience. Through Evelyn's story, Taylor Jenkins Reid explores themes of identity, fame, love, and the power of secrets to shape our destinies, weaving a mesmerizing narrative that will leave you enchanted and moved.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Daisy Jones & The Six: Reese's Book Club A Novel

    Taylor Jenkins Reid

    This novel masterfully recreates the glamour and excess of 1970s rock 'n' roll, spinning an addictive tale of love, fame, and tragedy.

    For: Fans of music-themed fiction, particularly those who enjoy the behind-the-scenes stories of iconic bands, will devour this book.

    Strengths: Taylor Jenkins Reid's storytelling is engaging and immersive, with vivid characterizations and a well-paced, suspenseful plot that keeps readers hooked.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the oral history style and multiple narrators slightly confusing or hard to follow at times.

    Comparable: If you enjoyed novels like The Perks of Being a Wallflower or Beautiful Trouble, with their focus on music and the highs and lows of youth culture, you'll likely appreciate Daisy Jones & The Six's similar blend of nostalgia, drama, and rock 'n' roll.

    “Reid is a stunning writer whose characters are unforgettable and whose stories are deeply emotional. . . . Her most gripping novel yet.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Malibu Rising: A Read with Jenna Pick A Novel

    Taylor Jenkins Reid

    "Escape to the sun-kissed shores of 1980s Malibu with Taylor Jenkins Reid's addictive novel Malibu Rising. As four glamorous siblings throw an epic end-of-summer bash, secrets, passions, and family drama simmer beneath the surface. With a star-studded cast of characters and a night that will forever change their lives, this captivating story blends romance, nostalgia, and the complexities of family ties. A must-read for fans of juicy, page-turning fiction."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Seven Days in June

    Tia Williams

    Get ready for a sizzling romance that will keep you turning the pages! "Seven Days in June" follows Eva, a single mom and bestselling erotica author, and Shane, a reclusive novelist, as they ignite a passionate second-chance at love years after a whirlwind summer together as teenagers. Full of humor, heart, and steamy chemistry, this novel delves into the complexities of creativity, motherhood, and finding your soulmate. With a tale that spans decades and will leave you breathless, "Seven Days in June" is a must-read for fans of smart, sexy romances.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The Seven Daughters of Dupree A Novel

    Nikesha Elise Williams

    In this sweeping, multi-generational epic, Nikesha Elise Williams masterfully explores the resilience of Black women through seven generations of Dupree family history. From a 1917 attempt to pass for white, to a 1934 love affair with a forbidden suitor, to the present-day quest of teenage Tati to uncover her father's identity, the Dupree women confront a legacy of pain, unbreakable familial bonds, and a mysterious curse that means they will only give birth to daughters. A powerful and moving portrayal of generational trauma and Black women's experiences, reminiscent of The Twelve Tribes of Hattie, The Seven Daughters of Dupree is a must-read for anyone fascinated by family sagas and the unyielding spirit of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Into the Blue: Reese's Book Club A Love Story

    Emma Brodie

    "Embark on a decades-spanning love story as two souls from different worlds forge an unbreakable bond, bridging the gap between reality and the silver screen. With irresistible charm, sizzling chemistry, and profound insight, Into the Blue is a literary romance that explores the power of true connection, the fragility of relationships, and the unyielding pursuit of a life lived on one's own terms."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What types of books does Reese's Book Club feature?
Reese's Book Club highlights a wide range of contemporary fiction, including women's fiction, romance, mystery, and more.
How often does Oprah's Book Club release new picks?
Oprah's Book Club typically selects one book every few months, with infrequent exceptions for special selections.
Can I find audiobook versions of these book club picks?
Yes, most titles in this collection are available as audiobooks, making it easy to enjoy these stories on-the-go.
