Timeless Classic Novels You Must Read

Immerse yourself in these 17 iconic works of English and American literature, spanning the 19th century to the early 20th century.

Who these books are for

  • Literature students seeking canonical works
  • Book clubs looking for classic novels with depth
  • Anyone seeking a rich reading experience in English
  • Fans of 19th century historical fiction

Start here

If you only read one classic novel, choose 'Great Expectations' by Charles Dickens for its complex characters and commentary on class and redemption.

These 17 novels form the bedrock of English and American literature. Written by the likes of Charles Dickens, Jane Austen, Henry James, and others, they span the 19th century to the early 20th century. From Dickens' vivid portrayals of Victorian London to James' psychological explorations of the American expatriate experience, these works showcase the evolution and diversity of the novel form during its Golden Age. Whether you're a student, book club member, or simply a reader seeking a rich literary experience, this collection offers timeless stories that continue to resonate with audiences today.

The collection

  1. Edgar Allen Poe Short Fiction

    Edgar Allen Poe

    Immerse yourself in the masterful short stories of Edgar Allan Poe, one of the pioneers of American literature. This comprehensive collection reunites all of Poe's short fiction, originally published in mesmerizing installments across 19th-century magazines. From the cryptic mystery of "The Gold-Bug" to the haunting descent into madness in "The Fall of the House of Usher," Poe's works span genres with eerie precision, cementing his reputation as a visionary of horror, science fiction, and the early detective novel. Dive into the dark, atmospheric tales that have captivated authors like Arthur Conan Doyle and H.P. Lovecraft, earning Poe a permanent place in the annals of literary greatness.

  2. The Extraordinary Adventures of Arsene Lupin

    Maurice Leblanc

    Embark on a thrilling ride with the charming and cunning Arsène Lupin as he masterminds daring heists of France's finest treasures, always staying one step ahead of his determined detective rival. In this highly entertaining collection of nine short stories, originally published in 1905, the clever gentleman thief proves to be an unforgettable creation.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Our Mutual Friend

    Charles Dickens

    Our Mutual Friend is a sweeping social satire that showcases Dickens' mastery of characterization, plotting and thematic depth, offering a darkly humorous portrait of Victorian London.

    For: Literary fiction enthusiasts, scholars of Victorian England and those who appreciate complex, layered storytelling.

    Strengths: Dickens' vivid, often grotesque characterizations; the novel's intricate web of relationships and plot twists; and its rich symbolism exploring themes of class, greed, death and renewal.

    Skip if: With its sprawling cast and dense, layered narrative, this is not an easy read; patience and close attention are required to fully appreciate its nuances.

    Comparable: In its scope and satirical tone, Our Mutual Friend compares to Dickens' other major works like Bleak House and Little Dorrit, offering a similarly immersive, socially conscious reading experience.

    “The great poet of the city. He was created by London”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Jude the Obscure

    Thomas Hardy

    In his unflinching final novel, Thomas Hardy probes the bitter realities of class and societal constraints through the tragic story of Jude Fawley, a stonemason whose dreams of knowledge and love are repeatedly dashed. As Jude's quest for intellectual fulfillment leads to an unconventional relationship with his cousin Sue Bridehead, they face disapproval and poverty's looming specter. With its groundbreaking exploration of taboo subjects, Jude the Obscure sparked a literary storm upon its 1895 publication, cementing Hardy's reputation as a fierce social critic. This Penguin Classics edition presents the unexpurgated original text, along with insightful introductions and annotations, making this searing tale of the human condition an essential read for literary enthusiasts.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. David Copperfield

    Charles Dickens

    David Copperfield is a masterpiece of Victorian literature, a sweeping coming-of-age story rich in memorable characters, vivid settings, and profound themes.

    For: Fans of classic English novels, literary enthusiasts, and anyone interested in the cultural and social context of 19th century England.

    Strengths: Dickens' evocative prose, nuanced characterizations, and exploration of childhood, love, identity, and class.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the episodic structure and large cast of characters initially overwhelming.

    Comparable: Other Dickens novels like Great Expectations and Oliver Twist share similar themes and atmospheric settings, offering a glimpse into the author's signature style and social commentary.

    “The most perfect of all the Dickens novels.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Howards End

    E. M. Forster

    In E.M. Forster's timeless masterpiece, Howards End, the tender relationship between Margaret Schlegel and Mrs. Wilcox sparks a contemplation of social class, love, and what truly defines a refined existence. This beautifully crafted novel explores the intersection of three families, each striving for harmony, while navigating the contrasts of English society at the turn of the 20th century. Forster's exquisite prose and poignant insights earn Howards End its reputation as a classic that continues to resonate with readers, making it a must-read for anyone seeking a deeply satisfying literary experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Middlemarch

    George Eliot

    Diving into George Eliot's sweeping masterpiece, Middlemarch, is akin to embarking on an intimate journey through the complexities of the human heart. As you turn the pages, you're enveloped in a richly layered world where love, ambition, and societal constraints intertwine, evoking profound empathy for the characters' triumphs and struggles. With each carefully crafted scene, Eliot masterfully weaves a tapestry of emotions that will leave you introspective, yet utterly captivated by the lives unfolding in this timeless Victorian tale. Prepare to be moved, challenged, and forever changed by this modern classic's haunting beauty.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Great Expectations

    Charles Dickens

    In the flickering light of a humble blacksmith's forge, Pip's fateful encounter with a convict sets him on a turbulent journey of love, betrayal, and redemption. Against the gritty backdrop of 19th century England, Dickens masterfully weaves Pip's rags-to-riches tale, as the young man navigates the treacherous waters of class, morality, and the inescapable pull of his own past. A timeless exploration of the human condition, 'Great Expectations' is a richly nuanced portrait of the complexities of the soul, forever etched in the pages of literary history.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. A Tale of Two Cities

    Charles Dickens

    In "A Tale of Two Cities," Charles Dickens masterfully weaves a sweeping historical epic that explores themes of revolution, redemption, and the indomitable human spirit. Set against the backdrop of the French Revolution, this timeless classic brings the tumultuous era to vivid life through vivid characters and Dickens' signature prose. Prepare to be transported to 18th century Paris and London as the novel's intricate plot, bursting with suspense and romance, unfolds at a breakneck pace. A true literary tour de force, "A Tale of Two Cities" is a must-read for anyone seeking an immersive, emotionally resonant experience in classic storytelling.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Bleak House

    Charles Dickens

    Bleak House is a landmark work of social commentary and satire, offering a scathing critique of the British legal system and the injustices faced by the poor in Victorian England.

    For: Readers interested in 19th-century British literature, social realism, and Dickens' unique brand of moral outrage and reformist fervor.

    Strengths: Dickens masterfully weaves together a sprawling cast of characters, vividly depicting the contrasts between the haves and have-nots of London society. His biting social commentary and vivid prose are unparalleled.

    Skip if: The dense, complex narrative can be challenging to follow at times, with multiple plot threads and character arcs unfolding over hundreds of pages.

    Comparable: Fans of Dickens' other novels like Oliver Twist and Great Expectations will appreciate Bleak House's similar themes of social injustice, memorable characters, and biting satire.

    “Perhaps Bleak House is his best novel. . . . When Dickens wrote Bleak House he had grown up.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Dracula

    Bram Stoker

    This 1897 Gothic horror novel has become a timeless classic, offering a richly atmospheric tale of suspense and the supernatural that continues to captivate readers.

    For: Fans of classic horror, vampire lore, and Victorian-era fiction will find Dracula a compelling read.

    Strengths: Bram Stoker crafts a masterful, slow-burning narrative that builds tension and dread, while exploring themes of identity, sanity, and the darker aspects of human nature.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the 19th-century language and pacing a bit slow for modern tastes.

    Comparable: Other Penguin Classics like Mary Shelley's Frankenstein and Robert Louis Stevenson's Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde share similar Gothic horror and supernatural elements.

    “Those who cannot find their own reflection in Bram Stoker”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Portrait of a Lady

    Henry James

    This seminal work showcases James' mastery of psychological insight and nuanced exploration of themes such as personal freedom, class, and the complexities of relationships.

    For: Literate readers interested in 19th century literature, character studies, and philosophical themes.

    Strengths: Richly detailed prose, multi-dimensional characters, and a thought-provoking examination of the human condition.

    Skip if: The narrative can be slow-paced and dense at times, requiring close attention to fully appreciate the author's subtleties.

    Comparable: Compared to other James works in the collection, The Portrait of a Lady stands out for its more overtly tragic tone and focus on a single protagonist's journey.

    “The Portrait of a Lady is entirely successful in giving one the sense of having met somebody far too radiantly good for this world.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. The Turn of the Screw

    Henry James

    Dear friend, if you delight in haunting, atmospheric tales that leave you questioning reality, then you must devour Henry James's mesmerizing 'The Turn of the Screw'. This novella, often classified as a ghost story, transports you to a foreboding English estate where a young governess descends into paranoia, her charges perhaps not entirely human or innocent. James's masterful, psychologically complex prose immerses you in the governess's fraying sanity, crafting a sense of eerily ambiguous terror that will linger long after you finish the final page. A must-read for fans of the eerie, the unknown, and the unsettling.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde

    Robert Louis Stevenson

    Immerse yourself in the haunting and thought-provoking world of "Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde", where the lines between good and evil blur into a mesmerizing dance of dual identities. As you delve into Stevenson's masterful prose, a chilling sense of unease will creep over you, mirroring Dr. Jekyll's own terrifying descent into madness. With each page-turn, you'll be pulled further into the dark heart of human nature, confronting the darkest shadows that lurk within us all. A riveting, psychological thriller that will leave you both enthralled and unsettled, long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Bulfinch's Mythology

    Thomas Bulfinch

    Dive into the captivating world of mythology with Thomas Bulfinch's timeless compilation. Designed for readers of English literature seeking to understand mythological allusions, this volume distills ancient legends into engaging narratives that illuminate the rich cultural heritage behind classic works. From the Age of Fable to the Legends of Charlemagne, Bulfinch's masterful retellings provide a comprehensive guide for anyone curious to decipher mythological references and expand their literary understanding.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Jeeves Stories

    P G Wodehouse

    Step into the debonair world of the illustrious Bertie Wooster and his ingenious valet Jeeves, where the boundaries between aristocracy and service blur, wit prevails, and escapades unfold. In this delightful collection, P.G. Wodehouse masterfully weaves together tales of love, social hierarchy, and the unbreakable bond between master and man, showcasing his signature blend of humor, satire, and charm. With these timeless Jeeves stories, get ready to be whisked away to an elegant Britain of yesteryear, where the absurd becomes artfully inevitable and blissful laughter ensues with every turned page.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Picture of Dorian Gray

    Oscar Wilde

    "Oscar Wilde's timeless, provocative masterpiece 'The Picture of Dorian Gray' is a must-read for anyone fascinated by the darker corners of the human psyche. Wilde's decadent exploration of moral decay, hedonism, and the corrupting influence of beauty will captivate lovers of classic Gothic and philosophical fiction. Prepare to be entranced by this sinister tale of a young man's descent into depravity as his portrait ages in secret, serving as a haunting mirror of his soul."

    Arthur Conan Doyle

    "Get ready to dive into a world of mystery, genius, and Victorian London with 'Adventures of Sherlock Holmes'! This unbeatable collection of short stories immerses you into the brilliant mind of the world's most iconic detective, Sherlock Holmes, as he solves an astounding array of baffling crimes. If you're a fan of intricate plots, witty banter, and unforgettable characters, then Holmes is your man. Frequently asked questions

What era are these classic novels from?
Most are from the 19th century, with a few early 20th century works included.
Are there any famous authors represented?
Yes, all the big names in English and American literature are here, including Dickens, Austen, James, Wilde, and more.
Are the books long and dense?
Generally, yes, these are substantial works of literary fiction, but their enduring themes and characters make the reading rewarding.
