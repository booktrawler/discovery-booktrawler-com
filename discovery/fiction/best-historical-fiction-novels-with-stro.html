Best Historical Fiction Novels with Strong Female Leads

Immerse yourself in these gripping historical fiction novels that feature complex, powerful women driving the story.

Who these books are for

  • Historical fiction readers
  • Book clubs looking for diverse discussions
  • Teenagers and young adults interested in strong female characters
  • Fans of literary fiction seeking complex, nuanced stories

Start here

If you only read one novel about a resilient woman navigating history, choose Homegoing by Yaa Gyasi.

Historical fiction novels with strong female leads offer a captivating blend of fact and fiction, allowing readers to connect with remarkable women who shaped history. These books delve into the complexities of female experiences, from the constraints of patriarchal societies to the triumphs of resilience and determination. By featuring complex, multidimensional women as protagonists, these novels provide a more nuanced understanding of the past and its impact on contemporary society.

The collection

  1. Their Eyes Were Watching God

    Zora Neale Hurston

    Rediscover the timeless Southern love story that was once overlooked but now shines as a masterpiece of African-American literature. In Their Eyes Were Watching God, Zora Neale Hurston brings to vivid life the journey of Janie Crawford, a strong-willed woman navigating love, heartbreak, and self-discovery against the backdrop of the Jim Crow South. With Hurston's powerful prose and indelible characters, this classic novel explores themes of identity, independence, and the resilience of the human spirit. A true American literary treasure, this Penguin Classics edition of Their Eyes Were Watching God is a must-read for anyone seeking a deeply soulful and unforgettable tale of love and perseverance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Golden Notebook A Novel

    Doris Lessing

    In Doris Lessing's seminal novel, The Golden Notebook, acclaimed author Anna weaves together four distinct narratives - a personal memoir, a disillusioned political chronicle, a fictional novel, and a private diary - into a profound meditation on identity, politics, love, and the writer's craft. Spanning decades, this groundbreaking work explores the complexities of a woman's life and mind, cementing its status as a modern classic that continues to resonate with readers decades after its initial publication.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Transit of Venus

    Shirley Hazzard

    Winner of the National Book Award, Shirley Hazzard's masterpiece, The Transit of Venus, is a sweeping tale of two orphan sisters navigating the complexities of love, loss, and identity from 1950s Australia to 1980s Stockholm. With lyrical prose and intricate plotting, this novel becomes a poignant meditation on the dislocations of modern life and the enduring bonds of family and desire. A must-read for fans of literary fiction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Rebecca

    Daphne Du Maurier

    Experience the timeless gothic romance and suspense of Daphne du Maurier's classic novel Rebecca. When a young woman marries wealthy widower Maxim de Winter, she's drawn into a labyrinth of dark secrets at his imposing estate, Manderley. The shadow of his beautiful, mysterious late wife Rebecca looms large, threatening to destroy their love. This haunting, mesmerizing tale of obsessive love, power, and the supernatural has enthralled readers and inspired iconic adaptations since its 1938 publication and remains a must-read for fans of literary fiction, psychological thrillers, and horror.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Isle in the Silver Sea

    Tasha Suri

    In this captivating standalone fantasy romance, Tasha Suri weaves a centuries-old tale of forbidden love between a knight and a witch, doomed to fall and rise together in an ever-repeating cycle. Against the vibrant backdrop of medieval England, Simran and Vina must navigate their desires and risk it all to break the curse, as a mysterious threat targets their kind of fated stories. A sensuous, haunting exploration of love that transcends time, The Isle in the Silver Sea will keep you enthralled until its breathtaking conclusion.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Flashlight A Novel

    Susan Choi

    Susan Choi's masterful storytelling and meticulous research make Flashlight a gripping, thought-provoking novel that explores the complexities of identity, family secrets, and historical trauma.

    For: Fans of literary fiction, historical drama, and complex character studies will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Choi's writing is evocative and immersive, transporting readers across time and place. The novel's intricate, multi-layered narrative keeps readers engaged and curious until the very end.

    Skip if: Some readers may struggle with the dense, layered storytelling and the slow reveal of the mystery.

    Comparable: In terms of its sweeping historical scope, complex characters, and layered narrative structure, Flashlight is reminiscent of works by authors like Michael Ondaatje and Toni Morrison.

    “A major world writer . . . Choi is in thrilling command.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Rainbow

    D H Lawrence

    D.H. Lawrence's visionary masterpiece, The Rainbow, delves into the complex, intertwined lives of three generations of the Brangwen family as they navigate the tumultuous transformation of a rural Midlands community into an industrial society. Praised for its provocative exploration of human sexuality and relationships, this 1915 novel was deemed obscene upon publication but is now recognized as one of the 100 greatest novels of all time by the Modern Library. Immersive and thought-provoking, The Rainbow is a must-read for anyone seeking a deeply layered and imaginatively realized portrayal of the human experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Handmaid's Tale A Novel

    Margaret Atwood

    Step into a chilling near-future where the United States has splintered into oppressive states like Gilead, where fertile women are enslaved and forced into reproductive servitude as "Handmaids." Margaret Atwood's masterful novel brings this dystopian nightmare to vivid life through the haunting narrative of Offred, a Handmaid struggling to hold onto her memories, her will to survive, and a glimmer of hope in a world devoid of humanity and freedom. This timeless, critically-acclaimed tale of resistance, resilience, and the indomitable human spirit is an instant classic not to be missed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Endling A Novel

    Maria Reva

    Endling is a stunning, genre-bending debut that seamlessly weaves together dark humor, gripping storytelling, and profound commentary on war, hope, and the human condition.

    For: Readers seeking a thought-provoking, ambitious novel that blends the absurd with the urgent.

    Strengths: Endling excels at crafting vivid, complex characters, and its unique premise — a biologist and sisters navigating a war-torn Ukraine — yields a riveting, unpredictable narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the non-linear structure and dense, lyrical prose challenging to follow, requiring a patient, attentive approach.

    Comparable: For fans of unconventional, thematically rich fiction like Jennifer Egan's A Visit from the Goon Squad or David Mitchell's Cloud Atlas, Endling offers a similarly innovative, boundary-pushing reading experience.

    “An impressive high-wire act—one that blends the horror of the war with all the bleak and unintended absurdity it has produced in its wake.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Another Country

    James Baldwin

    Immerse yourself in James Baldwin's vivid portrayal of interwoven passions spanning Greenwich Village, Harlem, and France. In this groundbreaking novel, the masks of identity are stripped away by the raw intensity of love and hate, revealing the elemental beauty and sublime complexity of the human experience. A must-read for anyone seeking a deeply moving and thought-provoking exploration of race, sex, politics, and art.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Sympathizer A Novel (Pulitzer Prize for Fiction)

    Associate Professor of English and American Studies and Ethnicity Viet Thanh Nguyen

    In this dazzling debut, Viet Thanh Nguyen introduces a singular narrator - a conflicted double agent navigating post-Vietnam War Los Angeles. With the pace of a thriller and prose likened to Greene and Bellow, this epic tale of love, betrayal, and identity weaves a sweeping espionage narrative that probes the complexities of refugee life and the American Dream. Winner of the 2016 Pulitzer Prize, The Sympathizer is a groundbreaking novel that will captivate readers with its bold, uncanny voice and unforgettable story.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Homegoing

    Yaa Gyasi

    Homegoing is a powerful, sweeping historical novel that sheds light on the devastating legacy of slavery and its impact on families across generations.

    For: Historical fiction enthusiasts, readers interested in the African diaspora, and anyone seeking a nuanced exploration of slavery's enduring effects.

    Strengths: Gyasi's masterful storytelling weaves together the parallel lives of two sisters and their descendants, creating a rich tapestry of experiences that span continents and centuries.

    Skip if: The sheer scope of the narrative can make it challenging to fully absorb each character's arc, particularly in the later generations.

    Comparable: Fans of epic, multigenerational family sagas like Toni Morrison's Beloved or Colson Whitehead's Underground Railroad will appreciate Homegoing's ambitious scope and emotional resonance.

    “Powerful. . . . Compelling. . . . Illuminating.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. If We Were Villains A Novel

    M. L. Rio

    Step into a world of rivalry, obsession, and deceit with M.L. Rio's riveting debut, If We Were Villains. Drawing comparisons to Donna Tartt's The Secret History, this captivating mystery unfurls as a group of young Shakespearean actors at an elite conservatory find their friendships turned toxic and their staged tragedies mirrored in a real-life tragedy. With clever nods to the Bard and a keen eye for the darker aspects of ambition, Rio crafts a gripping tale of innocence and guilt that will keep you enthralled to the electrifying conclusion.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Flashlight A Novel

    Susan Choi

    Flashlight is a masterful, sweeping novel that masterfully weaves together family secrets, historical trauma, and a gripping mystery, cementing author Susan Choi's status as a major literary talent.

    For: Readers who appreciate complex, thought-provoking fiction with a strong historical and cultural context will find much to admire in Flashlight.

    Strengths: Choi's prose is vivid, evocative, and astute, while the novel's intricate plotting and character development make for a riveting, unpredictable read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the non-linear narrative structure and dense historical detail overwhelming, requiring a level of focus and commitment.

    Comparable: Fans of literary fiction that explores the intersections of family, identity, and historical upheaval, such as works by authors like Haruki Murakami or Michael Ondaatje, will likely appreciate Flashlight's unique blend of styles and themes.

    “A major world writer . . . Choi is in thrilling command.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Between Two Fires

    Christopher Buehlman

    Dive into a dark medieval fantasy with Between Two Fires, a chilling tale of angels, demons, and the apocalypse. Disgraced knight Thomas joins a young orphan girl on a perilous quest to confront evil and find redemption, navigating a grotesque landscape of risen dead, demonic forces, and soaring angelic battles for humanity's very soul. This beautifully crafted hardcover edition is a must-read for fans of horror and historical fiction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. A Farewell to Arms The Hemingway Library Edition

    Ernest Hemingway

    Step into the trenches with Hemingway's masterpiece, A Farewell to Arms, a gripping and poignant novel that captures the harsh realities of war and the bittersweet pain of forbidden love. Through the eyes of an American ambulance driver and his English nurse, this classic tale unfolds against the devastating backdrop of World War I, reflecting Hemingway's own experiences and struggles. Featuring the author's handwritten notes and alternate endings, this edition offers a unique glimpse into Hemingway's creative process, making it a must-read for literature enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The End of the Affair

    Graham Greene

    Graham Greene's The End of the Affair is a gripping, poignant tale of forbidden love, betrayal, and redemption in war-torn London. As Maurice, a writer, and Sarah, a married woman, risk everything for their passionate affair, the novel masterfully probes the depths of human frailty, guilt, and the complexities of the heart. A classic of 20th-century literature, this beloved novel is a must-read for anyone seeking a singularly moving, beautifully written exploration of love's power and its costs.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. This Is How You Lose the Time War

    Amal El-Mohtar

    Diving into the meticulously crafted cosmos of 'This Is How You Lose the Time War' feels akin to stumbling upon a hidden treasure trove of interstellar secrets. Amal El-Mohtar's poignant, lyrical prose effortlessly weaves together dual narratives set across the fabric of time, evoking a profound sense of longing and wonder in the reader. Each word carries the weight of a thousand unspoken emotions, the struggle between rebellion and order, and the aching desire for connection that transcends eras and galaxies. Prepare to be transported, transformed, and forever altered by this masterful tale of time, love, and the human condition.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Life and Fate

    Vasily Grossman

    Grossman's sprawling, meticulous masterpiece, Life and Fate, weaves the epic struggle of WWII into the intimate story of a family torn apart by history's darkest forces. From the battlefields of Stalingrad to the haunting certainties of the Gulag, this unsparing yet visionary novel offers a searing reckoning of the 20th century's horrors and fragile hopes. Join this unforgettable journey into the hearts and minds of characters that span the spectrum of humanity, from victims to tyrants, in a work hailed as one of the greatest achievements of modern Russian literature.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  20. The Safekeep

    Yael van der Wouden

    This psychological novel's nuanced exploration of post-war trauma, repressed desires, and the complexities of human relationships, paired with its masterful writing and gripping plot, make it a standout.

    For: Literate readers seeking a thought-provoking, emotionally resonant story.

    Strengths: Van der Wouden's vivid prose, richly detailed setting, and expertly crafted tension and suspense make for a riveting read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the slow-burning pace and introspective nature of the characters challenging to engage with.

    Comparable: Fans of literary fiction exploring historical and psychological themes, such as Atonement or The Nightingale, will appreciate The Safekeep's depth and complexity.

    “A brilliantly executed twist elevates this love story into something much darker.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  21. The Ministry of Time A Novel

    Kaliane Bradley

    A dazzling, genre-bending debut, The Ministry of Time weaves a thrilling tale of time travel, espionage, and romance. When a modern civil servant is sent to monitor an 1845 Arctic expedition commander who's inexplicably appeared in the present, an unlikely bond forms and evolves into a profound love story. As secrets unfold, the protagonist must grapple with the consequences of her choices and whether she can alter the course of history. With wit, sensuality, and a captivating blend of science fiction and literary fiction, this original masterpiece by Kaliane Bradley will captivate readers and leave them pondering the power of love and change.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  22. I Who Have Never Known Men

    Jacqueline Harpman

    Dear friend, prepare for a haunting and immersive read with 'I Who Have Never Known Men' by Jacqueline Harpman! This stunning memoir, a cultural artifact from 1970s Belgium, chronicles the author's time as a young woman in a hippie commune where she was married off to a mysterious stranger. Harpman's lyrical prose, infused with surrealism and mysticism, will captivate those drawn to coming-of-age tales, experimental writing, and the complexities of feminist awakening. If you're ready for a surreal journey of self-discovery, then this cult classic is for you.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  23. The Nights Are Quiet in Tehran Shortlisted for the International Booker Prize 2026

    Shida Bazyar

    In this captivating and polyphonic novel, Shida Bazyar masterfully weaves the story of one family's turbulent journey through Iran's revolutionary decades. Spanning from the hopeful protests of 1979 to the student-led uprising of 2009, THE NIGHTS ARE QUIET IN TEHRAN is a powerful exploration of revolution, oppression, resistance, and the unrelenting human desire for freedom. With its deft portrayal of the complex, often fraught relationships between family members, this gripping literary work is sure to resonate deeply with readers. A Shortlisted nominee for the 2026 International Booker Prize.

Frequently asked questions

Why are strong female leads important in historical fiction?
Representing diverse perspectives and experiences enriches the genre, offering a more balanced view of history.
What historical periods are covered in these novels?
From ancient Africa to 20th-century Europe, these stories traverse various eras and continents.
Can I find books with both historical and speculative elements?
Yes, some titles blend historical accuracy with fantastical elements, offering unique narrative approaches.
Are these novels primarily centered around romance or relationships?
While romantic relationships may play a role, these books focus more on the historical context, social issues, and the protagonists' personal growth and struggles.
