12 Best Books That Blend Reality & Fantasy in Unique Ways

These captivating novels seamlessly merge the familiar with the extraordinary, creating immersive worlds that blur the lines between reality and fantasy.

Who these books are for

  • Fantasy & literary fiction readers
  • Book clubs seeking complex discussions
  • Anyone who enjoys imaginative storytelling

Start here

If you only read one book that deftly blends reality and fantasy, choose 'American Fantasy' by Emma Straub for its inventive and poignant portrayal of a family's struggles and triumphs.

Books that blend reality and fantasy offer readers a unique and captivating experience. These 12 novels masterfully combine elements of the everyday with the extraordinary, transporting you to immersive worlds that challenge your perceptions. From haunting magical realism to inventive speculative fiction, these stories will intrigue and enchant you.

The collection

  1. Bel Canto A Novel

    Ann Patchett

    In this enchanting novel, Ann Patchett masterfully weaves together the lives of hostages and their captors at a luxurious party in South America, where the lines between oppressor and oppressed blur in the face of shared humanity. As a renowned soprano enchants the international guests with her singing, an unexpected bloom of connections and romance blossoms amidst the peril, creating a poignant meditation on love, strength, and the transformative power of art. Bel Canto is a captivating tale of resilience, beauty, and the unifying force of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Sandwich A Novel

    Catherine Newman

    This novel masterfully weaves together humor, heart, and insightful exploration of family dynamics, middle age, and womanhood, making it a standout contemporary read.

    For: Lovers of character-driven fiction, families, and women's stories will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Newman's witty prose, relatable characters, and poignant portrayal of the complexities of family relationships and female experiences make this novel a triumph.

    Skip if: While the writing is superb, some readers may find the introspective nature and occasional meandering of the narrative pace a bit slow.

    Comparable: Fans of novels that examine the intricacies of family life, such as Ann Patchett's 'Commonwealth' or Elin Hilderbrand's 'The Identicals', will find 'Sandwich' to be a similarly engaging and emotionally resonant read.

    “A beach novel to pass on to your best friends, no matter what the season.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Infinite Jest (30th Anniversary Edition)

    David Foster Wallace

    Dive into this wildly inventive, laugh-out-loud funny, and profoundly philosophical contemporary classic. In Infinite Jest, David Foster Wallace masterfully explores the relentless pursuit of entertainment in America, the yearning for human connection, and the deep questions life poses. A sweeping tale set in an addiction rehab center and a tennis academy, it's a wildly entertaining ride through the joys and pitfalls of our obsession with pleasure and diversion. Don't miss this 30th anniversary edition, hailed by genius Stephen King as one of the two greatest American novels of the last half century. Prepare to be challenged, delighted, and forever transformed by Wallace's virtuosic, wickedly comic masterpiece.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. There There

    Tommy Orange

    In this powerful debut novel, Tommy Orange weaves together the lives of twelve urban Native Americans - each grappling with their own traumas and struggles - as they converge on a powwow in Oakland, California. Spanning generations, Orange delves into the complex interstices of identity, culture, family, and resilience in the face of colonialism's long shadow. With unflinching lyricism and a keen eye for detail, 'There There' grapples with the painful truths of the Native American experience, offering a testament to the enduring strength of a people often forgotten, yet forever present.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Midnight Library: A GMA Book Club Pick A Novel

    Matt Haig

    "Dear friend, if you're yearning for a tale that blends the surreal with profound truths about life, love, and the choices we make, then 'The Midnight Library' by Matt Haig is a must-read. With its unique concept of a liminal space where souls relive their existence, exploring various paths not taken, this novel will resonate deeply with anyone grappling with the big questions. Haig's lyrical prose and empathetic characters make this a haunting yet ultimately hopeful journey. Give this a try if you appreciate philosophical fiction and stories that challenge your perspective on existence."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Stag Dance A Quartet

    Torrey Peters

    "Stag Dance: A Quartet" by Torrey Peters is a tour-de-force - a collection of linked short stories that deftly blend the surreal and the absurd with deep emotional resonance. Through the lens of a fictional stag party, Peters masterfully explores themes of masculinity, identity, desire, and the search for connection in an often isolating world. Fans of unconventional, thought-provoking fiction will revel in Peters' bold, unflinching prose and relish the intricate character studies that reveal the complexities of the human experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Practice Makes Perfect A Novel

    Sarah Adams

    "A sweet and sexy romance that blooms when a simple, flower shop owner and an emotionally guarded bad boy spar in flirtation, challenge each other's assumptions, and discover their real love match in a charming small town setting. Annie and Will's chemistry is undeniable, their banter irresistible, making for an uplifting read about embracing the imperfect and finding true love."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Harlem Rhapsody

    Victoria Christopher Murray

    Step into the vibrant world of Harlem in the early 20th century, where a passionate editor named Jessie Redmon Fauset sparks a literary revolution in African American arts and letters. In this captivating historical fiction, bestselling author Victoria Christopher Murray masterfully chronicles Jessie's daring affair with W. E. B. Du Bois, her tireless quest to discover and nurture groundbreaking young writers like Langston Hughes and Zora Neale Hurston, and the immense cost of her trailblazing success. A gripping narrative at once a page-turning tale and a profound lesson in Black literary history, Harlem Rhapsody is a must-read from one of today's most acclaimed writers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Contrapposto A Novel

    Dave Eggers

    Embark on a sweeping, sixty-five-year journey of passion, artistry, and unyielding devotion with Cricket Dib and Olympia Argyros in Dave Eggers' extraordinary novel, Contrapposto. From their innocent playground provocations to navigating the treacherous art world, these soulmates—lifeselong partners in both love and creativity—search for meaning, dodge charlatans, and strive to bring new beauty into the world. A big-hearted, undulating tale of friendship, love, and the power of art, Contrapposto is an earthy, moving celebration of a lifelong partnership that will leave you uplifted and forever changed.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. What We Can Know A Novel

    Ian McEwan

    What We Can Know is a masterful blend of historical fiction, philosophical musing, and speculative futurism, exploring the nature of truth and memory across time.

    For: Literary fiction enthusiasts, fans of McEwan's unique style, and those interested in philosophical and historical themes.

    Strengths: Ian McEwan's lyrical prose, complex plotting, and thought-provoking ideas make for a rich and engaging read.

    Skip if: The non-linear structure and dense themes may be challenging for some readers; a second, slower read may be necessary to fully appreciate the depth of the narrative.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed McEwan's earlier works like Atonement and Saturday, as well as speculative fiction with philosophical undertones, such as Margaret Atwood's The Year of the Flood, will likely appreciate What We Can Know.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Midnight Train A Novel

    Matt Haig

    Step aboard the enchanting Midnight Train, where the line between past and present blurs. Relive the most cherished moments with Maggie, your soulmate, in the romantic city of Venice, and ponder the choices that shaped your life. Matt Haig's inventive tale of love, longing, and time travel will take you on an unforgettable journey, challenging you to face the consequences of second chances.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Super Sad True Love Story A Novel

    Gary Shteyngart

    In this wickedly funny and eerily prescient dystopian novel, Gary Shteyngart paints a darkly comic vision of America's near future, where social media addiction, looming fascism, and financial crisis threaten to crush the human spirit. Amidst this chaos, a tender love story blooms between Lenny, a book-loving Everyman, and Eunice, a captivating, unpredictable young woman. Their unlikely romance becomes a beacon of hope as they navigate the crumbling world around them, grappling with what it means to be human in an increasingly inhuman society. With its biting social commentary and heartwarming core, Super Sad True Love Story is a hilarious and haunting must-read.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Beartown A Novel

    Fredrik Backman

    "For a small town, Beartown has big dreams riding on its junior ice hockey team as they compete for a national title. But when a violent act shatters the community, loyalty, friendship, and the weight of responsibility come under scrutiny. With Backman's masterful storytelling, this heart-wrenching tale explores the complex bonds between people and the choices that define us, all set against the backdrop of a hypnotic forest town. Don't miss this gripping and profound novel that delves into the human condition."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. When the Tides Held the Moon

    Venessa Vida Kelley

    Immerse yourself in this enchanting historical fantasy, where a young Puerto Rican immigrant named Benny finds love and self-discovery in early 20th-century New York City. When Benny captures a mesmerizing merman for a Coney Island sideshow, a profound connection forms between them. But as Benny's heart swells with affection for Río, he must grapple with the cruel reality of the merman's captivity and decide whether to risk everything for their freedom. With lush, detailed illustrations throughout, "When the Tides Held the Moon" is a captivating, queer tale of elemental love that will resonate deeply.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Everything Was Beautiful and Nothing Hurt A Novel

    Ben Reeves

    This novel earns its place in the collection with its original premise, vivid characters, and profound exploration of mortality and the human experience.

    For: Fans of literary fiction, particularly those interested in philosophical themes and unique storytelling, will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Everything Was Beautiful and Nothing Hurt excels at crafting an immersive narrative voice through its Death protagonist, while also delivering poignant insights into love, loss, and the meaning of life.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the slow, introspective pacing challenging, particularly if they prefer faster-paced narratives.

    Comparable: This novel shares similarities with other thought-provoking, character-driven works like The Book Thief, The Lovely Bones, and A Monster Calls, offering a unique perspective on mortality and the human condition.

    “This novel has all the ingredients of unforgettability: a plot you”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Big Swiss A Novel

    Jen Beagin

    Embark on a wildly romantic, darkly comedic tale with Big Swiss, a National Bestseller that's been touted as "one of the funniest books of the last few years." Meet Greta, a transcriptionist enthralled by her boss Om's repressed client, Big Swiss. As Greta's fascination bleeds into infidelity, this provocative novel delves into the complexities of relationships, mental health, and sexual stereotypes. With its "fantastic, weird-as-hell" narrative, Big Swiss is a singular, thought-provoking read that will leave you laughing and questioning the boundaries of love.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Beach Read

    Emily Henry

    In Emily Henry's delightful romance "Beach Read," ambition and love storylines intertwine at the shore, where a struggling writer and a charming literary agent clash amidst the salty breeze and sun-kissed sands. Henry's witty prose and nuanced characterizations create an irresistible beach read, perfect for savoring while the waves caress the shore. This charming novel will appeal to fans of Elizabeth Gilbert and Jennifer Weiner with its thoughtful exploration of creativity, family, and the bittersweet chase of happiness.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. American Fantasy A Novel

    Emma Straub

    Emma Straub's witty, insightful novel captures the nostalgia and reawakening of middle age, with a unique premise and relatable characters.

    For: Fans of character-driven, humorous stories about identity, family, and finding one's place in the world will love this.

    Strengths: Straub excels at vividly conjuring settings and emotions, making the nostalgic 90s setting and shipboard community come alive. Her humor, especially in depicting middle-aged women's reactions, is frequent and hilarious.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow due to the focus on character development and social commentary over plot.

    Comparable: In its mix of nostalgia, humor, and character studies, American Fantasy is reminiscent of novels like Where'd You Go, Bernadette and The Vacationers.

    “Wickedly funny...A fizzy escapist thrill ride with real insights on the isolation of celebrity, the community of fandom, and the liberation of middle age.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. The Things We Never Say A Novel

    Elizabeth Strout

    This profound exploration of human connection and isolation, woven through a nuanced portrayal of a teacher's double life, earns its place with Strout's signature depth and empathy.

    For: Lovers of character-driven literary fiction seeking stories that probe the complexities of relationships and the human condition.

    Strengths: Strout's masterful character development, keen psychological insights, and thoughtful exploration of the mysteries of human connection.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the introspective, contemplative pace a slower burn.

    Comparable: Fans of Elizabeth Strout's other works, such as Olive Kitteridge and Lucy Barton, will appreciate the similar themes and character-driven narrative.

    “This is a profound, resplendent novel that stares our present moment in the face while throwing a lifeboat to cling to in the storm.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What are some popular examples of magical realism books?
Books like 'One Hundred Years of Solitude' by Gabriel Garcia Marquez, 'The Particular Sadness of Lemon Cake' by Aimee Bender, and 'The Night Circus' by Erin Morgenstern showcase this genre's imaginative storytelling.
Can I find books that blend fantasy with other genres, like sci-fi or mystery?
Absolutely. Novels like 'Infinite Jest', 'Super Sad True Love Story', and 'The Midnight Library' successfully integrate fantasy elements with other genres for diverse, engaging literary experiences.
