Epic Classical Literature

Explore timeless tales of heroism and adventure with these essential epic classical novels.

Who these books are for

  • Classical literature enthusiasts
  • History buffs
  • Students of Greek and Roman studies
  • Fans of adventure and epic storytelling

Start here

If you only read one epic classical novel, choose Homer's Odyssey – a foundational work of Western literature that has captivated readers for millennia.

Epic classical literature, encompassing works from ancient Greece and Rome, has left an indelible mark on the world of storytelling. These novels, written centuries ago, still resonate with readers today for their powerful tales of heroism, adventure, and the human condition. From Homer's iconic Odyssey to James Joyce's modernist reimagining in Ulysses, this curated collection of essential epic classical novels offers a fascinating journey through the evolution of literature and the timeless allure of ancient stories. Whether you're a scholar, a history buff, or simply a lover of great tales, these books are sure to captivate and inspire.

The collection

  1. Langston Hughes Poetry

    Langston Hughes

    Immerse yourself in the vibrant world of Langston Hughes, one of the Harlem Renaissance's most iconic voices. This Standard Ebooks edition brings together his earliest, most accessible works from the 1920s, offering a powerful glimpse into the lives and struggles of the Black working class, the rhythms of jazz and blues, and the dawn of racial awareness that defined his groundbreaking poetry.

  2. Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglas

    Frederick Douglass

    "Escape to a world of unflinching truth and unshakeable hope with Frederick Douglass's seminal slave narrative, a best-selling masterpiece that shattered delusions and fueled the abolitionist fire. Douglass's gripping account of life in bondage, his daring flight to freedom, and his subsequent rise as a powerful orator and writer is a must-read for anyone seeking a first-hand glimpse into the brutal realities of slavery and the indomitable human spirit that could not be broken."

  3. The Song of Achilles A Novel

    Madeline Miller

    "Dear friend, I just devoured 'The Song of Achilles' by Madeline Miller and it's left me utterly enchanted! This stunning debut novel breathes new life into Homer's 'Iliad', weaving a captivating love story between mortal hero Patroclus and the legendary Achilles. Miller's vivid prose, rich with mythological detail, will transport you to the epic world of ancient Greece. If you're a lover of historical fiction, mythology, or just crave an exquisite tale of forbidden love, this is an absolute must-read!"

  4. The Alchemist A Fable About Following Your Dream

    Paulo Coelho

    In 'The Alchemist', Paulo Coelho weaves a mesmerizing fable about the transformative power of following one's dreams. This deceptively simple tale, set against the backdrop of a young shepherd's mystical journey across the Sahara Desert, reveals profound insights into spirituality, self-discovery, and the unseen forces that guide us towards our destinies. Through lyrical prose and vivid imagery, Coelho creates an immersive reading experience that will resonate deeply with anyone seeking to unlock the secrets of their soul. Prepare to be enchanted, inspired, and forever changed by this timeless classic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Golden Bowl

    Henry James

    In this masterful psychological novel, Henry James proves his unparalleled skill at exploring the intricate, hidden dynamics of relationships and the fragility of identity. Through the story of Maggie Verver, an American heiress torn between love for her father and her husband, and the secrets entwined with a gilded crystal bowl, James crafts a nuanced and gripping tale that delves into the complexities of duty, desire, and the path to self-discovery.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Good Soldier

    Ford Madox Ford

    This 1915 novel showcases Ford Madox Ford's pioneering use of an 'unreliable narrator' technique, exploring psychological themes and the societal upheaval of its time.

    For: Literary students and scholars of Modernism, as well as readers interested in experimental narrative and early 20th-century social change.

    Strengths: Features a groundbreaking narrative structure, offers insightful commentary on the psychology and mores of the early 20th century, and sets the stage for later Modernist works.

    Skip if: Requires close attention and re-reading to fully grasp the complex, layered story, and some readers may find the writing style and historical context challenging.

    Comparable: Compared to other Modernist novels in the collection, The Good Soldier shares similarities with James Joyce's Ulysses in its experimental narrative techniques and exploration of the inner lives of its characters.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Odyssey

    Homer

    This fresh translation of Homer's ancient epic is a landmark achievement, capturing the beauty and drama of the original poem in vivid, contemporary language.

    For: Classics enthusiasts, literature students, and anyone interested in ancient Greek culture and epic storytelling.

    Strengths: Emily Wilson's authoritative translation brings new life to the timeless narrative, with a focus on naturalism and musicality that makes the work irresistibly readable.

    Skip if: The poem's dense, poetic language and complex themes may make it challenging for some readers to fully engage with.

    Comparable: Fans of epic poetry and classical literature may also appreciate translations of The Iliad, Virgil's Aeneid, or other ancient masterworks.

    “Irresistibly readable, Wilson's Odyssey turns Homeric epic into a poetic feast.--Froma Zeitlin, Princeton University”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Devotions

    Lucy Caldwell

    In Lucy Caldwell's captivating short story collection, Devotions, she masterfully explores themes of longing, love, and transformation. With her keen insight and lyrical prose, Caldwell weaves together eight unforgettable tales that transport readers to distant pasts and uncertain futures. From a Belfast theatre troupe's bold production of Hamlet to a musician's disorienting journey through time, these narratives showcase Caldwell's reputation as a "finest short form craftswoman" and offer profound insights into the human experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Ulysses

    James Joyce

    Embark on a timeless, ambitious odyssey through the streets of Dublin as James Joyce masterfully weaves the interconnected lives of Leopold Bloom and Stephen Daedalus over the course of a single day in 1904. With its innovative stream-of-consciousness narrative, vibrant characterizations, and rich symbolism, Ulysses is a groundbreaking literary masterpiece that defies conventions, probes the human condition, and rewards close reading with each revelatory reread. A must for any serious reader's shelf, this comprehensive edition offers the complete, corrected text along with pivotal historical context.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Sound and the Fury The Complete, Definitive Edition

    William Faulkner

    In this epic tale of a decaying Southern aristocratic family, Nobel laureate William Faulkner crafts a mesmerizing narrative that spans generations, delving into themes of legacy, despair, and the elusive nature of time. Through the fragmented voices and actions of characters like the enigmatic Caddy, the obsessive Quentin, and the brutal Jason, Faulkner masterfully weaves a tragic portrait of the Compson family, offering profound insights into the human condition in the process. This definitive edition of his masterpiece is a must-read for anyone seeking a literary epic that will linger in the mind long after the final page is turned.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Traveler

    Joseph Eckert

    Buckle up for an unforgettable father-son tale of love and destiny in The Traveler. When Scott Treder's mundane life abruptly catapults him an entire day into the future, he's unwittingly set on a quest to reconcile time and family amidst a chaotic multiverse. As Scott rockets forward, leaving his wife and precocious son Lyle in the dust, their bond becomes the only constant in a story spanning centuries of science, heartache, and hope - a poignant exploration of the human spirit's resilience in the face of the unimaginable.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Iliad

    Homer

    A revelatory, contemporary translation of Homer's revered epic that brings the ancient poem's thrilling battle scenes, emotional complexity, and divine machinations to life in crisp, resonant language.

    For: Classics enthusiasts, literature students, and anyone seeking a fresh, accessible interpretation of The Iliad.

    Strengths: Emily Wilson's translation captures the poem's intense emotions, vivid imagery, and rhythmic pace, making Homer's masterpiece feel immediate and visceral.

    Skip if: Skip if you prefer a more concise, abridged version, as this is a complete, unabridged translation.

    Comparable: Fans of The Odyssey, other epic poetry, and classical literature will appreciate this engaging, authoritative Iliad translation that complements Wilson's earlier work.

    “and with Wilson the great tragedy of the poem is felt afresh.--John Byron Kuhner”

  13. A Fine Balance A Novel

    Rohinton Mistry

    In this sweeping, Dickensian novel, four disparate individuals - a widow, a displaced student, and two migrant tailors - find solace and purpose in each other during India's 1975 State of Emergency. With masterful storytelling, Rohinton Mistry weaves their paths together, showcasing the resilience of the human spirit in the face of crushing oppression and societal turmoil. A Fine Balance is an unforgettable journey into the heart of India's past, and a testament to the transformative power of friendship and love.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Song of Solomon A Novel

    Toni Morrison

    In Toni Morrison's powerful and lyrical novel, Song of Solomon, Milkman Dead embarks on a transformative journey, fleeing the limitations of his rustbelt hometown for the distant origins of his enigmatic family. With vivid characters, poetic prose, and a profound exploration of the Black experience, Morrison weaves a breathtaking tapestry of love, identity, and the human struggle for freedom and self-discovery.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Stoner

    John Williams

    John Williams' novel is a masterpiece of understated storytelling, offering a poignant and deeply moving portrayal of an ordinary life that resonates with quiet tragedy.

    For: Academic readers, literary enthusiasts, and anyone seeking a profound exploration of the human condition.

    Strengths: Williams' luminous prose, nuanced characterization, and keen observations of the American experience make Stoner a work of quiet perfection.

    Skip if: The novel's deliberate pace and lack of dramatic flair may deter some readers from fully engaging with Stoner's quiet, introspective journey.

    Comparable: Fans of introspective, character-driven novels like The Crying of Lot 49, The Secret History, or The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao will appreciate Stoner's thoughtful, contemplative tone.

    “One of the great unheralded 20th-century American novels …Almost perfect.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. 70,000 Poems

    Lenna Jawdat

    Lenna Jawdat's 70,000 is a profound, multi-layered exploration of cultural erasure and resilience, born from the devastating loss of 70,000 books from Palestinian homes and libraries before and during 1948. Through inventive poetry, memoir, and visual collage, Jawdat transforms an individual act of grief into a collective gesture of remembrance and hope. This powerful, deeply moving book is a must-read for anyone grappling with the complexities of displacement, memory, and the enduring power of words to connect us across time and space.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Homer's Odyssey An Illustrated Retelling

    Barry B. Powell

    Powell's retelling of Homer's Odyssey offers a fresh, modern voice while still honoring the epic's timeless essence, beautifully complemented by lush illustrations.

    For: Classics enthusiasts, literature students, and anyone seeking a captivating, accessible Odyssey experience.

    Strengths: Powell's prose brings the ancient tale to life, his lyrical style mirroring Homer's; the woodcut artwork adds a striking visual dimension.

    Skip if: This is a dense, scholarly retelling; some readers may find it slower-paced than a standard translation.

    Comparable: While not a direct translation, Powell's Odyssey shares the spirit of other retellings aiming to make the classic accessible, such as Robert Fagles' or Emily Wilson's translations.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What defines an epic classical novel?
Epic classical novels are long narrative poems or prose works that focus on heroic deeds and extraordinary feats, often set in ancient times.
Why are these books considered essential reading?
These works have had profound influences on Western literature, shaping our understanding of history, mythology, and the human experience.
