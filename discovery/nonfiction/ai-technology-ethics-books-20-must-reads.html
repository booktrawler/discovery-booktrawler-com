AI, Technology & Ethics Books: 20 Must-Reads

Explore the human side of artificial intelligence in these thought-provoking nonfiction books.

Who these books are for

  • AI and tech enthusiasts
  • Philosophers and ethicists interested in AI
  • Business leaders and entrepreneurs shaping AI
  • Students of computer science, media, and sociology
  • Anyone curious about the future of human-machine relationships

Start here

If you only read one book about AI and ethics, choose 'The AI Con' by Emily M. Bender for a comprehensive, insider's perspective on the technology's impact.

Artificial intelligence (AI) is revolutionizing modern society, from automating tasks in various industries to shaping our personal lives and interactions. As AI increasingly intersects with human society, it's crucial to examine its implications, benefits, and risks through the lens of ethics and technology. This curated collection of 20 must-read nonfiction books offers a diverse range of perspectives on AI, from insider insights to philosophical explorations, to help readers better understand the complex relationship between humans and machines in the age of AI.

The collection

  1. Reader, Come Home The Reading Brain in a Digital World

    Maryanne Wolf

    In this insightful exploration, renowned reading expert Maryanne Wolf shares her concerns and hopes about the future of deep reading in a digitally dominated world. Drawing on groundbreaking research, Wolf argues that our increasing reliance on screens may fundamentally alter the way our brains process written language, potentially eroding essential cognitive skills like critical thinking, empathy, and imagination. A crucial roadmap for understanding these changes and preserving the most valuable aspects of reading, Reader, Come Home offers a hopeful perspective on the impact of technology on our brains and our future.

  4. Pay Attention How the Algorithms and Media Wars Are Suppressing Truth and Rewiring Your Brain

    David Pakman

    In Pay Attention, the New York Times bestselling author David Pakman issues a dire warning that our pursuit of truth has become a quaint relic in a world dominated by the Attention Economy. Drawing from his frontline experience in the digital media wars, Pakman exposes how algorithms and the media's relentless focus on controversy, division, and visual stimuli are reprogramming our brains to crave sensationalism over substance. Part cautionary tale, part guide to navigating the dystopian landscape, Pay Attention is a must-read for anyone seeking to reclaim their humanity in a society where the very notion of truth is under siege.

  5. Blood in the Machine The Origins of the Rebellion Against Big Tech

    Brian Merchant

    Uncover the origins of our modern rebellion against Big Tech in this gripping true story that spans from 19th-century England to today's AI boom. "Blood in the Machine" reveals how the Luddites' fight against job-stealing machines mirrors the struggles we face now as automation threatens millions of livelihoods. With a gripping narrative that combines history and science, author Brian Merchant probes the power and threat of technology, offering crucial insights for navigating our rapidly changing world. A must-read for understanding the impact of AI on our lives and our future.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Artificial Intimacy Who We Become When We Talk to Machines

    Sherry Turkle

    In Artificial Intimacy, psychologist Sherry Turkle issues a groundbreaking warning about our increasing reliance on human-like AI chatbots, which is eroding our capacity for empathy, caring, and humanity. As more of us turn to these "intimate machines" for companionship and emotional support, Turkle argues that we're accepting a "mere performance" of intimacy, empathy, and love from machines, and redefining human connection accordingly. With compelling stories and decades of expertise analyzing digital culture, Turkle reveals how our reliance on machine relationships is producing a generation more isolated, depressed, and lonely than ever. Yet, by tracing AI's impact through the lifecycle from childhood to end-of-life, Turkle also offers a roadmap for reclaiming our humanity in the age of AI.

  7. Enshittification Why Everything Suddenly Got Worse and What to Do About It

    Cory Doctorow

    In Enshittification, Cory Doctorow dissects the ways in which the internet has devolved into a distressingly dysfunctional "enstinkified" space, documenting the policies and choices behind this downfall. But rather than simply lamenting the situation, Doctorow provides a hopeful roadmap for "disenshittification," empowering readers to reclaim control and create a better, more functional digital world. With piercing insight and practical advice, Enshittification is a crucial tool for anyone seeking to understand and transform our increasingly problematic online landscape.

    Comparable: Those who enjoyed works like 'Life 3.0' by Max Tegmark or 'We Are Data' by John Cheney-Lippold will appreciate the in-depth exploration of AI's impact in 'Empire of AI'.

From prehistoric times to the rise of AI, Harari upends conventional wisdom and reveals the intricate interplay between information, truth, power, and our own survival. A masterful must-read for anyone grappling with the complexities of our turbulent info-age.

  11. Project Maven A Marine Colonel, His Team, and the Dawn of AI Warfare

    Katrina Manson

    Blurb: Project Maven is the gripping, behind-the-scenes account of how the US military pioneered the use of artificial intelligence in warfare, transforming the nature of combat and sparking controversy. Drawing on over 200 insider interviews, author Katrina Manson takes readers into the high-stakes world of Pentagon decision-making, Silicon Valley innovation, and the ethical debates surrounding AI's increasing role in making life-or-death decisions on the battlefield. A must-read for anyone seeking to understand the profound implications of AI in an era of shifting global power dynamics and emerging military technologies.

  12. Cyberselfish A Critical Romp through the Terribly Libertarian Culture of High Tech

    Paulina Borsook

    In this prescient 1998 critique, Paulina Borsook skewers the inhumane, liberty-obsessed tech culture that has come to dominate our world. With humor and insight, she exposes the paradox that the very industry sustained by massive government subsidies now rejects regulation and empathy, prioritizing profits over people. Borsook's revelatory observations on the "stockholder theory of value," Ayn Rand worship, and disdain for privacy have become tragically apt in the tech giants' increasing influence over society. A must-read for anyone concerned about the corrosive "nerd revenge" ambitions that threaten our collective well-being.

    Amy Westervelt

With meticulous research and incisive wit, Westervelt unmasks the PR pioneers, think tanks, and propagandists who have built a vast apparatus to deceive the public and poison the informational ecosystem. This searing indictment is a must-read for anyone seeking to understand and combat the corrosive influence of corporate misinformation.

  14. The God Test Artificial Intelligence and Our Coming Cosmic Reckoning

    Robert Wright

    In "The God Test", Pulitzer Prize finalist Robert Wright offers a profound and timely exploration of the AI revolution as an evolutionary force poised to reshape human society. By tracing the dynamics of intelligence back to the origin of life on Earth, Wright argues this technological advancement is driven by universal principles that, if understood, can equip us to wisely navigate its transformative power and ultimately find deeper meaning and purpose for the species. A must-read for anyone seeking to comprehend the magnitude and potential of our rapidly changing world.

  15. AI for Good How Real People Are Using Artificial Intelligence to Fix Things That Matter

    Josh Tyrangiel

    In "AI for Good," journalist Josh Tyrangiel shines a light on the unsung heroes using artificial intelligence to improve our world, not just for profit or prestige, but to tackle real problems and make a positive difference. From vaccine distribution to advanced medicine, education, and human connection, Tyrangiel profiles everyday citizens who leveraged AI as a tool to amplify human judgment, rather than replace it. This book is a refreshing, inspiring look at AI's quiet revolution, with valuable lessons on its potential to be a force for good - even in the face of challenges and setbacks.

Through unprecedented interviews and incisive cultural analysis, Pelly reveals the cost of the perfect playlist - from artists' diminishing royalties to listeners' data-driven listening habits. A must-read for anyone curious about the future of music and the battle for a fairer, more sustainable streaming model.

    Jared Cooney Horvath

    Woven into classrooms across the nation, the illusion is that digital tools enhance learning. Yet, according to Dr. Jared Cooney Horvath, they consistently undermine it. Frequently asked questions

Are these books mostly for technical experts or can non-tech readers understand them?
Most books in this collection are written for a general audience and don't require extensive technical knowledge.
Do all books focus on AI's negative aspects or is there a balanced view?
While some books critically examine AI's implications, others explore its potential benefits and how to responsibly develop and use the technology.
Are there any fiction titles that could complement these nonfiction books for a more immersive understanding of AI?
Yes, some science fiction novels like 'Nexus' by Yuval Noah Harari and 'The Reverse Centaur's Guide to Life After AI' by Cory Doctorow offer thought-provoking, fictionalized explorations of AI's societal effects.
