Books on Dopamine, Addiction, and Modern Mental Health

Explore the science, psychology, and societal impacts of dopamine and addiction through these revealing nonfiction titles.

Who these books are for

  • Mental health professionals
  • Addiction researchers
  • Individuals struggling with addiction
  • Those curious about modern psychological phenomena

Start here

If you only read one book about dopamine and addiction, choose 'Dopamine Nation' – a groundbreaking perspective from a medical expert.

In today's world, the concept of dopamine is ubiquitous, yet its complex interplay with addiction and mental health remains poorly understood. This collection of expertly crafted nonfiction books delves into the science, psychology, and societal impacts of dopamine and addiction, offering valuable insights for both experts and the general public. From renowned authors like Gabor Maté and Michael Pollan, to thought-provoking investigative journalism, these titles provide a multifaceted exploration of these crucial modern issues.

The collection

  1. The Myth of Normal Trauma, Illness, and Healing in a Toxic Culture

    Gabor Maté MD

    Gabor Maté's groundbreaking examination of the link between societal pressures and personal health challenges readers to rethink conventional wisdom and offers a holistic approach to wellness.

    For: This thought-provoking book is for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of the interplay between modern life, trauma, and chronic illness.

    Strengths: Maté's extensive clinical experience, engaging writing style, and comprehensive research make this a compelling and enlightening read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, academic content overwhelming or slow-paced, requiring patience and persistence.

    Comparable: While not identical, this book shares similarities with other works that challenge prevailing health paradigms, such as 'The Body Keeps the Score' by Bessel van der Kolk.

    “s many years of deep and painfully accumulated wisdom.”

  2. Famesick A Memoir

    Lena Dunham

    Lena Dunham's confessional, introspective memoir provides a candid look at the highs and lows of fame, creativity, and personal struggle, making it a compelling addition to any collection.

    For: Fans of Dunham's work, readers interested in celebrity memoirs, and those exploring themes of mental health, ambition, and identity will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: Famesick showcases Dunham's raw, engaging writing style, offering relatable insights into the pressures of fame and the complexities of her creative process.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the book's introspective, stream-of-consciousness approach challenging or meandering at times.

    Comparable: For those drawn to celebrity memoirs, works like Carrie Fisher's Wishful Drinking or Amy Schumer's The Girl with the Lower Back Tattoo offer similarly candid, humorous takes on fame and personal life.

  3. Famesick A Memoir

    Lena Dunham

    Lena Dunham's deeply personal and unflinching memoir offers a unique perspective on fame, ambition, and the struggles of maintaining one's identity amidst intense public scrutiny.

    For: Fans of candid celebrity memoirs, anyone navigating the challenges of career and personal growth, and readers who appreciate lyrical, introspective writing.

    Strengths: Dunham's storytelling is both relatable and revelatory, weaving together her experiences with illness, relationships, and professional pressures into a cohesive, engaging narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the book's introspective nature and Dunham's self-focused perspective occasionally overwhelming or hard to connect with.

    Comparable: Fans of other celebrity memoirs like Carrie Fisher's Wishful Drinking or Amy Schumer's The Girl with the Lower Back Tattoo may appreciate Dunham's similarly irreverent and in-depth exploration of her life and career.

  4. BoyMom Reimagining Boyhood in the Age of Impossible Masculinity

    Ruth Whippman

    In this deeply personal and thought-provoking memoir, Ruth Whippman, a mother of three boys, delves into the complexities of raising sons in today's fraught cultural landscape. With humor, empathy, and a gonzo feminist spirit, she explores the contradictory pressures boys face, the harmful effects of toxic masculinity, and the challenges of instilling healthy values in a generation often isolated, emotionally repressed, and adrift. Whippman's quest for a more expansive and fulfilling story of boyhood leads her to surprising insights and a refreshing perspective on parenting in our times. A must-read for anyone grappling with the challenges of raising boys and shaping a better future for young men.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Scattered Minds The Origins and Healing of Attention Deficit Disorder

    Gabor Maté MD

    Experience a transformative understanding of Attention Deficit Disorder with this groundbreaking work by Dr. Gabor Maté. Moving beyond the myth of genetics, Scattered Minds reveals how environmental stress shapes the condition, providing hope and pragmatic advice for parents and individuals seeking liberation from ADD's grip. Join Dr. Maté on a journey from pain to healing, as he shares his own experience and expert insights to empower you to promote healthy development, even in adulthood.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Addiction by Design Machine Gambling in Las Vegas

    Natasha Dow Schüll

    Immerse yourself in the fascinating, sometimes disturbing world of modern gambling with Addiction by Design. Anthropologist Natasha Dow Schüll's 15 years of in-depth Vegas research expose how sleek slot machines, video poker and more have transformed casinos into zones designed to keep patrons hypnotically engaged in a quest for the next spin, win or loss. Schüll skillfully reveals the strategic psychological and architectural tactics employed to maximize "time on device" and the personal, social and economic costs that result. A gripping exploration of human-machine entwinement and our society's compulsion with chance and technology.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. How to Change Your Mind What the New Science of Psychedelics Teaches Us About Consciousness, Dying, Addiction, Depression, and Transcendence

    Michael Pollan

    Join acclaimed author Michael Pollan on a fascinating journey as he delves into the revolutionary world of psychedelics, exploring their profound effects on the human mind and psyche. From depression and addiction to the pursuit of transcendence, Pollan's vivid account reveals the latest scientific discoveries and his own life-altering experiences with LSD and psilocybin. This insightful, engaging blend of memoir, history, and medicine is a must-read for anyone curious about the frontiers of consciousness and the quest for a more meaningful existence.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Burnout Society

    Byung-Chul Han

    In this incisive critique, Byung-Chul Han exposes how our society's relentless pursuit of efficiency, convenience, and positivity is eroding our capacity for contemplation, vulnerability, and resilience. Through a rich cultural and scientific lens, Han reveals how widespread practices of multitasking, stress, and exhaustion are symptoms of a deeper malaise – a burnout culture that imperils individual well-being and social cohesion. A provocative and timely work, The Burnout Society issues a urgent call to fundamentally rethink the values underpinning our hyper-connected world and reclaim space for introspection, depth, and authentic human experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Permission to Feel The Power of Emotional Intelligence to Achieve Well-Being and Success

    Marc Brackett Ph.D.

    In "Permission to Feel", renowned emotion scientist Marc Brackett offers a proven, high-impact approach to emotional intelligence that can transform the lives of children, parents, teachers, and schools. By understanding and mastering emotions wisely, Brackett shows how we can reduce stress, burnout, and bullying while improving well-being and academic achievement. Backed by decades of rigorous research, his RULER system is a beacon of hope for overcoming mental health crises and cultivating resilience in a chaotic world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Live Laugh Love The Secret History of White Christian Women and the World They Made

    Kristin Kobes Du Mez

    In "Live Laugh Love", historian Kristin Kobes Du Mez uncovers the fascinating history behind the Instagram-perfect culture of white Christian femininity, tracing its roots from the late 19th century to today's consumer brands. By revealing the overlap of disparate faith traditions and the rise of prosperity theologies, Du Mez exposes how positive thinking became a potent force, shaping not only spirituality but also politics and culture. This captivating cultural history illuminates how women created and internalized a powerful yet elusive feminine ideal, influencing countless lives along the way.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Tattoos on the Heart The Power of Boundless Compassion

    Gregory Boyle

    In Tattoos on the Heart, Father Gregory Boyle shares his profound wisdom gleaned from years working with gang members in Los Angeles. Through parables of love, redemption, and resilience, Boyle illuminates the power of compassion and encourages readers to meet the world with an open, loving heart. This inspiring book will awaken your faith and deepen your understanding of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Dopamine Nation Finding Balance in the Age of Indulgence

    Anna Lembke MD

    Dopamine Nation offers a compelling blend of scientific explanation and personal stories to help readers understand and manage our increasingly addictive relationship with pleasure and technology.

    For: Anyone seeking to better understand the psychological and neurological drivers of compulsive overconsumption, as well as those looking for strategies to achieve a healthier balance between pleasure and fulfillment.

    Strengths: The book's strengths lie in its clear, accessible explanation of complex neuroscience concepts and its gripping narrative woven from the author's patients' experiences, making the subject matter engaging and relatable.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense scientific material challenging to digest, requiring patience and a willingness to learn about the brain chemistry behind addiction.

    Comparable: Those interested in this topic may also enjoy books like Dopesick by Beth Macy, which explores the opioid epidemic from a similar interdisciplinary perspective.

    “Fascinating case histories, and a sensible formula for treatment.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts Close Encounters with Addiction

    Gabor Maté MD

    In the Realm of Hungry Ghosts offers a compassionate, scientifically-grounded perspective on addiction as a response to unmet emotional needs, challenging common stigmatizing views.

    For: This book is essential for anyone grappling with addiction, mental health professionals, and those seeking a deeper understanding of the complex factors driving substance use.

    Strengths: Maté's thought-provoking analysis, case studies, and synthesis of neuropsychiatric research provide a comprehensive, nuanced exploration of addiction's root causes and effective treatment approaches.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, academic tone and philosophical musings overwhelming, requiring patience and a willingness to engage with complex ideas.

    Comparable: For those interested in trauma-informed care and alternative approaches to addiction, other notable titles include Bruce Perry's The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog and Peter Levine's Waking the Tiger.

    “A riveting account of human cravings, this book needs to get into as many hands as possible. Maté”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Dopamine Kids A Science-Based Plan to Rewire Your Child's Brain and Take Back Your Family in the Age of Screens and Ultraprocessed Foods

    Michaeleen Doucleff

    In Dopamine Kids, journalist and mother Michaeleen Doucleff offers a groundbreaking 5-step plan to break the cycle of screen and processed food addiction in children, empowering parents to reclaim their families. By embracing a science-backed approach that shifts focus from depriving pleasure to cultivating joy, Dopamine Kids provides concrete strategies for establishing healthy habits, outfitting homes as screen-free sanctuaries, and guiding kids towards activities that truly fulfill their needs. A must-read for modern families seeking to raise confident, happy kids in an age of pervasive technology and unhealthy convenience foods.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Dog Days

    Emily Labarge

    In this electrifying memoir, Emily Labarge probes the indelible marks of trauma on the self and the limits of language in conveying its aftermath. Drawing upon the harrowing experience of being held hostage and the subsequent therapy that seeks to unearth repressed pain, Labarge craftily weaves film theory, literary criticism, and psychoanalytic thought to excavate how institutions and narratives shape our understanding of violence and care. Through her searing, lyrical prose, Labarge extrapolates a powerful practice of survival and opens up new possibilities for storytelling - a must-read for anyone interested in the complex intersections of art, psychology, and the human condition.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
