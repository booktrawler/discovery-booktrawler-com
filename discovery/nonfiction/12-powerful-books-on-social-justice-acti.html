12 Powerful Books on Social Justice, Activism and Community Change

Explore these 12 books for inspiring stories, practical advice, and thought-provoking ideas to fuel your social justice journey.

Who these books are for

  • Social justice advocates
  • Community organizers
  • Activists seeking inspiration
  • Philanthropists and nonprofit professionals
  • Students of sociology, anthropology, and related fields

Start here

If you only read one book, choose 'Consumed' by Aja Barber for a compelling exploration of how capitalist consumption shapes our lives and society.

These 12 books represent a diverse array of perspectives and approaches to social justice, from the personal to the structural, the local to the global. Whether you're an experienced activist or just starting to explore these ideas, there's something here to challenge, inspire, and equip you for making a positive impact in your community and the world at large.

The collection

  1. Big Hunger The Unholy Alliance between Corporate America and Anti-Hunger Groups

    Andrew Fisher

    Big Hunger exposes the stark reality behind America's anti-hunger efforts: a self-perpetuating "hunger industrial complex" driven by food charity and corporate donations, rather than addressing the root causes of income inequality and economic injustice. Andrew Fisher's incisive critique points the way towards a more visionary and effective path forward, centering public health, economic rights, and grassroots democracy to finally end hunger, not just manage it.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Crisis of the Common Good The Fight for Meaning and Connection in a Broken America

    Chris Murphy

    This book offers a thought-provoking analysis of the underlying societal issues driving political polarization and the erosion of American values.

    For: Progressives, political science enthusiasts, and anyone seeking a deeper understanding of the country's current malaise.

    Strengths: Senator Murphy draws upon his political experience and knowledge of history and philosophy to provide a nuanced, well-reasoned critique of the 'cults' that have hijacked the American spirit.

    Skip if: This is a dense, intellectually demanding work that assumes a certain level of familiarity with political theory and may challenge readers without a strong background in these subjects.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed books like Thomas Piketty's Capital in the Twenty-First Century or David R. Farris's The United States of Paranoia will appreciate Murphy's similar blend of economics, politics, and social commentary.

    “A deeper rot festers in the American soul.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Economix How Our Economy Works (and Doesn't Work), in Words and Pictures

    Michael Goodwin

    Experience the fascinating world of economics in a fresh, accessible way with Economix. Through witty writing and charming illustrations, this New York Times bestselling graphic novel demystifies complex economic theories and concepts, putting them into a relatable historical context. Whether you're a curious citizen or an economic enthusiast, Economix sheds light on the triumphs, failures, and future prospects of the global economy, making it an essential read for anyone seeking a deeper understanding of our financial landscape.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Revolution Will Not Be Funded Beyond the Non-Profit Industrial Complex

    Incite! Women of Color Against Incite!

    Join the incisive critique of the non-profit industrial complex in this bold collection, where radical thinkers expose the ways in which tax-exempt organizations, from art museums to advocacy groups, can undermine political change. Drawing from their own activist experiences, contributors offer strategies for transforming the system from within and working outside the non-profit model to forge a more just society. A compelling call to rethink the role of philanthropy in social justice movements.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. American Bulk Essays on Excess

    Emily Mester

    In this incisive and deeply personal collection, Emily Mester adeptly reframes the everyday rituals of consumption as a window into the human experience, peeling back the layers of our relationships with stuff and identity. With humor, vulnerability, and piercing insight, she navigates the contradictions and ironies of modern life, from the thrill of Costco hauls to the relics of family dysfunction in an Iowa hoard. A striking debut that offers a fresh lens on our obsession with abundance and the search for meaning in a consumer culture.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Tiny Gardens Everywhere The Past, Present, and Future of the Self-Provisioning City

    Kate Brown

    "Discover the hidden history of urban gardening and the dynamic, resilient people behind it in this captivating book. From London's makeshift allotments to post-Soviet Estonia's community gardens, learn how everyday citizens have consistently played a vital role in feeding themselves and building thriving communities. With inspiring contemporary examples, Tiny Gardens Everywhere reveals the transformative power of gardening in the face of economic hardship, political resistance, and environmental challenges - a manifesto for the next food revolution."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Reimagining Nonprofits and Philanthropy Unlocking the Full Potential of a Vital and Complex Sector

    Vu Le

    Reimagining Nonprofits and Philanthropy dares to challenge conventional wisdom, offering fresh perspectives and concrete solutions for the nonprofit sector's most pressing challenges. Vu Le's thought-provoking guide encourages readers to rethink everything from fundraising strategies to governance models, inspiring leaders to break free from outdated norms and unlock their organizations' full potential. Essential reading for anyone invested in driving meaningful change.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Reclaiming Your Community You Don’t Have to Move out of Your Neighborhood to Live in a Better One

    Majora Carter

    Join visionary urbanist Majora Carter on a powerful journey to reclaim and revitalize low-status communities. Arguing that talent retention is key to economic resilience, Carter provides a groundbreaking strategy to stem brain drain and gentrification. With deeply personal insights and a wealth of practical solutions, this essential read redefines poverty, development, and the true meaning of success.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Decolonizing Wealth, Second Edition Indigenous Wisdom to Heal Divides and Restore Balance

    Edgar Villanueva

    In this timely and provocative critique, Edgar Villanueva reveals how philanthropy and finance, despite their altruistic facades, mirror and perpetuate colonial dynamics, doing more harm than good. Drawing from Native wisdom, he diagnoses the fatal flaws in giving and offers radical solutions for true mutual liberation and balance. Decolonizing Wealth is a must-read for funders, activists, and anyone seeking a more just society.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Consumed The Need for Collective Change: Colonialism, Climate Change, and Consumerism

    Aja Barber

    Aja Barber's groundbreaking debut offers a nuanced exploration of the dark side of consumerism, delving into the historical roots and modern impacts of the fashion industry on labor, the environment, and social justice.

    For: Consumed is essential reading for anyone interested in sustainability, fashion, and social activism, as well as those seeking a deeper understanding of their own consumption habits and their role in shaping the world around them.

    Strengths: Barber's unflinching honesty, vivid storytelling, and accessible analysis make complex issues relatable and actionable, while her personal experiences lend an intimate, humanizing touch to the narrative.

    Skip if: This is a dense, academic work that may overwhelm some readers with its extensive research and detailed statistics.

    Comparable: For readers drawn to thought-provoking explorations of consumer culture, similar titles might include Naomi Klein's 'No Logo' or 'The Unfinished Revolution' by Naomi Wolf, though Consumed focuses specifically on the fashion industry and its global consequences.

    “Consumed takes us through the hideously complex topic of fashion and sustainability, from its knotty colonial roots to what everyday people can do to uproot those systems, today.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Reinventing Food Banks and Pantries New Tools to End Hunger

    Katie S Martin

    This book presents a thoughtful, data-driven approach to addressing hunger by shifting from a short-term emergency response to a long-term, community-focused strategy.

    For: Food bank and pantry professionals, volunteers, and anyone interested in hunger relief and social services.

    Strengths: Offers practical, actionable steps for improving food bank and pantry operations, backed by research and real-world examples. Provides a compelling case for a paradigm shift in the charitable food system.

    Skip if: Some chapters may require a basic understanding of nonprofit management and social services terminology.

    Comparable: Compared to other books on hunger relief, this stands out for its emphasis on empowerment, client choice, and building community connections.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. How to Protect Bookstores and Why The Present and Future of Bookselling

    Danny Caine

    Discover the crucial role bookstores play in our communities as cultural hubs, community anchors, and founts of new ideas in this impassioned and insightful examination by Danny Caine. Learn why protecting independent bookstores is vital for the health of our neighborhoods and the future of bookselling in this timely, must-read for book lovers and business enthusiasts alike.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Cleanup on Aisle Five Essential Work, Poverty Wages, and the View from Behind the Supermarket Register

    Ann Larson

    A captivating, character-driven exploration of the modern supermarket, revealing its complexities and injustices through the author's personal experience as a cashier.

    For: Readers interested in sociology, labor issues, and the impact of capitalism on everyday life will appreciate this thought-provoking memoir.

    Strengths: Larson's astute observations, vivid storytelling, and well-researched historical context make for a compelling critique of the supermarket industry.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the narrative a bit fragmented, pivoting between the author's personal story and broader industry analysis.

    Comparable: Fans of Barbara Ehrenreich's Nickel and Dimed and Benjamin Lorr's The Secret Life of Groceries will appreciate this insider's look at the often-overlooked world of supermarket labor and management.

    “Persuasive … amiable. This compassionate book will make some shoppers think twice. In a forward-thinking conclusion, Larson offers ideas for addressing ‘economic precarity”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Trash! A Garbageman's Story

    Simon Pare-Poupart

    Trash! offers a gripping, humorous, and thought-provoking memoir that sheds light on the often-overlooked world of waste management, while also reflecting on broader themes of consumerism and society.

    For: Readers with an interest in working-class experiences, sociology, environmentalism, and engaging narrative nonfiction.

    Strengths: The author's vivid storytelling, humor, and unique perspective as a former sociology student turned garbageman make for an entertaining and enlightening read. The book's blend of personal anecdotes and sociological insights provides a compelling exploration of the trash industry and its implications.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the subject matter grim or the writing style occasionally academic.

    Comparable: Fans of Anthony Bourdain's Kitchen Confidential or other behind-the-scenes memoirs about blue-collar work will appreciate Trash!'s similar blend of humor and sociological commentary.

    “Imagine if Anthony Bourdain were your garbage man.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Trash! A Garbageman's Story

    Simon Pare-Poupart

    In this unforgettable memoir, Simon Pare-Poupart sheds light on the gritty, often hilarious world of garbage collection - a profession most of us prefer to ignore. With humor, candor, and insightful sociological analysis, Pare-Poupart shares his 20-year journey as a Montreal garbage man, revealing the heroic labor, camaraderie, and brutal conditions that keep our cities clean. An unlikely tale of a highly educated man who chooses to stay on the trash truck for its physical thrill, rough-and-tumble colleagues, and the freedom he finds in this unglamorous work. A raw, entertaining and enlightening read that will make you rethink your relationship with waste and those who clear your path.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Immediacy Or, The Style of Too Late Capitalism

    Anna Kornbluh

    In Immediacy, Anna Kornbluh dissects the prevailing cultural values of speed, directness, and immersion that dominate our post-capitalist era. She argues that these aesthetics, born of economic disintermediation, sacrifice mediation and representation for ephemeral realness. A wake-up call for those seeking deeper meaning in art and politics, Kornbluh offers insights on streaming TV, literature, and artworld trends before pointing toward alternatives that prioritize distance, impersonality, and intellectual substance. A timely, thought-provoking analysis of contemporary style and its implications.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Our Members Be Unlimited A Comic about Workers and Their Unions

    Sam Wallman

    Embark on a graphic journey through the history of labor unions with Our Members Be Unlimited. Comics journalist Sam Wallman skillfully weaves together the past and present, shedding light on the struggles and triumphs of workers worldwide, from Amazon warehouses to Bangladesh factories to striking taxi drivers. This timely and visually striking work is an essential primer for understanding the power of collective action in the face of capitalist pressures.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What are some must-read books for social justice?
Start with this curated list of 12 powerful books that delve into activism, community change, and the fight for a more just world.
Can these books be enjoyed by a general audience or are they geared towards academics?
While they may resonate with scholars, these books are accessible and engaging for anyone curious about social justice and community change.
Are there any books on this list that offer practical advice for getting involved?
Yes, titles like 'Reimagining Nonprofits and Philanthropy' and 'Reclaiming Your Community' provide valuable insights and strategies for activism and social entrepreneurship.
What makes 'Consumed' stand out from the other books on this list?
Aja Barber's unique blend of personal narrative, cultural critique, and incisive analysis makes 'Consumed' a standout for its ability to connect individual consumer choices to systemic issues of inequality and sustainability.
