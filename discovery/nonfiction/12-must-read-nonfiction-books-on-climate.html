12 Must-Read Nonfiction Books on Climate Change, Sustainability & Activism

Inspire hope and action with these thought-provoking climate-focused books.

Who these books are for

  • Environmental enthusiasts
  • Climate change activists
  • Sustainability professionals
  • Educators seeking climate curriculum
  • Anyone looking to understand and engage with the issue

Start here

If you only read one book, choose 'All We Can Save' for a powerful, hopeful call to climate action.

From inspiring tales of grassroots activism to scientific explanations of the climate crisis, these 12 must-read nonfiction books offer a diverse perspective on the urgent issue of our time. Whether you're an environmentalist, an educator, or simply someone concerned about the planet's future, delving into these thought-provoking volumes will equip you with knowledge, motivation, and a deeper understanding of the complex challenges ahead.

The collection

  1. The Carbon Wave A Story of Democracy, Parenthood, and the Race to Protect Our Planet

    Leah C. Stokes

    Join a remarkable trio of new parents - Leah Stokes, Sonia Aggarwal, and Adrian Deveny - as they navigate the highs and lows of crafting groundbreaking climate legislation while balancing the challenges of parenthood. Stokes masterfully weaves their personal stories with behind-the-scenes moments from Capitol Hill, the White House, and the activist sphere, demonstrating the transformative power of collective action when dedicated individuals persevere against the odds. The Carbon Wave is a riveting, inspiring tale of unlikely heroes who bucked conventional wisdom to create a historic climate victory, offering hope that, even in our turbulent times, our efforts can still shape a sustainable future.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Art-Science Undisciplined A Playbook for Transformative Collaboration

    Janani Balasubramanian

    Embark on a groundbreaking collaboration between art and science with Janani Balasubramanian's Art-Science Undisciplined. This imaginative playbook, co-authored with astrophysicist Natalie Gosnell, invites you to transcend transactional exchanges and instead foster joyous, mutually transformative partnerships. Drawing from the authors' own experiences and those of other art-science innovators, this indispensable guide offers practical and conceptual tools to co-create a more expansive future, reshaping not only the practitioners but their worlds.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. What If We Get It Right? Visions of Climate Futures

    Ayana Elizabeth Johnson

    This book offers a unique blend of science, policy, culture, and justice to inspire a hopeful vision of our climate future.

    For: Readers interested in climate solutions, sustainability, and environmental leadership.

    Strengths: Provocative essays, vibrant conversations, and a diverse range of perspectives from visionary experts make for a compelling and informative read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense, academic style and occasional use of technical jargon challenging.

    Comparable: Fans of All We Can Save: Thoughts on the Future by Ayana Elizabeth Johnson and Katharine K. Wilkinson will appreciate this follow-up exploration of climate possibilities and solutions.

    “Rigorous, elucidating, and hopeful in the most catalytic sense, this volume will stiffen your political will and open your mind and heart. It could arrive at no better moment.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. All We Can Save Truth, Courage, and Solutions for the Climate Crisis

    Katharine K. Wilkinson

    This book stands out for its diverse perspectives and solutions-oriented approach to the climate crisis, offering a much-needed counterbalance to the often pessimistic tone of climate literature.

    For: Anyone concerned about climate change, particularly women and those seeking hope and inspiration from leaders in the field.

    Strengths: Provides a wide range of insightful essays from women at the forefront of climate action, showcases creative and justice-oriented solutions, and conveys a sense of hope and resilience in the face of the crisis.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the collection's emphasis on diverse voices and perspectives leads to a slightly disjointed flow between essays.

    Comparable: Other books that explore climate change through a feminist lens and offer practical solutions, such as 'This Changes Everything' by Naomi Klein, will resonate with readers of 'All We Can Save'.

    “A feast of ideas and perspectives, setting a big table for climate movement, declaring all are welcome.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Other Side of Change Who We Become When Life Makes Other Plans

    Maya Shankar

    In this deeply personal and scientifically-grounded exploration, acclaimed cognitive scientist Maya Shankar reveals how life's unexpected twists can catalyze profound personal growth and transformation. Through riveting stories of resilience and self-discovery, Shankar debunks the myth of change as solely negative and instead encourages us to see it as an opportunity to reimagine ourselves and unlock our full potential. A timely and thought-provoking read for anyone navigating life's big disruptors.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Leave the Lights On How Joyful Decisions Can Save Our Species

    Elizabeth Dunn

    This book offers a unique, science-backed approach to climate change mitigation by showing how joyful choices can have a positive impact, making it a valuable addition for anyone seeking practical yet uplifting advice.

    For: Environmentalists, psychology enthusiasts, and anyone looking to make sustainable lifestyle changes while maintaining happiness and well-being.

    Strengths: Grounded in rigorous research, provides actionable tips for low-carbon living that bring joy, challenges conventional wisdom on sacrifice, and offers a hopeful, empowering message.

    Skip if: May appeal more to readers interested in behavioral science and psychology than those seeking a purely environmental or self-help book.

    Comparable: Readers of 'Happy Money' by Elizabeth Dunn and Michael Norton, or 'The Happiness Lab' by Laurie Santos, will appreciate this book's intersection of personal happiness and planetary well-being.

    “This book makes it fun to fight climate change. A pair of experts in psychology and sustainability reveal how to protect the environment without making yourself miserable.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. We Love You An Optimistic Guide to Life on a Rock Floating Through Space

    Thomas Sullivan

    Embark on a thought-provoking journey with lifelong friends Andy Min and Thomas Sullivan as they explore humanity's relationship with nature, self, and the vast universe we inhabit. Through a mix of poetic prose, narrative, and stunning visuals, We Love You inspires readers to reexamine life's big questions and find meaning, joy, and hope in the everyday world we often take for granted. With its optimistic spirit and heartfelt wisdom, this National Bestseller is a must-read for anyone seeking a deeper connection to the world and themselves.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Climate Wayfinding Healing Ourselves and the Planet We Call Home

    Katharine K Wilkinson

    Climate Wayfinding offers a holistic, compassionate approach to climate action, providing a clear process for personal growth and collective change.

    For: Those seeking a transformative guide for their own climate journey, from beginners to experienced activists.

    Strengths: Wilkinson's unique blend of personal stories, collective wisdom, and practical exercises creates a deeply engaging, empowering read.

    Skip if: The book's introspective, lyrical style may not appeal to readers seeking a purely scientific or policy-focused climate resource.

    Comparable: Like All We Can Save, this book inspires and equips individuals to take meaningful climate action, but with a stronger emphasis on personal growth and wayfinding.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Hospicing Modernity Facing Humanity's Wrongs and the Implications for Social Activism

    Vanessa Machado de Oliveira

    In this unflinching, deeply philosophical book, Vanessa Machado de Oliveira confronts the global crises we've created through our modern, exploitative ways, from pandemics to climate change. Rather than offering tidy solutions, she challenges readers to mature and take responsibility, letting go of dissonant beliefs and habits that harm the Earth and ourselves. Through thought-provoking exercises and reflections, Machado de Oliveira encourages a radical shift in perspective, asking us to exist differently and co-create a more just, livable world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The People's Project Poems, Essays, and Art for Looking Forward

    Saeed Jones

    In this powerful anthology, Saeed Jones and Maggie Smith bring together twenty-seven visionary writers and artists offering hope, resilience, and strategies for moving forward in uncertain times. With poems, essays, and visual art, this "community in book form" shares diverse perspectives on what we can hold onto and work towards, from crip time to nature's strength, trans guidance to know-your-place defiance. A must-read for anyone seeking inspiration and fortitude in the face of adversity.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. A Few Rules for Predicting the Future An Essay

    Octavia E Butler

    In this poignant yet hopeful essay, science fiction icon Octavia E. Butler shares her wisdom on predicting and shaping the future. Reflecting on her influential novels and lessons from history, Butler presents four key rules for navigating uncertainty and inspiring positive change. A must-read for fans of her timeless works and anyone seeking inspiration for a brighter tomorrow, this beautifully designed book features stunning Afrofuturist artwork and offers a timely reminder that our individual choices and perspectives matter in creating the world we want to live in.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Emergent Strategy Shaping Change, Changing Worlds

    Adrienne Maree Brown

    Emergent Strategy offers a visionary, science-infused approach to navigating and shaping change in our tumultuous world, drawing on the inspiring ideas of Octavia Butler.

    For: Activists, organizers, and anyone seeking to adapt, thrive, and influence the future in the face of uncertainty and upheaval.

    Strengths: Brown's work excels at weaving together cutting-edge science, speculative fiction, and practical organizing strategies into an empowering framework for change.

    Skip if: The dense, layered concepts may require multiple readings and a background in Butler's Earthseed series to fully appreciate.

    Comparable: For those drawn to Butler's work, this book offers a companion piece that operationalizes her philosophical ideas into actionable strategies for transformation.

    “It was a revelation to discover adrienne maree brown”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Loving Corrections

    Adrienne Maree Brown

    Adrienne Maree Brown's visionary Loving Corrections offers a groundbreaking practice to mend fractures in our society and movements, cultivating collective power, true belonging, and ethical communication. Through "loving corrections," Brown reframes everyday interactions as rehearsals for revolution, providing practical wisdom and bold visions for a shared, liberated future. A must-read for anyone navigating the political crossroads, this indispensable book is an invitation to take a new path forward together.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. If Women Rose Rooted A Life-Changing Journey to Authenticity and Belonging

    Sharon Blackie

    Join a transformative journey to rediscover your authentic self and place in the world through Sharon Blackie's inspiring If Women Rose Rooted. Drawn from ancient mythology and modern role models, this beautifully written guide will nourish your spirit and empower you to claim your rightful role as a guardian of the land. An anthem for embracing a rewilded, resilient femininity, this essential read will resonate with anyone seeking connection, belonging, and the power to shape a better future.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. How to Fall in Love with the Future A Time Traveller's Guide to Changing the World

    Rob Hopkins

    In "How to Fall in Love with the Future", Rob Hopkins invites readers on a transformative journey through time to imagined futures they'll actually want to inhabit. By exploring historical visions of utopia and harnessing the power of imaginative "time travel" exercises, Hopkins provides a radical new approach to overcoming climate anxiety and inspiring positive change. This inspiring blueprint for a better world will galvanize readers to dream bigger and work towards making those dreams a reality.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. We Will Be Shelter

    Andrea Gibson

    In "We Will Be Shelter", Andrea Gibson masterfully uses the power of poetry to awaken empathy and inspire activism. This compelling anthology sheds light on urgent social justice issues, calling readers to contemplation and urging them to take transformative action. With eloquent verses that soar with evocative beauty, Gibson invites you to join the movement for a more just and equitable world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. No Straight Road Takes You There Essays for Uneven Terrain

    Rebecca Solnit

    In this visionary essay collection, award-winning author Rebecca Solnit explores the power of unexpected journeys, unforeseen consequences, and the stories we tell ourselves about the world and our place in it. From the wisdom of a centuries-old violin to the dreams of a prisoner, Solnit weaves together politics, literature, and personal narratives to offer a nuanced view of climate change, feminism, and the liberatory possibilities of embracing uncertainty and imperfection. With her signature lyrical prose and incisive insights, Solnit inspires us to heed the past, navigate the complexities of our present, and shape the future through our actions and the stories we choose to tell.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. When No Thing Works A Zen and Indigenous Perspective on Resilience, Shared Purpose, and Leadership in the Timeplace of Collapse

    Norma Wong

    In When No Thing Works, Zen Rōshi Norma Wong marries Zen philosophy with Indigenous wisdom to offer a refreshingly hopeful guide for navigating the chaos of our times. This poetic yet practical book invites you to transform from individual "I" to collective "we," finding resonance and resilience in shared purpose and leadership. A timely companion for those seeking a different way forward amidst accelerating collapse, Wong's words guide you to embrace the unknown, opening doorways to a new rhythm of being.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. Who We Are Becoming Matters The Courage, Wisdom, and Aloha We Need in a Timeplace of Collapse

    Norma Kaweloku Wong

    In a world shaped by climate crisis and social rupture, acclaimed author and Zen teacher Norma Kaweloku Wong offers a transformative guide to help us adapt and thrive. Who We Are Becoming Matters weaves together Zen wisdom, Indigenous Hawaiian insights, and political strategy to illuminate the essential human qualities—courage, compassion, aloha, and wisdom—that must be cultivated now to usher in a new era of collective responsibility and mutual stewardship. This powerful, deeply personal book challenges us to let go of our divisive tendencies and, instead, embrace the messy work of coming together to tend to the crises at hand. Part visionary framework, part story-poem-instruction manual, it's a vital roadmap for those seeking to evolve, grow, and lead in a time of unprecedented change.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
