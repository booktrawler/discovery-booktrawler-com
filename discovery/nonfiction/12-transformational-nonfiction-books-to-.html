12 Transformational Nonfiction Books to Read

Discover transformative insights on human nature, science, and society from acclaimed authors.

Who these books are for

  • Curious readers seeking new perspectives
  • Environmentalists and sustainability advocates
  • Scientists, nature lovers, and explorers
  • Educators, students, and lifelong learners
  • Anyone interested in human nature, science, and social change

Start here

If you only read one book about the human condition, choose 'Lab Girl' by Hope Jahren – a mesmerizing memoir of a scientist's journey.

In an era of unprecedented global challenges, these 12 transformative nonfiction books offer powerful insights into the natural world, human nature, and the societies we've built. From acclaimed authors across multiple disciplines, this diverse collection will inspire you to think differently about the world and our place in it. Whether you're a scientist, nature enthusiast, educator, or simply a curious reader, there's something here for everyone – books to spark new perspectives, ignite passions, and foster a deeper understanding of the complex issues we face today.

The collection

  1. Human Nature Nine Ways to Feel About Our Changing Planet

    Kate Marvel

    A unique blend of climate science, personal storytelling, and emotional exploration makes this book a standout and essential read for understanding the human side of environmental change.

    For: Anyone seeking a nuanced, empathetic perspective on climate change, from science enthusiasts to general readers, will find this accessible and engaging.

    Strengths: Marvel's masterful use of emotion to frame the science, combined with her engaging writing style and diverse references, creates a captivating narrative that educates and moves readers.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the chapter-by-chapter focus on a single emotion a bit disjointed, and the scientific explanations, while clear, occasionally slow the narrative pace.

    Comparable: Fans of science writing with a personal touch, like Hope Jahren's Lab Girl or Carl Zimmer's The Tangled Bank, will appreciate Marvel's approach.

    “A highly readable argument for tackling climate change that reckons with both cold hard facts and the human heart.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Invention of Nature Alexander von Humboldt's New World

    Andrea Wulf

    Meet Alexander von Humboldt, the visionary 19th-century naturalist whose groundbreaking ideas about interconnectedness in nature laid the foundation for modern environmentalism. In this captivating biography, Andrea Wulf brings to life Humboldt's daring expeditions, influential relationships, and lasting impact on luminaries like Darwin, Emerson, and Thoreau. A fascinating portrait of a man decades ahead of his time, The Invention of Nature upends our understanding of the natural world and the urgent need to protect it.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Story of More How We Got to Climate Change and Where to Go from Here

    Hope Jahren

    Hope Jahren's accessible, engaging writing style makes complex climate science comprehensible for a broad audience, while her personal insights and storytelling make the topic relatable and memorable.

    For: Anyone curious about climate change and its human impacts, seeking an informative yet engaging read.

    Strengths: Concise, highly readable chapters that explain the science behind key inventions and their environmental consequences; Jahren's personal narrative adds emotional resonance and motivation to understand and address the issue.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the scientific explanations and historical context dense and require close attention.

    Comparable: Similar in its accessible approach to other popular science titles like Elizabeth Kolbert's The Sixth Extinction, but with a stronger focus on human habits and their direct impact on the environment.

    “Hope Jahren asks the central question of our time: how can we learn to live on a finite planet? The Story of More is thoughtful, informative, and—above all—essential.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Life After Cars Freeing Ourselves from the Tyranny of the Automobile

    Sarah Goodyear

    In Life After Cars, Sarah Goodyear and The War on Cars co-hosts Doug Gordon and Aaron Naparstek expose the staggering, far-reaching harm cars inflict on communities, the environment, and our quality of life. Through vivid storytelling, data-driven insights, and expert interviews, this National Bestseller reveals the entrenched interests driving the unsustainable status quo and arms readers with the knowledge to demand a radically different, equitable future – one where cars no longer dominate our world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Silent Spring

    Rachel Carson

    In this groundbreaking 1962 classic, Rachel Carson's Silent Spring chronicles the devastating impact of pesticides like DDT on the natural world. Carson's passionate defense of the environment sparked a global movement, ultimately leading to the ban of DDT and landmark environmental legislation. With its cutting-edge science and eloquent prose, this seminal work continues to resonate, inspiring wonder and humility in readers as it underscores the delicate interconnectedness of all life on Earth. An essential read for anyone concerned about the future of our planet.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. I Wish My Teacher Knew How One Question Can Change Everything for Our Kids

    Kyle Schwartz

    In this powerful and touching book, teacher Kyle Schwartz shares the eye-opening responses she received from her students when asked "I wish my teacher knew _____." From humorous confessions to heartbreaking realities, these poignant answers reveal the unique challenges and joys each student faces. A must-read for educators, parents and anyone committed to creating a supportive and inclusive learning environment, Schwartz's inspiring story showcases the transformative power of empathy and understanding.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. The Elements of Marie Curie How the Glow of Radium Lit a Path for Women in Science

    Dava Sobel

    Join Pulitzer-finalist author Dava Sobel on a luminous journey to uncover the remarkable life of Marie Curie, lauded as the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, and the untold story of the young women she mentored and inspired to pursue careers in science. This masterful biography shines a light on Curie's groundbreaking research and her tireless advocacy for women in STEM, revealing a complex figure whose brilliance and passion continue to illuminate our understanding of the world today.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Longitude The True Story of a Lone Genius Who Solved the Greatest Scientific Problem of His Time

    Dava Sobel

    Join Dava Sobel on a captivating journey through the drama, heroism, and chicanery of John Harrison's 40-year quest to solve the longitude problem, a scientific conundrum that bedeviled explorers for centuries. With vivid storytelling and gripping detail, Longitude brings to life the epic tale of one man's revolutionary clockwork solution and its profound impact on the course of history. This enthralling blend of science, technology, and human obsession is a riveting read for anyone fascinated by the pioneers who shaped our understanding of the world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. On the Calculation of Volume (Book IV)

    Solvej Balle

    In Solvej Balle's enthralling Book IV of her seven-part novel, a cast of lost souls adrift in a prolonged November 18th converge on a Bremen mansion, forging an uncertain new civilization. As their makeshift community takes shape, linguistic and philosophical tensions arise - can a shared language be the key to a common purpose? This mesmerizing installment culminates in a shocking cliffhanger that propels the reader forward into the mysteries of Balle's hypnotic, time-bending epic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Lab Girl A Memoir

    Hope Jahren

    Lab Girl is a moving memoir that celebrates the passion, curiosity, and dedication of a scientist, offering a unique perspective on the natural world.

    For: Scientists, nature enthusiasts, and anyone interested in the intersection of personal story and scientific inquiry.

    Strengths: Engaging storytelling, vivid descriptions of plant life, and a compelling portrait of the author's journey as a woman in science.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the scientific details dense; however, Jahren's writing makes the complex accessible and fascinating.

    Comparable: Compared to other memoirs of scientists, Lab Girl is notable for its lyrical prose and the central role of the author's personal relationships in shaping her scientific pursuits.

    “Lab Girl by Hope Jahren is a beautifully written memoir about the life of a woman in science, a brilliant friendship, and the profundity of trees. Terrific.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Replaceable You Adventures in Human Anatomy

    Mary Roach

    In this unforgettable journey, acclaimed science writer Mary Roach delves into the fascinating world of human body replacement, from historic prosthetics crafted from unlikely materials to today's cutting-edge efforts to 3D print organs and regenerate tissue. With her trademark wit and infectious curiosity, Roach takes readers into the operating rooms, labs, and recovery rooms where advances in prosthetics, xenotransplantation, and regenerative medicine are changing lives. A compulsively readable exploration of what it means to be human, Replaceable You is a must-read for anyone who's ever pondered the incredible, flawed, and wonderful machine that is the body.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Problem with Plastic How We Can Save Ourselves and Our Planet Before It's Too Late

    Judith Enck

    This book provides a comprehensive, science-based overview of the plastic pollution crisis and its devastating impacts on the environment and human health, along with concrete solutions and a call to action.

    For: Anyone concerned about the plastic pollution crisis, interested in environmental issues, and looking for practical ways to make a difference.

    Strengths: The Problem with Plastic offers a well-researched, accessible examination of the plastics industry and its effects, balancing alarming facts with inspiring stories of activists and achievable actions for change.

    Skip if: The book's thoroughness can make it dense at times, requiring focused attention from readers.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy environmental exposés like Plastic Ocean or The Uninhabitable Earth will appreciate this book's urgent, data-driven exploration of a critical contemporary issue.

    “An exposé of the plastics industry warns of the damages done to human beings and the planet.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. UnPlug A Radiologist Explores the Damage Caused by Electropollution and How You Can Prevent It

    Rob Brown MD

    In UnPlug, physician Rob Brown MD shines a light on the alarming yet often overlooked health risks of living in a hyperconnected wireless world. Drawing parallels to the environmental pollution of the past, Dr. Brown exposes the detrimental impact of electromagnetic fields and microwave radiation on metabolism, immunity, and even mental health. With compelling real-life stories and accessible science, this vital book empowers readers to understand the invisible threat of "electropollution" and take concrete steps to reduce exposure and safeguard their well-being. A must-read for anyone concerned about the consequences of our technology-dependent lifestyles.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. The Loss of the Senses

    Ivan Illich

    Marrying philosophy, politics, and spirituality, Ivan Illich's The Loss of the Senses is a provocative collection of 18 essays that span topics from the rise of the service economy to the historical roots of our technological society. With his characteristic insight, Illich argues that our immersion in a techno-scientific world is disabling our native capacities for learning, healing, and living fully, obscuring the realm of felt experience in the process. This thought-provoking work is essential for anyone seeking to understand the profound impacts of our modern environment and to rediscover the power of our senses in a rapidly changing world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Hyperpolitics Extreme Politicization without Political Consequences

    Anton Jäger

    Dive into the contradictions of our hyperpoliticized world with Anton Jäger's Hyperpolitics. This incisive analysis explores how extreme politicization has become ubiquitous yet seemingly powerless to effect real change. From the collapse of traditional political infrastructure to the dominant role of viral outrage, endless culture wars, and fleeting digital causes, Jäger provides a nuanced understanding of our charged yet inconsequential era. A must-read for anyone seeking to grasp the complexities of our hyperpolitical moment.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. Maintenance of Everything Part One

    Stewart Brand

    Embrace the radical act of responsibility with Stewart Brand's groundbreaking exploration, Maintenance of Everything. From the high-stakes solo sailboat race to the battle against corrosion in manufacturing, Brand uncovers the profound importance of maintenance in our daily lives and on a global scale. This thought-provoking book invites you to expand your understanding of maintenance as not just preventative tasks, but as a grand process for keeping things - and life itself - going. With insights into vehicle maintenance, military sustainment, and the art and science of keeping things running smoothly, Maintenance of Everything is a paradigm-shifting read for anyone seeking to make a meaningful impact, from the tiny to the cosmic.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. Biological War A Scenario

    Annie Jacobsen

    In her gripping new book Biological War, acclaimed author Annie Jacobsen reveals a chilling future where a lab accident, bio-attack, or pandemic unleashes global destruction. Based on expert interviews, Jacobsen crafts a gripping, plausible narrative that charts the devastating hours, days, and weeks as society collapses into mass death, anarchy, and a dystopian wasteland. A vital roadmap of the potential consequences of biological warfare, Jacobsen's essential book prepares readers for the unthinkable and serves as a crucial call to action for our time.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  18. Light and Thread

    Han Kang

    In Light and Thread, Nobel laureate Han Kang weaves together essays, poems, and reflections to illuminate the intricacies of her life and art. This captivating memoir explores her creative process and the profound connection between writer and reader, all centered around the luminous north-facing garden she nurtures through reflected light, a potent symbol for the "gold thread" of language that binds us. A must-read for fans of Han Kang's singular voice and insight into the creative experience.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  19. The Beginning Comes After the End Notes on a World of Change

    Rebecca Solnit

    This sweeping survey of social, scientific, and cultural transformations over the past 75 years offers a compelling, interconnected vision of the future, arguing that despite backlash efforts, profound change is inevitable.

    For: Academic and general readers interested in history, sociology, environmentalism, and progressive politics.

    Strengths: Solnit's masterful synthesis of diverse ideas, her clear and evocative prose, and her insightful analysis of the interplay between individual and collective change make this a standout work.

    Skip if: The book's scope and ambition may make it too dense for casual readers seeking a quick overview.

    Comparable: Fans of Solnit's previous works, such as 'Hope in the Dark', as well as readers drawn to progressive thinkers like Naomi Klein or Wendell Berry, will appreciate this book's optimistic yet grounded exploration of societal evolution.

    “An urgent manifesto for our tumultuous time.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org
