12 Essential Non-Fiction Books on Racism, Identity & Social Justice

These powerful books shine a light on systemic racism, privilege, and the struggle for equality.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone seeking to deepen their understanding of racism and social justice
  • Educators looking for diverse texts to teach about race relations
  • Activists and organizers building a more equitable society

Start here

If you only read one book about dismantling white supremacy, choose 'Me and White Supremacy' by Layla F. Saad - it offers a bold, transformative framework.

These 12 groundbreaking non-fiction books tackle the complex, pressing issues of racism, privilege, and social justice head-on. Written by leading voices from diverse backgrounds, they offer insightful perspectives and actionable strategies for creating a more equitable world. Whether you're an educator, activist, or simply an engaged citizen, these essential works will expand your knowledge and inspire personal growth.

The collection

  1. Black in Blues How a Color Tells the Story of My People

    Imani Perry

    In Black in Blues, this National Book Award winner delves into the profound, multifaceted relationship between the colors black and blue, tracing their entwined histories and meanings through art, music, and personal experience. From the melancholy of feeling "Black and blue" to the hope of blue skies, this enchanting meditation reveals the depth and complexity of race and blackness in America, offering a vivid, rich, and striking journey that could only come from Imani Perry's singular voice and insight.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. The Painful Truth about Hunger in America Why We Must Unlearn Everything We Think We Know--and Start Again

    Mariana Chilton

    Mariana Chilton's groundbreaking book, The Painful Truth about Hunger in America, shatters conventional thinking on the issue, revealing that food insecurity is a result of structural power imbalances and rooted in historical trauma, gender-based violence, and our society's relationship with itself and nature. With 25 years of research and advocacy behind her, Chilton presents a radical, multi-faceted approach to ending hunger, requiring personal, political, and spiritual transformation. This powerful, timely work is essential reading for anyone seeking to uproot poverty, discrimination, and build a more nourishing, compassionate world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Blood Politics Race, Culture, and Identity in the Cherokee Nation of Oklahoma

    Circe Dawn Sturm

    Dive into the complex, ever-shifting dynamics of race, identity, and sovereignty within the Cherokee Nation of Oklahoma through Circe Dawn Sturm's groundbreaking inquiry, Blood Politics. By examining the historical evolution and contemporary implications of Cherokee citizenship based on blood degree, Sturm provocatively explores the tension between racial blending and cultural distinctiveness, raising crucial questions about the interplay of race and national identity. A nuanced and necessary read for anyone seeking to understand the intricate web of politics, culture, and power at play in Native American communities today.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. You Are Your Best Thing Vulnerability, Shame Resilience, and the Black Experience

    Tarana Burke

    This collection of essays showcases powerful personal narratives and thought-provoking discussions on vulnerability, shame, and resilience, particularly from the Black female perspective.

    For: Readers seeking authentic, unflinching explorations of the human experience, especially those interested in social justice, identity, and personal growth.

    Strengths: Compelling storytelling, diverse voices, and in-depth analysis on complex topics make this a standout in the self-help and memoir genres.

    Skip if: The dense, academic tone may not appeal to all readers seeking lighter, more accessible self-help material.

    Comparable: Fans of Brené Brown's work will appreciate this collaboration, as will readers of essay collections like 'The Fire This Time' or 'We Are Never Meeting in Real Life'.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. White Fragility Why It's So Hard for White People to Talk About Racism

    Dr. Robin DiAngelo

    White Fragility provides a groundbreaking analysis of systemic racism and the counterproductive reactions of white people when confronted with racial realities.

    For: Anyone seeking a deeper understanding of racial inequality and a roadmap for constructive change should read this essential book.

    Strengths: DiAngelo's expertise shines through in her meticulous examination of white fragility, offering both theoretical depth and practical tools for self-reflection and anti-racism work.

    Skip if: The academic tone and dense theoretical discussions may make this challenging for casual readers or those new to the subject.

    Comparable: In its focus on understanding and dismantling racism, White Fragility is akin to other influential works like So You Want to Talk About Race and How to Be an Antiracist.

    “The value in White Fragility lies in its methodical, irrefutable exposure of racism in thought and action, and its call for humility and vigilance.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Mega Milk Essays on Family, Fluidity, Whiteness, and Cows

    Megan Milks

    "Megan Milks' sparkling essays take readers on a wild journey through the dairy industry, queer intimacy, and the author's own family history, all while boldly exploring themes of whiteness, fluidity, and the complexities of human connection. With humor, wit, and unflinching honesty, Mega Milk shines as a must-read for fans of lyrical nonfiction, queer literature, and anyone curious about the intersection of nature, identity, and culture."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Black Stats African Americans by the Numbers in the Twenty-First Century

    Monique Couvson

    Black Stats is a vital resource for anyone seeking to understand the current state of Black America, providing crucial data to counter misinformation and inform discussions around racial justice.

    For: Advocates, educators, policymakers, and anyone interested in racial equity and social justice.

    Strengths: Compiles a wide range of up-to-date statistics on various aspects of Black life in the US, offering critical analyses and insights to challenge myths and misconceptions.

    Skip if: The dense presentation of data may make it challenging for non-experts to fully grasp the material without additional context or interpretation.

    Comparable: Similar in its mission to provide data-driven insights, though more focused on education, is Monique Morris' Pushout: The Criminalization of Black Girls in Schools.

    “Black Stats is an important resource for anyone seeking a better understanding of the status of Black America.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Me and White Supremacy Combat Racism, Change the World, and Become a Good Ancestor

    Layla F Saad

    Me and White Supremacy is a powerful tool for personal growth and racial awareness, offering a comprehensive 28-day journey to examine and dismantle white privilege.

    For: White people, particularly those in positions of privilege, will benefit most from this book's guidance on anti-racism and allyship.

    Strengths: The workbook-style format makes the complex topic of white supremacy accessible, with engaging prompts and real-life examples to facilitate self-reflection and change.

    Skip if: Me and White Supremacy is a deep, introspective read requiring dedication and a willingness to confront uncomfortable truths about one's biases and privilege.

    Comparable: Other books that explore racial awareness and social justice, such as So You Want to Talk About Race by Ijeoma Oluo, can complement this title for a more comprehensive understanding of racism and allyship in action.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Stories in the Seams A People's History of Black Quilts and Their Makers

    Dr Sharbreon Plummer

    Embark on a richly visual journey through the vibrant history of Black quiltmaking in America with Stories in the Seams. Scholar Dr. Sharbreon Plummer's deeply researched exploration reveals the artistry, resilience, and profound storytelling woven into quilts by scores of self-taught and celebrated makers from the 18th century to today. From stunning archival photographs to bold contemporary designs, and from renowned artists to unsung community creatives, this sweeping survey is an indispensable resource for anyone captivated by the beauty, diversity, and power of quilts as a tradition of healing, activism, and artistic innovation.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Multitudes The Multiracial Experience and How to Forge a Culture of Absolute Belonging

    Dr. Karabi Acharya

    Multitudes offers a groundbreaking exploration of the Multiracial experience, shedding light on the isolation and invisibility faced by this growing demographic, and providing a compelling call to action for greater inclusion and belonging.

    For: This book is essential reading for anyone interested in race, identity, public health, and social justice, particularly Multiracial individuals, antiracist advocates, and those working in healthcare and education.

    Strengths: Multitudes combines rigorous research with personal narratives and real-world examples, resulting in a stereotype-shattering, deeply human portrait of identity and resilience.

    Skip if: The academic tone and dense, research-heavy content may make it challenging for some readers, particularly those seeking a more accessible or narrative-driven book.

    Comparable: Readers may also appreciate other titles in our collection that explore identity, belonging, and social justice, such as 'Between the World and Me' by Ta-Nehisi Coates or 'Why Are All the Black Kids Sitting Together in the Cafeteria?' by Beverly Daniel Tatum.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. #Sayhername Black Women's Stories of Police Violence and Public Silence

    African American Policy Forum

    #Sayhername sheds light on the systemic invisibility and brutality faced by Black women, from young girls to elderly women, at the hands of police violence. This powerful book provides an urgent call to action, equipping readers with an analytical framework and tools to center Black feminist voices in the fight for racial justice, and affirm that #AllBlackLivesMatter.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
