Books to Help You Think Differently and Improve Your Life

Challenge your assumptions and unlock your potential with these thought-provoking books.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone seeking self-improvement and personal growth
  • Business leaders and entrepreneurs looking to innovate
  • Students and lifelong learners
  • Professionals seeking career development

Start here

If you only read one book about challenging assumptions, choose 'Think Again' by Adam Grant.

Embracing a growth mindset and being open to new ideas can transform your life and work. These 12 inspiring books offer fresh perspectives and practical strategies to challenge your assumptions, improve your thinking, and unlock your potential. From communication and creativity to self-improvement and innovation, this diverse collection has something for everyone seeking personal growth and professional success.

The collection

  1. On Writing Well The Classic Guide to Writing Nonfiction

    William Zinsser

    In this renowned guide, veteran author William Zinsser distills decades of writing expertise into lucid advice for crafting clear, engaging nonfiction. Whether you're a professional journalist, student, or simply seeking to communicate effectively, On Writing Well offers timeless principles and real-world insights to elevate your writing, whatever your subject. A classic resource for anyone who writes or wants to write well.

  2. Never Split the Difference Negotiating As If Your Life Depended On It

    Chris Voss

    Immerse yourself in the high-stakes world of FBI hostage negotiation and discover the secrets to successful persuasion in all aspects of life with Chris Voss's New York Times bestselling Never Split the Difference. Drawing from his experience as an FBI lead international kidnapping negotiator, Voss reveals the communication skills that matter most for achieving your goals, from establishing rapport to transforming conflict into collaboration. This masterclass in influencing others will arm you with the tools to navigate even the most challenging conversations with confidence and effectiveness. Don't miss out on the competitive edge this book provides for success in business, personal relationships, and beyond.

  3. The Way of Excellence A Guide to True Greatness and Deep Satisfaction in a Chaotic World

    Brad Stulberg

    In "The Way of Excellence: A Guide to True Greatness and Deep Satisfaction in a Chaotic World", Brad Stulberg offers a definitive guide to achieving your full potential in a distraction-filled life. Drawing from modern science, timeless philosophy, and practical daily habits, Stulberg shows that excellence is not just attainable, but essential to our humanity. With insights on focus, values, mastery, and more, this New York Times bestseller sets you on a path to reclaim excellence and become the best performer and person you can be, while experiencing greater aliveness and fulfillment along the way.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. What to Make of a Life Cliffs, Fog, Fire and the Self-Knowledge Imperative

    Jim Collins

    In this deeply researched and uplifting book, renowned author Jim Collins explores the transformative moments that shape our lives, offering a practical framework to navigate life's cliffs, fog, and fires. By studying the choices of remarkable individuals - from rock musicians to suffragists - Collins reveals how we can discover fulfilling roles, rebuild after setbacks, sustain personal momentum, and apply self-knowledge to every phase. With moving candor, Collins also shares his own journey of discovery, showing us how to construct a life of depth and purpose. A must-read for anyone seeking to maximize their potential and create a richly fulfilling existence.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Thinking, Fast and Slow

    Daniel Kahneman

    "Join Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman in this groundbreaking exploration of the human mind's two systems - intuitive, fast-thinking System 1, and deliberate, logical System 2. Packed with enlightening examples from business and everyday life, Thinking, Fast and Slow reveals how our decisions are shaped by cognitive biases and provides practical strategies to avoid mental glitches. A contemporary classic with over 2.6 million copies sold, this tour of the mind's inner workings will transform the way you think."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Stolen Focus Why You Can't Pay Attention--and How to Think Deeply Again

    Johann Hari

    In today's digitally saturated world, our ability to focus is under siege. Johann Hari's thought-provoking Stolen Focus exposes the relentless external forces stealing our attention, from pollution to corporate profit schemes. Rather than blaming personal failures of willpower, Hari offers a groundbreaking exploration of the 12 root causes of this crisis, highlighting potential solutions from Silicon Valley innovators and an office in New Zealand that dramatically boosted productivity. A Times bestseller, this essential read empowers individuals and society to reclaim their focus and live more deeply engaged lives.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. How Big Things Get Done The Surprising Factors That Determine the Fate of Every Project, from Home Renovations to Space Exploration and Everything In Between

    Bent Flyvbjerg

    Unlock the secrets to success with ambitious projects of all sizes with this seminal guide from "megaproject" expert Bent Flyvbjerg. Drawing from a lifetime of research and vivid real-world examples, How Big Things Get Done exposes the mistakes that lead to costly failures and reveals the principles that guarantee triumph. Whether launching a small business, renovating a home, or spearheading a global endeavor, this book provides the hard-won wisdom to navigate the challenges and achieve your goals with confidence.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Supercommunicators How to Unlock the Secret Language of Connection

    Charles Duhigg

    Supercommunicators offers practical strategies for effective communication through a blend of compelling stories and scientific research, making it a valuable resource for anyone seeking to improve their interpersonal and professional interactions.

    For: Professionals, leaders, and individuals looking to enhance their communication skills in personal and professional settings.

    Strengths: Duhigg's storytelling ability brings complex research to life, providing relatable examples to illustrate key concepts. The book's focus on identifying and navigating different types of conversations (practical, emotional, social) offers actionable advice for readers.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the theoretical explanations and research-heavy sections dense and slow-going.

    Comparable: For those interested in communication, leadership, or personal development, Supercommunicators shares similarities with other titles like 'The Art of Possibility' by Rosamund Stone Zander and Benjamin Zander, or 'Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion' by Robert Cialdini.

  9. Hidden Potential The Science of Achieving Greater Things

    Adam Grant

    Hidden Potential offers a fresh perspective on personal growth and development by challenging assumptions about innate talent and potential, providing actionable advice for readers to realize their full potential.

    For: This book is a must-read for anyone seeking to improve themselves, including parents, educators, leaders, athletes, and individuals looking to unlock their hidden potential.

    Strengths: Grant's engaging storytelling, accessible explanation of complex research, and practical takeaways make this an inspiring and informative read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the academic tone and dense research references a bit overwhelming.

    Comparable: For those interested in self-improvement and personal development, consider pairing Hidden Potential with other thought-provoking works like Carol Dweck's Mindset or Daniel Coyle's The Talent Code.

    “This brilliant book will shatter your assumptions about what it takes to improve and succeed. I wish I could go back in time and gift it to my younger self. It would”

  10. Tiny Experiments How to Live Freely in a Goal-Obsessed World

    Anne-Laure Le Cunff

    Discover a revolutionary approach to goals, creativity, and living authentically in a goal-obsessed world. Anne-Laure Le Cunff, a neuroscientist and entrepreneur, presents the transformative power of "tiny experiments" to replace linear thinking with a circular model of growth. Through simple yet meaningful tests, you'll break free from limiting beliefs, make smarter decisions, and pursue your most authentic ambitions. This guide will inspire you to reawaken your curiosity, drive, and purpose, leading to a more fulfilling life of self-discovery and contribution.

  11. Inside the Box How Constraints Make Us Better

    David Epstein

    Inside the Box offers a compelling argument about the benefits of constraints in stimulating creativity, innovation, and personal contentment, backed by research and fascinating case studies.

    For: This book is a must-read for anyone looking to optimize their work, creative pursuits, or personal growth by understanding the power of limits and boundaries.

    Strengths: Epstein's writing is engaging, the examples are diverse and thought-provoking, and the concept is both theoretically sound and practically applicable.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the scientific explanations and data-heavy sections dense and slow to digest.

    Comparable: Fans of Malcolm Gladwell's books and those interested in productivity, creativity, and the science of human performance will appreciate Inside the Box's unique perspective and well-researched insights.

    “From UPS routes to early computers to Bach”

  12. Mattering The Secret to a Life of Deep Connection and Purpose

    Jennifer Breheny Wallace

    Mattering offers a compelling argument for the importance of feeling valued and connected in today's society, presented through engaging storytelling and rigorous research.

    For: This book is a must-read for anyone feeling burnt out, disconnected, or lacking purpose, as well as those seeking to understand and foster deeper human connections.

    Strengths: Wallace's approach is empathetic, accessible, and actionable, providing readers with a clear framework to boost their sense of mattering and live more fulfilling lives.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the early chapters' philosophical musings a bit slow, but the payoff in practical guidance is well worth the initial investment.

    Comparable: For those who appreciated Never Enough or books like The Art of Possibility, Mattering offers a similarly thought-provoking exploration of human needs and potential for growth and connection.

    Ravi Gupta

    Transform your dreams into daily habits with The Invisible List, a step-by-step guide from educator and civic entrepreneur Ravi Gupta. By dedicating a year to mastering a single skill, Gupta found the passion and fulfillment missing from his high-achieving life. This inspiring blueprint shows you how to resurrect your long-deferred hobbies, from the practical and accessible to the ambitious and transformative, without sacrificing work or family responsibilities. Give yourself permission to prioritize pleasure and growth, and discover a life worth living.

    Dale Carnegie

    Maximize your relationships and success with this timeless, best-selling guide. Renowned as a classic in self-help and psychology, Dale Carnegie's How to Win Friends and Influence People offers proven strategies for effective communication, building connections, achieving goals, and leading others. Updated for today's readers, this indispensable book is a must-have for personal and professional growth in any era.

  15. Range Why Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World

    David Epstein

    Range is a paradigm-shifting read that challenges conventional wisdom on the path to success. By examining high achievers across diverse fields, David Epstein reveals that generalists, not specialists, often excel in complex and unpredictable domains. This refreshing perspective argues for embracing inefficiency, exploring multiple interests, and making unexpected connections to cultivate creativity and resilience in a rapidly changing world. A must-read for anyone seeking to thrive in an era of artificial intelligence and constant disruption.

    Angela Duckworth

    Discover the key to outstanding achievement that's not talent, but a unique blend of passion and persistence - grit. In Grit, acclaimed researcher Angela Duckworth shares fascinating insights from interviews with high achievers and translates groundbreaking psychological research into actionable strategies for cultivating the tenacity to succeed. From the early days at West Point to the National Spelling Bee, Duckworth illustrates grit in action and shows how it can be learned, regardless of IQ or circumstances. This inspiring, practical guide reveals what truly makes a difference - and will change how you approach your goals.

With a nuanced blend of self-help, politics, and cultural critique, "How to Do Nothing" empowers readers to break free from capitalist narratives and redefine what matters most. A must-read for anyone seeking a more meaningful, intentional life in a hyper-connected world.

    Brené Brown

    In Strong Ground, Brené Brown delivers a vital playbook for leaders navigating today's uncertain world. Grounded in her groundbreaking Dare to Lead work, Brown shares essential lessons on cultivating courageous leadership through deep connection, strategic risk-taking, and the discipline to unlearn and relearn.   20. Think Again The Power of Knowing What You Don't Know

    Adam Grant

    Think Again offers a timely and insightful exploration of the importance of questioning one's beliefs and assumptions in a rapidly changing world.

    For: Professionals, leaders, and individuals seeking to cultivate a more open, curious, and adaptable mindset.

    Strengths: Grant's engaging narrative weaves together compelling stories, research, and practical advice, making complex concepts accessible and actionable.

    Skip if: The book's dense, academic style may not appeal to readers seeking a lighter, more casual read.

    Comparable: Those who appreciated Grant's previous works on motivation, generosity, and creativity will find Think Again a natural extension of his thought-provoking ideas.
