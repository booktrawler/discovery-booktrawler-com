12 Uplifting Books for When You Need Comfort & Hope

Find solace and renewed optimism in these life-affirming nonfiction titles, each offering unique perspectives on resilience, meaning, and the human spirit.

Who these books are for

  • Those dealing with loss or hardship
  • Seekers of meaning and purpose
  • Readers looking for uplifting nonfiction
  • Anyone needing a boost of hope and optimism

Start here

If you only read one book to find comfort, choose Matt Haig's 'The Comfort Book'.

In times of struggle, we often seek out books that offer comfort, wisdom, and a renewed sense of hope. This carefully curated collection of 12 uplifting nonfiction titles provides a diverse range of perspectives and experiences to help you navigate life's challenges. From memoirs of resilience to philosophical explorations of meaning, each book in this selection has been chosen for its ability to inspire, console, and motivate readers. Whether you're facing personal adversity, seeking to make sense of the world, or simply in need of a positivity boost, these life-affirming works are here to guide you through the darkest moments and toward a brighter future.

The collection

  1. All My Dead Cats and Other Losses Practicing Good Grief in a Culture That Fears Mourning

    s. e. smith

    In "All My Dead Cats and Other Losses," s. e. smith invites readers to confront grief in all its forms, sharing personal stories alongside expert insights to create a safe space for honest mourning. By exploring the stages of grief and the complexities of disenfranchised sorrow, this book empowers individuals to navigate loss with courage and community, ultimately leading to a more fulfilling life. Gorgeously written and relatable, "All My Dead Cats" is a must-read for anyone seeking to understand and process their own mourning, and to better support those around them in their grief journeys.

  2. Communion Finding My Way Back to Faith

    J.D. Vance

    In Communion, J.D. Vance shares his deeply personal journey from disconnection to rediscovery of faith, weaving together his experiences as a child, young adult, father, and public figure to reveal how spirituality guides and grounds him. A profoundly relatable and thought-provoking memoir, it offers a glimpse into the evolving relationship between a man and his beliefs, and how faith intersects with the challenges and triumphs of everyday life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Comfort Book

    Matt Haig

    Matt Haig's The Comfort Book offers a collection of uplifting, relatable musings and stories to help navigate life's challenges and find hope, making it a valuable companion for anyone seeking inspiration and resilience.

    For: Recommended for readers in search of emotional support, personal growth, and a renewed sense of purpose, particularly those experiencing mental health struggles or feeling overwhelmed by modern life.

    Strengths: Haig's signature empathy and storytelling ability shine in this book, delivering bite-sized nuggets of wisdom and comfort that resonate deeply with readers. The diverse range of sources and perspectives adds richness and depth to the narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the book's reflective, meandering style a bit slow-paced or repetitive at times, as Haig revisits and expands on previous ideas.

    Comparable: Similar in tone and purpose to Haig's other works like Notes on a Nervous Planet, The Comfort Book offers a more intimate, personal collection of thoughts and stories that explore the human condition and the search for meaning and comfort in a complex world.

    “Haig is one of the most important writers of our time.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Restrung A Memoir of Music and Transformation

    Vijay Gupta

    In this unflinching memoir, Vijay Gupta recounts his meteoric rise as a prodigious violinist, from Carnegie Hall at 8 to the LA Philharmonic at 19, amidst addiction, estrangement, and private collapse. But Restrung is more than a tale of talent and turmoil - it's a profound exploration of how music became Gupta's lifeline, his connection to others and his own humanity. Through his journey, Gupta reveals how art has the power to transform not just audiences, but also the artists themselves. This searing, deeply moving memoir is for anyone who has ever found solace in music, and a testament to the resilience of the human spirit.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Righteous Mind Why Good People Are Divided by Politics and Religion

    Jonathan Haidt

    In this landmark work, acclaimed psychologist Jonathan Haidt reveals why good people are divided by politics and religion, uncovering the surprising roots of moral judgments in our unconscious emotions rather than logical reasoning. Through his groundbreaking research, Haidt shows that conservatives, liberals and libertarians each have compelling intuitions about right and wrong, demonstrating the value in embracing our differences for a deeper understanding of human cooperation and conflict. A must-read for anyone seeking to transcend anger and confusion, The Righteous Mind offers profound insights into the complexities of the human mind and the keys to a more harmonious society.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Make Believe On Telling Stories to Children

    Mac Barnett

    Embark on an inspiring journey through the power of storytelling and children's literature with acclaimed author Mac Barnett's Make Believe. Part love letter to the art of kids' books, part impassioned plea to champion reading and imagination in our lives and times, this vibrant and thought-provoking book will leave you marveling at the magic that unfolds when words and pictures come together to spark young minds and souls.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. There's Always This Year On Basketball and Ascension

    Hanif Abdurraqib

    In "There's Always This Year", Hanif Abdurraqib combines his love for basketball with powerful personal storytelling to explore themes of identity, family, and the complexities of success. Through lyrical prose infused with poetry, Abdurraqib delves into the 1990s Columbus basketball scene and the expectations placed on athletes, ultimately revealing rich insights into what it means to strive, to dream, and to find one's place in the world - both on and off the court. This heartfelt, critically acclaimed memoir is a must-read for anyone who has ever felt the pull of a passion, the sting of disappointment, or the promise of each new year.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Daily Dad 366 Meditations on Parenting, Love, and Raising Great Kids

    Ryan Holiday

    In "The Daily Dad," Ryan Holiday offers a daily devotional for parents, with 366 meditations on parenting, love, and raising great kids. Drawing on his own experience and wisdom from legendary figures, this book provides timeless guidance and practical advice for every stage of parenthood, from the sleep-deprived early days to guiding your child into adulthood. Whether new to parenting or seasoned, Holiday's meditations will inspire and equip you to be the role model your child needs.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Man's Search for Meaning

    Viktor E. Frankl

    Viktor Frankl's harrowing memoir and philosophical reflection on finding purpose in the face of unimaginable suffering makes it a timeless classic and must-read.

    For: Anyone seeking inspiration, resilience, and insights into human nature and the search for meaning, regardless of background or circumstances.

    Strengths: Frankl's gripping personal account of life in Nazi concentration camps, coupled with his thought-provoking logotherapy, provides a unique blend of survival narrative and psychological theory.

    Skip if: The book's dense, academic style may not appeal to readers seeking a fast-paced or easy-to-digest read.

    Comparable: Other memoirs of the Holocaust, such as Elie Wiesel's Night, offer similarly powerful explorations of human resilience and the quest for meaning in the face of tragedy.

    “This is a book I reread a lot . . . it gives me hope . . . it gives me a sense of strength.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Man's Search for Meaning Gift Edition

    Viktor E. Frankl

    This seminal memoir offers profound insights into human resilience, meaning, and the search for purpose in the face of unimaginable suffering.

    For: Anyone seeking inspiration, guidance, or perspective on life's challenges will find Viktor Frankl's story and philosophy deeply moving and thought-provoking.

    Strengths: Frankl's personal narrative is both harrowing and uplifting, while his logotherapy framework provides a unique lens for understanding the human drive towards meaning.

    Skip if: The writing can be dense and philosophical at times, requiring close attention and reflection.

    Comparable: Other classic survival memoirs like Elie Wiesel's Night or Anne Frank's Diary provide similar insights into the Holocaust experience, but Frankl's focus on logotherapy makes Man's Search for Meaning stand out.

    “This is a book I reread a lot . . . it gives me hope . . . it gives me a sense of strength.”

  11. When Breath Becomes Air Pulitzer Prize Finalist

    Paul Kalanithi

    This memoir tells a powerful, thought-provoking story of a brilliant young doctor grappling with mortality and the meaning of life, all while conveying deep insights into the human condition.

    For: Anyone seeking a profound, moving exploration of the intersection of medicine, philosophy, and personal story would benefit from reading this.

    Strengths: The author's lucid writing, intellectual curiosity, and emotional authenticity make for a compelling narrative that stays with the reader long after finishing.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the academic and philosophical references dense and the pace deliberate, which could slow their engagement.

    Comparable: Fans of memoirs that explore the intersection of medicine and personal struggle, such as 'Being Mortal' by Atul Gawande or 'The Emperor of All Maladies' by Siddhartha Mukherjee, will likely appreciate this book's depth and candor.

    “Kalanithi uses the pages in this book to not only tell his story, but also share his ideas on how to approach death with grace and what it means to be fully alive.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The One and the Ninety-Nine Forging Identity in the Age of Social Contagion

    Luke Burgis

    Is your authentic self getting lost in the sea of online tribes and ready-made identities? Luke Burgis's timely and thought-provoking book, The One and the Ninety-Nine, offers a vital guide to forming a solid, resilient identity amidst the pressures of modern group dynamics. A must-read for anyone seeking genuine belonging and meaningful connection in a world of social contagion, Burgis provides a practical roadmap for recognizing false community, escaping coercive influences, and cultivating the courage to stand alone—and together.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. Why We Sleep Unlocking the Power of Sleep and Dreams

    Matthew Walker PhD

    Discover the transformative power of sleep with Dr. Matthew Walker's groundbreaking book, Why We Sleep. This New York Times bestseller illuminates how sleep enriches our minds, optimizes our bodies, and can even extend life. From improving learning and memory to preventing disease and fostering creativity, Walker's cutting-edge science and actionable advice make a compelling case for prioritizing slumber. A fascinating and timely read that will forever change how you think about sleep.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  14. Hope in the Dark Untold Histories, Wild Possibilities

    Rebecca Solnit

    In a world rife with uncertainty and pessimism, Hope in the Dark offers a timely and inspiring case for radical hope, drawing on centuries of transformative victories in politics, culture, and the environment. Through a blend of activism, history, and personal reflection, Rebecca Solnit shows that hope is not a feeling, but a commitment to act in the face of the unknown. With a new introduction and afterword, this updated classic illuminates the power of hope to shape our future and reminds us that even in darkness, there is always a path forward.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  15. Every Day I Read 53 Ways to Get Closer to Books

    Hwang Bo-reum

    In this insightful and heartening book, acclaimed author Hwang Bo-reum delves into the profound joys of reading, inviting you to reflect on your own relationship with books. With 53 engaging essays, she explores the personal meaning and transformative power of immersing yourself in literature, and shares her own inspirations and book recommendations along the way. A perfect companion for any book lover seeking to deepen their appreciation for the written word and the quiet beauty of a life well-read.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  16. The Book of Delights

    Ross Gay

    This book delights in the everyday, reminding us to appreciate the small joys amidst life's complexities, through the poet's lyrical and observant essays.

    For: Readers seeking inspiration to notice and celebrate the beauty in the mundane, especially those appreciative of poetry and personal essays.

    Strengths: Offers a genre-defying collection of short lyrical essays celebrating ordinary wonders, with a unique voice and perspective.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the daily format and poetic style makes for a slow pace.

    Comparable: Fans of personal essay collections like Mary Karr's The Liars' Club and David Sedaris' Me Talk Pretty One Day will appreciate Gay's unique voice and celebration of the everyday.

    “s hard to read Gay and not to be won over.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  17. The Small Stuff How to Lead a More Gratifying Life

    Ian Bogost

    In The Small Stuff, bestselling author Ian Bogost makes a compelling case that our relentless pursuit of convenience is eroding the simple, satisfying moments that make life worth living. He argues that the things we overlook - like manually opening a door, scribbling a note, or cooking a meal - are, in fact, the building blocks of a more gratifying existence. Bogost's humorous, relatable reflections and practical insights show us how to rediscover these everyday pleasures, revealing that true happiness often lies in the small, deliberate actions we take. A must-read for anyone craving meaning and joy in the modern world.

Frequently asked questions

What types of books provide comfort and hope?
Nonfiction titles offering personal stories, philosophical insights, and uplifting messages can provide solace and inspiration.
Can fiction books also offer comfort?
While the collection focuses on nonfiction, fiction can certainly offer comfort too, especially when it explores universal human experiences and emotions.
How many books should I read at once?
Feel free to read as many or as few as you like, but try to focus on one at a time to fully absorb the messages and ideas.
