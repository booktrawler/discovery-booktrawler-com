12 Books on Subversive Tactics and Espionage for Thrill Seekers

Immerse yourself in the world of espionage and subversion with these gripping books on sabotage and undercover operations.

Who these books are for

  • History buffs seeking covert operations stories
  • Thrill-seekers interested in espionage and sabotage
  • Researchers studying WWII and Cold War strategies

Start here

If you only read one book, choose the timeless 'Simple Sabotage Field Manual' for a comprehensive guide to subversive tactics.

From classic guides to modern memoirs, these 12 books take you behind enemy lines, into the world of clandestine operations, and inside the minds of spies and saboteurs. Whether you're a history enthusiast, a thriller aficionado, or simply curious about the art of subterfuge, this collection has something for everyone. Prepare to be captivated by the daring exploits and ingenious strategies that have shaped the course of conflicts and covert affairs.

The collection

  1. Simple Sabotage Field Manual

    Office of Strategic Services (Oss)

    This manual provides a fascinating glimpse into the strategic thinking and tactics employed by the OSS during WWII, revealing the quiet power of everyday resistance.

    For: History buffs, strategic thinkers, and anyone interested in the psychological aspects of war will find this book compelling.

    Strengths: The manual's historical significance, unique perspective, and accessible content make it a standout in the collection.

    Skip if: While the manual's contents are intriguing, some readers may find the tone and language slightly dry due to its origins as a practical guide.

    Comparable: For those drawn to historical nonfiction and military strategy, titles like 'The Zookeeper's Wife' or 'Behind Enemy Lines' might also be of interest.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Lessons from Cats for Surviving Fascism

    Stewart Reynolds

    This book offers a fresh, humorous take on resisting authoritarianism through lessons drawn from cats' natural behaviors, making it an entertaining and thought-provoking read.

    For: Anyone looking for inspiration on how to stay independent and subversive in the face of control, particularly young adults and fans of social commentary.

    Strengths: Creative use of cat analogies to illustrate practical strategies for coping with oppressive systems, engaging humor throughout, and a bold, unapologetic stance against fascism.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the cat-centric approach too gimmicky or off-putting if they're not already fans of the author's style.

    Comparable: Fans of humorous social commentary and those who enjoyed

    “An imaginative appeal to be, as cats are, ungovernable.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Simple Sabotage Field Manual

    U S Office of Strategic Services

    This historical manual provides a fascinating glimpse into covert operations and strategic sabotage during World War II, serving as a valuable resource for military historians and enthusiasts.

    For: Military history buffs, World War II scholars, and those interested in clandestine operations will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: The manual delivers authentic, firsthand instructions on sabotage tactics and techniques, offering a unique perspective on wartime strategy and espionage.

    Skip if: The text is dense and technical, requiring a strong background in military operations to fully understand.

    Comparable: Fans of espionage and military history may also enjoy other classic works like 'The Spy Who Came in from the Cold' or 'The Art of Intelligence'.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Simple Sabotage Field Manual A Declassified CIA Guide to Subverting Fascism through Weaponized Incompetence

    United States Office of Strategic Services

    This declassified guide from the OSS offers a fascinating look at the art of everyday sabotage, providing insight into how ordinary citizens can effectively resist oppressive regimes.

    For: Anyone interested in history, politics, or finding creative ways to challenge authority will appreciate this unique manual.

    Strengths: The Simple Sabotage Field Manual is a masterclass in strategic incompetence, offering clever techniques for disrupting bureaucratic processes and stymying productivity.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the tone and style, which is geared towards a specific historical context, a bit dry or challenging to relate to in the modern era.

    Comparable: Fans of unconventional history, political satire, or books on resistance and activism may enjoy other titles like 'The Art of War' by Sun Tzu or 'Yes, Prime Minister' by Jonathan Lynn and Antony Jay.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The American School of Spies The Archaeologists Who Fought the Nazis and Saved the Treasures of Ancient Greece

    Stephan Talty

    In this gripping true story, New York Times bestselling author Stephan Talty reveals how a team of American archaeologists and classicists, recruited by OSS chief Wild Bill Donovan, went undercover as spies to thwart the Nazi threat to Greece's ancient treasures. As the war intensified, these unlikely agents engaged in a cat-and-mouse game, hiding priceless artifacts and creating decoys to deceive the enemy, in a desperate bid to protect the world's most significant historical relics. With heart-pounding action and extraordinary historical detail, The American School of Spies is a thrilling tale of bravery, ingenuity, and the unbreakable bonds between scholars, soldiers, and a besieged nation.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. How to Close a Camp Dispatches from the Fight Against Immigrant Detention

    John Washington

    This urgent polemic and practical guide provides a clear-eyed case for dismantling the immigrant detention system, offering strategies and lessons from successful campaigns to close camps and block new openings.

    For: Activists, organizers, scholars, and concerned citizens seeking to understand and combat the immigrant detention system.

    Strengths: Washington's book excels in its rigorous research, moral clarity, and powerful storytelling, making a compelling case for the need to close camps and build a more just world.

    Skip if: The dense, academic tone may make it challenging for some readers to engage with the complex issues and strategies presented.

    Comparable: For those interested in immigrant rights, anti-detention activism, and social justice, this book is comparable to works by scholars like Silky Shah and Sarah Stillman, offering a unique blend of analysis, history, and practical guidance.

    “choice to lock up migrants and brings to life the need--and possibility--of ending this type of human bondage.”
