The collection

  1. Burn the Haystack Decode Dating, Torch the Duds, and Make Room for Men Who Matter

    Jennie Young, PhD

    Burn the Haystack offers a refreshingly critical perspective on online dating, empowering readers to recognize and avoid toxic communication patterns.

    For: Women interested in online dating, particularly those seeking a more meaningful connection.

    Strengths: Young's expertise in feminist rhetoric shines through in her astute deconstruction of men's messaging tactics, providing practical advice for navigating the often treacherous world of dating apps.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the academic tone and jargon-heavy explanations off-putting, requiring patience and focus to fully absorb the concepts.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate Sarah McBride's The Breakup Bible or Esther Perel's Mating in Captivity may appreciate the author's incisive analysis of relationship dynamics and communication strategies.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. I'll Believe in Anything The Making of Heated Rivalry Season 1 (the Annotated Scripts)

    Jacob Tierney

    This behind-the-scenes companion provides unique insights into the creative process behind the acclaimed series Heated Rivalry, showcasing the vision and expertise of showrunner Jacob Tierney.

    For: Fans of the Heated Rivalry series, aspiring screenwriters, and those interested in the making of successful television adaptations will appreciate this book.

    Strengths: I'll Believe in Anything delivers in-depth annotations, exclusive content, and thoughtful analysis of the series' innovative storytelling and production techniques.

    Skip if: The technical focus may be overwhelming for casual viewers seeking a general overview of the show.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoyed the making-of books for other popular TV series or are interested in the intersection of literature and screen adaptations will find this book of similar appeal.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Eight Dates Essential Conversations for a Lifetime of Love

    John Gottman PH D

    This book offers a practical, research-based guide to strengthening long-term relationships through thoughtful conversation. Its unique 'eight dates' format makes complex relationship concepts accessible and engaging.

    For: Couples in committed relationships, from newlyweds to those looking to refresh their partnership, will benefit from the Gottmans' expert advice and exercises.

    Strengths: The Gottmans' decades of relationship research shine through in the book's well-structured, actionable conversations and activities. The variety of topics and interactive nature keep the content engaging.

    Skip if: The book's focus on conversation rather than quick fixes or romantic notions may appeal less to readers seeking a light, inspirational read.

    Comparable: Fans of relationship self-help books like 'The Seven Principles for Making Marriage Work' or 'Hold Me Tight' will appreciate the Gottmans' signature blend of science and practical relationship advice in 'Eight Dates'.

    “2019 Nautilus Book Award -Gold in the Communications Relationships category --2019 Nautilus Book Award”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Burn the Haystack Decode Dating, Torch the Duds, and Make Room for Men Who Matter

    Jennie Young

    Burn the Haystack is a necessary, empowering guide for women navigating the toxic and often absurd world of online dating, offering a refreshing, feminist approach to decoding men's messages and finding a worthy partner.

    For: Women aged 25-45 who have had frustrating experiences with online dating and are seeking a strategic, humorous, and empowering way to approach it.

    Strengths: Provides a comprehensive, research-backed framework for identifying and avoiding toxic communication patterns in men's profiles and messages, while offering practical tips for crafting a dating profile and engaging in healthy, equal online interactions.

    Skip if: The writing can feel academic and dense at times, requiring a certain level of comfort with rhetorical theory and feminist criticism.

    Comparable: Readers may also appreciate Susan Walsh's Avoiding Dating Disasters or Helen Fisher's Anatomy of Love for further insights into the psychology and sociology of modern dating, but Burn the Haystack stands out for its unique focus on rhetorical analysis and feminist strategy.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Never Search Alone The Job Seeker's Playbook

    Phyl Terry

    Never Search Alone offers a comprehensive, support-driven approach to job searching, ensuring readers' emotional well-being and career success.

    For: Leaders, product managers, and professionals seeking to land their ideal job and build fulfilling careers.

    Strengths: Provides a structured, multi-step process with actionable tools and templates; emphasizes the importance of a job search council for emotional support; offers insights into candidate-market fit and effective negotiation strategies.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the book's length and dense content overwhelming; it's geared towards mid-to-senior level professionals and may not be as relevant for entry-level job seekers.

    Comparable: Compared to other career development books, Never Search Alone stands out for its focus on job search support groups and negotiation tactics, making it a valuable resource for those seeking a more holistic approach to career advancement.
