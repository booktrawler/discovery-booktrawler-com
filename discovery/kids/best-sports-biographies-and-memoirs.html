Best Sports Biographies and Memoirs

Delve into the inspiring stories and triumphs of your favorite athletes with these captivating sports biographies and memoirs.

Who these books are for

  • Sports enthusiasts
  • Book clubs exploring personal stories
  • Fans of specific athletes or teams
  • Anyone seeking inspiring true accounts

Start here

If you only read one sports bio this year, choose 'Coach' by Jason Reynolds for a gripping, lyrical exploration of a basketball coach's journey.

For readers who crave the authentic stories behind sports legends, this curated collection offers a diverse range of compelling biographies and memoirs. From the hardwood to the gridiron and beyond, these books deliver inspiring, in-depth looks at the lives and careers of accomplished athletes. Whether you're a die-hard sports fan or simply appreciate the human side of competition, these engaging stories are sure to captivate and inform.

The collection

  1. Basket Ball The Story of the All-American Game

    Kadir Nelson

    Basket Ball offers a visually stunning journey through the history of basketball, celebrating its evolution and iconic players with vivid oil paintings and engaging profiles.

    For: Basketball fans, sports enthusiasts, and anyone interested in American cultural history.

    Strengths: Kadir Nelson's breathtaking artwork brings the game to life, while the text provides a comprehensive and accessible overview of key players, milestones, and developments in the sport's history.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the dense historical context and lengthy profiles overwhelming, particularly for younger audiences.

    Comparable: Fans of We Are the Ship: The Story of Negro League Baseball will appreciate Nelson's similar visual storytelling approach in capturing the spirit and significance of another iconic American sport.

    “Jam-packed with well-researched historical context, each chapter is compelling.... A backboard-shattering slam dunk.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Messi Life Lessons from a Legend

    Gilang Bogy

    Dive into the inspiring true story of Lionel Messi, the soccer legend who overcame odds to become a global icon. From his early struggles to shattering records, "Messi: Life Lessons from a Legend" shares the Argentine master's extraordinary journey, packed with motivational quotes, stirring artwork, and invaluable lessons for anyone pursuing their dreams.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Jalen Plays It All

    Jalen Brunson

    Jalen Brunson's inspiring memoir offers an authentic, relatable portrayal of a champion's journey, making it a compelling addition for any sports or self-improvement collection.

    For: Middle grade readers and young athletes, particularly those seeking motivational stories of perseverance and character development.

    Strengths: Brunson's vivid anecdotes and accessible writing style make his message about the value of hard work, resilience, and self-discovery resonate with readers of all backgrounds. The book's focus on the importance of trying new things and learning from mistakes is particularly empowering for young audiences.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the text's structure, jumping between Brunson's various childhood sports, a bit disjointed or hard to follow at times.

    Comparable: Fans of athlete memoirs like LeBron James's 'I Promise' or Serena Williams's 'On the Line' will appreciate Brunson's similarly uplifting, sports-centric coming-of-age story.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Baby Dunks-A-Lot: The Day the Basketballs Stopped Bouncing A Picture Book

    Jayson Tatum

    This entertaining and imaginative picture book combines Jayson Tatum's NBA expertise with a humorous story, colorful illustrations, and a fun mystery to solve. It's a unique blend of sports, humor, and adventure.

    For: Children aged 4-8 who enjoy sports, humor, and imaginative stories, as well as parents and educators seeking engaging picture books.

    Strengths: Engaging story with a relatable sports theme, humor, and a clear call to action; colorful, dynamic illustrations; and a fun, accessible mystery to solve.

    Skip if: Some may find the story a bit predictable and the basketball knowledge aimed at younger readers rather than advanced sports enthusiasts.

    Comparable: Fans of humorous sports-themed picture books like 'The Boss Baby' or those seeking lighthearted stories with NBA connections will enjoy 'Baby Dunks-A-Lot'.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Better Than a Touchdown

    Jalen Hurts

    This uplifting, autobiographical tale showcases Jalen Hurts' inspiring leadership and teamwork values, making it a standout addition for readers seeking motivational stories.

    For: Children aged 8-12 and parents looking for inspiring, relatable stories about perseverance and community.

    Strengths: Authentic portrayal of a young athlete's challenges and triumphs, engaging full-color illustrations, and an empowering message about the power of unity.

    Skip if: Some younger readers may find the football terminology and school setting less relatable compared to more universally appealing stories.

    Comparable: Fans of sports-themed picture books like 'The Wall: Growing Up Behind the Iron Curtain' or 'MVP: Most Valuable Primate' will appreciate this heartwarming tale of teamwork and determination.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. EXTRAordinary! A Little EXTRA to Reach BIG Dreams

    Caitlin Clark

    This heartwarming picture book shares a personal message of gratitude for the people who make us extraordinary, beautifully illustrated and perfect for reading aloud.

    For: Children aged 4-8 and their families will appreciate this uplifting story.

    Strengths: Vibrant illustrations, rhyming text, and a relatable message of family and friendship make this a standout picture book.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the rhyming a bit forced in places, which disrupts the flow.

    Comparable: Fans of inspirational, sports-themed picture books like 'The Basketball Book' by Gwendolyn Brooks will enjoy this.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Coach

    Jason Reynolds

    In Coach, Jason Reynolds masterfully spins a poignant coming-of-age tale as young Otie Brody navigates the highs of youthful ambition to become a track star amidst the turbulence of family upheaval. With heart and humor, Reynolds explores the complexities of missing parents, the allure of iconic heroics, and the empowering struggles of self-discovery, all through Otie's relatable quest to reconcile his fading perceptions of his father with the harsh realities of abandonment. A beautifully crafted story that will resonate with readers of all ages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
