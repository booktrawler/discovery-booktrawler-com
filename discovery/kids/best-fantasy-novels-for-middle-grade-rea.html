Best Fantasy Novels for Middle Grade Readers

Immerse your preteen in enchanted worlds with these timeless fantasy novels, perfect for middle grade readers.

Who these books are for

  • Middle grade readers (8-12 years old)
  • Parents seeking engaging books for their kids
  • Teachers looking for enriching classroom reads

Start here

If you only read one fantasy series with your middle grade reader, choose the beloved 'Howl's Moving Castle' by Diana Wynne Jones.

Delve into the captivating realms of fantasy with these timeless and modern classics that have captivated the imaginations of middle grade readers for decades. From beloved British authors to award-winning American storytellers, these novels weave enchanting tales of magic, mythical creatures, and epic quests that will transport your preteen to new worlds and spark a lifelong love of reading.

The collection

  1. Howl's Moving Castle

    Diana Wynne Jones

    Imagination, humor, and intricate plotting make this classic fantasy a must-have for fans of the genre.

    For: Fantasy readers of all ages, especially those who enjoy complex storytelling and magical worlds.

    Strengths: Diana Wynne Jones' signature blend of wit, charm, and inventive world-building keeps readers engaged.

    Skip if: Some may struggle with the initially slow pace and multiple characters, requiring patience and close attention.

    Comparable: Readers who appreciate books like The Phantom Tollbooth, The Neverending Story, or The Princess Bride will enjoy Howl's Moving Castle's unique blend of fantasy and humor.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Howl's Moving Castle Collector’s Deluxe Edition

    Diana Wynne Jones

    This deluxe edition of Howl's Moving Castle is a must-have for fans of Diana Wynne Jones's imaginative storytelling and Hayao Miyazaki's iconic film adaptation.

    For: Middle grade and adult readers who enjoy classic fantasy with humor, adventure, and memorable characters.

    Strengths: Unique blend of magic, humor, and heart, with a strong, clever heroine and vivid world-building.

    Skip if: Some readers may find Sophie's transformation into an elderly woman initially unsettling.

    Comparable: Fans of classic fantasy series like The Chronicles of Narnia and The Phantom Tollbooth will appreciate Howl's Moving Castle's imaginative storytelling and whimsical tone.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe Deluxe Collector’s Edition

    C. S. Lewis

    A timeless classic of epic fantasy, The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe transports readers to the magical land of Narnia, where themes of sacrifice, redemption, and the struggle between good and evil come alive.

    For: Readers of all ages who appreciate classic fantasy and are ready to embark on an imaginative journey.

    Strengths: Richly detailed world-building, memorable characters, and a compelling narrative that balances adventure with moral depth.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the Christian allegory and symbolism dense or heavy-handed, requiring close attention to fully appreciate.

    Comparable: Compared to other fantasy classics, The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe shares similarities with works like J.R.R. Tolkien's The Hobbit and George R.R. Martin's A Game of Thrones in its epic scope, mythic themes, and memorable characters.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Holes

    Louis Sachar

    This imaginative tale of crime, punishment, and redemption stands out with its unique blend of dark humor, quirky characters, and thought-provoking themes, earning it numerous prestigious awards.

    For: Middle-grade readers and adults seeking a compelling, layered story.

    Strengths: Inventive plot, memorable characters, and a richly woven narrative that balances humor and seriousness.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing slow or the magical realism confusing.

    Comparable: Fans of Neil Gaiman's Coraline or Roald Dahl's works will appreciate Holes' blend of humor, adventure, and moral depth.

    “A dazzling blend of social commentary, tall tale and magic realism.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

Are these books suitable for advanced or struggling readers in the middle grade age range?
Yes, the reading levels and themes in these novels cater to a range of abilities and interests within the middle grade demographic.
Can I expect these books to have illustrations or graphic elements?
Some of the books in this collection feature illustrations or deluxe collector's editions with enhanced artwork, while others are pure prose.
Do these novels focus primarily on magic and adventure or also deal with more serious themes?
While all offer exciting fantasy elements, some books in this collection also explore deeper themes like friendship, morality, and social issues, making for well-rounded, thought-provoking reads.
