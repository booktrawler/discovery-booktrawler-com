Must-Read Nonfiction for Pet Lovers

For a deeper understanding of our furry and feathered friends, explore these insightful nonfiction books.

Who these books are for

  • Pet owners seeking a deeper understanding of animal behavior and emotions
  • Animal lovers looking for engaging nonfiction to broaden their knowledge
  • Researchers, students, and professionals in fields related to animal studies

Start here

If you only read one book, make it 'Crouton' by Kristine A. Lombardi – a heartwarming exploration of the bond between humans and pets.

For those who cherish their animal companions, nonfiction books offer a rich tapestry of knowledge, heartwarming stories, and profound insights into the human-animal bond. This carefully selected collection brings together titles that delve into the fascinating world of animal behavior, explore the emotional connections we share with our pets, and celebrate the joy and companionship they bring to our lives.

The collection

  1. The Skeleton and the Cat

    Brandon James Scott

    This charming early reader showcases the power of unexpected friendship through its delightful, expressive illustrations and humorous storytelling.

    For: Children aged 4-8, as well as cat lovers and those who appreciate whimsical tales of companionship.

    Strengths: The book excels in its ability to balance humor and tenderness, creating an endearing and relatable friendship between the skeleton and cat. The digital illustrations are vibrant and rich in emotional detail.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the text's straightforward tone and simple sentences a bit unadventurous compared to more complex picture books.

    Comparable: Fans of Mo Willems' Elephant and Piggie series and classic tales like Frog and Toad will appreciate the similar buddy story dynamic and humorous tone in The Skeleton and the Cat.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Vampurr

    Justin Colón

    Vampurr is a delightful, spooky-sweet story about acceptance and finding your forever home, paired with adorable illustrations.

    For: Children aged 4-8, animal lovers, and parents seeking Halloween-themed picture books with heart.

    Strengths: This book charms with its unique vampire kitten protagonist, playful text, and endearing artwork. It tackles themes of self-acceptance and family in a lighthearted way.

    Skip if: Some may find the pacing a touch slow, as the story meanders through Vampurr's unsuccessful adoption attempts before finally settling on her perfect match.

    Comparable: Fans of quirky, humorous animal tales like 'Creepy Crayon!' will appreciate Vampurr's similar blend of spookiness and warmth, making it a great addition to Halloween-themed collections.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. I Really Want to Be a Cat

    Helen Hancocks

    A playful exploration of the joys and 'cons' of being a cat, with lively illustrations and a relatable protagonist.

    For: Children aged 4-8 who love cats, and parents looking for a lighthearted story time read.

    Strengths: Colorful, expressive artwork brings the cat's-eye view to life, while the simple narrative flows well for young readers.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the ending a bit abrupt or the humor not to their taste.

    Comparable: Fans of animal-centric picture books like 'Z is for Moose' or 'The Cat in the Hat' will appreciate this feline-focused tale.

    “An upbeat, happy read... Read this to the cat lover who is sure to recognize the many universal kitty poses and mannerisms.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. What a Small Cat Needs

    Natalia Shaloshvili

    This charming picture book celebrates the universal need for love and connection, inviting young readers to identify with a adorable small cat's journey to find warmth and comfort in the arms of a human.

    For: Children aged 2-6, parents, educators, and cat lovers.

    Strengths: Beautifully paced, tender storytelling with emotionally astute moments, paired with warm, funny, and relatable illustrations of high artistic quality that will resonate with children.

    Skip if: While the book's simple, repetitive text makes it accessible for early readers, some may find the lack of a more complex storyline a slight weakness.

    Comparable: Similar in tone and style to other Enchanted Lion picture books like 'The Gardener' and 'The House in the Night', 'What a Small Cat Needs' shares a warm, lyrical storytelling approach and exquisite illustrations.

    “I love this thoughtful, sweet picture book featuring a little cat celebrating all the little things it needs, especially the warm hands and gentle heart of a human.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Ruthie

    Esmé Shapiro

    Meet Ruthie, a pampered royal pup who's grown weary of castle comforts. When a chance to explore beyond the walls arises, Ruthie discovers the great outdoors isn't all it's cracked up to be - especially when you're cold, hungry, and downright stinky! A hilarious and heartfelt adventure ensues as Ruthie navigates the woods, helps from his new forest friends, and ultimately learns to appreciate home sweet home again. With delightful illustrations and a side-splitting story, Ruthie is a royally entertaining read for anyone who's ever wondered what lies beyond the comfort of familiar surroundings.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Dog Who Was (Almost) Perfect

    Jack Kurland

    Meet Doris, the dog who was nearly perfect in every way - until a wobble in one of her tricks shakes her confidence. This delightful tale, for kids and the kid in all of us, gently nudges readers to embrace imperfection and understand that making mistakes is part of the journey. With its warm humor and heart, The Dog Who Was (Almost) Perfect reassures us that being loved, flaws and all, is what truly matters.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Crouton One Cat's Adoption Tale

    Kristine A. Lombardi

    A heartwarming, relatable pet adoption tale told from the shelter cat's perspective, perfect for cat lovers and those who've adopted a pet.

    For: Children aged 4-8, cat lovers, families, and anyone who's adopted a pet.

    Strengths: Playful storytelling, adorable illustrations, and a sweet exploration of companionship and belonging.

    Skip if: The simple, straightforward narrative may appeal more to younger readers.

    Comparable: Fans of 'Because of Winn-Dixie' or 'The One and Only Ivan' will appreciate this lighthearted, pet-centric story.

    “With warmth and sincerity, Lombardi touches on themes of patience, companionship, and belonging....An especially sweet pet adoption tale.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Milton Makes a Move

    Jonathan Graziano

    Meet Milton, a curious and adventurous pug who's about to embark on the biggest move of his life - to his forever home with a new human, Jonathan. Join this charming duo as they navigate new territories, both physically and emotionally, in this heartwarming tale of bravery and friendship. Brought to life by the creator of the viral "Bones or No Bones" TikTok videos, Milton Makes a Move is a delightful picture book that celebrates the power of embracing change and making room for love in our lives.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

The collection spans various aspects of the human-animal relationship, including animal behavior, emotional connections, and personal pet stories.
Are these books suitable for readers of all ages?
While geared towards adults, some titles may appeal to mature teenagers and younger readers with a strong interest in animals.
Can I find fiction books about pets on this page?
No, this curated selection exclusively features nonfiction titles for a factual, informative reading experience
