12 Must-Read Fantasy Novels for Curious Young Minds

Embark on thrilling adventures and broaden your imagination with these top fantasy books for kids and tweens.

Who these books are for

  • Kids aged 8-12 looking for exciting stories
  • Tweens interested in fantasy and adventure
  • Parents seeking books to spark their child's imagination

Start here

If you only read one fantasy novel with your child, make it 'Alice with a Why' – its clever wordplay and imaginative world-building will captivate both of you.

Imagination knows no bounds in these captivating fantasy novels, perfect for curious young minds eager to explore enchanting worlds, encounter magical creatures, and learn valuable lessons about courage, friendship, and growing up. From beloved classics to modern favorites, these timeless tales of wonder will transport you and your child on unforgettable adventures.

The collection

  1. Each and Every Spark

    Claire Swinarski

    In this engrossing, intertwined tale, 1940s Paris under Nazi rule meets modern-day Paris as two teenage girls, one desperate to join the French Resistance, the other struggling to belong in a new city, find connection through a mysterious painting. Perfect for fans of historical thrillers and stories of youthful courage, Each and Every Spark weaves a timely narrative of resilience and hope across generations.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. James and the Giant Peach

    Roald Dahl

    This whimsical tale of a young boy's giant peach adventure captures the imagination and delivers Dahl's signature blend of humor, adventure, and poignant themes.

    For: Children aged 8-12 and parents who appreciate classic storytelling.

    Strengths: Vivid world-building, memorable characters, and a compelling narrative that balances humor and emotion.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow compared to Dahl's other works.

    Comparable: Fans of Roald Dahl's other children's classics like Charlie and the Chocolate Factory will enjoy this early example of his unique storytelling style.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Little Women

    Louisa May Alcott

    Join the beloved March sisters - independent Jo, gentle Beth, fashionable Meg, and artistic Amy - as they navigate sibling rivalry, first loves, and the challenges of living during the Civil War era. With heartwarming scenes of sisterly devotion and poignant moments of hardship, Louisa May Alcott's timeless novel explores the joys and sorrows of growing up. Illustrated with beauty by Anna Bond's Rifle Paper Co. designs, this deluxe hardcover edition invites readers of all ages to fall in love with Little Women anew.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. A Wrinkle in Time (Newbery Medal Winner)

    Madeleine L'Engle

    A Wrinkle in Time is a timeless science fiction and fantasy classic that has captivated readers for over 60 years with its imaginative storytelling and exploration of complex themes.

    For: Middle grade readers, science fiction and fantasy enthusiasts, and anyone drawn to thought-provoking stories with memorable characters.

    Strengths: Introduces readers to unique sci-fi concepts, such as tesseracts and the 'dark force,' in an accessible way; explores themes of family, friendship, and self-discovery.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the writing style and pacing a bit slow and dense.

    Comparable: Fans of classic science fiction and fantasy may also enjoy other Newbery Medal winners like Island of the Blue Dolphins or The Giver, which share a similar blend of imaginative storytelling and literary depth.

    “This imaginative book will be read for a long time into the future.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Chronicles of Whetherwhy: The Season of Flames

    Anna James

    In the captivating sequel, Chronicles of Whetherwhy: The Season of Flames, young hero Elio embarks on a perilous quest to protect the magical balance of his enchanted island. Leaving behind the wonders of the nomadic Circus Astra, Elio must navigate treacherous politics, forge unexpected alliances, and outwit formidable foes as he races against time to restore harmony to his world. A thrilling, heartwarming adventure full of mesmerizing magic and stirring messages about the power of friendship and acceptance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Alice with a Why

    Anna James

    This imaginative retelling of Alice in Wonderland offers a fresh perspective while still honoring the original's whimsy and logic.

    For: Fans of classic fantasy, particularly those who love Lewis Carroll's work, as well as readers looking for a delightful, imaginative adventure.

    Strengths: Clever reinterpretation of Wonderland's characters and politics, engaging modern narrative voice, and beautiful black-and-white illustrations.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow as James takes time to establish Wonderland's new conflicts and characters.

    Comparable: Compared to other reimaginings of Alice, this stands out for its unique blend of humor, social commentary, and heartfelt family drama.

    “The Things You Need To Know Before We Begin”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Magic Tree House Fact Tracker Graphic Novel: Titanic

    Mary Pope Osborne

    Dive into the story of the Titanic with Jack and Annie from the Magic Tree House in this nonfiction graphic novel. Through their eyes, explore life on the ship, the tragic events of that fateful night, and the aftermath. With updated facts and fun tidbits, this engaging format brings history to life, perfectly complementing your favorite Magic Tree House adventures.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Percy Jackson and the Olympians, Book One: The Lightning Thief

    Rick Riordan

    This book brings Greek mythology to life in an engaging, fast-paced adventure that will captivate readers of all ages.

    For: Middle grade readers and fans of mythology, fantasy, and adventure.

    Strengths: Richly detailed world-building, relatable protagonist, and clever twists on classic myths make this a standout series starter.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the large cast of characters and mythological references overwhelming at first.

    Comparable: For fans of mythology-infused storytelling, compare to other Rick Riordan series like The Heroes of Olympus and Magnus Chase.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. The Unchosen One

    Amy Sparkes

    Unleash the laughs and magic with The Unchosen One! In this delightful romp, prophecies are turned upside down when the reluctant heroine Tassie decides to strike back against her destined doom. Joining forces with her wisecracking griffin friend Spin, they embark on a hilarious, action-packed quest to defy fate and find a brighter adventure. Tongue firmly in cheek, this book is a delightful twist on traditional Chosen One tales that will leave you grinning from ear to ear.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Thunder and Mercy A Novel

    Jennifer Robin Barr

    Thunder and Mercy is a captivating mystery that seamlessly weaves together historical espionage during the American Revolution with a modern-day adventure, all through the lens of a relatable and spirited young protagonist.

    For: Middle grade readers who enjoy historical fiction, puzzles, and coming-of-age stories will devour this immersive novel.

    Strengths: Barr expertly blends genres, crafting a suspenseful plot filled with well-developed characters, historical details, and clever codes to decipher. The dual timeline adds depth and complexity to the narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the historical context and Revolutionary War specifics dense and require extra background knowledge to fully appreciate the story's nuances.

    Comparable: Fans of 'The Westing Game' by Ellen Raskin and 'The Mysterious Benedict Society' by Trenton Lee Stewart will appreciate the puzzle-solving and historical elements in Thunder and Mercy.

    “This novel is that killer combination of a dazzling plot with characters who are rich and real and unforgettable.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Traitor's Gambit

    Amanda Foody

    In the heart-pounding fifth installment of the Wilderlore series, Barclay Thorn must confront his darkest fears and unlock the true power of his wild Lore to save the Jungle from Audrian Keyes' malevolent grasp. With unorthodox allies and a desperate quest ahead, this gripping tale of friendship, sacrifice, and self-discovery will keep you on the edge of your seat until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Odyssey A Modern Retelling

    Liv Albert

    Embark on an epic, action-packed adventure as Liv Albert reimagines Homer's timeless classic in this stunning middle-grade retelling of The Odyssey. With striking illustrations and rich historical details, meet a complex Odysseus tested by monsters, gods, and his own flaws. Packed with mythic facts and fascinating insights, this thrilling journey brings ancient Greece to vivid life - the perfect gift for young fans of history and legend alike.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What age range are these fantasy novels suitable for?
Most are geared towards kids and tweens aged 8-14, although some older readers may also enjoy them.
Are all the books in this collection part of a series?
No, while some have sequels, each title stands alone as a complete story.
Can these fantasy novels be used to teach literary skills?
Absolutely – these engaging stories incorporate rich vocabulary, character development, and plot structure to enhance reading comprehension and critical thinking.
