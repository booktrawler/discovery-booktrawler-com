Whimsical and Imaginative Nonfiction for Adults and Children

Explore fantastical worlds and unconventional topics through these seven offbeat books.

Who these books are for

  • Children aged 4-12 looking for quirky reading material
  • Adults seeking nonfiction with a whimsical twist
  • Gift-givers for book lovers with eclectic tastes

Start here

If you only read one unconventional nonfiction book, choose 'The Underwearwolf' by Gideon Sterer for its unique blend of facts and fantasy.

Imagination meets fact in this collection of seven unconventional nonfiction books. From a moon landing guide to a mythological creature's belly, these titles blend the surreal with the informative. Whether for yourself or a book-loving child, these whimsical reads offer an offbeat way to engage with the world.

The collection

  1. The Cow in the Dark at Night

    Jess Hannigan

    A delightfully absurd tale of a curious cow's nighttime escapades, paired with Jess Hannigan's signature bold, graphic illustrations, makes this a standout picture book.

    For: Children aged 4-8, fans of offbeat humor and graphic art, and parents seeking a unique storytime experience.

    Strengths: Hannigan's vibrant, minimalist artwork and witty text perfectly capture the cow's misadventures in a way that will have young readers giggling and engaged.

    Skip if: Some may find the cow's antics a bit too silly or the story a tad meandering.

    Comparable: For fans of Jon Klassen's deadpan humor and X. Fang's whimsical illustrations, The Cow in the Dark at Night offers a similarly playful take on a curious animal's nighttime explorations.

    “Bold, flat compositions—heavy on saturated greens, oranges, and purples, with a crisp, cut-paper sensibility—match the text”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Life on the Moon

    Matthew Swanson

    Join 12-year-old Leo on an unforgettable journey to the moon, where he uncovers a shocking secret: life exists on the lunar surface, but it's being kept hidden from Earth. Alone and determined to find his missing father, Leo discovers a world of extraordinary creatures, but also danger and deception. A thrilling and timely tale of courage, curiosity, and standing up for the truth, even when no one else wants to believe it.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. You Are Now Old Enough to Hear This

    Aaron Starmer

    Unravel the twisted threads of family legends and magical secrets in this enchanting middle-grade speculative novel. When Roman's eccentric grandfather dies, he must clear out the house and uncover the truth behind bizarre tales like the Toe Beast. But the more he digs, the more he discovers, revealing extraordinary secrets from the past that challenge everything he thought he knew about his peculiar family. A thrilling journey of discovery through the power of storytelling, perfect for young readers of imaginative fare.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Room on the Broom Special Edition

    Julia Donaldson

    Room on the Broom Special Edition is a delightful, engaging read-aloud that combines imaginative storytelling with fun, festive illustrations, making it an excellent choice for Halloween celebrations.

    For: This book is perfect for families to enjoy together, especially children aged 3-7.

    Strengths: The rhyming text and playful illustrations bring the story to life, while the themes of friendship and resourcefulness make it an enjoyable and educational read.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the repetitive rhymes and simple language a bit predictable and lacking in complexity.

    Comparable: Fans of Julia Donaldson's work, such as The Gruffalo and Stick Man, will likely appreciate the similar blend of whimsy and clever wordplay found in Room on the Broom Special Edition.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Wolf's Belly

    John August

    Wolf's Belly is a captivating, imaginative reworking of Little Red Riding Hood that delves into the wolf's belly to uncover a rich, magical world and a heroine's journey to unite its inhabitants.

    For: Readers of all ages who appreciate clever, layered storytelling and are looking for a fresh take on classic fairy tales.

    Strengths: This graphic novel excels at world-building, character development, and thematic depth, balancing humor and metaphor to create a compelling narrative.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the complex, fractured fairy tale structure and multiple storylines initially disorienting.

    Comparable: Fans of inventive, genre-bending graphic novels like Lightfall and This Was Our Pact will appreciate Wolf's Belly's creative approach to beloved stories.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Underwearwolf

    Gideon Sterer

    This humorous picture book combines relatable kid humor with a fun, fantastical twist, making it a standout in its genre.

    For: Recommended for ages 4-8 who enjoy playful, imaginative stories with a touch of silliness.

    Strengths: Expertly balances clever wordplay and visual humor with a simple yet engaging storyline, showcasing both author and illustrator's talents.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the repetitive warnings about wearing the underwear under the full moon a bit overplayed.

    Comparable: Fans of quirky, lighthearted picture books like 'Creepy Pair of Underwear' will appreciate 'The Underwearwolf's' similar blend of kid-friendly humor and whimsical art.

    “Best read beneath a gibbous moon to truly make your intended audience howl…with laughter.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Judgy Bunny and the Terrible Beach

    Scott Rothman

    This humorous picture book cleverly conveys a positive message about being open to new experiences, making it an engaging and memorable read.

    For: Children aged 4-8 and their parents, caregivers, or educators seeking a lighthearted story with a valuable lesson.

    Strengths: Judgy Bunny's relatable grumpiness and the story's unexpected twists make it an entertaining read, while the colorful illustrations add to the book's appeal.

    Skip if: The narrative can feel a bit repetitive at times as Judgy Bunny consistently finds fault in each beach activity.

    Comparable: Fans of humorous, character-driven picture books with a touch of satire, such as Don't Let the Pigeon Drive the Bus! or The Stinky Cheese Man, will likely enjoy Judgy Bunny's antics.

    “The message to find joy where you can is couched by sardonic humor and buoyant art, limiting a didactic moral to the story.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org
