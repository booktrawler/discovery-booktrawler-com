Magical Coming-of-Age Novels for Middle Grade Readers

Immerse your young bookworm in the enchanting stories of a group of young heroes discovering their magical talents and forging their paths to adulthood.

Who these books are for

  • Middle grade readers aged 8-12
  • Parents looking for inspiring books for their children
  • Teachers seeking enriching classroom reads

Start here

If you only read one magical coming-of-age novel, choose 'Kiki's Delivery Service: Deluxe Edition' for its timeless charm and relatable heroine.

Embark on a captivating journey with this curated collection of magical coming-of-age novels. These timeless tales follow a group of young protagonists as they navigate the challenges of adolescence, discover their unique abilities, and learn valuable life lessons. From the whimsical charm of 'Kiki and the New Magic' to the poignant storytelling of 'The Second Life of Snap', these books will transport you to fantastical worlds and remind you of the magic that lies within every young spirit.

The collection

  1. The Second Life of Snap

    Erin Entrada Kelly

    A gripping dystopian survival story with relatable themes and compelling characters, powered by a resourceful girl and her robot companion.

    For: Upper elementary and middle school readers looking for a thought-provoking, character-driven tale with timely themes.

    Strengths: Authentic portrayal of a near-future shaped by climate change, and the bonds of friendship and community in the face of adversity.

    Skip if: Emotionally charged and intense, this may be a challenging read for some due to its somber tone and themes of mortality.

    Comparable: Fans of 'The Wild Robot' and 'A Rover's Story' will appreciate this stand-alone novel's heartwarming and suspenseful blend of human and artificial intelligence.

    “Set in a convincing near-future shaped by climate change, two-time Newbery Medalist Kelly”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Pokécology An Illustrated Guide to Pokémon Ecology Pikachu Press (Edited and translated by)

    Pikachu Press

    This meticulously researched guide offers a unique blend of science and fantasy, providing a fascinating new perspective on Pokémon biology and behavior.

    For: Pokémon enthusiasts, educators, and anyone curious about the intricate world of these beloved creatures.

    Strengths: Richly detailed, scientifically grounded exploration of Pokémon ecology, enhanced by stunning illustrations and clear explanations from experts in the field.

    Skip if: Technical language and dense, academic tone may make it challenging for casual readers to follow without prior knowledge of ecology and biology.

    Comparable: Compared to other Pokémon books, this stands out for its emphasis on scientific accuracy and in-depth analysis, appealing to fans looking for a more cerebral approach.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. The Tea Dragon Society

    K. O'Neill

    The Tea Dragon Society wins over readers with its charming, whimsical world of tea dragons and the heartfelt story of a blacksmith apprentice learning to care for these magical creatures.

    For: Fans of gentle, all-ages fantasy and those who appreciate stories about friendship, craft, and inclusivity will delight in this book.

    Strengths: O'Neill's warm, expressive artwork and thoughtful pacing make this a delightful read, while the unique premise and diverse cast of characters add to its charm.

    Skip if: Some may find the slow pace and focus on tea dragon care a bit quiet or slow to develop plot.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy Princess Princess Ever After and other works by K. O'Neill will appreciate the similar blend of whimsy, inclusivity, and heartfelt storytelling in The Tea Dragon Society.

    “A gentle fantasy perfect for introducing young readers to graphic novels.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Kiki and the New Magic

    Eiko Kadono

    Kiki and the New Magic is a captivating continuation of the beloved Kiki's story, exploring themes of self-discovery, friendship, and the challenges of growing up.

    For: Middle-grade readers and fans of the Studio Ghibli film will particularly enjoy this enchanting tale of a young witch navigating the complexities of fame and adolescence.

    Strengths: The book shines in its authentic portrayal of Kiki's inner struggles and relationships, making her a relatable and endearing protagonist.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit slow, as the story takes time to delve into Kiki's emotional journey.

    Comparable: Those who appreciate magical realism and coming-of-age stories set in imaginative worlds will enjoy this sequel alongside other classics like

    “Bright and quirky; offers an affectionate portrayal of adolescent change.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. Kiki's Delivery Service: Deluxe Edition The classic that inspired the beloved animated film

    Eiko Kadono

    This timeless tale of self-discovery, friendship, and finding one's place in the world has captivated readers for generations with its relatable heroine, charming characters, and poignant themes.

    For: Middle-grade readers and adults alike who appreciate gentle, character-driven stories with a touch of magic and whimsy.

    Strengths: Kiki's journey is authentic and endearing, with vivid character development and a richly detailed setting that brings the seaside town of Koriko to life.

    Skip if: Some readers may find Kiki's initial struggles with making friends and establishing her business a tad slow or meandering.

    Comparable: Fans of Hayao Miyazaki's Studio Ghibli films and readers of other classic coming-of-age tales like Anne of Green Gables will appreciate this charming, character-focused story.

Frequently asked questions

What age range are these books suitable for?
The novels in this collection are primarily aimed at middle grade readers aged 8-12.
Do the stories focus solely on magic?
While magic plays a significant role, these books also explore themes of friendship, family, identity, and self-discovery.
Can adults appreciate these tales as well?
Absolutely! The stories are crafted with depth and nuance, making them enjoyable for readers of all ages.
