Sports in Graphic Novels for Middle Grade Readers

Explore the intersection of sports and graphic novels with these engaging titles for middle grade readers.

Who these books are for

  • Middle grade readers (ages 8-12) interested in sports and graphic novels
  • Parents and educators seeking diverse, engaging books for reluctant readers

Start here

If you only read one sports-themed graphic novel, choose 'Offside: A Graphic Novel' by Christina Diaz Gonzalez for its compelling soccer storyline and authentic Latinx characters.

Graphic novels offer an exciting way to combine athletic pursuits with engaging storytelling and dynamic illustrations. This curated list of sports-themed graphic novels for middle grade readers showcases diverse protagonists, thrilling competitions, and relatable coming-of-age themes. From soccer to basketball, these titles will captivate young sports enthusiasts and reluctant readers alike, providing a unique reading experience that blends action, humor, and heart.

The collection

  1. The Crossover A Newbery Award Winner

    Kwame Alexander

    Experience the gritty, inspirational tale of Josh and Jordan Bell, twin brothers dominating the basketball court while navigating the complex challenges of growing up. With a Newbery Medal and Coretta Scott King Award under its belt, Kwame Alexander's novel-in-verse "The Crossover" delivers a heart-pounding, deeply moving story of family, brotherhood, and the high-stakes game of life. Don't miss this slam-dunk masterpiece that's a must-read for anyone who's ever grappled with the pressure to succeed and the power of finding your own rhythm.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. One Life: Young Readers Edition

    Megan Rapinoe

    Meet trailblazing soccer star Megan Rapinoe in this inspiring young readers' adaptation of her memoir! Through her own compelling stories, Megan shows how every person, even kids, can make a difference in the fight for equality and justice. From her early days playing soccer and advocating for social change to her high-profile activism as a professional athlete, Megan reveals the power of speaking up and taking action, empowering young readers to get involved and create positive change in their communities.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Offside: A Graphic Novel

    Christina Diaz Gonzalez

    This graphic novel stands out for its creative blend of soccer action, character-driven storytelling, and exploration of themes like equality, community, and teamwork.

    For: Middle grade readers, especially soccer fans, will appreciate the relatable characters, engaging plot, and inspiring message.

    Strengths: Inventive visual storytelling, diverse and well-developed characters, and a compelling narrative that balances sports action with timely social themes.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the storyline a bit predictable, relying on familiar sports drama tropes.

    Comparable: Fans of sports-themed graphic novels like Raina Telgemeier's 'Smile' and 'Drama' will enjoy 'Offside's' mix of humor, heart, and athletic excitement.

    “Truly unique... An entertaining and spirited reading experience.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Separate Is Never Equal Sylvia Mendez and Her Family's Fight for Desegregation

    Duncan Tonatiuh

    "In this powerful picture book, award-winning author-illustrator Duncan Tonatiuh sheds light on a pivotal yet overlooked chapter in American civil rights history. Before Brown v. Board of Education, the Mendez family courageously challenged California's segregation laws, paving the way for desegregation years ahead of the national norm. A masterful work of text and art, Separate Is Never Equal makes an important and inspiring story accessible to younger readers, offering a relevant reminder of the enduring power of standing up for equality."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. El Deafo

    Cece Bell

    In this heartwarming and relatable graphic novel, Cece Bell recounts her journey of self-discovery as a student with hearing loss and a powerful new hearing aid. With humor and poignancy, she chronicles her transformation into superhero "El Deafo," a powerful listener, but also her struggles to find friendship and belonging in a new school, bringing readers on an emotionally resonant quest for connection and understanding.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Where Ella Went

    Laurie Morrison

    In this clever, multimedia whodunit, eighth-graders Sadie and Pug team up to solve the mystery of their beloved soccer captain Ella's sudden disappearance. As they dig deeper into Ella's life, they uncover secrets that reveal her seemingly perfect world wasn't as flawless as it seemed. With the end of the soccer season on the line, Sadie and Pug must decide if risking it all to find the truth is worth the risk of potentially losing their friend forever. A funny, absorbing mystery that explores friendship, teamwork, and the pressures of middle school.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Saving the Team

    Alex Morgan

    Alex Morgan's inspiring true story and authentic voice shine through in this engaging, relatable novel about perseverance and teamwork, making it a must-have for soccer enthusiasts and young readers alike.

    For: Middle grade readers, especially girls, who love sports, friendship stories, and coming-of-age themes.

    Strengths: Authentic portrayal of a young athlete's journey, engaging characters, and a compelling team dynamics storyline that promotes resilience and camaraderie.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the soccer details and terminology overwhelming if they aren't familiar with the sport.

    Comparable: Fans of sports-themed novels like 'The Crossover' by Kwame Alexander and 'The Mighty Miss Addis Ababa Girls' by Naomi Shihab Nye will appreciate the realistic portrayal of a young athlete's experiences.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Academy VI: Rise of the Scorpions

    T Z Layton

    In the thrilling sixth installment of The Academy series, up-and-coming soccer star Leo K. Doyle faces his toughest season yet as he navigates a ruthless new rival team, the Southwark F.C. Scorpions, while dealing with personal challenges and an intense race for the title. With its inspiring tale of perseverance, friendship, and the unbreakable spirit of youth athletes, The Academy VI: Rise of the Scorpions is a must-read for young soccer fans and anyone who loves a gripping underdog story.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Ghost

    Jason Reynolds

    Ghost is a powerful coming-of-age story that explores themes of trauma, identity, and the transformative power of sports, all told through vivid prose and relatable characters.

    For: Teenagers, especially those interested in sports and stories about overcoming adversity.

    Strengths: Jason Reynolds' lyrical writing, the dynamic and diverse cast of characters, and the authentic portrayal of the challenges faced by urban youth make Ghost a standout in contemporary YA literature.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the non-linear narrative structure and poetic style challenging to follow at times.

    Comparable: Fans of realistic fiction and sports-themed novels like The Crossover and Hoops by Walter Dean Myers, or more recently, Long Way Down by Jason Reynolds himself, will appreciate Ghost's emotional depth and athletic action.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Opting Out

    Maia Kobabe

    Join Saachi as she navigates the complexities of adolescence, rebelling against binary gender norms, and forging her own identity. With humor, heart, and expert illustration, this coming-of-age tale explores themes of friendship, self-discovery, and embracing one's individuality, offering a relatable and inspiring story for any young person questioning societal expectations.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Offside: A Graphic Novel

    Christina Diaz Gonzalez

    A heartwarming graphic novel about soccer, friendship, and teamwork, with relatable characters and a layered exploration of access, recognition, and equality.

    For: Middle-grade readers who enjoy sports, coming-of-age stories, and diverse characters.

    Strengths: Engaging storytelling, inventive visual design, and a message about inclusivity and the power of collaboration.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the soccer specifics and team dynamics less accessible if they're not familiar with the sport.

    Comparable: Fans of Raina Telgemeier's graphic novels and stories about sports, friendship, and social issues will appreciate Offside.

    “Truly unique... An entertaining and spirited reading experience.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. The Endless Game

    J.D. Amato

    In this "thrilling... often profound" graphic novel debut, Fred Townsend discovers Lakeside's intense summer game of capture the flag, passed down through generations, where alliances are forged and friendships tested as the entire town is divided. Perfect for fans of The Cardboard Kingdom and Craig of the Creek, The Endless Game delves into the power of community, individual identity, and unforgettable summers that shape us forever.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  13. The Twin Stars and the Soccer Superstar A Novel

    Kristine Rudolph

    A heartwarming and suspenseful story that skillfully weaves together themes of soccer, grief, and self-discovery, set against the vibrant backdrop of Texas Hill Country.

    For: Middle-grade readers and soccer enthusiasts looking for a relatable, character-driven mystery with an emotional depth charge.

    Strengths: The Twin Stars and the Soccer Superstar excels at crafting authentic, multidimensional young protagonists and their relationships, while using the sport as a rich metaphor for life's challenges and triumphs.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a bit uneven, with the mystery unfolding at a slower pace than the character development.

    Comparable: Fans of character-driven sports stories like Up for Air and Where Ella Went will resonate with this novel's focus on the emotional journeys of its young characters.

    “The Twin Stars and the Soccer Superstar is a beautifully written story about the lengths to which we will go to process grief, and the weight it carries in all that we do.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org
