12 Incredible Fantasy and Adventure Books for Young Readers

Immerse your child in magical worlds with these unforgettable tales of creatures, quests, and self-discovery.

Who these books are for

  • Children aged 8-14
  • Parents and educators seeking inspiring books
  • Fantasy and adventure enthusiasts

Start here

If you only read one book about incredible creatures, choose Katherine Rundell's 'Impossible Creatures'.

Embark on thrilling escapades and make new friends in these captivating fantasy and adventure novels tailored for young readers. From mythical creatures to brave quests, these stories will ignite your child's imagination and sense of wonder. Dive into the enchanting worlds within and discover why these timeless tales have stood the test of time.

The collection

  1. Lightfall: A Place Between A Graphic Novel

    Tim Probert

    Embark on the next thrilling chapter of the Lightfall series! When Cad and Bea find themselves in the mystical realm between worlds, they must unravel the secrets behind their quest to restore light to their dark planet. Packed with adventure, ancient magic, and shocking revelations, Lightfall: A Place Between is perfect for fans of Amulet and Avatar, with its richly imagined world and unforgettable heroes.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Impossible Creatures

    Katherine Rundell

    Impossible Creatures is a captivating fantasy adventure that seamlessly blends imagination, intelligence, and heart, with a richly detailed world and memorable characters.

    For: Middle grade readers who love epic fantasies with magical creatures and high-stakes quests will devour this book.

    Strengths: Rundell's world-building is unparalleled, crafting a lush, immersive Archipelago teeming with imaginative creatures. The characters of Christopher and Mal are well-developed and relatable, driving the compelling narrative forward.

    Skip if: The complex world-building and intricate plot may make it challenging for some younger or less experienced readers to fully absorb.

    Comparable: Fans of Philip Pullman's His Dark Materials series, Rick Riordan's Percy Jackson, and classic fantasy tales like The Neverending Story will find much to appreciate in Impossible Creatures.

    “A glorious fantasy adventure....Brimming with intelligence, inventiveness and generosity of heart.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Impossible Creatures

    Katherine Rundell

    Impossible Creatures is a captivating fantasy adventure with memorable characters, imaginative world-building, and timeless themes of friendship, courage, and the importance of preserving wildness.

    For: Middle grade and young adult readers who enjoy epic, imaginative storytelling with a strong sense of adventure and a touch of magic.

    Strengths: Vivid descriptions, a unique magical world, well-developed protagonists, and a compelling quest make this an engaging read.

    Skip if: The pacing can be slow in parts, and the story relies on some convenient plot devices.

    Comparable: Fans of authors like Rick Riordan, Philip Pullman, and J.R.R. Tolkien will appreciate Impossible Creatures' epic fantasy elements and richly imagined world.

    “A glorious fantasy adventure....Brimming with intelligence, inventiveness and generosity of heart.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Poisoned King

    Katherine Rundell

    This book delivers an immersive, thrilling fantasy adventure with memorable characters, vivid world-building, and an epic quest that will captivate readers of all ages.

    For: Fantasy enthusiasts, middle grade readers, and adults who enjoy imaginative storytelling.

    Strengths: Richly detailed, magical world; engaging characters; high-stakes plot with plenty of action, danger, and emotional resonance.

    Skip if: With its complex world, multiple protagonists, and intricate plot, this may be a slower, more challenging read for younger or less experienced fantasy fans.

    Comparable: Fans of J.R.R. Tolkien, Cressida Cowell, and other epic fantasy series for middle grade and young adult readers will find parallels in this immersive, mythic tale.

    “A spectacular return to a magical world.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Neverfear

    Katherine Rundell

    A thrilling third installment in the Impossible Creatures series, The Neverfear delivers non-stop action, heart-pounding suspense, and richly imagined magical world-building.

    For: Children and young adults who crave adventure fantasy with memorable characters and detailed settings.

    Strengths: Rundell's masterful pacing keeps readers on the edge of their seats, while her vivid descriptions of the fantastical Archipelago bring the story to life.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the complex mythology and numerous characters challenging to follow, requiring close attention to maintain the narrative thread.

    Comparable: Fans of series like Harry Potter, Percy Jackson, and The School for Good and Evil will enjoy the immersive fantasy world and heroic quests in The Neverfear.

    “This is a perfectly paced, mythology-laced tale of action, adventure, and heart....Brilliant.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. The Island at the Edge of Night

    Lucy Strange

    Step into Lucy Strange's eerie world where science and magic collide on a remote island school shrouded in secrets. Faye Fitzgerald, still haunted by the night that upended her life, uncovers a sinister web of truth and deception threatening the children and the enigmatic headmaster. As danger lurks in every shadow, Faye's connection to the island's mysterious heart, "The Knife," propels her into a perilous quest to unravel the mysteries of her botanist father's research and a murdered prince. With each step across the desolate landscape, Faye must confront her own darkness and the healing power of nature to survive. An enthralling mystery with all the hallmarks of a Queen of Gothic tale.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Griffin Speaker

    Jan M. Flynn

    Griffin Speaker delivers an immersive, high-stakes fantasy adventure with a compelling heroine, rich world-building, and a timely message about social inequality and the power of friendship and determination.

    For: Middle-grade readers looking for a thrilling, character-driven story with a strong female lead and fantastical creatures.

    Strengths: Engaging protagonist, vivid world-building, well-paced action, and a thoughtful exploration of class and privilege.

    Skip if: Some supporting characters feel underdeveloped.

    Comparable: Fans of series like Keeper of the Lost Cities, The School for Good and Evil, and The Girl Who Drank the Moon will enjoy Griffin Speaker's blend of fantasy, friendship, and coming-of-age themes.

    “A brave new take with fabulous animals, friendship, strong girls, and victory over a hard world. Readers of all ages will hunger for more. I think this is seriously cool!”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. The Eyes and the Impossible (Newbery Medal Winner)

    Dave Eggers

    Experience the world anew through the eyes of Johannes, a free-spirited dog who has made an urban park by the sea his home. With the help of his animal friends, Johannes observes the humans and creatures around him, ensuring harmony in the park until everything changes with the arrival of mysterious new buildings and a sudden influx of goats. This captivating tale, beautifully illustrated with Shawn Harris' artwork, explores themes of friendship, liberation, and seeing the world in a fresh light, perfect for readers of all ages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Unicornia: Camping in the Clouds

    Ana Punset

    Join Claudia and Pippa on an unforgettable, cloud-camping adventure filled with unicorn magic in this uplifting seventh installment of the series. With Sara grappling to overcome her fears, it's a thrilling mystery that will captivate chapter book readers as they soar through the sky alongside these endearing friends.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Lightmare

    Lauren Magaziner

    In Lauren Magaziner's gripping fantasy continuation, Lightmare, Fiora and her team of Thistles face their darkest challenges yet as they plot a desperate fight against sorcerer threats. But when shocking secrets divide the group, Fiora must grapple with the blurred lines between good and evil. A thrilling, thought-provoking read perfect for fans of immersive upper-middle-grade epic tales like Keeper of the Lost Cities and The Marvellers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. The Curse Breaker

    Jen Calonita

    In this thrilling sequel to The Curse of Calamity Quill, Everly "Benny" Benedict must navigate treacherous island secrets and a mysterious stranger to uncover the last piece of pirate treasure needed to break a centuries-old curse. With time running out and a friend's betrayal threatening everything, Benny embarks on a heart-pumping adventure to save her new friends and secure her inheritance. Jen Calonita's Isle of Ever series delivers pulse-pounding excitement and a rich mystery that will keep readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  12. Mythspeaker

    Christopher Roubique

    Join this unlikely band of teenage heroes as they embark on an epic quest to steal back the mythical Egg of the World Turtle from ruthless invaders. Inspired by Indigenous American folklore, Mythspeaker brings to life magical creatures, thrilling heists, and the power of teamwork and self-belief. With witty banter, heart-pounding action, and a hero's journey that will keep you on the edge of your seat, this vibrant fantasy adventure is perfect for fans of Race to the Sun and the Aru Shah series.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What age range is this collection targeted towards?
The books in this collection are suitable for children aged 8-14.
Are all the books part of a series?
No, this selection includes standalone novels and the first book in a series.
What genres are represented in this collection?
Fantasy, adventure, and mythical stories dominate this selection, with elements of magic realism appearing in a few titles.
Are there any books with representations of diverse protagonists?
Yes, several books feature protagonists from a range of ethnic and cultural backgrounds.
