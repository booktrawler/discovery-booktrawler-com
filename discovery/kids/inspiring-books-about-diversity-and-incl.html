Inspiring Books About Diversity and Inclusion for Kids

Discover uplifting stories that celebrate individuality and promote empathy in this curated collection of diverse children's books.

Who these books are for

  • Parents seeking diverse books for their kids
  • Teachers looking for inclusive classroom reads
  • Kids aged 4-12 seeking stories that reflect their experiences

Start here

If you only read one book about embracing differences, choose 'Wonder' by R.J. Palacio.

Reading diverse stories is crucial for fostering empathy, self-acceptance, and a richer understanding of the world. This carefully curated selection of books for kids celebrates individuality and promotes positive social change, making them perfect for home, classroom, or library shelves.

The collection

  1. New Kid A Graphic Novel

    Jerry Craft

    New Kid is a timely, honest, and relatable graphic novel about navigating identity, diversity, and systemic issues in middle school, earning its well-deserved awards and acclaim.

    For: Middle-grade readers, especially those of color, who can identify with Jordan's struggles and find solace in his resilience and humor.

    Strengths: Craft's authentic portrayal of microaggressions, socioeconomic disparities, and white privilege shines a light on crucial topics, while his engaging art and witty dialogue balance the heavier themes.

    Skip if: While the graphic novel effectively explores complex issues, some readers may find the pacing a bit slow or the emotional weight overwhelming at times.

    Comparable: Fans of realistic, character-driven middle-grade fiction and graphic novels like Raina Telgemeier's works will appreciate New Kid's genuine story and relatable protagonist.

    “Genuine characters propel this funny, warm, biting, fearless story. Entertaining and insightful, it will surely offer affirmation for some readers, revelation for others.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Planting Stories: The Life of Librarian and Storyteller Pura Belpré

    Anika Aldamuy Denise

    Meet Pura Belpré, the pioneering librarian who spread the magic of storytelling across America, sharing Puerto Rican folktales and planting the seeds for bilingual literature. An inspiring picture book biography, brought to vivid life by lyrical text and stunning illustrations, celebrates her remarkable legacy. Perfect for young pioneers who will continue her legacy, this must-have book dares you to dream big, just as Pura did.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Wonder

    R. J. Palacio

    This modern classic tells an unforgettable story of acceptance, empathy, and the transformative power of kindness, making it a must-have for any collection that values inspiring and thought-provoking fiction for readers of all ages.

    For: Recommended for readers aged 8 and up, as well as educators and parents seeking books to spark important conversations about inclusivity and compassion.

    Strengths: R. J. Palacio's masterful storytelling weaves together a diverse cast of characters, each with their own nuanced voices and relatable struggles, creating a rich tapestry of human experience.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the narrative's emotional intensity overwhelming, particularly scenes depicting bullying and social isolation.

    Comparable: For readers who appreciate character-driven stories tackling complex social issues, other notable titles include To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee and The Giver by Lois Lowry.

    “Palacio has an exceptional knack for writing realistic conversation and describing the thoughts and emotions of the characters...A well-written, thought-provoking book.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Day You Begin

    Jacqueline Woodson

    "In The Day You Begin, Jacqueline Woodson's stirring verse and Rafael López's vibrant illustrations beautifully capture the courage it takes to navigate feeling alone and different. This poignant tale celebrates the bravery of stepping into the unknown and sharing one's story, reminding us that connection and understanding can bloom when we bravely open ourselves to others. A powerful, heartening book for anyone who's ever felt like an outsider."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. The Hero Next Door A We Need Diverse Books Anthology

    Olugbemisola Rhuday-Perkovich

    This anthology stands out for its diverse range of voices and protagonists, celebrating small acts of kindness and redefining traditional notions of heroism.

    For: Middle-grade readers seeking relatable stories with a positive message.

    Strengths: Features a talented array of established and emerging authors, offering authentic, engaging tales that explore bravery in various forms.

    Skip if: At 13 stories, the collection may be too long for some younger readers or those with shorter attention spans.

    Comparable: Compared to Flying Lessons & Other Stories, The Hero Next Door also focuses on diverse voices and protagonists but narrows its theme to acts of bravery and heroism.

    “A stellar collection that, in celebrating heroes, helps readers find the universal in the specific.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Black Boy Joy 17 Stories Celebrating Black Boyhood

    Kwame Mbalia

    Black Boy Joy is a vibrant celebration of the power and joy of Black boyhood, featuring 17 uplifting stories by acclaimed Black authors. From intergalactic adventures to finding one's voice, this collection brings readers a range of perspectives and experiences that showcase the resilience, creativity, and spirit of Black boys. A must-read for anyone looking to revel in the beauty of Black joy.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. A World Worth Saving (National Book Award Finalist)

    Kyle Lukoff

    A World Worth Saving is a timely, thought-provoking novel that seamlessly blends fantasy and real-world issues, making it a must-read for middle-grade audiences seeking authentic trans experiences.

    For: Middle-grade readers, especially those interested in LGBTQ+ stories and fantasy adventures.

    Strengths: This book masterfully tackles transphobia and its impacts through a unique Jewish mythology lens, offering a powerful and hopeful story with a satisfying resolution.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the frequent shifts between fantasy and reality disorienting.

    Comparable: Fans of Hell Followed with Us by Andrew Joseph White will appreciate the similarly themed and atmospheric middle-grade fantasy novel with a strong LGBTQ+ focus.

    “This is a welcome fantasy starring a trans and Jewish hero that engages thoughtfully with both those identities.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Out of My Mind

    Sharon M. Draper

    In this powerful and moving novel, Melody, an 11-year-old girl with cerebral palsy, reveals her sharp intellect and vivid memories through her thoughts, challenging societal perceptions and prejudices. With a compelling and authentic voice, Sharon Draper crafts a story that sparks empathy and inspires readers to reevaluate assumptions about those with disabilities. Perfect for fans of uplifting, thought-provoking fiction.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. How Do You Spell Belong?

    Tiffany Hammond

    This heartfelt, visually engaging picture book humanizes the autistic experience, promoting acceptance and inclusivity for all children.

    For: Parents, educators, and caregivers seeking a positive, relatable portrayal of autism to share with children.

    Strengths: Vivid, sensory-rich illustrations and an authentic narrative voice convey Aidan's emotional journey, fostering empathy and understanding.

    Skip if: The themes and message may resonate more with an older, more socially aware audience than very young children.

    Comparable: Readers of A Day with No Words will appreciate How Do You Spell Belong? for its similarly impactful exploration of autism and social acceptance.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. When Aidan Became a Brother

    Kyle Lukoff

    Experience a heartwarming tale of family love and acceptance with When Aidan Became a Brother. This Stonewall Award-winning picture book chronicles a transgender boy's journey from self-discovery to embracing his new role as a big brother. Aidan's story teaches valuable lessons about honesty, communication, and unconditional love, making it an uplifting read for all ages.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What ages are these books suitable for?
Most titles are for kids aged 4-12, although some may be more suitable for younger or older readers.
Are all the books fiction?
No, the collection includes both fiction and non-fiction titles.
How do these books promote empathy and understanding?
Through relatable characters, diverse experiences, and storylines that encourage readers to walk in others' shoes.
