12 Must-Read Nonfiction Books for Animal Lovers

Discover engaging stories and insightful facts about amazing creatures with these top picks for animal enthusiasts.

Who these books are for

  • Pet owners
  • Wildlife enthusiasts
  • Nature lovers
  • Kids aged 4-12
  • Adults seeking animal insights

Start here

If you only read one book about animal companionship, choose 'Better Pets' by Leigh Bardugo.

Immerse yourself in the fascinating world of animals with this thoughtful selection of nonfiction books. From heartwarming stories of human-animal bonds to informative explorations of the natural world, these titles cater to a wide range of interests. Whether you're a seasoned wildlife enthusiast or simply an animal lover, you're sure to find something captivating in this collection.

The collection

  1. The Bakery Dragon

    Devin Elle Kurtz

    This heartwarming tale of an unlikely hero and his passion for baking showcases Kurtz's imaginative storytelling and adorable illustrations, making it a standout addition to any picture book collection.

    For: Recommended for early readers and parents looking for a feel-good story to share.

    Strengths: Kurtz's artwork brings Ember's world to life with charming details and emotive expressions, while the narrative celebrates the joy of learning, friendship, and the power of sharing.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the pacing a tad slow with a focus on Ember's apprenticeship.

    Comparable: Fans of whimsical, food-themed picture books like 'The Little Mouse, the Red Ripe Strawberry, and the Big Hungry Bear' by Don and Audrey Wood will enjoy Kurtz's 'The Bakery Dragon'.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. Dragons Love Tacos

    Adam Rubin

    Get ready for a hilarious romp that will leave you in stitches! Adam Rubin's Dragons Love Tacos brings together the most unlikely of friends - dragons and tacos. This laugh-out-loud tale is the perfect read-aloud for kids and adults alike, full of silly antics and spicy surprises. With Rubin's witty words and Daniel Salmieri's whimsical illustrations, this #1 New York Times bestseller is a must-have for any book collection.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. InvestiGators: Weather or Not

    John Patrick Green

    In the ninth groove-tastic installment of the InvestiGators series, John Patrick Green brings the zany fun: when milk takes over the city's water supply, Mango and Brash must solve the soggy mystery, aided by simian scientists and some curious feline agents. With signature slapstick humor and colorful illustrations, this slippery case is sure to delight young detectives once again.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. Better Pets

    Leigh Bardugo

    This whimsical tale of pet adoption cleverly pokes fun at trends while promoting empathy for the right match, making it a delightful addition to any children's library.

    For: Children aged 4-8 and their parents or caregivers will enjoy this humorous, heartwarming story.

    Strengths: Better Pets offers a lighthearted, engaging exploration of the importance of compatibility between pet and owner, with a dash of humor and imagination.

    Skip if: Some readers may find the story's message, while well-intentioned, a bit simplistic and its humor somewhat one-note.

    Comparable: Fans of humorous, imaginative picture books like The True Story of the Three Little Pigs or The Stinky Cheese Man and Other Fairly Stupid Tales may appreciate Better Pets' playful approach to storytelling.

    “The message—that the right pet is the one that fits your life—lands with grace... A witty charmer, rife with both heart and puns.”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. It's My Bird-Day!

    Mo Willems

    Join the lovable, scheming Pigeon for his most anticipated day yet - BIRD-DAY! In this hilarious new adventure from Caldecott honoree Mo Willems, the Pigeon's big day unfolds with surprise, delight, and laughter as he eagerly awaits presents and a special birthday treat. Get ready to celebrate the joy of bird-days with this charming, eagerly-awaited addition to the beloved Pigeon series.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. And Tango Makes Three

    Justin Richardson

    Celebrate the power of love and family with this heartwarming true story in board book form. At the Central Park Zoo, Roy and Silo, two male penguins, build a devoted partnership and, with the help of a thoughtful zookeeper, adopt an adorable chick of their own. Perfect for little hands, this sturdy edition of And Tango Makes Three shares a universal message of acceptance and the joy of a nontraditional family.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. Pug Goes to Hollywood: A Branches Book (Diary of a Pug #15)

    Kyla May

    Join Bella and her adorable pug Bub on a hilarious and heartwarming adventure to Hollywood in this exciting new installment of the Diary of a Pug series! With full-color illustrations, humorous misadventures, and a powerful friendship message, this early chapter book is a must-read for any pug lover or young reader looking for a fun, relatable tale. See if Bub can turn his Hollywood disappointment into an inspiring role model moment in this charming and engaging story.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Big Rig Pig

    Claire Tattersfield

    Big Rig Pig's engaging rhymes, humorous illustrations, and heartwarming message make it an excellent addition to any children's book collection.

    For: Preschool to early elementary school age children, especially those who enjoy stories about vehicles and friendship.

    Strengths: The book's bouncy rhymes and playful art create an entertaining read-aloud experience, while its exploration of community and accepting help provides a valuable life lesson for young readers.

    Skip if: Some parents may find the story's repetitive structure and Miles' single-minded focus on helping others a bit simplistic.

    Comparable: Big Rig Pig shares a similar lighthearted tone and focus on friendship with popular books like Little Blue Truck and Goodnight, Goodnight, Construction Site.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Leo's Lobo

    Melissa Cristina Márquez

    In Melissa Cristina Márquez's delightful picture book Leo's Lobo, young Leo discovers a magical animal shelter on a family outing and bonds with an alebrije named Lobo. The story celebrates Mexican culture and the importance of community as Leo learns that raising a pet, even a fantastical one, requires teamwork. Whimsical illustrations by Maria Gabriela Gama bring this charming tale to life, making it a must-read for kids and pet lovers alike.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. The Bakery Dragon and the Giant Cookie

    Devin Elle Kurtz

    Ember's heartwarming holiday journey, combined with Kurtz's vivid illustrations, make this a delightful seasonal read perfect for families and fans of whimsical fantasy.

    For: Children aged 4-8, parents, and anyone seeking a cozy, festive story.

    Strengths: Captures the spirit of Christmas with a charming dragon protagonist, engaging narrative, and beautiful illustrations that bring the snowy world to life.

    Skip if: The plot is relatively straightforward and may not offer surprising twists for older readers.

    Comparable: Readers who enjoy The Polar Express, How the Grinch Stole Christmas!, or other festive children's books with a touch of magic will appreciate this holiday tale.

    “This tale treads familiar ground, but with its lush visuals and likable characters, it”

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  11. Coco and Stephen, Together Forever How a Kitten and Bunny Became Best Friends

    Beth Stern

    This heartwarming tale of an unlikely friendship between a kitten and a bunny, inspired by the author's real-life experiences, promotes the joy of rescue and adoption while showcasing the special bond between pets and people.

    For: Children aged 4-8, animal lovers, and families looking for a gentle, uplifting story.

    Strengths: Softer digital illustrations bring the adorable duo to life, while the simple, repetitive text makes it accessible for early readers. The story's message about finding forever homes and friendship shines through.

    Skip if: Some may find the digital art less engaging than traditional illustrations. The narrative is straightforward, lacking a complex plot.

    Comparable: Fans of animal-centric stories like 'Warthog Princess' or 'The One and Only Ivan' will appreciate this tale of interspecies friendship and finding a forever home.

    Buy on Bookshop.org
