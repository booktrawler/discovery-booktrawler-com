Where to Start with The Lost Book of Lancelot

Begin with John Glynn's debut novel, The Lost Book of Lancelot, which offers a fresh take on the Arthurian legend, blending historical fiction with magical realism.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of historical fiction and fantasy seeking a unique retelling of the Arthurian mythos
  • Readers interested in magical realism and its applications in literary fiction

Start here

If you only read one book that reimagines the Arthurian legend, choose The Lost Book of Lancelot for its compelling narrative and innovative storytelling.

John Glynn's The Lost Book of Lancelot offers a captivating reinvention of the Arthurian legend, skillfully blending historical accuracy with magical realism to create a unique reading experience. This debut novel serves as an excellent introduction to Glynn's imaginative storytelling style, perfect for those looking to explore the timeless mythos of King Arthur and his knights in a new and captivating light.

Steeped in medieval myth, "The Lost Book of Lancelot" is an immersive, unforgettable tale of war, fate, and the transformative power of love at the heart of Camelot.

Frequently asked questions

What is the main plot of The Lost Book of Lancelot?
The novel follows a young man named Jack who uncovers a mysterious, ancient book said to hold the secrets of King Arthur and his knights, leading him on a quest to understand the true nature of the legendary figures.
Is The Lost Book of Lancelot part of a series?
No, The Lost Book of Lancelot stands as a standalone novel, allowing readers to fully immerse themselves in its self-contained narrative.
How does The Lost Book of Lancelot differ from other retellings of the Arthurian legend?
Glynn's novel combines historical fiction with magical realism, offering a fresh and imaginative perspective on the well-known characters and events of the legend.
