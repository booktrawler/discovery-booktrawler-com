Where to Start with Self-Improvement and Personal Growth

Begin with Rachel Mansfield's 'More, Please!' for an engaging, relatable guide to setting boundaries and cultivating self-care.

Who these books are for

  • Anyone seeking personal growth and self-improvement
  • Individuals looking to manage stress and prioritize their well-being
  • Relationships and friendships seeking better communication and boundaries

Start here

If you only read one, choose 'More, Please!' for its practical, real-life advice.

Embark on a personal growth journey with our curated selection of self-help books. 'More, Please!' by Rachel Mansfield is an excellent starting point, providing practical guidance on setting boundaries, self-care, and cultivating a more fulfilling life. From there, delve into other titles that resonate with your goals and interests, such as 'Codependent No More' for overcoming addictive relationships or 'Fair Play' for achieving a more balanced partnership. These books offer a supportive path to enhance your well-being, relationships, and overall quality of life.

    Rachel Mansfield

    Ditch the stress and delight your family with Rachel Mansfield's warm, inviting cookbook, More, Please! With an impressive 1.5 million followers hanging on her every culinary suggestion, Mansfield understands the struggle of balancing busy lives with wholesome meals. This game-changing guide features a clever, non-traditional approach, offering flexible solutions for any time of day. From stress-free breakfasts and lunches to satisfying dinners and snacks, every approachable recipe is designed to be quick, reliable, and repeatable for even the pickiest eaters and most hectic schedules. Whether you're gluten-free, dairy-free, or just looking for delicious shortcuts, Mansfield's recipes deliver on flavor and ease, perfect for the modern family on the go.   2. More Than Enough A Novel

    Anna Quindlen

    In Anna Quindlen's heartwarming novel More Than Enough, a high school English teacher named Polly Goodman's life is upended when she discovers the surprising results of an ancestry test. As Polly delves into her past for answers, the crises that beset her book club circle of four friends force Polly to confront the meanings of family, friendship, and finding oneself once again. With Quindlen's signature warmth, humor, and insight, this moving story explores how the relationships that define us can lead to profound personal growth and transformation.

    Chelsea Fagan

    Host like a pro with this modern guide, packed with practical tips and stylish inspiration for planning and enjoying every type of gathering. From cocktail hours to dinner parties, adult sleepovers to being a great guest, author Chelsea Fagan shares her expertise on curating spaces, menus, and moods for unforgettable connections. With a dash of history, humor, and gorgeous visuals, Having People Over will make you the perfect host and foster deeper communities in your life.

No!: A First Conversation About Consent" introduces young children to the concept of bodily autonomy and respectfully saying "No." By normalizing these discussions from age two, you'll foster self-awareness, self-esteem, and a more empathetic, empowered child.

  5. Somewhere Beyond the Sea

    TJ Klune

    Dive into a mesmerizing tale of forbidden love and second chances with TJ Klune's heart-stirring novel, Somewhere Beyond the Sea. When navigator Dani Ramos returns to his hometown years after a shattering summer, he's forced to confront the ocean of emotions he never thought he'd feel again - until he reconnects with the one person able to chart a course for his heart: Ryan, his first love and the reason he left in the first place. Klune masterfully weaves together themes of identity, family, and the power of embracing one's true self in this poignant, deeply romantic exploration of what it means to find home in both the places we're from and the people we love.

    Ezra Klein

    In Abundance, Ezra Klein and Derek Thompson boldly challenge the prevailing ideology of scarcity that has hindered progress. Drawing on groundbreaking research, they argue that today's crises - from housing shortages to climate inaction - stem from our failure to adapt 20th-century solutions for the 21st century. The duo offers a visionary roadmap to revitalize the politics of plenty, rebuilding institutions to meet modern challenges and unlock a future of shared abundance for all.
