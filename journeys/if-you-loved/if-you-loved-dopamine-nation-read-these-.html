If You Loved Dopamine Nation, Read These 8 Books

Dopamine Nation fans will revel in these equally thought-provoking and deeply personal explorations of addiction, culture, and the human condition.

Who these books are for

  • Fans of Dopamine Nation looking for similar thought-provoking reads
  • Anyone interested in addiction, culture, and the human condition

Start here

If you only read one, choose Dopamine Kids by Michaeleen Doucleff for its equally insightful look at the impact of technology and addiction on young minds.

Dopamine Nation's unflinching exploration of addiction, technology, and the human condition has captivated readers. If you're eager for more books that delve into these weighty topics with equal insight and personal depth, this curated list is for you. From non-fiction memoirs to imaginative fiction, these 8 reads offer a diverse yet interconnected series of literary journeys that will resonate with fans of Tara Haelle's groundbreaking work.

The collection

  1. Dopamine Kids A Science-Based Plan to Rewire Your Child's Brain and Take Back Your Family in the Age of Screens and Ultraprocessed Foods

    Michaeleen Doucleff

    In Dopamine Kids, journalist and mother Michaeleen Doucleff offers a groundbreaking 5-step plan to break the cycle of screen and processed food addiction in children, empowering parents to reclaim their families. By embracing a science-backed approach that shifts focus from depriving pleasure to cultivating joy, Dopamine Kids provides concrete strategies for establishing healthy habits, outfitting homes as screen-free sanctuaries, and guiding kids towards activities that truly fulfill their needs. A must-read for modern families seeking to raise confident, happy kids in an age of pervasive technology and unhealthy convenience foods.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  2. To Kill a Mockingbird

    Harper Lee

    In the sweltering summer of 1935, racial tensions simmer just beneath the surface of Maycomb, Alabama. Against this searing backdrop, Scout Finch learns the harsh realities of justice, innocence, and the unyielding human spirit. Through the eyes of a child, Harper Lee masterfully weaves a timeless tale of moral courage in 'To Kill a Mockingbird', a novel that continues to challenge and inspire generations with its poignant exploration of a community grappling with the shadows of its past.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  3. Carl's Doomsday Scenario

    Matt Dinniman

    Dive into the hilarious and action-packed adventures of Carl and his unlikely sidekick, Princess Donut, as they navigate a perilous post-apocalyptic world transformed into a dystopian video game. With new challenges and shocking mysteries emerging in the ravaged "Over City," Carl must summon bravery, wit, and somehow pants to unravel the darkness threatening all. Will their ratings and unbreakable team dynamic be enough to save the day? Find out in thisSide-splitting, fast-paced ride full of fantasy, humor, and heart.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  4. The Phantom Tollbooth

    Norton Juster

    Step through the Tollbooth and discover a world where words come alive and even the most mundane day can be transformed into an unforgettable adventure. Join Milo on his whimsical journey as he outwits the watchdog Tock, rescues Rhyme and Reason, and learns that the blandest existence can hold the most exciting secrets. With Jules Feiffer's iconic illustrations illuminating Norton Juster's clever wordplay, The Phantom Tollbooth is a timeless classic that will charm and delight generations of readers.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  5. A Little Devil in America In Praise of Black Performance

    Hanif Abdurraqib

    In "A Little Devil in America: In Praise of Black Performance", Hanif Abdurraqib weaves together essays that celebrate the resilience, heartbreak, and joy of Black art and music from various eras. Drawing from Soul Train, Aretha Franklin, James Brown to Beyoncé, he reveals the multilayered significance of performances in American culture. Abdurraqib's prose--brimming with jubilation and pain--makes the familiar and timeless feel desperately urgent. This masterful exploration is a must-read for anyone who loves Black performance and its profound impact on our world.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  6. Another Country

    James Baldwin

    Immerse yourself in James Baldwin's vivid portrayal of interwoven passions spanning Greenwich Village, Harlem, and France. In this groundbreaking novel, the masks of identity are stripped away by the raw intensity of love and hate, revealing the elemental beauty and sublime complexity of the human experience. A must-read for anyone seeking a deeply moving and thought-provoking exploration of race, sex, politics, and art.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  7. American Fantasy A Novel

    Emma Straub

    Join Annie on a delightful journey of self-discovery as she boards the American Fantasy cruise ship, where '90s heartthrobs reign supreme. A lighthearted and nostalgic tale, this novel reignites passion and possibility in middle age, as Annie reconnects with her youth and finds new meaning in unexpected places. A witty exploration of fame, aging, and love, Emma Straub's American Fantasy is the perfect escape.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  8. Do the Work! An Antiracist Activity Book

    W Kamau Bell

    "Ready to make a meaningful impact? DO THE WORK! reveals the insidious nature of systemic racism through engaging activities, games, and thought-provoking exercises. With everything from interactive puzzles to challenging conversation scripts, this innovative workbook equips you with the tools to critically examine and dismantle racism. Dive in and discover hundreds of ideas to amplify your antiracist actions."

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  9. Famesick A Memoir

    Lena Dunham

    In her candid memoir Famesick, Lena Dunham peers unflinchingly at the toll fame takes on her body and psyche. From wrestling with chronic illness to grappling with the loneliness and darker shadows of celebrity, Dunham incisively explores the costs of chasing her creative ambitions. With humor, vulnerability, and hard-won insight, she confronts the wreckage and finds a path to self-discovery, love, and redemption amidst the glamour and chaos of her remarkable life.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

  10. Harriet Tubman: Live in Concert A Novel

    Bob the Drag Queen

    In this inventive reimagining of Harriet Tubman's story, the iconic abolitionist teams up with a former hip-hop producer to create a revolutionary album and live show. As they craft music that will captivate the nation, they must also confront their shared histories of oppression and find a path to healing and liberation. With its unique blend of music, history, and emotional depth, Harriet Tubman: Live in Concert is a groundbreaking novel that will resonate long after the final note fades away.

    Buy on Bookshop.org

Frequently asked questions

What kind of books are recommended in these Dopamine Nation read-alikes?
The suggested books are a mix of memoir, non-fiction, and fiction that explore addiction, the human condition, and cultural insights.
Are there any fictional works in this list?
Yes, The Phantom Tollbooth and American Fantasy offer imaginative storytelling that, like Dopamine Nation, critiques societal norms and ponders existential questions.
Can I trust these recommendations if I haven't read Dopamine Nation?
Each book shares some thematic overlap with Dopamine Nation, such as probing the complexities of addiction, identity, and human connection, making them suitable for readers looking for similar thought-provoking journeys.
